**The Eternal Relay**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The party reunites a childhood swimming team that once shared a relay victory before drifting apart. Each member has their own relationship with swimming—one swims to feel free, another to prove themselves, another out of family legacy obligation. Through rebuilding friendship and competing in prestigious meets, they discover swimming isn't about beating each other but making each other faster. The relay race becomes a metaphor for their eternal bonds—four individuals swimming perfectly to pass the baton as one team.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail their childhood relay victory that bonded the team before circumstances separated them.
    *   Write the history of Iwatobi High's swimming program and its facilities.
    *   Describe Rin's father's swimming legacy and how his death shaped Rin's competitive drive.
    *   Explain the progression of swim meets: local, regional, nationals, and qualifying times.
    *   Detail the four competitive strokes and their technical requirements.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **Iwatobi High Swim Team** (Major): Reformed team swimming for friendship and personal growth.
    *   **Samezuka Academy** (Major): Elite boarding school's team focused on victory and excellence.
    *   **Regional Rival Schools** (Minor): Various schools with competitive programs.
    *   **National Swimming Federation** (Minor): Organizing body managing meets and records.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Haruka Nanase (free spirit), Rin Matsuoka (rival), Makoto Tachibana (heart), Rei Ryugazaki (theorist), Gou Matsuoka (manager).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Swimming Coach" (technical trainer), "Rival Swimmer" (competitive opponents), "Team Captain" (leadership roles), "Former Champion" (mentors and inspiration).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Iwatobi High Pool:** School facility with regulation 50m pool, slightly aged but maintained, locker rooms, modest spectator area.
*   **Samezuka Academy:** Elite boarding school with Olympic-quality pool, training facilities, conditioning rooms, state-of-the-art equipment.
*   **Beach Training Camp:** Coastal facility for conditioning—ocean swimming, running on sand, endurance training.
*   **National Aquatics Center:** Modern championship venue with Olympic-regulation pools, timing systems, spectator seating for thousands, broadcast facilities.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Team achieves perfect relay exchange → **THEN:** Time bonus, confidence boost, synchronized flow state unlocked.
*   **IF:** Rin loses to friendship-based team → **THEN:** Questions his motivations, begins remembering joy of swimming.
*   **IF:** Individual breaks personal best → **THEN:** Confidence increases, inspires teammates, closer to national qualifying time.
*   **IF:** Qualify for Nationals → **THEN:** Face elite competition, media attention, pressure to perform at peak.
*   **IF:** Achieve synchronized flow state → **THEN:** Perfect relay performance, each swimmer feels the others, time slows in the water.
