# The Archetypal Realm - Creative Brief

## Visual Style
**Aesthetic:** Jungian psychology meets world mythology meets religious iconography. Every symbol, every figure, every landscape resonates with universal meaning.

**Color Palette:**
- **Primordial Self**: Pure white light containing all colors
- **Hero's Journey**: Sunrise golds, quest reds, victory blues
- **Shadow Lands**: Deep purples, blacks, hidden silvers
- **Great Mother**: Earth tones, womb reds, comfort pastels
- **Trickster's Domain**: Impossible colors, shifting hues
- **The Self Mandala**: Geometric perfection, balanced spectrum

## Art Direction
- **Archetypal Figures**: Classical but universal—every culture's version simultaneously
- **Symbolic Landscapes**: Literal psychology—rivers of memory, forests of family
- **The Collective Unconscious**: M.C. Escher meets religious art meets Cave of Forgotten Dreams
- **Mandalas**: Geometric perfection indicating wholeness/fragmentation
- **Cultural Overlap**: Norse, Greek, Egyptian, Hindu, Native, all mythologies coexisting

## Music and Sound
**Main Theme:** Orchestral grandeur meets primordial drumming—ancient and eternal
**Archetypal Themes**: Each archetype has musical signature:
- Hero: Triumphant brass
- Shadow: Dissonant strings
- Trickster: Unpredictable percussion
- Great Mother: Warm cello
- Wise Old Man: Meditative winds
- Primordial Self: OM chant, harmonic convergence

**Sound Design:**
- Sacred geometry sounds (frequencies of shapes)
- Multilingual whispers (all languages at once)
- Mythological ambience (timeless caves, eternal forests)
- Mandala resonance (harmony vs. discord)

## Voice Direction
**Primordial Self**: All voices at once, speaking in unison, no individual tone
**Wise Old Man**: Every mentor actor (Ian McKellen + James Earl Jones + Morgan Freeman essence)
**Shadow**: Character's own voice but darker, knowing
**The Trickster**: Gleeful chaos, shifting accents, unreliable narration
**Great Mother**: Warm comfort turning to possessive need
**Archetypal versions**: Each player voices their archetype differently than their character

## Cinematic Moments
- **Entering Collective Unconscious**: Reality becoming symbol
- **Meeting Your Shadow**: Confronting what you deny
- **The Hero's Journey**: Classic monomyth visualized
- **Primordial Self Contact**: Experiencing unity
- **Creating Your Mandala**: Art as character development
- **Final Integration**: Wholeness achieved or lost

## References
- **Visual**: Religious iconography, Jungian diagrams, Joseph Campbell's work, world mythology
- **Films**: What Dreams May Come, The Fountain, The Fall
- **Literature**: Jung's writings, Campbell's Hero's Journey, Sandman by Gaiman
- **Art**: Mandala designs, religious art across cultures, archetypal imagery
- **Games**: Persona series, Jungian themes in games
