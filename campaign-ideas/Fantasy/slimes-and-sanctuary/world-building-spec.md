### **World-Building Specification Brief**

This document is the primary instruction set for the world-building AI pipeline. Each section provides explicit directives for specialized agents to generate the necessary assets for a complete and dynamic campaign world.

---

**Slimes and Sanctuary**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise.
*   **Content:** Outcasts with "weak" abilities—particularly the power to tame only slimes and other "worthless" creatures—begin a journey helping rejected monsters and people. They discover that beings dismissed as useless have unique potential when treated with kindness. The campaign follows their journey to establish a permanent sanctuary for outcasts, proving that worth isn't measured by power but by the community we build.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the world's power hierarchy—strong tamers command dragons and dire beasts, weak tamers are exiled.
    *   Explain the cultural belief that worth equals combat power and economic utility.
    *   Chronicle the history of "pest extermination"—treating weak monsters as nuisances to eliminate.
    *   Describe the rare historical sanctuaries that protected outcasts and how most failed.
    *   Write Grandmother Willow's backstory—her exile, building the Grove, maintaining hope for decades.
    *   Detail the Network—underground society of outcasts helping each other survive.
    *   Explain why slimes and similar creatures are considered worthless despite useful abilities.
    *   Chronicle previous attempts to prove weak creatures have value and why they failed before.
    *   Describe Baron Valorin's childhood trauma and how rejection shaped his cruelty.
    *   Detail Thornwatch Fortress's history—built to protect, abandoned, reclaimed by outcasts, now sanctuary site.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Outcasts (Party & Allies)** (Minor, Protagonist)
        *   **Goals:** Survive, help others, build sanctuary for rejected beings.
        *   **Hierarchy:** No formal structure, consensus-based decisions, found family.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Wanderers helping anyone in need.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Revolutionary act—proving worth without power.
        *   **Relationships:** Supported by the Network, opposed by traditionalists, slowly winning hearts.

    *   **The Network** (Minor, Allied)
        *   **Goals:** Mutual aid for outcasts, survive hostile world, spread message of acceptance.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Decentralized web of safe houses and allies.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Appears non-existent—operates in secret.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Building alternative society based on compassion.
        *   **Relationships:** Provides resources and information to party, eventually becomes sanctuary residents.

    *   **Traditional Tamer Guilds** (Major, Opposed)
        *   **Goals:** Maintain power hierarchy, preserve guild prestige, control taming profession.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Ranked by power of tamed creatures—dragonlords at top.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Protect society from dangerous monsters.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Maintain social stratification benefiting elite tamers.
        *   **Relationships:** See party as threat to established order, attempt to discredit them.

    *   **Monster Extermination Corps** (Minor, Antagonistic)
        *   **Goals:** Eliminate "pest" creatures for profit.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Independent contractors and small companies.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Public safety.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Profit from fear and elimination quotas.
        *   **Relationships:** Direct opponents until some are redeemed.

    *   **Accepting Communities** (Minor, Allied)
        *   **Goals:** Live peacefully welcoming diversity.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Democratic, community-led.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Prove acceptance creates stronger society.
        *   **Relationships:** Support sanctuary project, provide model for what's possible.

    *   **Noble Courts** (Major, Mixed)
        *   **Goals:** Maintain order, manage territories, respond to political pressure.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Feudal—Baron Valorin rules local region under distant king.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Govern fairly.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Most nobles prioritize power and prestige over justice.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially oppose sanctuary, can be convinced through demonstration.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **Grandmother Willow:** Elderly druid maintaining first sanctuary, party's mentor.
    *   **Pip Brightstone:** Magicless child inspired by party's kindness.
    *   **Jax Ironfang:** Monster hunter on redemption path.
    *   **Lady Seraphine Goldleaf:** Noble patron using wealth for good.
    *   **Baron Aldric Valorin:** Primary antagonist wounded by own rejection.
    *   **Glorp:** First slime companion, mascot of found family.
    *   **Whisper:** Mimic companion proving creatures can create rather than deceive.

*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Archetypes):**
    *   "Rejected Tamer (various weak specialties)"
    *   "Weak Monster (slimes, rust monsters, mimics, awakened plants, etc.)"
    *   "Network Member (various outcast types)"
    *   "Traditionalist Tamer (elite guild members)"
    *   "Sympathetic Villager (willing to change views)"
    *   "Prejudiced Authority (needs convincing)"
    *   "Fellow Sanctuary Builder (helping establish community)"

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **The Wandering Road:** Pastoral paths connecting settlements, where outcasts meet and travel.
    *   **Village of Stone Hearts:** First community party helps despite receiving no gratitude, begins softening prejudice.
    *   **The Grove of Small Things:** Grandmother Willow's sanctuary, peaceful refuge for injured creatures.
    *   **Festival Town (Brighthollow):** Rare accepting community showing society can be better.
    *   **Thornwatch Fortress:** Ancient ruins becoming permanent sanctuary, represents hope made manifest.
    *   **Baron Valorin's Estate:** Sterile perfection hiding inner wounds, contrast to sanctuary's organic growth.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** Party consistently helps without expectation of reward: Network supports them, resources appear when needed, Kindness Karma high.
    *   **IF:** Party acts selfishly or cruelly: Network withdraws support, harder journey, fewer allies during final conflict.
    *   **IF:** Party successfully befriends "worthless" creatures: Discover unique abilities, create tactical options, prove worth to skeptics.
    *   **IF:** Party neglects bonded creatures: Creatures withdraw, lose abilities, emotional consequence of taking kindness for granted.
    *   **IF:** Party redeems Jax the hunter: Gain powerful ally and advocate, hunter network stops opposing sanctuary.
    *   **IF:** Jax remains antagonist: Recurring enemy complicates sanctuary defense, represents failure to extend compassion.
    *   **IF:** Party impresses at festival: Lady Seraphine offers land, noble support increases, public opinion shifts.
    *   **IF:** Festival goes poorly: Sanctuary project delayed, must find alternative path.
    *   **IF:** Party defends Thornwatch creatures without violence: Shows consistency of compassion, easier to redeem Baron.
    *   **IF:** Party uses violence against Baron's forces: He sees them as hypocrites, harder redemption path.
    *   **IF:** Party reaches Baron's heart: He joins sanctuary as redeemed member, powerful ally, represents hope for change.
    *   **IF:** Baron remains antagonistic: Final conflict is combat rather than conversation, bittersweet victory.
    *   **IF:** Sanctuary proves social value: Royal recognition granted, permanent protection, model for future sanctuaries.

**7. Weak Creature Abilities System**
*   **Philosophy:** Every creature has value when treated with kindness. Traditional power hierarchies blind society to unique potentials.

*   **Slime Types & Abilities:**
    *   **Common Slimes:** Acid immunity aura for allies, storage space in body, environmental purification.
    *   **Healing Slimes:** Minor HP regeneration, cure poison/disease, soothing presence.
    *   **Metal Slimes:** Heat resistance, metal reshaping, rare and shy.
    *   **Crystal Slimes:** Light refraction, illuminate darkness, communication through color.
    *   **All slimes:** Shape-shifting for utility, non-threatening combat support, maximum adorableness.

*   **Other Weak Creatures:**
    *   **Rust Monsters:** Careful metal cleaning, curse removal from items, nonlethal weapon disabling.
    *   **Mimics:** Perfect disguise for infiltration, performance art, teaching through example.
    *   **Awakened Plants:** Medicinal herb growth, defensive terrain, food production, environmental restoration.
    *   **Gelatinous Cubes:** Waste disposal, dungeon cleaning, transparent barriers.

*   **Bonding Requirements:** Time, kindness, understanding individual needs, never exploitation. Treat as friends, not tools.

**8. Kindness Karma System**
*   **Directive:** Track party's genuine compassion throughout campaign.
*   **High Karma Benefits:** The Network provides resources, NPCs offer help, lucky coincidences favor party, inspiration points awarded, advantage on social checks with good-aligned beings.
*   **Low Karma Consequences:** World becomes harsher, fewer allies, resources scarce, cynical NPCs resist party.
*   **Mechanical Representation:** Acts of selfless kindness grant Karma points. Cruel or selfish acts reduce them. Karma level affects world state.
*   **Philosophy:** Represents truth that communities support those who support others.

**9. The Journey Timeline**
*   **Session 1:** Party exiled, meet on road, bond with first outcasts, establish pattern of helping.
*   **Session 2:** Help ungrateful village, prove kindness doesn't require acceptance, escape persecution.
*   **Session 3:** Discover the Grove, meet Grandmother Willow, see what sanctuary can be.
*   **Session 4:** Confront monster hunter, opportunity for redemption, protect sanctuary.
*   **Session 5:** Festival success, showcase creatures' value, receive sanctuary offer.
*   **Session 6:** Discover Thornwatch Fortress, meet resident creatures, face extermination threat.
*   **Session 7:** Defend sanctuary creatively, diplomatic appeal, confront Baron's pain.
*   **Session 8:** Resolution through compassion or conflict, sanctuary recognized, legacy established.

**10. Baron Valorin's Psychology**
*   **Childhood:** Born weak, mocked mercilessly, rejected by peers.
*   **Overcompensation:** Became obsessed with strength and eliminating anything weak.
*   **Projection:** Sees weak creatures as reminder of painful past self.
*   **Defense Mechanism:** Cruelty to outcasts makes him feel powerful and distant from former weakness.
*   **Redemption Path:** Seeing outcasts find joy despite rejection challenges his worldview. Party's consistent kindness even to enemies reaches his heart. He must choose: continue running from past or accept all parts of himself.
*   **Redeemed Arc:** Becomes sanctuary's most passionate defender, using authority to protect outcasts, healing through helping.

**11. Timeline (If Party Takes No Action)**
*   **Session 1:** Outcasts wander alone, isolated, eventually die from exposure or persecution.
*   **Session 2:** Village's pollution worsens, child Pip gives up hope.
*   **Session 3:** The Grove is discovered and destroyed by exterminators. Grandmother Willow killed.
*   **Session 4:** Hunter Jax continues killing creatures, accumulating guilt but no redemption.
*   **Session 5:** No festival success, weak creatures continue being exterminated systematically.
*   **Session 6:** Thornwatch creatures massacred by Baron's forces.
*   **Session 7:** Baron's cruelty unchallenged, continues persecuting outcasts.
*   **Session 8:** No sanctuary established. Outcasts remain scattered, hidden, hopeless. Society unchanged. Tragedy of inaction.

This specification creates a world where kindness is revolutionary, worth is inherent rather than earned, and building community with outcasts can genuinely change society. The campaign proves that compassion is not weakness but the foundation of true strength.
