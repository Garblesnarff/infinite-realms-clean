# Heavy Metal Hell - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Seven Demon Courts

**Thrash Court - The Speed Lords**
- **Territory:** The Rust Plains and Velocity Citadel
- **Population:** ~100,000 demons, mostly shock troops and speed demons
- **Government:** Meritocracy based on combat speed and aggression
- **Current Status:** Civil war between three contenders for Metallicus's throne
- **Military:** Fast attack forces, berserkers, speed-enhanced warriors
- **Culture:** Value raw aggression, speed, and directness; no deception
- **Conflicts:** Internal power struggle threatens to destroy the court

**Death Court - The Corpse Kingdom**
- **Territory:** The Bone Wastes and Catacomb Colosseum
- **Population:** ~200,000 (half living demons, half undead)
- **Government:** Necrocracy—immortal rulers guided by ancient dead
- **Current Status:** Stable under Lord Morbidus
- **Military:** Undead legions, necromancers, plague-bearers
- **Culture:** Intellectual, orderly, view undeath as evolution
- **Conflicts:** Debate over whether to end existence vs. seek immortality solution

**Black Metal Court - The Frozen Heresy**
- **Territory:** The Frost Wastes and Black Cathedral
- **Population:** ~75,000 zealot demons
- **Government:** Theocracy worshiping the Frozen Flame (paradox deity)
- **Current Status:** Planning coup against other courts
- **Military:** Frost knights, ice demons, winter elementals
- **Culture:** Fanatical, believes in cosmic purification through cold
- **Conflicts:** Secret war with Heaven, manipulated by angels

**Doom Court - The Slow Kingdom**
- **Territory:** The Crushing Wastes and Throne of Depression
- **Population:** ~50,000 (many are catatonic)
- **Government:** None (total anarchy since Desolatus went catatonic)
- **Current Status:** Collapsed, ruled by warlords
- **Military:** Impossibly powerful but incredibly slow warriors
- **Culture:** Nihilistic, believes existence is suffering
- **Conflicts:** Doom Wastes are actually the source of the Silence

**Power Metal Court - The Shining Empire**
- **Territory:** The Glory Peaks and Valorious Citadel
- **Population:** ~150,000 warrior demons
- **Government:** Absolute monarchy under divine-right philosophy
- **Current Status:** Strongest court militarily, plotting conquest
- **Military:** Dragon riders, paladins of hell, celestial warriors
- **Culture:** Heroic, believes in glory through conquest and honor
- **Conflicts:** Valorious secretly plans to betray everyone and escape hell

**Progressive Court - The Impossible Academy**
- **Territory:** The Shifted Lands and Academy of Eternity
- **Population:** ~80,000 scholar demons
- **Government:** Complex rotating committee system that changes by time signature
- **Current Status:** Neutral, studying the Silence
- **Military:** Reality-warpers, time mages, theoretical warriors
- **Culture:** Values complexity, knowledge, and innovation
- **Conflicts:** Chronostorm has solution to Silence but withholds it for leverage

**Nu-Metal Court - The Concrete Kingdom**
- **Territory:** The Broken Cities and Rage Dome
- **Population:** ~60,000 young demons and hybrid souls
- **Government:** Mob rule with Rage-Core as figurehead
- **Current Status:** Unstable, easily manipulated
- **Military:** Urban guerrillas, rage elementals, chaos bringers
- **Culture:** Anti-establishment, yet creates own tyranny
- **Conflicts:** Infiltrated by Silence cultists

### Minor Factions

**The Roadie Monks**
- Neutral order maintaining hell's infrastructure
- ~5,000 members across all territories
- Run safe havens, neutral zones, and equipment stores
- Led by Council of Twelve under Brother Lemmy
- Secret: Actually prison guards for the true purpose of hell
- Bound by ancient pact to never interfere in wars

**The Silence Cult**
- Growing movement worshiping the First Silence
- Unknown membership (cells operate secretly)
- Believe oblivion is preferable to eternal torment
- Sabotage demon lords' efforts
- Some members genuinely mercy-motivated, others nihilistic
- Led by The Quietus, mysterious figure (possibly Metallicus)

**The Damned Bards**
- Musicians who weren't good enough to rule but too good to be slaves
- ~10,000 across hell performing in various venues
- Possess significant soft power through information gathering
- Neutral but can be swayed by patronage
- Some are spies, some are just trying to survive
- Led by The Chorus, collective of legendary failed musicians

**The Forge Masters**
- Demon smiths who create Infernal Instruments
- ~2,000 artisans in the Seven Forges
- Technically serve all courts but maintain independence
- Possess secrets of how to make reality-warping artifacts
- Led by Vulcan Blackhammer, ancient demon craftsman
- Secret: Know how to forge weapon that could kill the Silence

**The Mosh Elementals**
- Beings of pure kinetic chaos energy
- Live in eternal mosh pits across hell
- Not intelligent individually but collective consciousness
- Can be bargained with through perfect moshing
- Neutral in wars but respect strength and fury
- Oldest force in hell, predate demon lords

## Location Blueprints

### The Velocity Citadel (Thrash Territory)

**Main Structure:**
- Built from crashed vehicles and scrapped speed demons
- Constantly vibrating from music and machinery
- 7 levels, each faster-paced than the last

**Key Areas:**
- **The Throne Hall:** Empty throne, three factions claim it
- **The Speed Trials:** Testing ground for fastest demons
- **The Armory:** Weapons powered by velocity
- **The War Room:** Maps of hell, planning conquest
- **The Lightning Forges:** Weapons made from captured lightning

**Defenses:**
- Speed-based traps (only fast enough can dodge)
- Automated weapon systems
- Berserker patrols
- Reality warps that slow intruders

### The Catacomb Colosseum (Death Territory)

**Structure:**
- Underground mega-dungeon
- 20 levels deep
- Each level is fighting arena

**Key Areas:**
- **The Grand Arena:** Seats million spectators
- **The Gladiator Pits:** Where undead warriors train
- **The Corpse Gardens:** Beautiful in horrific way
- **The Bone Library:** Knowledge carved in bones
- **Morbidus's Study:** Surprisingly cozy despite being in catacomb

**Features:**
- Necromantic energy sustains everyone
- Death doesn't stick (resurrect for next match)
- Blood irrigation system
- Spectator spirits of famous fighters

### The Frozen Cathedral (Black Metal Territory)

**Structure:**
- Cathedral made of black ice
- Corpses frozen in walls as decoration
- Five spires representing five blasphemies

**Key Areas:**
- **The Altar of Frozen Flame:** Paradox fire that freezes
- **The Choir Chamber:** Where Corpse-Painted Choir performs
- **The Confession Crypts:** Torture chambers
- **The Winter Throne:** Lord Infernus's seat
- **The Forest of Corpses:** Surrounding the cathedral

**Atmosphere:**
- Perpetual blizzard
- Screaming winds
- Frost that causes despair
- Religious horror

### The Throne of Depression (Doom Territory)

**Structure:**
- Massive chair carved from single mountain
- Lord Desolatus sits motionless
- Surrounded by dead land

**Key Areas:**
- **The Slow Fields:** Time moves at 1/10 speed
- **The Weight Chambers:** Gravity increases exponentially
- **The Echo Valleys:** Sound takes minutes to travel
- **The Temple of First Silence:** Where it all began

**Features:**
- Everything is crushing and oppressive
- Hope dies here
- Even visitors feel despair
- Some areas so slow they seem frozen

### Valorious Citadel (Power Metal Territory)

**Structure:**
- Shining fortress on mountain peak
- Gold and silver architecture
- Pennants flying, trumpets blaring

**Key Areas:**
- **The Golden Throne:** Valorious's magnificent seat
- **The Dragon Forges:** Where legendary weapons are made
- **The Training Yards:** Elite warriors practice
- **The Tournament Arena:** Constant competitions
- **The Trophy Halls:** Conquered artifacts displayed

**Atmosphere:**
- Epic and heroic
- Actually beautiful despite being in hell
- Inspiring rather than terrifying
- Deceptive—evil wearing shining armor

### The Impossible Academy (Progressive Territory)

**Structure:**
- Escher architecture
- Constantly reconfiguring
- Exists in multiple dimensions simultaneously

**Key Areas:**
- **The Library of All Songs:** Every song ever written
- **The Time Chambers:** Practice rooms where time dilates
- **The Laboratory of Sound:** Studying music as physics
- **Chronostorm's Observatory:** Watches dimensional barriers
- **The Equation Halls:** Mathematics made physical

**Features:**
- Gravity changes by room
- Time flows differently everywhere
- Some rooms exist only in certain time signatures
- Navigation requires music theory knowledge

### The Rage Dome (Nu-Metal Territory)

**Structure:**
- Converted prison complex
- Industrial hellscape
- Graffiti covers everything

**Key Areas:**
- **The Mosh Pit Central:** Massive circular arena
- **The Angst Manifold:** Where emotions become physical
- **The Concrete Gardens:** Urban decay aesthetic
- **Rage-Core's Throne:** Made from broken chains

**Atmosphere:**

## Historical Causality Chains

### Creation of Infernum Rex

**Timeline:**
- **Before Time:** Void existed, with First Silence
- **The First Note:** Creation began with music
- **The Musical War:** Gods of different genres fought
- **The Hell Forge:** Losers were cast into Infernum Rex
- **The Seven Courts:** Demon lords emerged from strongest fallen gods
- **The Eternal Concert:** Hell began operating as it does now

**Purpose:**
Hell isn't punishment for mortals—it's prison for fallen music gods. Demons are their descendants. The eternal torment serves to:
1. Keep them contained
2. Prevent them from returning to mortal realms
3. Drain their power through constant warfare
4. Eventually, complete their punishment and allow redemption (but this would take eons)

### The Rise of Metallicus

**History:**
- Started as minor demon in Thrash Court
- Rose through Battle of Bands competitions
- United Thrash demons under single rule
- Established balance of power between seven courts
- Created treaties preventing total war
- Became most respected demon lord
- Discovered the Silence threat
- Made terrible choice to become its avatar to control it

### The Silence's Previous Attacks

**Ancient History:**
- Destroyed three other musical dimensions
- Each time grew stronger
- Pattern: targets most creative/diverse realms
- Infernum Rex is most musically diverse (ironic)
- Previous attacks ended when dimensions were consumed
- This is first time it's been resisted

### The Millennium of Screams Prophecy

**Origin:**
- Prophesied by Ozzy the Eternal 1,000 years ago
- Written in the Tome of Inevitable Songs
- States: "When seven become six, and silence spreads, mortals shall forge the final chord"
- Interpretation was unclear until now
- Multiple possible meanings (designed by Ozzy to be flexible)

**Reality:**
Ozzy is excellent at pattern recognition. He saw:
- Dimensional barriers weakening
- Silence's pattern of attacks
- Mathematical certainty that mortals would arrive
- Wrote "prophecy" that would guide them
- Not divinely inspired—just really smart

## Additional Lore

### The Seven Infernal Instruments

Each demon lord wields a legendary instrument:

**1. Metallicus's Thunder Guitar** (Currently lost)
- Thrash personified
- Every chord causes lightning
- Location: Hidden in Velocity Citadel

**2. Morbidus's Corpse Drums**
- Made from ancient giant's bones
- Every beat raises the dead
- Can resurrect demons thought destroyed

**3. Infernus's Frozen Organ**
- Pipes made of frozen souls
- Plays hymns that cause blizzards
- Can freeze even fire

**4. Desolatus's Bass of Entropy**
- So heavy it bends space
- Notes resonate for hours
- Playing it risks dimensional collapse

**5. Valorious's Golden Harp**
- Strings made from dragon sinew
- Music inspires heroic fervor
- Can mind control weak-willed

**6. Chronostorm's Temporal Synthesizer**
- Anachronistic technology
- Changes time signatures of reality
- Can rewind small moments

**7. Rage-Core's Scream Mic**
- Amplifies emotions to dangerous levels
- Feedback loop can shatter dimensions
- Stolen from material plane (modern tech)

### The First Silence (Cosmology)

**Nature:**
Not a being, but a force—the natural end of all things. Before creation, there was silence. When creation ends, silence returns. It's not evil, it's inevitable. Like entropy, heat death, the end of all music.

**Consciousness:**
It shouldn't be conscious, but observers give it shape. By fearing it, fighting it, worshiping it, demons have given it will. It's become a god through belief.

**Goal:**
Return everything to the primordial silence. Not out of malice, but because that's its nature. A fire isn't evil for burning; silence isn't evil for silencing.

**Weakness:**
Music. Creativity. The act of making sound. As long as someone plays, it cannot fully manifest. The Eternal Concert is literally the only solution—music so powerful it rewrites the Silence's nature.

### Magic Item Lore

**Infernal Instruments (PC weapons):**
- Forged from soul-stuff of demons
- Bond to wielder permanently
- Grow in power as PC levels
- Each has personality (semi-sentient)
- Can be upgraded at the Seven Forges
- Ultimate form reveals true name

**Amplification Artifacts:**
- Amplifiers that boost musical magic
- Rarest: The Eternal Amp (can amplify entire band)
- Side effects: addiction, hearing damage, power corruption
- Black market trade in stolen/forged amps

**The Picks of Destiny:**
- Seven legendary guitar picks
- Each grants mastery of one genre
- Collecting all seven = ultimate power
- Scattered across hell
- Side quest material

### Social Dynamics

**Demon Society Hierarchy:**
1. **Demon Lords:** Untouchable rulers
2. **Court Members:** Nobles and generals
3. **Lesser Demons:** Soldiers and workers
4. **Damned Souls:** Servants and slaves
5. **The Silenced:** Those who failed musically (lowest caste)

**Path to Power:**
- Win Battle of Bands competitions
- Survive mosh pits
- Master an Infernal Instrument
- Gain fame through legendary performances
- Make pacts with powerful demons
- Accumulate followers

**Cultural Values:**
- Musical skill above all
- Strength and power respected
- Weakness punished
- Loyalty to band/court
- Honor in combat (even if brutal)
- Innovation vs. tradition (varies by court)

### Daily Life in Hell

**For Demons:**
- Constant competition
- Practice instruments for power
- Attend/perform at concerts
- Fight in wars
- Plot against rivals
- Surprisingly routine

**For Damned Souls:**
- Torment is their job (performers in eternal concert)
- No breaks, no escape
- Torture is literal performance art
- Some find meaning in it
- Others break completely
- Potential for promotion if talented

**Economy:**
- Soul currency (literal souls traded)
- Service-based (performance, combat, craft)
- Barter common
- Debt bondage widespread
- Infernal Instruments extremely valuable

### Religion and Philosophy

**The Fallen Faiths:**
Demons worship perverted versions of good gods:
- The Twisted Chord (evil music deity)
- The Screaming Void (anti-creation god)
- The Frozen Flame (paradox worship)

**Philosophical Schools:**
- **Eternalists:** Embrace endless existence
- **Nihilists:** Believe nothing matters
- **Redemptionists:** Seek way out of hell
- **Accelerationists:** Want to make hell worse to trigger transformation

**Death in Hell:**
Demons can "die" but reform in their court's resurrection chamber. True death requires:
- Complete soul destruction
- Consumption by greater demon
- Silence claiming them
- Voluntary dissolution

### The Secret Purpose

**Truth (DM eyes only):**
Hell was created by a coalition of good gods to contain the most dangerous fallen deities. It's a prison, not punishment. The eternal warfare keeps them drained. The Roadie Monks are guards. If hell falls, these beings could escape to material plane and other dimensions. The Silence threatens this containment—if hell ends, they're freed. Players must decide: maintain the prison (keep hell), transform it (reform), or risk destruction (free prisoners).

This moral complexity is the campaign's heart.
