# Eternal Nations Reborn

A Dungeons & Dragons 5e campaign about reincarnating as your favorite strategy game faction and building a nation from nothing.

*   **Genre**: Strategy Isekai, Nation Building, Tactical Fantasy
*   **Tone**: Strategic with Heart
*   **Difficulty**: Medium
*   **Length**: Medium Campaign

***

## Campaign Overview

*   **Title**: Eternal Nations Reborn
*   **Campaign Type/Genre**: Strategy game isekai focusing on nation building, tactical decision-making, and faction management
*   **Estimated Length**: A medium campaign of 7-8 sessions.
*   **Player Level Range**: Starting at Level 5, ending around Level 11.
*   **Tone**: Strategic and thoughtful with genuine emotion. Grand strategy meets personal stories. Building something from nothing. Underdog faction becoming powerful through smart choices. Mix of tactical combat, resource management, and character moments.
*   **Core Premise**: The party's leader was an obsessive player of "Eternal Nations," a grand strategy game where different factions vie for world domination. They loved playing as Mynoghra, the weakest evil god faction—underpowered demons with terrible starting position but unique mechanics. Upon death, they reincarnate into the game world as Mynoghra's god-king with the party as their first servants/advisors. Starting with nothing but a ruined shrine and handful of followers, they must build a nation capable of surviving in a world where other powerful factions want them destroyed. Success requires strategic thinking, tactical innovation, diplomatic maneuvering, and proving that "evil" faction can be more than its stereotype.

## Difficulty Context

**Medium Difficulty**: This campaign balances tactical combat, resource management, and strategic nation-building. Combat encounters are tactical—positioning, unit composition, and faction abilities matter more than raw power. Resources must be carefully managed—food, materials, population, research. Diplomatic choices affect which factions become enemies or allies. Rest opportunities exist but nation crises create time pressure. The difficulty comes from juggling multiple systems (combat, building, diplomacy, research) while maintaining faction survival. Death is possible but respawn mechanics exist (Mynoghra's unique trait). Standard rest rules with campaign layer complications.

## Story Arc

The narrative follows a nation-building progression over 7-8 sessions, from refugee settlement to recognized power.

*   **Act I: From Ashes We Rise (Sessions 1-2)**: The god-king awakens as Mynoghra's avatar with fragmented game knowledge. The party are their first divine servants. Together they find the destroyed Mynoghra capital—ruins inhabited by a handful of desperate demon refugees. With nearly nothing, they must establish basic infrastructure, recruit initial population, and survive first threats. Other factions see Mynoghra's revival as threat or opportunity. The party must prove their nation deserves to exist.

*   **Act II: The Growing Shadow (Sessions 3-5)**: With basic survival secured, Mynoghra begins expanding. The party completes quests to recruit unique units, research powerful technologies, establish trade routes, and forge alliances with unexpected factions. Each session introduces new strategic opportunities and threats. Human kingdoms fear demon resurgence. Monster factions test Mynoghra's strength. The party must balance growth with diplomacy, showing that Mynoghra can be strategic partner rather than apocalyptic threat.

*   **Act III: The Eternal Throne (Sessions 6-8)**: A crisis threatens all factions—an ancient sealed horror (the true apocalypse bringer) begins awakening, far worse than Mynoghra ever was. The god-king must unite former enemies, leverage their nation's unique abilities, and face the truth: Mynoghra was never the world's destroyer but its potential savior. The final battle requires combining all strategic choices, alliances made, units recruited, and technologies researched into comprehensive defense. Victory establishes Mynoghra as legitimate power.

## Session Breakdown

*   **Session 1: The God-King Awakens**
    *   **Focus**: Introduction, Nation Building Basics, First Crisis
    *   **Key Events**: The party's leader—formerly obsessive strategy gamer named Takuto—awakens as the god-king of Mynoghra after sudden death. They find themselves in ruined temple with game interface visible only to them. The party are their first divine servants—unique individuals drawn by divine call. They discover the Mynoghra capital is destroyed ruins with 50 desperate demon refugees surviving in shelters. Using strategy game knowledge, the god-king begins rebuilding: assign workers to gather food and materials, construct basic buildings, establish defensive perimeter. First threat: bandits attack refugee camp seeing easy targets. The party must defend with minimal resources, showcasing Mynoghra's unique ability—sacrificial resurrection (fallen units respawn at cost).
    *   **Strategic Choice**: Focus initial resources on food (population grows), materials (build faster), or military (better defense)?
    *   **Escalation**: Scouts from human kingdom Sanctum Regis discover Mynoghra's revival, report back. The god-king realizes other factions will respond.

*   **Session 2: Diplomacy and Danger**
    *   **Focus**: Faction Introduction, Resource Management, First Alliance
    *   **Key Events**: As Mynoghra rebuilds, representatives from neighboring factions arrive. The Sylvan Theocracy (elf nation) demands Mynoghra disband or face extermination. The Ironclaw Confederation (beastfolk) offers trade if Mynoghra helps them against mutual enemy. Sanctum Regis sends spies. The god-king must navigate diplomacy while continuing development. The party splits: some defend the capital, others pursue alliance quest with Ironclaw (hunting dangerous monster threatening both nations). First research completes—choose specialization (military, economy, magic, or hybrid). Success with Ironclaw creates first ally but angers elves.
    *   **Strategic Choice**: Ally with beastfolk (resources and trade), appease elves (peace but restrictions), or reject both (independence but isolation)?
    *   **Escalation**: The Sylvan Theocracy declares Mynoghra a threat, begins mustering forces. The party has sessions to prepare for invasion.

*   **Session 3: The First War**
    *   **Focus**: Tactical Combat, Defense Strategy, Unit Abilities
    *   **Key Events**: Sylvan Theocracy invades with superior numbers and quality. The party must use strategy rather than strength. Mynoghra's unique mechanics become crucial: resurrection allows trading lives for time, demon units have special abilities (fear auras, berserker rages, shadow walking), and the god-king can invoke divine powers (limited uses). The battle occurs across multiple fronts—walls, gates, central temple. The party commands units, uses terrain, activates faction abilities. Victory requires clever tactics, not raw power. Defeating the invasion earns Mynoghra respect (strength recognized) or mercy creates potential ally (demonstrate they're not monsters).
    *   **Tactical Choice**: Hold defensive position (fewer casualties, longer battle) or aggressive counterattack (riskier, faster resolution)?
    *   **Escalation**: Victory attracts attention of powerful neutral faction—the Obsidian Compact (merchant guild). They offer advanced trade and unique recruits... for a price.

*   **Session 4: Expansion and Innovation**
    *   **Focus**: Territory Growth, Unique Units, Research Paths
    *   **Key Events**: With first war won, Mynoghra expands into unclaimed territories. The party explores abandoned ruins discovering ancient Mynoghra technology (strategy game lore made real). They recruit unique units: the Ebon Knights (elite demon cavalry), Shadow Weavers (stealth mages), or Hellforged Automatons (demon-tech golems). Research unlocks advanced buildings and abilities. The god-king makes strategic choice about nation's direction: militaristic expansion, economic powerhouse, magical supremacy, or diplomatic federation. Each path unlocks different capabilities. Meanwhile, refugees from other nations arrive seeking Mynoghra's protection—unexpected moral choice about accepting non-demons.
    *   **Strategic Choice**: Specialize in one path (powerful but inflexible) or balance multiple (versatile but slower)?
    *   **Escalation**: A mysterious message arrives from another strategy player-turned-god-king ruling different faction. They propose meeting... possibly alliance, possibly challenge.

*   **Session 5: The Council of God-Kings**
    *   **Focus**: Meta-Narrative, Grand Strategy, Alliance Building
    *   **Key Events**: The god-king meets other reincarnated players: Aria ruling the Radiant Ecclesia (light/holy faction), Jin controlling the Ironclaw Confederation (revealed to also be player), and mysterious Nox commanding the Void Covenant (cosmic horror faction). Each brought their faction knowledge from the game. They discuss the implications—why were they brought here? What's their purpose? Can former opponents cooperate? The god-king learns their choices affected other god-kings' games. They form tentative alliance against common threats. This session is heavy roleplay and strategy discussion—planning coordinated actions, sharing resources, debating philosophy of ruling.
    *   **Strategic Choice**: Full alliance (strong but complicated), limited cooperation (flexible), or competition (prove Mynoghra superior)?
    *   **Escalation**: During meeting, all god-kings receive system warning: "Primordial Seal: 67% degraded. Apocalypse Protocol approaching threshold."

*   **Session 6: The True Apocalypse Rises**
    *   **Focus**: Revelation, Crisis Management, Desperate Preparation
    *   **Key Events**: The party investigates the warning, discovering horrifying truth: The world of "Eternal Nations" wasn't just a game—it was preparation. The Primordial Abyss, an entity predating all factions, is sealed beneath the world. The original Mynoghra and other ancient factions created "Eternal Nations" as training simulation, seeding it across dimensions to find worthy commanders. The god-kings were chosen because they mastered the game. The Abyss is awakening and all factions must unite or perish. The party mobilizes Mynoghra for total war, calling in alliances, maximizing production, researching ultimate technologies. This session is frantic preparation—final building projects, mass recruitment, diplomatic summit organizing, fortification construction.
    *   **Strategic Choice**: Focus on Mynoghra's strength (powerful but isolated) or coordinate multi-faction strategy (weaker individually, stronger together)?
    *   **Escalation**: The Abyss's vanguard emerges—reality-warping horrors that corrupt everything, including strategy game mechanics. The god-king's interface glitches. They must fight without meta-knowledge safety net.

*   **Session 7: The Siege of Reality**
    *   **Focus**: Epic Battle, Tactical Mastery, Alliance Coordination
    *   **Key Events**: The Primordial Abyss launches full assault on all factions simultaneously. The party commands Mynoghra forces while coordinating with other god-kings. The battle spans multiple fronts—defending capital, supporting allies, launching counteroffensives. Every strategic choice matters: unit positioning, resource allocation, ability timing, diplomatic coordination. Mynoghra's resurrection mechanic is crucial—tactical sacrifices buy time for others. The party faces corrupted versions of units they recruited, forcing emotional decisions. NPCs they've befriended fight valiantly. The god-king must make terrible choices: save this city or that ally? Sacrifice units for strategic advantage?
    *   **Tactical Choice**: Multiple simultaneous decisions across different battlefronts—cannot save everyone, must prioritize.
    *   **Escalation**: The Primordial Abyss manifests personally. All god-kings must combine their faction ultimate abilities for any chance of victory.

*   **Session 8: Eternal Throne Established**
    *   **Focus**: Climactic Resolution, Nation's Future, Legacy Building
    *   **Key Events**: The final confrontation with the Primordial Abyss. The god-kings use their combined faction abilities—Mynoghra's resurrection creating infinite reinforcements, Radiant Ecclesia's purification magic, Ironclaw's unstoppable berserkers, Void Covenant's reality manipulation. The strategy is complex coordination requiring all previous session's learnings. Victory requires sacrifice—possibly permanent death of some NPCs, possibly reduction of god-king's power, possibly sealing the Abyss at great cost. The Abyss is defeated/resealed. In aftermath, the world recognizes Mynoghra as savior rather than threat. The god-king decides their nation's future: expansionist empire, peaceful federation member, isolated but respected power, or democratic reformation.
    *   **Resolution**: Mynoghra transforms from weakest faction to established power. Former enemies become partners in post-war reconstruction. The god-king can choose to remain divine ruler or step back, having completed their purpose.
    *   **Epilogue**: Shows Mynoghra years later based on player choices—thriving military power, economic hub, magical research center, or multicultural federation. The party's NPCs have grown into leaders. The god-king's legacy is secure.

## Major NPCs

*   **Takuto/The God-King (PC)**
    *   **Former Life**: Obsessive strategy gamer who loved underdog factions.
    *   **Current Role**: Divine avatar of Mynoghra, sees strategy interface, has faction powers.
    *   **Personality**: Strategic thinker, empathetic despite demon faction, proves "evil" alignment doesn't mean evil actions.
    *   **Growth**: From game player to responsible ruler caring for actual people.

*   **Atropos (First Servant)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Devoted demon general, believes in Mynoghra's purpose, protective of god-king.
    *   **Background**: Survived Mynoghra's previous destruction, witnessed nation's fall.
    *   **Role**: Military advisor and loyal companion, represents old Mynoghra's values.
    *   **Development**: Learns new Mynoghra can be different while honoring past.

*   **Lyris Moonwhisper (Refugee Leader)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Non-demon refugee seeking shelter, initially skeptical of demon nation.
    *   **Background**: Human whose kingdom fell to war, sought Mynoghra out of desperation.
    *   **Role**: Represents multicultural potential, challenges assumptions about demon factions.
    *   **Development**: Becomes advocate for Mynoghra's reformed approach, bridge to other nations.

*   **High Justiciar Elyndra (Sylvan Theocracy Leader)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Elf leader convinced demons are inherently evil, driven by trauma.
    *   **Background**: Lost family to ancient demon invasion, sees Mynoghra's return as existential threat.
    *   **Role**: Primary early antagonist who can become ally if convinced of change.
    *   **Arc**: Either remains enemy (tragedy of inflexibility) or finds redemption recognizing individual merit.

*   **Chieftain Grommash Ironclaw (Beastfolk Leader)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Pragmatic warrior, values strength and honor, judges by actions.
    *   **Background**: Leads confederation of beast tribes, respects those who prove themselves.
    *   **Role**: First potential ally, values results over faction prejudice.
    *   **Development**: Becomes staunch friend if player honors alliances.

*   **Aria Lightbringer (Fellow God-King)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Former player ruling Radiant Ecclesia, idealistic but learning harsh realities.
    *   **Background**: Played "good" faction in game, now faces moral complexity.
    *   **Role**: Ally and philosophical foil—light vs. dark cooperation.
    *   **Relationship**: Friendship or rivalry depending on player choices.

*   **Jin Stormblade (Fellow God-King)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Tactical genius ruling Ironclaw, competitive but fair.
    *   **Background**: Min-maxed beastfolk faction to perfection.
    *   **Role**: Strategic partner, values efficient cooperation.
    *   **Arc**: Develops respect for god-king's different strategic approach.

*   **Nox the Unknowable (Fellow God-King)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Mysterious ruler of Void Covenant, cryptic but ultimately allied.
    *   **Background**: Mastered cosmic horror faction through strange strategies.
    *   **Role**: Wild card ally, provides unique perspectives.
    *   **Mystery**: Their true motivations remain unclear until endgame.

*   **The Primordial Abyss**
    *   **Nature**: Reality-warping cosmic horror, predates all factions.
    *   **Goals**: Consume existence, spread entropy.
    *   **Design**: Lovecraftian nightmare mixed with corrupted strategy game mechanics.
    *   **Role**: Ultimate threat forcing all factions to unite.

## Key Locations

*   **Mynoghra Capital (Erebus Sanctuary)**: Ruins being rebuilt. Starts as refugee camp, grows into impressive city. Physical representation of player's strategic choices.

*   **The Eternal Throne**: Sacred site where god-king receives visions and accesses strategy interface. Source of divine power.

*   **Sylvan Theocracy (Luminessa)**: Elf nation of gleaming white cities and ancient forests. Initially hostile, possibly ally.

*   **Ironclaw Confederation (Savage Peaks)**: Beastfolk tribal federation in mountain ranges. Trade partner and military ally.

*   **The Obsidian Markets**: Neutral trade hub where all factions interact. Information gathering and recruitment site.

*   **The Primordial Seal**: Ancient site containing the Abyss. Party must defend it in climax.

## Central Conflict

The central conflict operates on multiple levels:

**Strategic**: Build nation from nothing while managing limited resources, hostile neighbors, and time pressure.

**Tactical**: Fight battles where positioning, unit abilities, and faction mechanics matter more than levels.

**Diplomatic**: Navigate faction politics, forge alliances, prove Mynoghra deserves to exist.

**Philosophical**: Challenge assumptions about "evil" factions. Demonstrate that alignment doesn't dictate morality.

**Existential**: Face threat that makes faction squabbles irrelevant. Unite former enemies against apocalypse.

**Meta**: Explore implications of being strategy gamer in game world. What does mastery mean when lives are real?

## Side Quests

*   **Unique Unit Recruitment**: Special missions to unlock faction-specific legendary units. Each has requirements and rewards.

*   **Research Projects**: Invest resources in technological/magical advancement trees. Choices affect available strategies.

*   **Territory Expansion**: Claim and develop new lands. Each area offers different resources and strategic positions.

*   **Diplomatic Missions**: Send party on quests for other factions, building relationships and intelligence networks.

*   **Ancient Mynoghra Lore**: Discover what original faction was like, learning from history to avoid past mistakes.

*   **The Strategy Simulator**: God-king can access game simulations to test strategies, but results vary when emotions are involved.

## Unique Mechanics

*   **Difficulty - Medium**: Balanced tactical combat and strategy management. Resources matter, choices have consequences. Death possible but resurrection available (faction ability).

*   **Nation Building System**:
    *   Track Mynoghra's resources: Food, Materials, Gold, Population, Research Points.
    *   Between sessions, assign workers to various tasks: gathering, building, recruitment, research.
    *   Construct buildings granting bonuses: barracks (better units), workshops (crafting), temples (divine powers), etc.
    *   Research technologies unlocking new capabilities and units.
    *   Balance growth with sustainability—overexpansion creates vulnerabilities.

*   **Faction Abilities (Mynoghra)**:
    *   **Sacrificial Resurrection**: Fallen units respawn at capital (costs resources). Tactical death trading becomes viable strategy.
    *   **Shadow Network**: Enhanced intelligence gathering and infiltration.
    *   **Fear Aura**: Demon units cause morale penalties to enemies.
    *   **Adaptive Evolution**: Units gain experience and improve, unlike most factions' static forces.
    *   **God-King's Vision**: Access strategy interface showing faction stats, warnings, and optimization suggestions.

*   **Tactical Combat**:
    *   Combat encounters use positioning, terrain, unit types, and special abilities.
    *   Party commands units rather than just fighting personally.
    *   Unit composition matters—counters and synergies affect battles.
    *   Faction abilities can turn tide: resurrect fallen, activate fear effects, use divine interventions.
    *   Victory through strategy rather than simply winning initiative rolls.

*   **Diplomatic Influence**:
    *   Track relations with major factions on scale from hostile to allied.
    *   Actions affect standing: helping earns favor, betrayal destroys trust, competence earns respect.
    *   Can form alliances, trade agreements, non-aggression pacts, or declare rivalries.
    *   Other god-kings factor into diplomacy—fellow players understand strategy but have competing goals.
    *   Endgame requires coordinating multiple factions against common threat.

*   **Strategic Layer**:
    *   Campaign map showing territories, resources, faction positions.
    *   Long-term planning required: building chains, research paths, military production.
    *   Time matters—faction crises create deadlines.
    *   Choices have cascading consequences: early specialization affects late-game options.
    *   No perfect strategy—must adapt to changing circumstances and rival factions' moves.

***

*Campaign design emphasizes strategic thinking, tactical mastery, and nation building. Players experience satisfaction of growing faction from nothing to power, prove that "evil" doesn't mean evil, and unite former enemies against true apocalypse. Strategy game knowledge becomes literal superpower requiring wisdom to use responsibly.*
