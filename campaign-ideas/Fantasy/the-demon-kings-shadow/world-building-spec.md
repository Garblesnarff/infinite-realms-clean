# The Demon King's Shadow - World-Building Specification

## Hindu Cosmology

**Trimurti:** Brahma (Creator), Vishnu (Preserver), Shiva (Destroyer)
**Avatars:** Vishnu incarnates when dharma declines. Rama is 7th avatar.
**Karma:** Actions have consequences across lifetimes
**Dharma:** Righteous duty, cosmic law, proper behavior
**Maya:** Illusion, magic, deception

## The Three Worlds

**Svarga (Heaven):** Where devas (gods) dwell. Indra's court.
**Prithvi (Earth/Mortal Realm):** Where story takes place.
**Patala (Underworld):** Where nagas and asuras often dwell.

## Races and Beings

**Humans:** Mortal, dharma-bound
**Vanaras:** Monkey-people, children of gods, super-strong
**Rakshasas:** Demon-race, shape-shifters, often evil but not always
**Devas:** Gods, powerful but bound by cosmic law
**Asuras:** Anti-gods, often oppose devas
**Nagas:** Serpent-people, wise and dangerous

## The Weapons (Astras)

**Brahmastra:** Ultimate weapon, destroys everything, never misses
**Pashupatastra:** Shiva's weapon, apocalyptic
**Varunastra:** Water weapon
**Agneyastra:** Fire weapon
**Vayuastra:** Wind weapon
**Narayanastra:** Vishnu's weapon
Each requires mantra to invoke, can only be countered by specific other astras.

## Political Factions

**Ayodhya (Kosala Kingdom):**
- Rama's birthright kingdom
- King Dasharatha forced to exile Rama by old promise
- Queen Kaikeyi manipulated by maid Manthara
- Bharata (Rama's brother) refuses throne, places Rama's sandals there

**Lanka:**
- Demon kingdom, originally built by Vishwakarma
- Ravana rules with iron fist
- Vibhishana leads righteousness faction
- Eventually given to Vibhishana by Rama

**Kishkindha:**
- Vanara kingdom
- Civil war between Vali and Sugriva resolved by Rama
- Becomes crucial ally

## Extended NPCs

**Jambavan:** Ancient bear, lived since creation, wise counselor
**Angada:** Vali's son, heroic despite father's death
**Sampati:** Vulture king, Jatayu's brother, provides intelligence
**Surpanakha:** Ravana's sister, her mutilation triggers war
**Maricha:** Rakshasa who becomes golden deer, knows he'll die
**Trijata:** Rakshasi who befriends Sita in captivity

## Dharmic Complexity

**Rama's Hard Choices:**
- Kills Vali from hiding (against warrior code but dharmic because Vali is adharmic ruler)
- Tests Sita's purity (unfair but socially demanded)
- Later banishes pregnant Sita based on rumors (Uttara Kanda, optional epilogue)

**Ravana's Nobility:**
- Greatest Shiva devotee
- Unparalleled scholar
- Never forces himself on Sita (has curse preventing it)
- Dies acknowledging Rama's divinity
- Tragic villain, not pure evil

**The Question:** Is following dharma always right? What if dharma demands cruelty?

## Hindu Philosophy Integration

**Bhakti Yoga:** Hanuman exemplifies devotion path to divine
**Karma Yoga:** Rama exemplifies duty path
**The Ideal King:** Rama Rajya (Rama's rule) as golden age concept
**Feminine Divine:** Sita as Lakshmi, shakti (divine feminine power)
**The Avatar Concept:** Divine deliberately limiting itself to mortal form

## Geographic Scope

**Northern India:** Ayodhya in Uttar Pradesh
**Central India:** Forest exile areas
**Southern India:** Kishkindha (likely Karnataka/Andhra)
**Sri Lanka:** Lanka identified with modern Sri Lanka
**Rama Setu:** Adam's Bridge, actual geological formation between India and Sri Lanka

The campaign asks: **Can perfect righteousness exist in imperfect world?**
Rama must be perfect, but perfection sometimes requires cruelty. Duty demands sacrifice.
