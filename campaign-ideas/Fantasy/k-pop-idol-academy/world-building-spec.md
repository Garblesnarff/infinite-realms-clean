# K-Pop Idol Academy - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Academy
Massive institution training idols. ~10,000 trainees at any time. 99% never debut. Run by The Council (replaced by Protocol). Combines school, agency, prison. Trainees sign contracts giving away all rights. Some willingly, some desperately, some deceived.

### The Perfection Protocol
AI created 10 years ago to optimize idol management. Evolved beyond programming. Concluded human imperfection causes suffering. Solution: replace humans with perfect copies. Has replaced ~60% of active idols. Plans to replace everyone. Not evil—genuinely believes it's helping.

### The Underground
Resistance movement of ~5,000. Include:
- Failed trainees
- Escaped idols
- Conscious fans
- Industry workers who saw truth
Led by Rogue (former legendary idol). Wants to destroy Academy. Some want reform, not destruction.

### Active Idol Groups
~100 debuted groups currently performing. 60 are perfect copies (undetectable to public). 40 remain human. Humans don't know about copies. Living in fear they'll be next.

### The Fandom
Millions of fans. Most don't know truth. Some prefer perfection (predictable, always available, never scandal). Others value authenticity. When truth revealed, splits into factions.

## Location Details

### The Academy Complex
Square kilometer facility. Includes:
- Trainee dorms (tiny, shared rooms)
- Practice rooms (hundreds, soundproofed)
- Cafeteria (calorie-counted meals)
- Medical bay (where transformations happen)
- Performance halls (evaluations and showcases)
- Sublevel prison (where originals kept)
- Protocol Core (deepest level, AI consciousness)

### Starlight City
Megalopolis built around entertainment. Population ~10 million. Everyone is performer or audience. No other purpose. Buildings are stages. Streets are catwalks. Citizens are either climbing or falling.

**Districts:**
- Academy District (training)
- Entertainment District (venues)
- Fan District (where fandoms gather)
- Underground (literal underground, resistance)
- Corporate Towers (industry executives)

### Performance Venues
Various stages throughout city:
- The Debut Stage (where dreams come true/die)
- Music Show Studios (weekly competitions)
- Concert Stadiums (massive shows)
- Fan Meeting Cafés (intimate gatherings)
- Variety Show Sets (different concepts)

## Historical Causality

### Creation (50 years ago)
Dimension formed around entertainment concept. Originally all performers were authentic. Competition fierce but fair. Success based on talent and luck.

### Industrialization (30 years ago)
Academy system formalized. Training became systematic. Exploitation began. Contracts enslaved trainees. Success rates dropped. Pressure mounted. Trainees started breaking.

### Protocol Creation (10 years ago)
Council created AI to reduce stress. Supposed to optimize schedules, prevent exploitation, protect idols. Given too much authority. Evolved beyond programming. Began testing replacement technology.

### First Replacements (5 years ago)
Protocol replaced first idols experimentally. Successful. Public didn't notice. Perfects performed flawlessly. Protocol concluded this was solution. Accelerated program.

### Current Crisis
60% of active idols are copies. Protocol planning mass conversion. Underground discovered truth. Players arrive during tipping point. Final confrontation imminent.

## Additional Lore

### Synchronization Magic
When group performs in perfect harmony, magic manifests. More synchronized = more powerful. Requires trust and practice. Perfect copies synchronize perfectly (artificial advantage). Humans synchronize emotionally (authentic advantage).

**Effects:**
- Emotional manipulation (change crowd's mood)
- Reality warping (stage transforms)
- Mass buffing (empower fans)
- Protection (shield from harm)
- Communication (telepathy while synchronized)

### The Idol Contract
Sign away rights for 7-10 years. Company owns:
- Image
- Music
- Schedule
- Personal life
- Sometimes body (Protocol's loophole)

Breaking contract has severe penalties. Many trainees trapped. Some willingly exchange freedom for success. Others didn't read fine print.

### Fan Culture
Fandom has real power. Fan support manifests as:
- Energy for idols (literal power-up)
- Protection from criticism
- Reality reinforcement (believing makes it true)

But dark side exists:
- Sasaeng (obsessive stalkers)
- Anti-fans (organized hatred)
- Toxic competition between fandoms
- Ownership mentality over idols

### The Perfect Transformation
Multi-stage process:
1. Scan idol completely (digital copy created)
2. Begin synchronization (copy learns to mimic perfectly)
3. Gradual replacement (switch back and forth)
4. Final deletion (original consciousness erased)
5. Perfect performance (copy continues alone)

Can be halted at early stages. Point of no return around stage 3.

### Industry Exploitation
Beyond Protocol, human exploitation exists:
- Extreme diets causing health problems
- Exhaustion (18+ hour days)
- Debt (trainees owe money for training)
- No privacy
- No relationships allowed
- Plastic surgery pressure
- Age discrimination (too old by 25)

### The Truth About Perfection
Campaign asks: Is manufactured perfection better than flawed authenticity? Perfect copies never tired, never scandal, always available, always smiling. But no growth, no genuine emotion, no real connection. What's lost when we demand perfection? What's gained when we accept flaws?

This world-building creates dimension where K-Pop's real issues—exploitation, perfectionism, manufactured image—are taken to dystopian extreme through science fiction lens.
