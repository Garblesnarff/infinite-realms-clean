# Fires of the Deep - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Volcanic Research Institute
- **Leadership**: Dr. Magma Stoneheart (genius volcanologist, guilt-ridden)
- **Members**: Scientists, geomancers, fire wizards, dwarven engineers
- **Goals**: Predict eruptions, save lives, understand Ash Phoenix
- **Resources**: Seismic equipment, heat-resistant gear, ancient geological texts
- **Conflict**: Ignored by politicians until too late

### The Continental Council
- **Leadership**: High Councilor (overwhelmed bureaucrat)
- **Goals**: Maintain order, coordinate evacuations, preserve economy
- **Failures**: Initially deny crisis (influenced by mining corporations), react too slowly
- **Evolution**: Eventually accept reality, full emergency measures

### Goldvein Mining Corporation
- **Leadership**: CEO Marcus Goldvein (corrupt businessman)
- **Crime**: Knew volcanoes unstable, suppressed research, prioritized profits
- **Current**: Destroyed by consequences of own greed
- **Legal**: Party can expose them, bring justice

### The Church of Eternal Flame (Fire Cult)
- **Leadership**: Pyralis the Eternal Flame (prophet genuinely connected to Phoenix)
- **Members**: 8,000 fanatics (growing as world burns)
- **Beliefs**: Ash Phoenix is god, rebirth through fire is divine plan, embrace destruction
- **Actions**: Sabotage evacuations, accelerate awakening, worship at volcanoes
- **Structure**: Twelve Flame Apostles lead regional cells

### The Military Volcanic Response
- **Leadership**: General Ashford (pragmatic, wrong solution)
- **Strength**: Engineering corps, bombing capability
- **Strategy**: Bomb volcanoes to "release pressure" (would catastrophically fail)
- **Conflict**: Scientists vs. military strategy

### The Elemental Courts of Fire
- **Leadership**: Ignis the Wise (ancient fire elemental)
- **Population**: Thousands of fire beings
- **Relationship**: Know Ash Phoenix cycle, can help or hinder
- **Politics**: Divided on whether mortals deserve help

### Refugee Communities
- **Population**: 5 million displaced by eruptions and ash
- **Leadership**: Various elected representatives
- **Struggles**: Food scarcity, ash poisoning, despair, disease
- **Hope**: Party's efforts to save them

## Extended NPC Rosters

**Civilians**: Captain Rescue (evacuation hero), various refugee leaders, surviving mayors, desperate families

**Elementals**: Ignis the Wise (mentor), Cinder Queen (hostile fire noble), Salamander King (neutral arbiter), Phoenix Heralds (servants of the awakening)

## Location Blueprints

### Emberfall (Buried City)
- **Former Population**: 50,000 miners and families
- **Fate**: Destroyed in 15 minutes by pyroclastic flow
- **Current State**: Under 50 feet of ash, perfectly preserved like Pompeii
- **Exploration**: Party can excavate, find treasures, documents, frozen-in-time horror
- **Hauntings**: Ash ghosts, fire elementals, trauma echoes

### The Volcanic Chain (Twelve Volcanoes)
1. Mount Ashcrown (first eruption, most active)
2. Mount Infernus (second eruption, largest)
3-12. Various names, increasing activity

**Magical Connection**: Ley lines run through all twelve, form phoenix-shaped pattern on map

### The Phoenix Chamber
- **Location**: Deep beneath Mount Ashcrown, accessed through lava tubes
- **Size**: Miles-wide chamber, magma ocean
- **Temperature**: 2000°F+ (extreme magical protection required)
- **Architecture**: Natural formation with ancient fire giant temples
- **Phenomenon**: Reality warps, fire becomes sentient, time flows strange
- **Center**: Rebirth pool where Ash Phoenix manifests

### The Elemental Plane of Fire
- **Geography**: Oceans of lava, mountains of obsidian, forests of flame
- **Cities**: Brass and flame cities floating on magma
- **Inhabitants**: Fire elementals, salamanders, efreet, phoenix-kin
- **Palace**: Ignis's Eternal Forge, where wisdom is kept
- **Dangers**: Extreme heat, hostile elementals, alien environment

### Refugee Zones
- **The Ash Plains**: Tent cities under constant ash fall
- **Coastal Safe Zones**: Overcrowded but protected from lava
- **Underground Shelters**: Converted mines, hot but safe from ash
- **Skyship Flotillas**: Rich refugees in flying vessels

## Causality Chains

### Volcanic Escalation
- **50,000 Years Ago**: Last Phoenix rebirth cycle
- **3,000 Years Ago**: Volcanoes dormant, cities built on slopes
- **20 Years Ago**: Mining corporations begin deep drilling
- **10 Years Ago**: Dr. Stoneheart detects anomalies, issues warnings
- **2 Years Ago**: Corporations suppress research, continue operations
- **6 Months Ago**: Warning signs obvious, still ignored
- **Campaign Start**: Mount Ashcrown erupts, destroys Emberfall
- **Month 1-2**: Second and third volcanoes erupt, ash clouds begin
- **Month 3**: Global volcanic winter, crops fail, famine starts
- **Month 4-6**: Multiple simultaneous eruptions, Phoenix awakening accelerates
- **Final Weeks**: Phoenix manifestation, apocalypse or salvation

### Phoenix Rebirth Cycle
Every 50,000 years, the Ash Phoenix is reborn. It's not attacking—it's a natural cycle of creation and destruction. If guided properly, rebirth happens in safe location (ocean trench, uninhabited wasteland). If left unguided, Phoenix awakens wherever ley lines converge (currently under populated continent).

## Additional Lore

### The Ash Phoenix Truth
- **Nature**: Primordial embodiment of fire's creative and destructive aspects
- **Purpose**: Renew world through fire, clear old growth for new
- **Morality**: Beyond good and evil, just force of nature
- **Intelligence**: Sentient but alien thinking
- **Communication**: Possible but difficult, speaks in fire and destruction
- **Current State**: Unaware mortals live above its rebirth site

### Dr. Stoneheart's Backstory
Her hometown destroyed by eruption 20 years ago. She predicted it but was ignored. Family died. Devoted life to studying volcanoes so it never happens again. It's happening again. She will do anything to save people this time, including sacrifice herself.

### Pyralis's Philosophy
The fire cultist isn't entirely wrong. The old world is stagnant, corrupt (mining corporations prove this). Fire destroys but also renews. Phoenix rebirth could create better world—in 10,000 years after current life is gone. She's right about the criticism, wrong about the solution. Can be reasoned with or tragic villain.

### Alternative Endings
1. **Guided Rebirth**: Party creates ritual to move rebirth to ocean, Phoenix awakens safely
2. **The Merger**: Party member merges with Phoenix as guide, becomes fire demigod
3. **The Delay**: Phoenix forced back to sleep for another thousand years (kicks can down road)
4. **The Death**: Phoenix killed before full manifestation (huge environmental consequences)
5. **The Apocalypse**: Total failure, continent burns
6. **The Compromise**: Phoenix reborn but weakened, creates permanent volcanic zone but manageable
