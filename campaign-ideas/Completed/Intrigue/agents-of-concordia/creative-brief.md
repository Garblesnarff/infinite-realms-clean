# Agents of Concordia - Creative Brief

## Art Style: 1960s Glamorous Spy-Fi

**Stylish Cold War Action** - Bright colors, bold fashion, retro-futuristic magic-tech. Think 1960s European glamour meets spy thriller. Everything looks expensive and cool.

## Color Palettes
- **Rome**: Warm Mediterranean golds, terracotta, azure
- **Berlin**: Cold war greys divided by bright propaganda colors
- **Monte Carlo**: Luxury golds, casino reds, champagne bubbles
- **Action**: High contrast, saturated colors, comic book vibrant

## Music
**Orchestral 1960s Spy Jazz** - Horns, bongos, electric guitar. Henry Mancini meets John Barry. Cool, sophisticated, driving action themes. Period-appropriate soundtrack.

**Key Themes**:
- Solo's Theme: Smooth jazz, confidence
- Kuryakin's Theme: Classical Russian influences, controlled intensity
- Partnership Theme: Both themes interweaving

## Voice Direction
- **Napoleon Solo**: Charming American smoothness (Robert Vaughn/Henry Cavill)
- **Illya Kuryakin**: Russian accent, intellectual precision (David McCallum/Armie Hammer)
- **Waverly**: British authority, dry wit
- **Gaby**: German efficiency meets warmth
- **Victoria**: Italian temptress, dangerous elegance

## Cinematic Moments
- **The Forced Team-Up**: Both after same target, ambushed, must cooperate
- **The Fake Marriage**: Posing as couple, chemistry developing
- **Car Chase**: 1960s vehicles through European streets, style and speed
- **The Dinner Argument**: Banter disguising real conversation about feelings
- **The Cruise Ship**: Parallel action, unknowingly helping each other
- **The Final Choice**: Partners choosing each other over nations
- **The Kiss**: When did fake become real?
