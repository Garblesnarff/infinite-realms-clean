# Creative Brief: The Color Code Conspiracy: Strangers in Blood

This document outlines the core creative direction for the assets of this campaign, ensuring a consistent and fitting tone across all visuals, audio, and more.

## Art Style: Violent Paranoia (Reservoir Dogs meets The Thing)

Inspired by the stylized criminal violence of *Reservoir Dogs* and the paranoid claustrophobia of *The Thing*. This style aims to capture the pressure-cooker intensity of armed strangers trapped together, the blood-soaked consequences of betrayal, and the escalating violence of mutual suspicion.

### Core Principles for Prompts:

*   **Emphasize Violent Tension:** Focus on armed confrontations, standoffs, and the ever-present threat of violence.
*   **Claustrophobic Paranoia:** Create oppressive, enclosed spaces where escape is impossible and trust is fatal.
*   **Color-Coded Identity:** Use the color aliases as visual motifs for each character.

---

### Detailed Style Guide for Image Generation:

**1. Overall Vibe & Mood:**
*   **Keywords:** Paranoid, violent, claustrophobic, betrayed, desperate, bloody, tense.
*   **Feeling:** A sense of imminent violence and inescapable suspicion. The atmosphere should evoke the pressure of being trapped with armed strangers who may be enemies. The world feels confined, hostile, and soaked in blood.

**2. Line Work & Form:**
*   **Style:** Sharp, aggressive lines emphasizing weapons, wounds, and confrontational body language.
*   **Forms:** Cramped warehouse interiors, characters in defensive positions, drawn weapons, blood spatter patterns, color-coded visual elements.
*   **Prompt Example:** "A detailed, aggressive illustration in the style of Reservoir Dogs, showing criminals in color-coded suits drawing weapons on each other in a warehouse, sharp lines emphasizing tension and imminent violence."

**3. Color Palette:**
*   **Style:** Each character associated with their color code (ruby red, emerald green, sapphire blue, etc.) against the grimy warehouse setting. Blood red is omnipresent.
*   **Luminosity:** Harsh overhead warehouse lighting creating stark shadows, dim corners hiding potential threats, the gleam of weapons and blood.
*   **Prompt Example:** "...a stark color palette dominated by warehouse grays and blacks, with each character visually associated with their color code—suits, ties, or auras—and blood red as a constant, spreading presence."

**4. Character Design:**
*   **Style:** Criminals in color-coded business attire (the professional uniform of the heist), progressively damaged and blood-stained as events unfold.
*   **Betrayal Elements:** Body language showing suspicion—sideways glances, hands near weapons, defensive positioning. Wounds and blood showing the violence already done.
*   **Expression:** Faces show paranoia, desperation, fury, pain, the calculated violence of self-preservation.
*   **Prompt Example:** "A group of criminals in a color-coded lineup—each in a different colored suit or tie (ruby, emerald, sapphire, gold, crimson)—all showing signs of violence: blood stains, wounds, weapons visible, expressions ranging from fear to fury to cold calculation."

**5. Backgrounds & Environments:**
*   **Style:** The warehouse safehouse dominates—industrial, cramped, with limited exits. The vault and ambush point shown through fragmented, violent flashbacks.
*   **Atmosphere:** Claustrophobic and oppressive. Every space feels like a potential kill-zone. No comfort, no safety, nowhere to run.
*   **Details:** Warehouse world-building: loading bay with blocked exits, office where interrogations happen, bloodstains showing escalating violence, windows showing law enforcement siege.
*   **Prompt Example:** "A claustrophobic warehouse interior with harsh overhead lighting, blood pooling on concrete floors, drawn weapons visible, windows showing police lights outside, cramped spaces creating confrontational proximity, no visible escape routes."

**6. Shading & Lighting:**
*   **Style:** Harsh, industrial lighting with deep shadows. Violence-emphasizing illumination that highlights blood, weapons, and suspicious expressions.
*   **Shadows:** Heavy shadows create paranoid atmosphere—unclear what or who lurks in darker areas. Silhouettes of armed figures.
*   **Purpose:** Lighting creates zones of confrontation and concealment, emphasizing the dangerous unpredictability of every interaction.
*   **Prompt Example:** "...harshly lit by industrial overhead lights creating stark shadows and bright pools of illumination, with blood gleaming wetly and weapons catching light, shadows deep enough to hide threats."

**7. Composition:**
*   **Style:** Confrontational framing with characters facing off, standoff compositions with multiple armed figures, claustrophobic tight shots emphasizing trapped proximity.
*   **Techniques:** Mexican standoff circular compositions, low angles making characters loom threateningly, tight framing removing escape routes from composition itself.
*   **Prompt Example:** "A circular Mexican standoff composition showing five color-coded criminals with drawn weapons, each aimed at another, creating a lethal geometry where any movement triggers cascading violence, tight framing emphasizing inescapable proximity."

## Voice

A voice that shifts between smooth criminal cool and explosive violent rage. During calm moments: controlled, even darkly humorous, using the stylized dialogue of professional criminals. During confrontations: sharp, aggressive, profane, on the edge of violence. The pacing alternates between tense, extended standoffs and sudden explosive action. The emotion is paranoid intensity mixed with dark humor—the voice of criminals who know they're probably going to die but maintain grim professionalism. Audio quality features warehouse acoustics—echo and reverb on voices, the click of weapons being cocked, sirens outside, dripping blood, heavy breathing, the silence before violence. Role: The voice of a criminal trapped in a bad situation that's getting worse by the second.
