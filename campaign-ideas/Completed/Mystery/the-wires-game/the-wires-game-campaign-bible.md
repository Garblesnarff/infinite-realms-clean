# Campaign Bible: Ironport - The City of Rust and Wire

[TAG: LORE_CORE]
# 1. Deep Lore & History

### The Origin of the Rot
Ironport was not founded; it was dredged. Established on a swamp of industrial runoff and salt marshes, the city was designed as a "Closed Loop Economy" in the late 19th centuryâ€”a utopian experiment where the workers lived in the factories and the factories owned the housing. The experiment failed, but the legal charter remained. Now, Ironport exists as a "Sociological Singularity": a place where the collective psychic weight of bad decisions, bureaucratic deadlock, and institutional cruelty has created a tangible "gravity" that prevents anyone from leaving. The city feeds on hope and excretes statistics.

### Timeline of Tragedy
1.  **1892 - The Dredging:** The marshes are drained; the first foundations sink 3 feet immediately.
2.  **1920 - The Iron Pact:** City Council signs a charter protecting unions, which unintentionally freezes all technological advancement in the port for 80 years.
3.  **1945 - The Victory Riot:** Soldiers return home to find their jobs automated; the first "Corner" is established as an economic protest.
4.  **1968 - The Great Fire of West Side:** A clerk misfiles a fire safety inspection; three blocks burn. The city zones the ash as "Historic," preventing rebuilding.
5.  **1982 - The Cocaine Flood:** The port becomes the primary entry point for the cartel.
6.  **1994 - The Paperwork Purge:** To save space, City Hall burns 50 years of property records. Ownership becomes a matter of "who is standing there with a bat."
7.  **2002 - The Towers Fall:** The high-rise projects are demolished, not to improve lives, but to disperse the crime into easier-to-hide row houses.
8.  **2008 - The "Ghost Budget":** The Mayor admits the city has been operating on a theoretical budget for six years. Schools are funded by lottery tickets.
9.  **2015 - The Gray Spring:** Police go on an unofficial strike ("The Blue Flu"). Crime drops because no one is there to report it. Stats improve.
10. **2024 - The Algorithm:** The Department of Justice installs an AI to predict crime; it immediately recommends arresting the entire City Council. It is unplugged.

### The Metaphysics: " The Juke"
Reality in Ironport is defined by **The Juke**. If a thing is not written down, it didn't happen. If a murder is reclassified as a "suspicious passing," the ghost does not haunt. The conflict is not Good vs. Evil; it is **The Truth vs. The File**. The players are fighting to make the reality match the paperwork.

**Details:** An amphitheater of poverty. Orange sofas on the grass.
**Sensory:** Smell of sulfur and cheap perfume. Sound of "Got that WMD!" shouted in rhythm.

**Zone:** The Harbor
**Details:** Mountains of rusting shipping containers arranged like a maze. The union hall bar.

**Zone:** East Side
**Details:** Row houses boarded up with plywood. Interiors are time capsules from the 90s.
**Sensory:** Smell of wet rot and lime. Sound of absolute, terrifying silence.

### Hamsterdam (Experimental Zone)
**Zone:** The Western District
**Details:** A post-apocalyptic free market. string lights over drug markets.
**Sensory:** Smell of urine and campfire smoke. Sound of discordant jazz and screaming.

### The Bullpen (Homicide)
**Zone:** Police HQ
**Details:** Desks crammed together, phones taped together, "The Board" (red names and black names).
**Sensory:** Smell of stale coffee and fear sweat. Sound of ringing phones that no one answers.

**Zone:** Downtown
**Details:** Marble floors, high ceilings, oppressive atmosphere of compromise.
**Sensory:** Smell of floor wax. Sound of hushed whispers and expensive shoes clicking.

# 5. Quest Architecture

### Main Campaign: The Wire (Linear Path)
1.  **The Buy:** Conduct a hand-to-hand drug buy (Tutorial).
2.  **The Beating:** Witness police brutality; choose to report or ignore.
3.  **The Clone:** Discover the pagers are using cloned codes.
4.  **The Judge:** Intimidate/Persuade Judge Phelan to sign the Wire order.
5.  **The Tap:** Install the bug in the low-rise courtyard (Stealth).
6.  **The Code:** Decipher the phone codes (Puzzle).
7.  **The Stash:** Locate the main supply house.
8.  **The Raid:** Execute a warrant; it goes wrong (Ambush).
9.  **The Flip:** Turn a mid-level lieutenant into an informant.
10. **The Shot:** The informant is shot; party must hide the body or solve it.
11. **The Port:** Investigation shifts to the Docks (The Greeks).
12. **The Container:** Find the container with the trafficking victims.
13. **The Computer:** Hack the shipping tracking system.
14. **The Money:** Trace the politicians' contributions.
15. **The Betrayal:** A party member is offered a promotion to kill the case.
16. **The Riots:** Hamsterdam is exposed; city falls into chaos.
17. **The Election:** Provide evidence to one of the mayoral candidates.
18. **The Purge:** The Organization cleans house; save the kids.
19. **The King:** Arrest the Kingpin (Avon/Marlo).
20. **The Cost:** The case is closed, but the villain walks on a technicality. Ending monologue.

# 8. Mechanics

### The Stat Game (Reputation System)
The party does not track Experience Points (XP) in the traditional sense. They track **Clearance Rate**.
*   **Black Board:** Unsolved cases. High Black Board = Budget cuts, hostile Command.
*   **Red Board:** Solved cases. High Red Board = Promotions, but high target for criminals.
*   **Juking:** Players can choose to "Juke" the stats (lie about a crime's severity) to lower the Black Board, but this increases the **City Corruption** meter.

### Wiretap Rules
To tap a phone, players must:
1.  **Exhaustion:** Gather evidence (Intel checks).
2.  **Persuasion:** Convince a Judge (DC 15-25 depending on evidence).
3.  **Patience:** Listen to audio logs. The DM reads boring transcripts. The players must identify the **Code**.
    *   *Example:* "I need 2 tickets to the movie" = 2 Kilos of Cocaine.

### Cynicism & Burnout
Instead of Sanity, characters have **Burnout**.
*   Starts at 0. Max 10.
*   Gain Burnout when: A case is dropped by politics, a child dies, you work 48 hours straight.
*   **Effect:** At Burnout 5, Disadvantage on Charisma. At Burnout 10, the character quits the force or becomes corrupt (NPC).
*   **Recovery:** Drinking (temporary), solving a real case (permanent).

# 9. Session Guide (Roadmap)

### Phase 1: The Street (Sessions 1-4)
*   **Focus:** Surveillance, learning the code, low-level arrests.
*   **Beat:** The party realizes they are underfunded.
*   **Combat:** Bar fight, chase scene.

### Phase 2: The Wire (Sessions 5-8)
*   **Focus:** Tech, puzzles, legal maneuvering.
*   **Beat:** The first major wiretap is approved.
*   **Combat:** Raid execution.

### Phase 3: The Politics (Sessions 9-12)
*   **Focus:** Command interference, leaking to press.
*   **Beat:** The target is protected by a Senator.
*   **Combat:** Social combat (The Mayor's Office).

### Phase 4: The Tragedy (Session 13)
*   **Focus:** Consequences.
*   **Beat:** Someone the party likes dies because of their investigation.

### Phase 5: The Endgame (Session 14+)
*   **Focus:** The final bust.
*   **Beat:** They catch the bad guy, but the System remains.
