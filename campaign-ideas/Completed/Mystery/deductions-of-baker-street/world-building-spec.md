### **World-Building Specification Brief: Deductions of Baker Street**

**1. Core Concept:**
Consulting detectives in a Victorian-inspired fantasy city solve impossible mysteries through observation and deduction, ultimately confronting the criminal mastermind Professor Moriarty who has orchestrated an elaborate web of crimes to test and destroy them.

**2. Lore & History:**
*   Detail the city's history with crime and justice—traditional watch methods vs. new scientific approaches
*   Explain the emergence of "consulting detective" as a profession
*   Describe famous unsolved cases that haunt the city's memory
*   Write about the class divisions—nobility, merchant class, underworld
*   Detail Victorian-era scientific understanding (forensics, chemistry, medicine in D&D context)
*   Explain Moriarty's background: brilliant academic who turned to crime as intellectual exercise
*   Describe the criminal underworld structure and Moriarty's control over it

**3. Factions:**
*   **The Consulting Detectives** (Major) - The party and allied investigators, solve impossible cases
*   **The City Watch** (Major) - Traditional law enforcement, competent but conventional
*   **Moriarty's Network** (Major) - Hidden criminal organization controlling smuggling, blackmail, theft
*   **The Nobility** (Major) - Clients with scandals to hide, victims of elaborate crimes
*   **The Baker Street Irregulars** (Minor) - Street urchin information network
*   **The Diogenes Club** (Minor) - Exclusive society of brilliant anti-social minds
*   **The Criminal Underworld** (Minor) - Traditional thieves, murderers, and lowlifes

**4. NPCs:**
*   **Tier 1:** Sherrinford Holmes, Dr. Watson, Professor Moriarty, Irene Adler, Inspector Lestrade, Colonel Moran
*   **Tier 2:** Mrs. Hudson, Mycroft Holmes, Various titled nobles with scandals
*   **Tier 3:** "Street Informant," "Suspicious Butler," "Wrongly Accused," "Grieving Widow," "Corrupt Official," "Hired Thug," "Blackmail Victim"

**5. Locations:**
*   **221B Baker Street** - Detective headquarters, evidence storage, chemistry lab
*   **Scotland Yard Equivalent** - City Watch headquarters, morgue, evidence rooms
*   **The Gaslit Streets** - Scene of crimes, atmospheric investigation locations
*   **The Diogenes Club** - Information gathering, meeting unusual allies
*   **Noble Estates** - Locked room mysteries, high society crimes
*   **The Docks** - Smuggling operations, underworld contacts
*   **Moriarty's Observatory** - Hidden mastermind lair (final act)
*   **Reichenbach Falls** - Dramatic waterfall for final confrontation

**6. Causality:**
*   IF party solves cases too quickly THEN Moriarty escalates schemes and complexity
*   IF party's reputation grows too high THEN Moriarty begins direct sabotage operations
*   IF party uses violence instead of detection THEN lose access to high society clients
*   IF party fails to protect a client THEN Moriarty gains leverage and confidence
*   IF party discovers Moriarty's identity early THEN he fakes his death and operates through proxies
*   IF party outsmarts Moriarty at Reichenbach THEN he either dies or becomes recurring nemesis
*   IF party maintains detailed casebook THEN gain advantage in final confrontation by recognizing patterns
