# Campaign Bible: Murder on the Astral Express

## 1. DEEP LORE & HISTORY
[TAG: LORE_CORE]

### Origin Myth: The First Timetable
Before space and time were separate concepts, reality was a single, infinitely dense point. The **First Conductor**, a primordial being of pure order, sought to create distance and duration. It took the "point" and stretched it into a "line," inventing the concept of a journey. The Astral Express is not a train; it is the physical manifestation of the First Conductor's original thought, a self-propelled argument for the existence of "here" and "there." Its tracks are the ley lines of causality, and its destinations are moments in time as much as places in space. The "engine" is a captured paradox: a clock that runs backward and forward simultaneously, powering the train with the energy released by its constant self-contradiction.

### Timeline of Events
1.  **The Un-Point (-1,000,000 Y):** The First Conductor creates the concept of a "journey." The Astral Express begins its first, endless run.
2.  **-8000 Y (The First Passenger):** A lost god stumbles upon the train and boards, becoming the first being to travel between planes.
3.  **-6500 Y (The Modron Contract):** The Modrons of Mechanus, recognizing the train as a pinnacle of order, sign a contract to become its eternal staff in exchange for a single, perfect gear from the engine.
4.  **-4200 Y (The Githyanki Heist):** Githyanki pirates, seeking to control the flow of time, successfully board the train and steal the Dining Car. They are repelled, but the car is never recovered, replaced by a new one with weaker enchantments.
5.  **-3000 Y (The Year of Three Wednesdays):** The engine's paradox-core hiccups, causing a single day to repeat three times for all passengers. This surreal event establishes the train's reputation for temporal weirdness.
6.  **-1500 Y (The Neutrality Accords):** After a war between devils and angels spills into the Lounge Car, the Express is declared neutral ground by all major planar powers. Violence is magically forbidden aboard the train (a rule with loopholes).
7.  **-700 Y (The Sleeper Car Incident):** A Night Hag attempts to turn the Sleeper Car into a permanent annex of the Plane of Nightmares. She is sealed within a single cabin, which is now permanently locked.
8.  **-100 Y (The Unscheduled Stop):** The train makes its first-ever unscheduled stop in a dimension of pure, silent thought. It returns a week later, with every passenger's inner monologue now faintly audible to others.
9.  **-10 Y (The Murder of the Conductor):** The First Conductor is found shattered into a million pieces of glass. Its replacement is a lesser being, a Deva, who is more of a manager than a cosmic entity.
10. **Present Day:** The Archmage of Paradoxes, a being who was studying the engine, is found murdered in the Dining Car. The train is put on lockdown between dimensions, and the party is deputized to solve the crime.

### The "Why": The Fragility of Causality
The murder is not just a crime; it is a metaphysical attack on the concept of linear time. The victim was not just a person; he was the "lock" on the engine's paradox-core. With him dead, the engine is becoming unstable. The train is beginning to "skip" on the tracks of causality, causing time loops, spatial distortions, and paradoxes. If the murderer is not found and the engine is not stabilized before the train reaches its next destination (The End of Time), all of history could unravel, re-shuffling events into a chaotic, meaningless sequence.

## 2. FACTIONS
[TAG: FACTION_DATA]

### 1. The Conductors of Order (The Staff)
**Type:** Modron Labor Union / Inflexible Bureaucracy
**Leader:** Conductor Unit 734
**Asset:** **The Rulebook:** A thousand-page tome detailing every regulation on the train. They can weaponize bureaucracy to paralyze any investigation that breaks protocol.
**Rivals:** The Stowaways.

### 2. The Planar Police (The "Transits")
**Type:** Inter-dimensional Law Enforcement
**Leader:** Detective Hercule Poinsettia (a Githzerai with obsessive-compulsive disorder).
**Asset:** **The Jurisdiction Key:** A magical key that allows them to open a door to any plane to call for backup.
**Rivals:** The Syndicate of Shadows.

### 3. The Syndicate of Shadows
**Type:** Criminal Organization / Smugglers
**Leader:** The Veiled Lady, a mysterious noble.
**Asset:** **The Ghost-Cargo:** They smuggle goods in the Ethereal Plane, which runs parallel to the train's interior.
**Rivals:** The Planar Police.

### 4. The Chronos-Historians
**Type:** Academic Society / Time-Travel Enthusiasts
**Leader:** Professor Eldrin Kai, a Tortle who has lived for 10,000 years.
**Asset:** **The Temporal Anchor:** A device that prevents them from being affected by minor changes in the timeline.
**Rivals:** The Cult of the Unwritten.

### 5. The Cult of the Unwritten
**Type:** Nihilistic Cult / Literary Vandals
**Leader:** "The Editor," a being who seeks to erase "badly written" history.
**Asset:** **The Red Pen:** A magical artifact that can "edit" a person's recent past, making them forget clues or actions.
**Rivals:** The Chronos-Historians.

### 6. The Honeymooners
**Type:** A Celestial and a Devil on their honeymoon.
**Leader:** Zariel (the angel) and Asmodeus Jr. (the devil).
**Asset:** **The Aura of Awkwardness:** Their conflicting auras create a zone of social discomfort, making interrogations difficult. They are technically neutral but cause chaos.
**Rivals:** Each other's families.

### 7. The Grand Game Hunters
**Type:** Aristocratic Bloodsport Enthusiasts
**Leader:** Lord Harrington, a Rakshasa who hunts "the most dangerous game."
**Asset:** **The Trophy Room:** A private train car filled with the stuffed heads of creatures from a hundred planes.
**Rivals:** The victim (who was an animal rights activist).

### 8. The Sleeper's Agents
**Type:** Dream-Warriors / Spies
**Leader:** Morpheus, a Sandman from the Plane of Dreams.
**Asset:** **The Dream Car:** They have almost complete control over the Dream Car, where they can interrogate sleeping passengers' subconscious minds.
**Rivals:** The Planar Police (who consider dream-interrogation illegal).

### 9. The Stowaways
**Type:** Refugee Community / Saboteurs
**Leader:** Anya, a young woman who was born on the train.
**Asset:** **The Maintenance Tunnels:** They know the train's secret passages and can move unseen from car to car.
**Rivals:** The Conductors of Order.

### 10. The Engine-Singers
**Type:** Mystic Technicians / Paradox Worshippers
**Leader:** The Chief Engineer, a being made of pure sound.
**Asset:** **Control of the Engine:** They can subtly influence the train's temporal hiccups, causing time to speed up or slow down in certain cars.
**Rivals:** The victim (who wanted to "fix" their paradox-god).

16. **The Widow** (Medusa) - The victim's estranged wife. **Voice:** A sharp, hissing whisper. **Goal:** To inherit the victim's research. **Secret:** She turned the victim to stone an hour before he was stabbed, meaning there are two causes of death.

19. **The Bodyguard** (Shield Guardian) - The victim's magically-bound bodyguard. **Voice:** It has no voice, but communicates by banging its shield. **Goal:** To protect the victim (a goal it has failed). **Secret:** It is now bound to the first person who picks up its control amulet.
20. **The Cat** (Familiar) - The victim's black cat, which is now wandering the train. **Voice:** A normal meow. **Goal:** To find someone to feed it. **Secret:** It is a spy for the Veiled Lady.

## 4. LOCATIONS
[TAG: LOCATIONS_MAIN]

### Zone 1: The First Class Cars
*   **Concept:** Opulent, magically-enhanced carriages for the elite.
*   **Loc 1: The Dining Car:** The scene of the crime. A beautiful art deco car with tables set with crystal and silver. **Smell:** Faint scent of blood and ozone under the aroma of roasted duck. **Sound:** The unnerving silence that has replaced the usual clatter of dinner service.
*   **Loc 2: The Lounge Car:** A smoky, jazz-filled bar where secrets are traded. **Smell:** Old leather, expensive brandy, and the strange, metallic scent of Efreeti smoke. **Sound:** A mournful saxophone playing a tune that seems to be about loss.
*   **Loc 3: The Observation Car:** A car with a glass dome ceiling, offering a breathtaking, terrifying view of the Astral Plane. **Smell:** Clean, sterile air. **Sound:** The faint, psychic hum of the cosmos.
*   **Loc 4: The First Class Sleepers:** Luxurious private cabins, each with a window looking out onto a different, personal dreamscape.
*   **Loc 5: The Spa Car:** A car containing a swimming pool of memory-enhancing water and a sauna that sweats out lies.

### Zone 2: The Passenger Cars
*   **Concept:** The standard, bustling carriages for merchants, adventurers, and tourists.
*   **Loc 1: The Garden Car:** A greenhouse car with plants from a thousand worlds. **Smell:** Damp earth and a thousand competing floral scents. **Sound:** The buzzing of construct-bees and the rustle of leaves.
*   **Loc 2: The Casino Car:** A den of iniquity where passengers gamble with souls, memories, and years of their life. **Sound:** The constant shuffling of cards, the rattle of dice, and the occasional scream of someone losing their identity.
*   **Loc 3: The Library Car:** A quiet car with books that read themselves aloud and shelves that rearrange into mazes. **Smell:** Old paper and binding glue.
*   **Loc 4: The Second Class Sleepers:** Cramped, bunk-bed style cabins where dreams and nightmares leak between compartments.
*   **Loc 5: The Market Car:** A chaotic, single-car bazaar where anything can be bought or sold.

### Zone 3: The Staff & Service Cars
*   **Concept:** The functional, dangerous, and hidden parts of the train.
*   **Loc 1: The Kitchen Car:** A frantic, hellishly hot kitchen run by a Fire Giant. **Smell:** A thousand wonderful and terrible smells at once. **Sound:** The roar of the stove-fire and the clang of steel.
*   **Loc 2: The Cargo Hold:** A vast, dimly lit car filled with crates, cages, and the occasional forgotten monster.
*   **Loc 3: The Maintenance Tunnels:** A network of cramped, dark tunnels that run the length of the train, known as "The Warrens." **Smell:** Grease and old metal.
*   **Loc 4: The Modron Barracks:** A stark, white, perfectly geometric car where the Modron staff recharge.
*   **Loc 5: The Engine Room:** The heart of the train. A room containing a massive, ticking, brass-and-crystal clockwork device that radiates temporal energy. **Sound:** The deafening, asynchronous ticking of a million clocks.

## 5. QUEST ARCHITECTURE
[TAG: QUEST_MAIN]

### Main Quest: The Chronos-Cide
1.  **The Body:** The Archmage of Paradoxes is found dead in the Dining Car. The train is locked down.
2.  **The Deputation:** Detective Poinsettia, overwhelmed, deputizes the party to assist.
3.  **The Crime Scene:** The party must investigate the Dining Car, gathering initial clues.
4.  **The Suspects:** The party is given a list of the 8 primary suspects who were in the Dining Car.
5.  **The First Interview:** The party questions the Honeymooners (Zariel & Asmodeus Jr.), who were arguing with the victim.
6.  **The Second Interview:** The party questions Lord Harrington, who wanted to hire the victim.
7.  **The Alibi-Breaker:** The party discovers a clue that proves one of the suspects is lying about their whereabouts.
8.  **The Dream-Clue:** The party convinces Morpheus to let them view the victim's dying dream in the Dream Car.
9.  **The Stowaway's Secret:** The party finds Anya, the stowaway, who witnessed a key moment but is afraid to talk.
10. **A Second Death:** Another suspect is murdered, complicating the investigation.
11. **The Temporal Glitch:** The train's engine hiccups, causing a 10-minute time loop in the Lounge Car that the party must escape.
12. **The Hidden Motive:** The party discovers the victim wasn't just a scholar; he was planning to "fix" the paradox engine, an act of heresy to the Engineers.
13. **The Engineer's Confession:** The party confronts the Chief Engineer, who confesses to wanting the victim dead, but insists they are not the killer.
14. **The Gathering of Suspects:** Poinsettia gathers all the suspects in the Lounge Car for the final accusation.
15. **The Accusation:** The party must publicly declare who they believe the murderer is, presenting their evidence.
16. **The Reveal:** The true murderer is revealed (potentially someone unexpected, like the Butler or the Time-Lost Child).
17. **The Final Confrontation:** The murderer, cornered, uses a stolen artifact to try and hijack the engine, leading to a final battle in the Engine Room as causality unravels.

## 6. ITEM DATABASE
[TAG: ITEM_LEGENDARY]

### Artifacts of the Express
1.  **The Timetable of Inevitability:** A train schedule that shows the "scheduled" time of death for anyone on board. It can be altered, but doing so requires "paying" the lost time from your own lifespan.
2.  **The Master-Key of Departures:** A key that can open a door on the train to any location the user has previously been. Each use, however, leaves the door open for 1 minute, risking an unscheduled passenger.
3.  **The Conductor's Whistle:** A silver whistle that can stop time for 6 seconds (1 round) for everyone except the user. It can only be used once per "stop."
4.  **The Paradox-Gear:** A gear from the engine's core. It exists in multiple states at once. It can be used to force a single d20 roll to be both a success and a failure simultaneously, creating a chaotic but potentially useful outcome.
5.  **The Black Box:** A small, indestructible box from the original Dining Car. It contains a perfect, incorruptible recording of one event. The party must choose which event to record over the course of the investigation.

## 8. MECHANICS
[TAG: RULES_CUSTOM]

### Subsystem: The Deduction Board
*   **The Board:** The GM maintains a "Deduction Board" (physical or virtual) with pictures of all the suspects.
*   **Clues & Testimonies:** When the party discovers a key piece of evidence or testimony, they get a physical card to place on the board. Clues can be linked to suspects with string.
*   **Making Accusations:** To make a formal accusation, the party must have at least three clues linked to a single suspect. A false accusation will cause the accused to become uncooperative for the rest of the investigation.
*   **The Final Reveal:** During the final gathering, the party must use the board to lay out their entire theory of the crime, from motive to opportunity to means.

### Physics: Temporal Hiccups
*   At the start of each "day" (or major act), the GM rolls a d20. On a 1-3, a "Temporal Hiccup" occurs. The GM rolls on a table of strange time-based effects:
    *   **Time-Loop:** The last 6 seconds repeat 1d4 times.
    *   **De-Sync:** One party member is 3 seconds ahead of everyone else (acts first, but can't interact with others).
    *   **Echoes:** Ghostly after-images of the passengers replay events from an hour ago.
    *   **Reverse:** Everything in one train car happens in reverse for one minute.

## 9. SESSION GUIDE
[TAG: DM_GUIDE]

*   **Session 1: All Aboard.** The party boards the train, meets the diverse cast of characters. The murder happens. The train is locked down. *Loot:* The initial crime scene clues.
*   **Session 2: The First Suspects.** The party interviews the Honeymooners and Lord Harrington. They explore the First Class cars. *Combat:* A "social combat" argument with Zariel.
*   **Session 3: The Dream Car.** The party investigates the Sleeper and Dream Cars. They bargain with Morpheus. *Exploration:* A surreal journey into the victim's dying dream.
*   **Session 4: Below Deck.** The party explores the service cars and meets the Stowaways. *Social:* Gain Anya's trust. *Loot:* A secret map of the train's tunnels.
*   **Session 5: A Second Body.** Another suspect is found dead, ruled a suicide. A Temporal Hiccup occurs, complicating the investigation. *Puzzle:* The time loop.
*   **Session 6: The Engine Room.** The party investigates the Engine Room and meets the strange Engineers. *Combat:* Fight off Paradox-Hounds leaking from the engine.
*   **Session 7: The Web of Lies.** The party focuses on breaking the alibis of the remaining suspects. *Social:* A high-stakes interrogation of a key suspect.
*   **Session 8: The Gathering.** Detective Poinsettia declares it is time. The party must prepare their final theory.
*   **Session 9: The Accusation.** The party presents their case in the Lounge Car. The murderer is revealed.
*   **Session 10: Confrontation.** The murderer flees to the Engine Room, attempting to hijack the train. *Chase:* A chase scene through the length of the train.
*   **Session 11: The Battle for Time.** Final boss fight in the Engine Room as reality unravels.
*   **Session 12: The Destination.** The train arrives at its destination. The party must give their final report to the Planar Police. Epilogue.
