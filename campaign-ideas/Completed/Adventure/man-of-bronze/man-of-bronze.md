# Man of Bronze

*A Scientific Adventure / Superscience Pulp campaign — Medium Campaign (12–15 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Man of Bronze
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Scientific Adventure / Superscience Pulp / Team Action
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 12–15 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **10–12**
**Tone (keywords):** scientific heroism, gadget mastery, team specialists, fantastic science, Fortress of Solitude, bronze perfection, "let me elicit your cooperation"
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party are a team of specialists—each a master of their field—led by a bronze-skinned superman trained from birth to be the perfect human specimen. They tackle threats too strange for normal authorities: mad scientists, lost civilizations, superscience weapons, and criminal masterminds. Operating from their hidden Arctic fortress and Manhattan headquarters, they use science, gadgets, and coordinated teamwork to protect humanity. They fight with honor, never kill, and believe science can solve any problem.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Puzzle Solving:** Scientific mysteries requiring specialized knowledge.
*   **Team Coordination:** Each member has unique skills; success requires cooperation.
*   **Non-Lethal Combat:** Heroes never kill; must subdue, not destroy.
*   **Superscience Threats:** Mad science devices, impossible inventions, weird technology.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 12–15 sessions)

*   **Act I — The Man of Bronze (Sessions 1–3):** Team formation. First case involves father's death and mysterious inheritance. Introduction to the Fortress of Solitude. Encounter with ancient Mayan technology.
*   **Act II — The Land of Terror (Sessions 4–8):** Investigation of lost valley where prehistoric creatures still live. Mad scientist using dinosaur DNA for weapons. Rescue mission becomes scientific expedition.
*   **Act III — The Phantom City (Sessions 9–12):** Discovery of hidden city beneath Manhattan. Ancient civilization with advanced technology. Criminal organization seeks to weaponize it. Time-pressure mission.
*   **Act IV — The Devil Genghis (Sessions 13–15):** International conspiracy to destabilize world peace using superscience weapons. Final showdown against mastermind who represents everything Doc opposes. Fortress secrets revealed.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Doc Savage (Clark Savage Jr.)** — *The Man of Bronze*
    *   Trained from infancy to be perfect. Genius-level intelligence, Olympic-level physique, master of all sciences. Bronze skin, golden eyes. Never kills. Party leader or allied NPC.

2.  **The Fabulous Five** — *The Specialists*
    *   **Monk Mayfair:** Chemist, impossibly strong, ape-like appearance, comic relief
    *   **Ham Brooks:** Lawyer, dapper dresser, constantly feuds with Monk
    *   **Long Tom Roberts:** Electrical engineer, pale and sickly-looking, genius with voltage
    *   **Renny Renwick:** Engineer, massive fists, says "Holy cow!" constantly
    *   **Johnny Littlejohn:** Archaeologist/geologist, uses big words, wears monocle

3.  **John Sunlight** — *The Criminal Mastermind*
    *   Only villain who truly challenges Doc intellectually. Escaped from Fortress prison. Seeks Doc's inventions for conquest. Main nemesis.

4.  **Patricia Savage** — *The Cousin*
    *   Doc's cousin, red-haired spitfire. Wants to join adventures despite Doc's protective nature. Actually quite capable.

5.  **The Lost Tribe** — *Ancient Guardians*
    *   Descendants of Mayans who guard powerful artifacts. Doc's sworn duty to protect them.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Fortress of Solitude:** Arctic hideaway carved into ice. Contains laboratory, library, prison for criminals being reformed, and Doc's most dangerous inventions.
2.  **86th Floor Headquarters:** Manhattan skyscraper. Public face of operations, communication hub, garage for specialized vehicles.
3.  **The Lost Valley:** Hidden prehistoric preserve. Dinosaurs and ancient technology coexist.
4.  **The Phantom City:** Underground metropolis beneath New York. Advanced ancient civilization.
5.  **Doc's Garage:** Contains specialized vehicles—armored cars, autogyros, submarines, all equipped with gadgets.

---

## Central Conflict

Science versus superstition, cooperation versus competition. Doc and team represent the ideal that science and reason can overcome any obstacle. They face mad scientists who pervert knowledge for selfish ends, ancient powers that defy explanation, and criminals who exploit both. The team must work together—no one member can succeed alone. They also grapple with Doc's non-killing philosophy: is it moral to let villains live if they'll strike again?

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Specialist Expertise**
*   Each party member is unparalleled in their field. Auto-succeed on checks within specialty, grant advantage to allies.

### 2. **Gadget Arsenal**
*   Doc's inventions: anesthetic gas bombs, super-flashlights, listening devices, chemical tracers, bulletproof vests (made of silk), portable lab kits.

### 3. **The Bronze Voice**
*   Doc's distinctive trilling sound—a form of unconscious exercise performed when thinking. Can be detected by enemies but also intimidates.

### 4. **Non-Lethal Takedowns**
*   Required to subdue, not kill. Mercy bullets, gas grenades, nerve pinches. Killing is failure.

### 5. **Team Coordination**
*   Special team maneuvers where specialists combine their talents for incredible effects.

### 6. **Scientific Deduction**
*   Complex investigation mechanics using specialized knowledge to piece together mad science plots.

### 7. **The Fortress Prison**
*   Captured villains sent to Fortress for "reform through brain surgery" (ethically questionable by modern standards but played straight in genre).

*   **The Supermachine Pistol:** Fires mercy bullets (anesthetic) with perfect accuracy.
*   **Doc's Vest:** Bulletproof silk, saved his life countless times.
*   **The Autogyro:** Advanced aircraft for rapid transport.
*   **Ancient Mayan Gold:** Powerful artifacts from lost civilization.
*   **The Death Ray:** Captured from villain, too dangerous to use, locked in Fortress.

## Tone and Atmosphere

Optimistic superscience pulp. Problems can be solved through reason and teamwork. Heroes are genuinely heroic—they help everyone, spare enemies, and never pursue personal gain. 1930s aesthetic meets impossible technology—death rays, giant robots, lost civilizations, all treated as scientific possibilities. Banter between team members provides levity. Action is thrilling but not graphically violent. The world is full of wonder and danger, but good guys win through intelligence and cooperation. Doc himself is nearly superhuman but his humanity shows in protecting others.

---

## Why This Campaign Is Memorable

*   **Team Dynamics:** Each player has unique specialty; must work together.
*   **Superscience Gadgets:** Impossible inventions that nonetheless follow "scientific" logic.
*   **Non-Lethal Heroism:** Challenge of defeating villains without killing.
*   **The Fortress:** Iconic base with laboratory, library, and secrets.
*   **Optimistic Heroism:** Good guys who are genuinely, uncomplicatedly good.

## Adventure Hooks

*   Message from Doc's late father leads to mysterious inheritance.
*   Reports of dinosaurs in remote valley—scientific expedition needed.
*   Mad scientist threatens city with stolen superweapon.
*   Ancient artifact surfacing at auction—multiple factions competing.
*   Team member kidnapped by villain seeking their specialized knowledge.

---

## Ending Options

**Epic Victory:** Villain defeated and sent to Fortress for reform. Superweapon secured or destroyed. World saved through science. Team stronger than ever.

**Pyrrhic Victory:** Fortress compromised, must be abandoned. Dangerous inventions destroyed to prevent misuse. Victory achieved but at cost of resources.

**Bittersweet:** Villain reformed but innocent lives lost in the process. Team questions Doc's no-killing rule. Science proved inadequate against some threats. Victory is incomplete.
