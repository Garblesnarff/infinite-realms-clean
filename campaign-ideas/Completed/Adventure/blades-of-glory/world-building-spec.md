### **Template: World-Building Specification Brief**

**Blades of Glory**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** A fallen figure skater who crumbled under pressure at their last major competition encounters Viktor Nikiforov, a living legend, who mysteriously offers to coach them. Through rediscovering their love of skating, developing routines that combine technical perfection with emotional storytelling, and overcoming anxiety, they compete in the Grand Prix series facing international champions. The coach-student relationship deepens as both discover what they truly want from skating—and from each other.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the history of competitive figure skating and its evolution from sport to art form.
    *   Write Viktor Nikiforov's legendary career—five consecutive world championships and his style innovations.
    *   Describe the Grand Prix series structure: six events, top six scorers advance to Finals.
    *   Explain the scoring system: Technical Elements Score and Program Components Score.
    *   Detail famous skating venues and their unique characteristics.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **International Skating Union** (Major): Governing body managing rules, competitions, and rankings.
    *   **Russian Skating Federation** (Major): Powerhouse producing champions like Viktor and Yuri Plisetsky.
    *   **Various National Teams** (Minor): Japanese, American, Swiss, Chinese teams each with unique styles.
    *   **Coaching Community** (Minor): Network of coaches who train and compete through their students.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Viktor Nikiforov (coach/legend), Yuri Plisetsky (rival/former student), Christophe Giacometti (competitor/friend).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Grand Prix Competitor" (international skater), "Choreographer" (program designer), "Veteran Coach" (experienced trainer), "Former Champion" (retired legend), "Sports Psychologist" (mental game support).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Hasetsu Ice Castle:** Small-town rink with single regulation rink, spectator seating for 200, family-owned warmth, protagonist's home ice.
*   **Yutopia Hot Springs:** Traditional Japanese inn with natural hot springs, guest rooms, dining area, recovery and bonding location.
*   **Grand Prix Venues:** Rotating international locations—Moscow's grandeur, Beijing's modernity, Paris's elegance, Barcelona's passion—each with 5,000+ seat arenas.
*   **Training Facilities:** Elite rinks with video analysis, choreography studios, conditioning gyms, ice time scheduling.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Protagonist lands clean quad in competition → **THEN:** Confidence increases, media attention grows, rivals take them seriously.
*   **IF:** Performance anxiety causes mistake → **THEN:** Confidence drops, must work with Viktor to rebuild mental game.
*   **IF:** Place top 2 in Grand Prix event → **THEN:** Advance toward Finals qualification, pressure intensifies.
*   **IF:** Qualify for Grand Prix Final → **THEN:** Face six best skaters in world, international spotlight, career-defining moment.
*   **IF:** Viktor announces comeback → **THEN:** Coach-student relationship evolves to equals, future competitions against each other.
