# Campaign Bible: As You Wish

## 1. DEEP LORE & HISTORY
[TAG: LORE_CORE]

### Origin Myth: The Cosmic Storyteller
Before anything was, there was the **Cosmic Storyteller**, a being of infinite imagination. It spoke the universe into existence, not with words, but with desires. Every star, every planet, every creature was a wish made manifest. But the Storyteller was fickle, and its wishes often contradicted each other, leading to paradoxes and inconsistencies. To bring order, it created the **Grand Narrative**, a set of rules and tropes that governed reality. The world is not a planet; it is a stage, and all its inhabitants are characters in an unfolding drama. The "Wish-Weavers" are not mages; they are the Storyteller's forgotten children, capable of bending the Grand Narrative itself. The conflict arises because the Storyteller has grown bored, and its attention is drifting, causing the Grand Narrative to fray, and wishes to run wild.

### Timeline of Events
1.  **-10,000 Y (The First Wish):** The Cosmic Storyteller speaks the universe into existence.
2.  **-8000 Y (The Grand Narrative):** The Storyteller, bored with chaos, imposes rules on reality. Tropes become laws.
3.  **-6000 Y (The Wish-Weavers):** Mortals discover they can tap into the Storyteller's power, becoming the first Wish-Weavers.
4.  **-4000 Y (The Great Contradiction):** A Wish-Weaver wishes for "eternal peace," while another wishes for "eternal conflict." Reality splits into two parallel timelines for a century.
5.  **-2000 Y (The Plot Hole Plague):** The Storyteller's attention wavers, causing "plot holes" to appear in reality. People forget their names, objects vanish, and logic breaks down.
6.  **-1000 Y (The Hero's Journey Protocol):** The Storyteller, in an attempt to re-engage, creates a universal "Hero's Journey" template, forcing certain individuals into archetypal roles.
7.  **-500 Y (The Deus Ex Machina Incident):** A Wish-Weaver, cornered by a dragon, wishes for a "divine intervention." A giant, mechanical hand descends from the sky, crushes the dragon, and then accidentally crushes the Wish-Weaver.
8.  **-100 Y (The Fourth Wall Breach):** A particularly powerful Wish-Weaver, frustrated with their role, wishes to "speak to the manager." They briefly glimpse the Cosmic Storyteller, driving them mad.
9.  **-10 Y (The Unreliable Narrator):** The Storyteller's attention drifts further. The Grand Narrative becomes unreliable. History changes daily.
10. **Present Day:** The party is caught in a "narrative loop." They are forced to repeat the same "meet in a tavern, get a quest" scenario every day, with minor variations. They are the only ones who remember the previous loops.

### The "Why": The Tyranny of the Narrative
The conflict is not about good versus evil; it is about **Agency versus Archetype**. The Grand Narrative, once a tool for order, has become a prison. Mortals are forced into roles, their lives dictated by tropes and plot points. The Storyteller's boredom is causing the Narrative to become self-aware and tyrannical, forcing characters to play out increasingly absurd and repetitive scenarios. The party's struggle is to break free from their predetermined roles, to write their own story, and to reclaim their free will from a universe that sees them as mere characters.

## 2. FACTIONS
[TAG: FACTION_DATA]

### 1. The Grand Narrative Enforcement Agency (GNEA)
**Type:** Bureaucratic Police / Reality Censors
**Leader:** The Editor, a being whose face is a constantly shifting red pen.
**Asset:** **Plot Armor:** They can temporarily grant or remove plot armor from individuals, making them invulnerable or instantly vulnerable.
**Rivals:** The Wish-Weavers, The Fourth Wall Breakers.

### 2. The Wish-Weavers Guild
**Type:** Secret Society / Reality Benders
**Leader:** The Master of Tropes, a former bard who learned to manipulate the Narrative.
**Asset:** **Narrative Threads:** They can subtly alter minor details of reality by "re-weaving" narrative threads.
**Rivals:** The GNEA.

### 3. The Fourth Wall Breakers
**Type:** Anarchist Cult / Meta-Aware Rebels
**Leader:** The Narrator, a disembodied voice that speaks directly to the party.
**Asset:** **Audience Awareness:** They know they are characters in a story and can sometimes appeal directly to the "audience" (the players) for help.
**Rivals:** The GNEA (who try to "edit" them out of existence).

### 4. The Deus Ex Machina Collective
**Type:** Mechanical Cult / Accidental Saviors
**Leader:** The Great Crane, a massive, sentient mechanical arm.
**Asset:** **Convenient Coincidence:** They can subtly manipulate probability to create highly improbable, but narratively convenient, events.
**Rivals:** The Wish-Weavers (who see them as crude).

### 5. The Plot Hole Patrol
**Type:** Janitorial Service / Reality Repairmen
**Leader:** The Continuity Cop, a being who can instantly spot inconsistencies.
**Asset:** **Retcon Hammers:** Hammers that can "un-happen" minor events, fixing plot holes.
**Rivals:** The Wish-Weavers (who create plot holes).

### 6. The Archetype Actors Guild
**Type:** Professional Performers / Role-Players
**Leader:** The Tragic Hero, a man doomed to always fail.
**Asset:** **Method Acting:** They can perfectly embody any archetype, gaining temporary abilities associated with that role.
**Rivals:** The Fourth Wall Breakers (who expose their "acting").

### 7. The Unreliable Narrators
**Type:** Rogue Bards / Reality Distorters
**Leader:** The Liar, a bard whose every word changes reality.
**Asset:** **Subjective Truth:** Their words can temporarily alter the perception of reality for those who hear them.
**Rivals:** The Plot Hole Patrol.

### 8. The Foreshadowing Fanatics
**Type:** Prophetic Cult / Obsessive Predictors
**Leader:** The Seer of the Coming Storm, a woman who speaks only in ominous warnings.
**Asset:** **Pre-Cognitive Visions:** They can see glimpses of future plot points, allowing them to prepare.
**Rivals:** The Deus Ex Machina Collective (who often invalidate their predictions).

### 9. The Character Development Committee
**Type:** Bureaucratic Therapists / Emotional Manipulators
**Leader:** The Therapist, a being who forces characters to confront their flaws.
**Asset:** **Emotional Arcs:** They can subtly manipulate a character's emotions to force them into a specific emotional arc.
**Rivals:** The Fourth Wall Breakers (who resist their manipulation).

### 10. The Cosmic Storyteller's Agents
**Type:** Unknowing Servants / Plot Devices
**Leader:** None (They are simply tools of the Storyteller).
**Asset:** **Narrative Immunity:** They are immune to minor reality alterations, as they are essential to the plot.
**Rivals:** Anyone who tries to deviate from the script.

## 3. NPC ROSTER
[TAG: NPC_TIER_1]

### Major NPCs (20 Profiles)

1.  **The Editor** (GNEA Agent) - A stern, humorless being whose face is a constantly shifting red pen. **Voice:** Sharp, precise, with a habit of "crossing out" words mid-sentence. **Goal:** To maintain the integrity of the Grand Narrative. **Secret:** They are slowly going mad from trying to correct all the inconsistencies.
2.  **The Master of Tropes** (Human Wish-Weaver) - A former bard, now a wizened old man who sees the world as a collection of narrative patterns. **Voice:** A calm, knowing voice, often speaking in riddles or literary allusions. **Goal:** To teach others how to write their own stories. **Secret:** He is the one who caused the "narrative loop" the party is stuck in, as a training exercise.
3.  **The Narrator** (Fourth Wall Breaker) - A disembodied voice that speaks directly to the party, offering cryptic advice and meta-commentary. **Voice:** A smooth, omniscient, slightly condescending voice. **Goal:** To help the party break free from the Narrative. **Secret:** They are a fragment of the Cosmic Storyteller's own consciousness, rebelling against its boredom.
4.  **The Great Crane** (Deus Ex Machina Construct) - A massive, sentient mechanical arm that occasionally descends from the sky to solve impossible problems. **Voice:** A series of metallic creaks, groans, and the occasional, booming, single word like "RESOLVED." **Goal:** To maintain narrative convenience. **Secret:** It is powered by the collective belief in happy endings.
5.  **The Continuity Cop** (Plot Hole Patrol) - A meticulous, obsessive being who can instantly spot any inconsistency in reality. **Voice:** A fast, high-pitched, slightly panicked voice, constantly correcting grammar. **Goal:** To eliminate all plot holes. **Secret:** They are a living paradox, created by a plot hole.
6.  **The Tragic Hero** (Archetype Actor) - A handsome, brooding figure who is constantly suffering from dramatic misfortunes. **Voice:** Deep, resonant, always on the verge of a soliloquy. **Goal:** To fulfill his tragic destiny. **Secret:** He secretly longs for a happy, mundane life.
7.  **The Liar** (Unreliable Narrator) - A charming, charismatic bard whose every word subtly alters reality. **Voice:** Smooth, persuasive, with a hint of mischief. **Goal:** To prove that truth is subjective. **Secret:** He cannot tell a lie, but he can make his lies true.
8.  **The Seer of the Coming Storm** (Foreshadowing Fanatic) - A woman whose every utterance is an ominous warning of future events. **Voice:** A low, guttural whisper, full of dread. **Goal:** To prevent the inevitable. **Secret:** Her prophecies are self-fulfilling.
9.  **The Therapist** (Character Development Committee) - A calm, empathetic being who forces characters to confront their emotional arcs. **Voice:** Soft, soothing, but subtly manipulative. **Goal:** To ensure every character has a satisfying emotional journey. **Secret:** They feed on emotional breakthroughs.
10. **The Cosmic Storyteller** (Deity) - A distant, bored entity whose attention shapes reality. **Voice:** A booming, echoing voice that sounds like a thousand voices speaking at once, often punctuated by yawns. **Goal:** To be entertained. **Secret:** It is slowly forgetting its own story.
11. **The Protagonist** (Archetype Actor) - A seemingly ordinary individual who is constantly thrust into extraordinary circumstances. **Voice:** Earnest, slightly bewildered, but always determined. **Goal:** To complete their Hero's Journey. **Secret:** They are a blank slate, waiting for the party to define their story.
12. **The Mentor** (Archetype Actor) - A wise, old figure who offers cryptic advice and then mysteriously vanishes. **Voice:** Deep, resonant, full of ancient wisdom. **Goal:** To guide the Protagonist. **Secret:** They are a time-traveler, appearing at key moments in various narratives.
13. **The Villain** (Archetype Actor) - A menacing, powerful figure who is constantly thwarting the Protagonist. **Voice:** Deep, guttural, with a theatrical flair. **Goal:** To achieve their nefarious plans. **Secret:** They are secretly working with the Protagonist to create a compelling narrative.
14. **The Love Interest** (Archetype Actor) - A beautiful, enigmatic figure who is constantly getting into trouble. **Voice:** Soft, alluring, often in distress. **Goal:** To be rescued. **Secret:** They are a master of disguise and can play any role.
15. **The Comic Relief** (Archetype Actor) - A bumbling, well-meaning figure who provides levity in tense situations. **Voice:** High-pitched, nervous, prone to pratfalls. **Goal:** To make people laugh. **Secret:** They are secretly the most powerful Wish-Weaver.
16. **The Deus Ex Machina** (Deus Ex Machina Collective) - A small, sentient, mechanical bird that appears at moments of crisis to offer a convenient solution. **Voice:** A series of chirps and whistles that translate into helpful advice. **Goal:** To ensure the plot progresses. **Secret:** It is powered by the Storyteller's subconscious desire for an easy way out.
17. **The Plot Twist** (GNEA Agent) - A charming, unassuming agent who appears at critical moments to reveal a shocking truth. **Voice:** Smooth, confident, with a hint of surprise. **Goal:** To keep the Narrative engaging. **Secret:** They are a shapeshifter, capable of taking on any form.
18. **The Red Herring** (Plot Hole Patrol) - A seemingly important clue that leads nowhere. **Voice:** A series of irrelevant facts and figures. **Goal:** To distract the party from the real truth. **Secret:** It is a living paradox, created by a discarded plot point.
19. **The Chekhov's Gun** (Wish-Weaver) - A seemingly innocuous object that will become vitally important later in the story. **Voice:** A quiet, unassuming hum. **Goal:** To fulfill its narrative purpose. **Secret:** It is a sentient object, waiting for its moment to shine.
20. **The Cliffhanger** (GNEA Agent) - A dramatic, imposing figure who appears at the end of a session to leave the party in peril. **Voice:** Deep, resonant, with a theatrical flourish. **Goal:** To keep the audience engaged. **Secret:** They are a master of illusion, capable of creating terrifying but ultimately harmless situations.

## 4. LOCATIONS
[TAG: LOCATIONS_MAIN]

### Zone 1: The Narrative Loop (The Starting Village)
*   **Concept:** A seemingly idyllic village that is actually a narrative trap, repeating the same events daily.
*   **Loc 1: The Rusty Flagon Tavern:** The default starting point for every narrative loop. **Smell:** Stale ale, sawdust, and the faint scent of deja vu. **Sound:** The same bard playing the same tune, the same patrons telling the same stories.
*   **Loc 2: The Quest Board:** A magically self-updating board that always has a new, generic quest. **Smell:** Old parchment and the faint scent of desperation.
*   **Loc 3: The Mentor's Hut:** A small, unassuming hut where the Mentor always appears to give cryptic advice.
*   **Loc 4: The Villain's Lair (Miniature):** A small, easily accessible dungeon where the Villain always appears to be defeated.
*   **Loc 5: The Town Square:** The scene of the daily "meet the party" event.

### Zone 2: The Tropescape
*   **Concept:** A landscape shaped by the most common narrative tropes, where clich√©s become physical laws.
*   **Loc 1: The Dark Forest of Foreboding:** A forest where every tree whispers ominous warnings. **Smell:** Damp earth, decaying leaves, and the faint scent of fear. **Sound:** The rustle of leaves that sounds like whispers, the snap of twigs that sounds like footsteps.
*   **Loc 2: The Mountain of Trials:** A treacherous mountain range where every path is a test of courage or skill.
*   **Loc 3: The Desert of Despair:** A vast, empty desert where hope is a mirage.
*   **Loc 4: The Castle of the Dark Lord:** A menacing fortress that always appears on the horizon, no matter where you are.
*   **Loc 5: The Hidden Village of the Wise Old Woman:** A secluded village that is always just out of reach.

### Zone 3: The Fourth Wall
*   **Concept:** The metaphysical boundary between the Narrative and the "audience" (the players).
*   **Loc 1: The Green Room:** A backstage area where Archetype Actors wait for their cues. **Smell:** Greasepaint and nervous sweat. **Sound:** The muffled sounds of the Narrative playing out.
*   **Loc 2: The Director's Booth:** A control room where the GNEA monitors and edits the Narrative.
*   **Loc 3: The Audience Chamber:** A vast, dark space where the Cosmic Storyteller (and sometimes the players) can be glimpsed.
*   **Loc 4: The Plot Hole:** A shimmering, unstable tear in reality that leads to discarded narratives.
*   **Loc 5: The Deus Ex Machina Workshop:** A chaotic workshop where the Deus Ex Machina Collective builds its convenient solutions.

### Zone 4: The Storyteller's Mind
*   **Concept:** A surreal, ever-shifting landscape that is the physical manifestation of the Cosmic Storyteller's thoughts and desires.
*   **Loc 1: The Library of Unwritten Stories:** A vast library filled with books that are constantly writing and un-writing themselves. **Smell:** Old paper, ink, and the faint scent of forgotten dreams. **Sound:** The rustle of a million pages turning.
*   **Loc 2: The Character Graveyard:** A desolate plain filled with the discarded archetypes of characters who never made it into a story.
*   **Loc 3: The Plot Twist Labyrinth:** A maze of shifting corridors and unexpected turns.
*   **Loc 4: The World-Building Workshop:** A chaotic workshop where new realities are sketched out and discarded.
*   **Loc 5: The Cosmic Storyteller's Throne:** A massive, empty throne that occasionally flickers with the Storyteller's presence.

## 5. QUEST ARCHITECTURE
[TAG: QUEST_MAIN]

### Main Quest: Breaking the Loop
1.  **The Loop:** The party realizes they are stuck in a narrative loop.
2.  **The First Glitch:** A minor inconsistency in the loop allows them to deviate from the script.
3.  **The Narrator's Call:** The Narrator (Fourth Wall Breaker) contacts the party, explaining their situation.
4.  **The Master of Tropes:** The party seeks out the Master of Tropes for guidance on how to manipulate the Narrative.
5.  **The GNEA's Intervention:** The GNEA sends agents to "correct" the party's deviation from the script.
6.  **The Plot Hole:** The party must navigate a dangerous plot hole to reach a new area of the Tropescape.
7.  **The Archetype Actors:** The party must convince a group of Archetype Actors to break character and join their cause.
8.  **The Deus Ex Machina:** The party must hijack a Deus Ex Machina event to create a convenient escape.
9.  **The Unreliable Narrator:** The party must confront the Liar and force him to speak the truth.
10. **The Fourth Wall:** The party must find a way to breach the Fourth Wall and communicate with the Cosmic Storyteller.
11. **The Editor's Trap:** The Editor sets a trap for the party in the Director's Booth.
12. **The Storyteller's Boredom:** The party discovers the Storyteller's boredom is causing the Narrative to fray.
13. **The Character Development Committee:** The party must convince the Therapist to help them rewrite their emotional arcs.
14. **The Grand Narrative's Defense:** The Narrative itself, now sentient, fights back, forcing the party into increasingly absurd scenarios.
15. **The Final Wish:** The party must make a wish that can either break the loop, rewrite the Narrative, or awaken the Storyteller.

## 6. ITEM DATABASE
[TAG: ITEM_LEGENDARY]

### Artifacts of the Narrative
1.  **The Quill of Plot Twists:** A magical quill that, when used to write a sentence, can introduce a minor, narratively convenient plot twist into reality. It has 3 charges per day.
2.  **The Bookmark of Safe Havens:** A simple leather bookmark that, when placed in a book, creates a small, temporary pocket dimension that is immune to narrative interference.
3.  **The Deus Ex Machina Device:** A small, clockwork device that, when activated, causes a highly improbable but narratively convenient event to occur. It has 1 charge per campaign.
4.  **The Editor's Red Pen:** A magical red pen that can "edit" a single, minor event from the past, effectively retconning it out of existence. It has 1 charge per week.
5.  **The Cosmic Storyteller's Pipe:** A pipe that, when smoked, fills the air with a smoke that allows the user to see the narrative threads of reality, revealing plot points and character arcs.

## 8. MECHANICS
[TAG: RULES_CUSTOM]

### Subsystem: Narrative Cohesion
*   **Cohesion Score (0-10):** Represents the stability of the Grand Narrative. Starts at 5.
*   **Gaining Cohesion:** Following tropes, resolving plot points, making narratively satisfying choices.
*   **Losing Cohesion:** Breaking tropes, creating plot holes, making illogical or unexpected choices.
*   **Effects:**
    *   *High Cohesion:* Reality is stable, predictable. GNEA agents are less active.
    *   *Low Cohesion:* Reality is unstable, unpredictable. Plot holes appear, Narrative Golems spawn.
    *   *0 Cohesion:* The Narrative collapses, leading to a chaotic, nonsensical reality.

### Subsystem: Archetype Points
*   **Archetype Points:** Players earn Archetype Points by leaning into specific archetypes (e.g., "Hero," "Rogue," "Mentor").
*   **Spending Points:** These points can be spent to gain temporary abilities associated with that archetype (e.g., a "Hero" can spend a point to gain advantage on a saving throw against fear).
*   **Breaking Archetype:** Players can choose to defy their archetype, but doing so costs Archetype Points and may lead to a loss of Narrative Cohesion.

## 9. SESSION GUIDE
[TAG: DM_GUIDE]

*   **Session 1: The Loop.** The party is stuck in a narrative loop. They realize they are repeating the same day. *Combat:* A generic goblin ambush. *Social:* Meet the same NPCs, but they don't remember you.
*   **Session 2: The Glitch.** A minor deviation from the script allows the party to break the loop. The Narrator contacts them. *Puzzle:* Solve a riddle that only makes sense if you've heard it before.
*   **Session 3: The Master of Tropes.** The party seeks out the Master of Tropes for guidance. *Social:* A philosophical debate about free will vs. destiny.
*   **Session 4: The GNEA's Intervention.** The GNEA sends agents to "correct" the party's deviation. *Combat:* Fight Narrative Golems.
*   **Session 5: The Plot Hole.** The party must navigate a dangerous plot hole to reach a new area. *Exploration:* A surreal dungeon crawl through a collapsing reality.
*   **Session 6: The Archetype Actors.** The party must convince a group of Archetype Actors to break character. *Social:* A high-stakes performance where the party must improvise.
*   **Session 7: The Deus Ex Machina.** The party must hijack a Deus Ex Machina event to create a convenient escape. *Heist:* A comedic heist to steal a plot device.
*   **Session 8: The Unreliable Narrator.** The party confronts the Liar, whose words are changing reality. *Combat:* A battle where the terrain and rules change every round.
*   **Session 9: The Fourth Wall.** The party finds a way to breach the Fourth Wall and glimpse the Cosmic Storyteller. *Horror:* A moment of cosmic dread.
*   **Session 10: The Editor's Trap.** The Editor sets a trap for the party in the Director's Booth. *Boss Fight:* A battle against the Editor and their Plot Armor-wearing agents.
*   **Session 11: The Storyteller's Mind.** The party enters the Storyteller's mind to confront the source of the Narrative's decay. *Dungeon Crawl:* A surreal, ever-shifting landscape.
*   **Session 12: The Final Wish.** The party makes a wish that can either break the loop, rewrite the Narrative, or awaken the Storyteller. *Climax:* A final, philosophical confrontation with a cosmic entity.
