**The Ghost of Midgard**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** A legendary god-killer and his son journey to scatter ashes from the highest peak in all realms. They navigate Norse mythology made real—gods, giants, and prophecy—while the father struggles to be better than the monster he was. The boy learns he's Loki, prophesied to start Ragnarok, as father and son grow closer through brutal combat and quiet teaching moments.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the Nine Realms of Norse cosmology and how they connect through Yggdrasil the World Tree.
    *   Write the history of the Aesir-Vanir War and Freya's forced exile to Midgard.
    *   Describe Odin's genocide of the giants and his obsession with avoiding his prophesied death at Ragnarok.
    *   Explain the father's true origin—Kratos, the Ghost of Sparta, who destroyed the Greek pantheon.
    *   Detail the prophecy of Ragnarok, Fimbulwinter, and Loki's role in the gods' destruction.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Aesir Gods** (Major): Odin's pantheon ruling from Asgard, obsessed with preventing Ragnarok, willing to commit atrocities.
    *   **The Vanir Gods** (Minor): Old gods of nature, mostly disappeared or imprisoned after the war.
    *   **The Giants** (Major/Historical): Mostly extinct, genocided by Odin, their prophetic murals remain showing possible futures.
    *   **The Dwarves** (Minor): Blacksmiths Brok and Sindri, master craftsmen, comedic relief but deeply skilled.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Kratos/The Father (protagonist), Atreus/The Boy (companion), Freya (ally-turned-enemy), Baldur (primary antagonist), Mimir (guide/head).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Thor's Sons" (Magni and Modi, overconfident gods), "Dwarf Blacksmith" (Brok or Sindri), "Valkyrie Queen" (optional super boss), "Draugr" (undead warriors), "Troll" (massive boss encounters).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The Lake of Nine:** Central hub in Midgard, water level changes reveal new areas, connects to all realms.
*   **Midgard:** Norse mortal realm, frozen forests, mountain peaks, ruins of giant civilization scattered everywhere.
*   **Alfheim:** Realm of light elves, golden temple, caught in civil war with dark elves.
*   **Helheim:** Land of the dead, frozen ships, ruled by Hel, travelers face their past.
*   **Jotunheim:** Abandoned giant realm, massive architecture, murals showing prophecy and history.
*   **Freya's Garden:** Hidden grove in Midgard, magical sanctuary, cannot leave but safe from Asgard.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Father uses Spartan Rage frequently → **THEN:** The boy becomes frightened and comments on his father's violence.
*   **IF:** They kill Magni and Modi → **THEN:** Thor becomes aware of them as threats, increasing divine attention.
*   **IF:** Baldur's invulnerability is broken → **THEN:** He can finally feel, driving him to madness with sensation.
*   **IF:** They show mercy to Baldur → **THEN:** He attacks Freya, forcing them to kill him anyway—mercy has a cost.
*   **IF:** Baldur dies → **THEN:** Fimbulwinter begins immediately, snow falls for three years, Ragnarok countdown starts.
*   **IF:** Boy learns he's Loki → **THEN:** His powers manifest more strongly but he questions his identity and destiny.
