
# Bracers of Liberl: Campaign Bible

## [TAG: LORE_CORE]
### Origin Myth
The world of Zemuria is a living, feeling entity. Its lifeblood is not magma, but "Orbal Energy," a force generated by the emotional resonance of the planet's crust. For millennia, this energy was drawn upon in small, sustainable ways by mystics, artists, and empaths. It was the force that made songs moving, paintings beautiful, and love profound. Fifty years ago, the "Orbal Revolution," pioneered by the brilliant Professor Epstein in the Kingdom of Liberl, changed everything. Epstein discovered how to mechanize the extraction and use of Orbal Energy, creating a technological utopia of airships, firearms, and automated factories. But this industrial-scale extraction came at a terrible cost. The revolution is "strip-mining" the planet's soul. The world is literally fading; colors are becoming muted, flavors are less intense, music is becoming formulaic, and people are slowly sinking into a placid, grey apathy. The "Bracers" are a non-governmental guild of adventurers, investigators, and peacekeepers, founded to deal with the societal upheaval caused by the Orbal Revolution. They are the world's therapists and problem-solvers, tasked with helping the common people, but they are now finding themselves on the front line of a metaphysical crisis they were never meant to handle.

### Timeline
1.  **The Age of Resonance (Ancient History):** All magic is "emotional," powered directly by the user's connection to the planet's soul. Great works of art are also great works of magic.
2.  **The First Disharmony (c. -2,000 years):** A great, emotionless, dragon-like being, a "Hollow," appears and consumes the emotional energy of an entire empire, leaving it a grey, silent ruin. It is defeated by a hero who "out-feels" it.
3.  **The Dark Ages (c. -1,200 to -500 years):** A period of chaos and war. The constant negative emotions create "Distortions" in the Orbal field, spawning monsters and making life difficult.
4.  **The Founding of the Bracer Guild (c. -450 years):** The Bracer Guild is founded to protect civilians from monsters and act as a neutral peacekeeping force between nations.
5.  **The Orbal Revolution (c. -50 years):** Professor Epstein's discoveries in Liberl lead to the mass production of Orbal technology. Airships, factories, and modern conveniences spread rapidly.
6.  **(Surreal) The Great Fade (c. -25 years):** Over the course of a single year, the color blue becomes 10% less vibrant across the entire world. Only a few artists and mystics notice. This is the first sign of the planet's "emotional drain."
7.  **The Hundred Days War (c. -10 years):** The technologically advanced Erebonian Empire invades the Kingdom of Liberl, seeking to control its Orbal technology. The war is fought with airships and Orbal rifles. Liberl, with the help of the Bracer Guild, repels the invasion, but the massive expenditure of Orbal energy accelerates the "fading."
8.  **(Surreal) The Day of Silence (c. -5 years):** For 24 hours, no one on the planet is capable of laughing. All attempts at humor fail, all jokes fall flat. The silence is deafening.
9.  **The Appearance of the Hollow Zones (c. -1 year to present):** The first "Hollow Zones" appearâ€”areas where the Orbal field has completely collapsed. Within these zones, magic fails, technology sputters and dies, and emotions are non-existent. People who wander in become placid, empty shells.
10. **(Surreal) The Color-Thief (c. -1 week):** A mysterious phenomenon begins where people's most cherished, colorful memories are turning to black and white in their minds. The planet's soul is beginning to "reclaim" its emotional energy in a desperate attempt to survive.

### The "Why"
The metaphysical conflict is a battle between Utility and Beauty, Progress and Passion. The Orbal Revolution is not an evil, but a "solution" that has created a far greater problem. The world is dying not from a plague or a war, but from apathy. The players, as Bracers, are uniquely positioned to deal with this. Their job is to help people, but how can you help a world that is forgetting how to feel? The central conflict is not about defeating a dark lord, but about finding a way to re-ignite the world's soul. Can they find a "new," sustainable way to use Orbal energy? Can they convince the world to give up its modern conveniences to save its own heart? Or will they be forced to fight the ultimate enemy: a creeping, comfortable, and utterly soul-crushing ennui?

## [TAG: FACTION_DATA]
### The Bracer Guild
**Type:** Non-Governmental Peacekeeping Organization
**Leader:** The Guildmaster, a rotating position. Currently, Cassius Bright.
**Asset:** International neutrality, public trust, and a network of highly-skilled agents (the players). They are the "problem-solvers" of the world.
**Rivals:** The Erebonian Intelligence Division, Ouroboros.

### The Royal Army of Liberl
**Type:** Technologically Advanced Military
**Leader:** General Morgan
**Asset:** A fleet of advanced airships and a well-trained, professional army. They are proud, and wary of foreign interference.
**Rivals:** The Erebonian Empire.

### The Erebonian Empire's Intelligence Division
**Type:** Imperialist Secret Police
**Leader:** The Iron Chancellor, a ruthless and brilliant political mastermind.
**Asset:** A vast network of spies and saboteurs. They seek to destabilize Liberl and steal its advanced Orbal technology for the glory of the Empire.
**Rivals:** The Bracer Guild, The Royal Army of Liberl.

### The Chromatic Cantors
**Type:** Artistic Terrorists
**Leader:** The Maestro, a composer who believes he can write a "symphony of salvation."
**Asset:** The ability to weaponize emotion and art. They can create paintings that inspire fanatical devotion, or songs that cause riots. They seek to create a "masterpiece" of such emotional power it will reset the entire Orbal network.
**Rivals:** The Septian Church, The Bracer Guild (who have to clean up their messes).

### The Septian Church
**Type:** Monotheistic Religious Institution
**Leader:** The Pontiff of the Holy City of Arteria.
**Asset:** Spiritual authority, a history of fighting "Hollows," and their own "divine" knights who use pre-revolution, faith-based magic. They believe the Orbal Revolution is a form of blasphemy.
**Rivals:** The Zeiss Central Factory, The Chromatic Cantors.

### Zeiss Central Factory (ZCF)
**Type:** Industrial and Research Conglomerate
**Leader:** Professor Russell, one of Epstein's original disciples.
**Asset:** The world's leading producer of Orbal technology. They are constantly pushing the boundaries of science, often without regard for the consequences.
**Rivals:** The Septian Church.

### Ouroboros
**Type:** Secret Society of Metaphysical Anarchists
**Leader:** The Grandmaster, a being of unknown origin and immense power.
**Asset:** Enforcers who wield lost and forbidden forms of magic, and a plan that operates on a cosmic scale. They see the "fading" not as a crisis, but as an "interesting development" to be manipulated for their own inscrutable goals.
**Rivals:** Everyone.

### The Jaeger Corps
**Type:** Mercenary Guilds
**Leader:** Varies by corps. The most famous are the "Red Constellation" and "Zephyr."
**Asset:** Highly-trained, elite soldiers for hire, equipped with the latest military hardware. They fight for the highest bidder, regardless of morality.
**Rivals:** The Bracer Guild (professional rivals).

### The Artists' Collective of Ruan
**Type:** Traditionalist Art Guild
**Leader:** The Master Painter, a woman who remembers the "true colors" of the world.
**Asset:** A secret cache of pre-revolution pigments and instruments. They are trying to preserve the "memory of beauty" before it fades completely.
**Rivals:** The Chromatic Cantors (whom they see as dangerous radicals).

### The Hollow-Walkers
**Type:** Apathetic Survivors of the Hollow Zones
**Leader:** None. They are barely sentient.
**Asset:** They are immune to emotional manipulation and fear. They wander out of the Hollow Zones, spreading their "apathy" like a plague.
**Rivals:** None. They have no desires or goals.

**Father Kevin Graham** (Human Cleric) - A traveling priest of the Septian Church. Friendly, lazy, and loves to joke around. **Voice:** A laid-back, friendly, and slightly sarcastic drawl. **Goal:** To "observe" the strange phenomena in Liberl for the Church. **Secret:** He is not a priest, but a "Gralsritter," one of the Church's elite, secret knights, tasked with hunting heretics and recovering dangerous artifacts.

A master swordsman, honorable and deadly. **Voice:** A calm, deep, and sad voice. **Goal:** To fulfill the Grandmaster's plan, which he believes will ultimately save humanity from itself. **Secret:** He was once a hero of a small village that was destroyed by political corruption, leading to his nihilistic worldview.
**Tita Russell** (Gnome Artificer) - Professor Russell's young, genius granddaughter. **Voice:** A shy, quiet, and sweet voice. **Goal:** To build machines that help people. **Secret:** She has unknowingly created a fully sentient AI in her favorite Orbal-powered Gatling gun.
**Zin Vathek** (Human Monk) - A senior Bracer from the Calvard Republic. A giant of a man, jovial and loves a good drink. **Voice:** A deep, booming, and hearty laugh. **Goal:** To eat, drink, and be merry, and to punch anyone who tries to stop him. **Secret:** He is a master of a secret martial art that allows him to directly manipulate his own emotional energy, making him immune to the "fading."
**Agate Crosner** (Human Fighter) - A young, angry, and abrasive senior Bracer. **Voice:** A harsh, gravelly, and impatient bark. **Goal:** To become strong enough to protect everyone he cares about. **Secret:** His anger is a mask for his grief over his younger sister, who was killed by monsters.
**Olivier Lenheim** (Human Bard) - A flamboyant, eccentric, and ridiculously charming traveling musician. **Voice:** A theatrical, flowery, and over-the-top tenor. **Goal:** To find "true beauty" and to woo every pretty girl he meets. **Secret:** He is actually Olivert Reise Arnor, the crown prince of the Erebonian Empire, traveling incognito.
**The Grandmaster** (Unknown) - The unseen leader of Ouroboros. **Voice:** A telepathic voice that is a perfect copy of your own, but it is calm, confident, and knows all your secrets. **Goal:** To observe and guide humanity's "evolution" by forcing it through world-ending crises. **Secret:** Its identity is the campaign's greatest mystery.
**The Master Painter** (Elf Expert) - The leader of the Artists' Collective. **Voice:** A quiet, sad, and gentle voice. **Goal:** To preserve the memory of the "true colors" of the world. **Secret:** She has created a single, perfect painting that contains the last "true" blue in the world, and she would die to protect it.
**A Hollow-Walker** (Human Commoner) - A survivor from a Hollow Zone. **Voice:** A flat, empty, and emotionless monotone. **Goal:** None. **Secret:** None. It is a blank slate.
**General Morgan** (Human Fighter) - The stern, by-the-book leader of the Royal Army. **Voice:** A gruff, military bark. **Goal:** To protect Liberl from all foreign threats, including the Erebonian Empire and, if necessary, the Bracer Guild. **Secret:** He secretly resents Cassius Bright for being the "hero" of the war, while he was the one handling the logistics.
**Mayor Dalmore** (Human Rogue) - The corrupt, greedy mayor of a port town. **Voice:** An oily, sycophantic, and untrustworthy voice. **Goal:** To make as much money as possible. **Secret:** He is on the payroll of the Erebonian Intelligence Division.
**Scherazard Harvey** (Human Swashbuckler) - A senior Bracer known as the "Silver Streak." **Voice:** A sultry, teasing, and confident alto. **Goal:** To live a life of freedom and excitement. **Secret:** She is deeply in debt to a casino in the Calvard Republic.
**The Red Constellation Jaeger** (Human Fighter) - A member of the infamous Red Constellation mercenary corps. **Voice:** A cold, professional, and bored voice. **Goal:** To complete his contract. **Secret:** He finds his job utterly boring and secretly wishes he could be a baker.
**The Blue-Haired Boy** (Unknown) - A mysterious, blue-haired boy who appears and disappears at will, often giving cryptic advice. **Voice:** A quiet, child-like, and sad voice. **Goal:** Unknown. **Secret:** He is a personification of the planet's fading soul, a "ghost" of its former self.

## [TAG: LOCATIONS_MAIN]
### The Kingdom of Liberl
A small, prosperous, and fiercely independent nation, known for its beauty and its advanced technology.
*   **Rolent, the Vibrant Town:** A small, peaceful town surrounded by bright green fields and vibrant flower gardens. One of the last places where the colors feel "real." **Smell:** Flowers, fresh-cut grass, and baking bread. **Sound:** The chirping of birds, the laughter of children, the gentle hum of the local Orbal generator.
*   **Bose, the Commercial Hub:** A bustling market town, known for its large, chaotic market and its proximity to the Erebonian border. **Smell:** A thousand different spices, roasting meat, and the scent of money. **Sound:** The shouts of merchants, the haggling of customers, the rumble of cargo airships.
*   **Ruan, the Seaside City:** A beautiful port city, famous for its artists, its seafood, and its annual "Carnival of Color." **Smell:** Salt air, fresh fish, and turpentine. **Sound:** The cry of gulls, the lapping of waves, the distant sound of an opera singer practicing.
*   **Zeiss, the Factory City:** The industrial heart of Liberl, home to the Zeiss Central Factory. **Smell:** Ozone, hot metal, and chemical fumes. **Sound:** The constant, deafening hum and clang of the factories.
*   **Grancel, the Royal Capital:** The capital city, a place of white stone, grand architecture, and political intrigue. **Smell:** Clean stone, old money, and the faint scent of conspiracy. **Sound:** The marching of the Royal Guard, the polite chatter of nobles, the chiming of the Grand Cathedral's bells.

### The Erebonian Empire
A massive, militaristic, and expansionist empire to the north of Liberl.
*   **Heimdallr, the Imperial Capital:** A sprawling, gothic metropolis of black iron and red banners. **Smell:** Coal smoke, industry, and the scent of military power. **Sound:** The marching of armies, the roar of factories, the speeches of the Iron Chancellor.
*   **The Black Workshop:** A secret, high-tech weapons development facility, where the Empire tries to replicate and surpass Liberl's Orbal tech. **Smell:** Ozone, forbidden magic, and burning ambition. **Sound:** The hum of strange energies, the hiss of experimental weapons.
*   **The Military Academy of Thors:** An elite academy where the Empire's future leaders are trained. **Smell:** Polished leather, sweat, and youthful arrogance. **Sound:** The clash of swords, the bark of drill sergeants.

### The Hollow Zones
Areas where the Orbal field has collapsed, leaving behind grey, silent, and emotionless voids.
*   **The Grey Farmstead:** A farm where the grass, the trees, and the animals are all a uniform, lifeless grey. **Smell:** The absence of smell. **Sound:** The absence of sound.
*   **The Silent Village:** A village whose inhabitants have all become Hollow-Walkers. They go about their daily routines in perfect, silent synchronicity. **Smell:** Dust. **Sound:** The soft, unsettling shuffle of feet.
*   **The Dead Zone:** The center of a Hollow Zone, a place where reality itself is thin. **Smell:** Nothing. **Sound:** A silence so profound it feels like a pressure on your ears.

### The Holy City of Arteria
The center of the Septian Church, a city built on a floating island in the sky.
*   **The Grand Cathedral:** The central cathedral of the Church, a masterpiece of pre-revolution architecture. **Smell:** Incense, old stone, and faith. **Sound:** A constant, beautiful, choral chant.
*   **The Gralsritter's Keep:** The secret fortress of the Church's holy knights. **Smell:** Polished steel, holy water, and dedication. **Sound:** The clash of practice swords, the murmur of prayers.

### The Secret Realm of Ouroboros
Places that exist outside of normal space and time, the hidden bases of the secret society.
*   **The Phantasma:** A "ghost" airship, the mobile headquarters of Ouroboros. It can travel between dimensions. **Smell:** Ozone, and a faint, alien scent you can't identify. **Sound:** A low, almost sub-sonic hum.
*   **The Liber Ark:** A massive, ancient space station, the ultimate goal of the Grandmaster's plan. **Smell:** Sterile, recycled air and ancient technology. **Sound:** The hum of energies beyond mortal comprehension.

## [TAG: QUEST_MAIN]
1.  **A Bracer's Life for Me:** The players take their final Bracer exam, a simple mission to exterminate some monsters outside Rolent.
2.  **The Disappearing Mayor:** The mayor of Rolent disappears. The players investigate, uncovering a simple kidnapping plot that leads them to the local branch of the Erebonian intelligence service.
3.  **The Black Market of Bose:** The trail leads the players to the city of Bose, where they must infiltrate the black market to find a stolen Orbal device.
4.  **The Carnival of Ruan:** The players travel to Ruan to investigate a series of strange "accidents" plaguing the annual Carnival. They uncover a plot by the Chromatic Cantors to use the carnival as a massive "emotional ritual."
5.  **The Factory's Secret:** The trail of the stolen device leads to Zeiss, where the players must sneak into the Central Factory. They discover the device is part of a secret, dangerous experiment by Professor Russell.
6.  **The Hollow Zone:** The experiment goes wrong, creating the first, temporary Hollow Zone. The players must rescue the scientists and shut down the device before the zone becomes permanent.
7.  **The Coup in Grancel:** The players are summoned to the capital, where the Intelligence Division of the Royal Army, led by a radical colonel, is attempting a coup, believing the Queen is too soft on Erebonia.
8.  **The Prince and the Pauper:** The players are aided by a flamboyant musician named Olivier, who helps them navigate the political intrigue of the capital.
9.  **The Attack on the Guild:** Ouroboros makes its first move, attacking the Bracer Guild directly to retrieve an ancient artifact they were guarding.
10. **The Truth of the Fading:** Cassius Bright reveals the truth of the world's "fading" and the true nature of Orbal energy.
11. **The Symphony of Color:** The Maestro, believing the crisis is his moment, prepares to perform his "Symphony of Colors" in Grancel, an act that will either save the world or destroy it.
12. **The Iron Chancellor's Gambit:** The Erebonian Empire, under the guise of "helping," invades Liberl, seeking to seize control of the Orbal network during the chaos.
13. **The Gralsritter's Hunt:** Father Kevin Graham reveals his true identity and enlists the players to help him stop both the Maestro and Ouroboros.
14. **The Ancient Dragon:** The players must awaken an ancient, pre-revolution dragon, one of the last beings with a true connection to the planet's soul, to ask for its help.
15. **The Heart of the Matter:** The dragon reveals that the only way to save the world is to travel to the "heart" of the Orbal network, a place deep beneath Zeiss.
16. **The Grandmaster's Game:** The players confront the leaders of Ouroboros in the heart of the network, who reveal that this entire crisis was part of their plan to "accelerate" humanity's evolution.
17. **A New Resonance:** The players must make a choice: destroy the Orbal network and plunge the world back into a dark age, "re-tune" it to a new, sustainable frequency, or seize its power for themselves.

## [TAG: RULES_CUSTOM]
### Physics/Magic: The Law of Resonance
Orbal energy is emotional energy. This has tangible effects on all magic and technology.
*   **Emotional Casting:** When casting a spell, a player can choose to tie it to a strong, personal emotion. If they can roleplay this effectively, they can add their Charisma modifier to the spell's damage or healing. However, if they do this, they also risk "emotional feedback," taking 1d6 psychic damage per level of the spell.
*   **Technological Apathy:** Orbal devices function less effectively in areas of low emotion. In a sad, apathetic town, an Orbal lamp might only produce dim light. In a Hollow Zone, it will not work at all. Conversely, in a place of high emotion (a festival, a battlefield), devices may become "overcharged," becoming more powerful but also more unstable.
*   **Art as Magic:** A truly great work of art (a song, a painting, a poem) can function as a magic spell. A Bard's song might literally be able to heal, or a painter's work might be able to create a wall of force. This is rare and difficult, requiring a very high performance or craft check.

### Subsystems: The Fading and Resonance Points
*   **The Fading:** This is a background mechanic that the DM tracks. As the campaign progresses and more Orbal energy is used, the world becomes more "faded." The DM should reflect this in their descriptions: food is less flavorful, colors are more muted, etc. This can have mechanical effects, such as imposing disadvantage on checks to perceive beauty or find inspiration.
*   **Resonance Points:** Players can gain Resonance Points (RP) by performing acts of great passion, creativity, or emotion. This can be anything from giving a rousing speech, to painting a beautiful picture, to declaring their love. These points can be spent to "boost" an action, adding a powerful emotional component to it. They can also be used to temporarily "re-ignite" a small area, pushing back the Fading and allowing Orbal tech to function at full capacity.

## [TAG: DM_GUIDE]
### Session-by-Session Breakdown (1-12)
*   **Session 1:** **Primary Beat:** The Bracer Exam. The players complete their final exam and become junior Bracers. Their first mission is a simple "find my lost cat" quest. **Combat:** The "cat" is a monster that has eaten the cat. **Social:** Dealing with the grateful, but strangely subdued, cat owner. **Loot:** Their official Bracer notebooks.
*   **Session 2:** **Primary Beat:** The Fading Town. The players are sent to a small town where the Orbal lamps are failing. **Combat:** Fighting strange, apathetic monsters that are drawn to the town's ennui. **Social:** Trying to cheer up a town that has forgotten how to feel. **Loot:** A broken Orbal lamp.
*   **Session 3:** **Primary Beat:** The Art of Rebellion. The players travel to Ruan, where the Chromatic Cantors are planning a "protest." **Combat:** A chaotic battle in the middle of a festival, trying to stop the Cantors without harming civilians. **Social:** A debate with the Maestro about art and responsibility. **Loot:** A painting that inspires minor feelings of rebellion.
*   **Session 4:** **Primary Beat:** The Factory of Secrets. The players trace a piece of the Cantors' tech to Zeiss. **Combat:** A stealth mission into the ZCF, avoiding Orbal security drones. **Social:** Bribing or tricking a factory worker for information. **Loot:** Professor Russell's secret research notes.
*   **Session 5:** **Primary Beat:** The Hollow Zone. The players investigate a report of a "silent fog" and discover the first Hollow Zone. **Combat:** There is no combat. The monsters here are passive. The challenge is surviving the psychic damage from the profound emptiness. **Social:** Rescuing a catatonic explorer from the zone. **Loot:** A perfectly smooth, grey stone from the zone's center.
*   **Session 6:** **Primary Beat:** The Empire's Shadow. The players uncover a plot by the Erebonian Intelligence Division to steal ZCF research. **Combat:** A chase scene, trying to stop an Erebonian spy from escaping on an airship. **Social:** A tense political meeting with the mayor of Zeiss. **Loot:** A coded Erebonian message.
*   **Session 7:** **Primary Beat:** The Church's Judgment. Father Kevin Graham arrives to "investigate" the Hollow Zone. **Combat:** A "test" against a Septian Church Knight. **Social:** A philosophical and religious debate with Kevin. **Loot:** A holy symbol of the Septian Church.
*   **Session 8:** **Primary Beat:** The Mercenary's Contract. A Jaeger corps is hired by an unknown party to "secure" the Hollow Zone. **Combat:** A tactical battle against the professional and well-equipped Jaegers. **Social:** An attempt to bribe or out-bid the Jaegers' employer. **Loot:** A high-quality piece of military hardware.
*   **Session 9:** **Primary Beat:** The Secret Society. Ouroboros makes their move, revealing they were the Jaegers' employer. **Combat:** A battle against a powerful Ouroboros Enforcer like Loewe. **Social:** A cryptic conversation with the Enforcer, who seems to know everything about them. **Loot:** A strange, snake-themed artifact.
*   **Session 10:** **Primary Beat:** The Legend's Wisdom. Cassius Bright, seeing the gravity of the situation, sends the party to find a legendary, pre-revolution hero. **Combat:** A journey through a "vibrant," dangerous, and emotionally-charged part of the world. **Social:** Convincing the old hero to share their knowledge. **Loot:** A piece of ancient, emotion-powered wisdom.
*   **Session 11:** **Primary Beat:** The Truth Beneath the World. The players learn the location of the central Orbal network hub. **Combat:** A dungeon-crawl through an ancient, pre-revolution ruin, filled with emotion-based traps. **Social:** A final, heartfelt conversation with their friends and family before the final mission. **Loot:** A key to the central hub.
*   **Session 12:** **Primary Beat:** The Heart of the World. The party enters the hub to find Ouroboros, the Erebonian Empire, and the Chromatic Cantors all there, fighting for control. **Combat:** A massive, multi-factional final battle. **Social:** The party must make their final choice and "sell" it to the other factions, or fight them all. **Loot:** The fate of the world's soul.
