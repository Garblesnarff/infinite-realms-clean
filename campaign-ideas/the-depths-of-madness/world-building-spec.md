# The Depths of Madness - World Building Specification

## The Deep Ones: Complete Biology

### Physical Characteristics
- Bipedal fish-frog hybrids, 6-8 feet tall
- Gray-green scaly skin, webbed appendages
- Bulging eyes (360-degree vision underwater)
- Gills on neck, lungs for land
- Immortal (do not age after maturity)
- Amphibious, prefer deep ocean cold

### Life Cycle
1. Human cultist mates with Deep One
2. Hybrid born looking mostly human
3. Puberty: First changes (webbing, scales)
4. Age 30-40: The Change accelerates
5. Age 50+: Fully transformed into Deep One
6. Leave land, join deep city
7. Immortality achieved, humanity lost

### Society
- Monarchy ruled by ancient Deep Ones
- Dagon worshiped as god-king
- Breeding program with surface to expand
- Cities in deepest trenches worldwide
- Patient—plans span centuries
- Y'ha-nthlei is capital

## Innsmouth: Town of Secrets

### History
- Founded 1643 by Obed Marsh
- 1846: Captain Obed makes pact with Deep Ones (gold for hybrid children)
- 1846-1900: Prosperity but isolation, interbreeding begins
- 1900-Present: Entire population descended from hybrids
- Everyone transforms eventually
- Outsiders forbidden, truth hidden

### Demographics
- Population: 1,200 (all hybrid bloodline)
- 60% aware of their nature
- 30% in denial
- 10% actively resist (secretly)
- No children (all leave when they transform)

### Economy
- Fishing (catches sold to unknowing markets)
- Gold from Deep Ones (ancient treasure)
- Complete isolation from outside economy
- Self-sufficient by necessity

## The Esoteric Order of Dagon

### Public Face
- "Legitimate" religion in Innsmouth
- Most residents are members
- Services held at Esoteric Order temple
- Missionary work (recruitment to town)

### Secret Truth
- Death cult worshiping Dagon
- Orchestrating awakening ritual
- Sacrifices to Deep Ones
- Converting surface dwellers
- Protecting the transformation process

### Leadership Hierarchy
1. Father Marsh (High Priest)
2. The Elders (fully transformed, ancient)
3. Devoted (awaiting transformation)
4. Initiates (new converts)
5. The Unknowing (townfolk in denial)

## Y'ha-nthlei: The Deep One City

### Architecture
- Non-euclidean geometry (angles wrong)
- Carved from undersea mountains
- Bioluminescent lighting (colors painful to human eyes)
- Breathable air pockets in temples
- Population: 50,000 Deep Ones

### Districts
- **The Spawning Grottos**: Hybrid nurseries
- **Temple of Dagon**: Central worship, ritual site
- **The Dreaming Spires**: Where Dagon's dreams manifest
- **Treasure Vaults**: Millennia of collected wealth
- **The Archives**: Forbidden knowledge library
- **The Transformation Pools**: Final change occurs here

### Navigation Challenges
- Normal directions don't work
- Sanity checks to comprehend layout
- Geometries that loop impossibly
- Spaces larger inside than outside
- Some areas exist in multiple realities

## Dagon: The Sleeping God

### Nature
- Great Old One (cosmic entity)
- Beyond good and evil (amoral)
- Dreams influence reality
- Imprisoned/sleeping in deepest trench
- Size: Miles across, shapeshifting mass

### Powers
- Reality warping in vicinity
- Drive mortals mad with presence
- Command all ocean life
- Merge material plane with Far Realm
- Immortal, unkillable (only sealable)

### The Seals
1. **First Seal**: Physical barrier (easiest to break)
2. **Second Seal**: Magical ward (medium difficulty)
3. **Third Seal**: Reality anchor (hardest, requires sacrifice)

### Awakening Consequences
- Ocean becomes nightmare realm
- All water connected to Far Realm
- Mass transformation of coastal populations
- Weather apocalypse (permanent hurricanes)
- Deep Ones ascendant
- Humanity's extinction or enslavement

## Madness System Detailed

### Types of Madness

**Phobias** (Low sanity)
- Aquaphobia (fear of water)
- Monophobia (fear of being alone)
- Paranoia (everyone is hybrid)

**Manias** (Mid sanity)
- Obsession with forbidden texts
- Compulsion to swim/drown
- Speaking in tongues (Deep One language)

**Delusions** (Severe)
- Believe you're already transformed
- Hear Dagon's voice constantly
- See Deep Ones everywhere
- Time dilation (lose track of reality)

**Permanent Effects** (0 sanity)
- Catatonic
- Willingly join cultists
- Suicide by drowning
- Violent psychosis

### Recovery
- Long rest in safe place: Restore 1d6 sanity
- Greater Restoration spell: Restore 2d10 sanity
- Therapy sessions: Restore 1d4 per week
- Cannot recover sanity lost to certain revelations
- Maximum sanity decreases permanently for cosmic truths learned

## Forbidden Knowledge Tomes

**The Ponape Scripture**
- Origin of Deep Ones
- Reading: 1d10 sanity loss
- Grants: Knowledge of Dagon's location

**The R'lyeh Text**
- Non-euclidean mathematics
- Reading: 2d10 sanity loss
- Grants: Navigate impossible spaces

**The Pnakotic Manuscripts**
- History of Great Old Ones
- Reading: 3d10 sanity loss
- Grants: Weakness of Dagon (theoretical)

**Dagon's Dreams** (Cultist text)
- Direct psychic link to sleeping god
- Reading: 5d10 sanity loss
- Grants: Cosmic truth (permanently reduces max sanity)

## The Truth of Origins

### Revelation 1: Deep Ones are Ancient
Humanity evolved recently; Deep Ones predate us by millions of years

### Revelation 2: We Were Cattle
Ancient Deep Ones experimented on early humans, shaped our evolution

### Revelation 3: Inevitable Transformation
All coastal human populations carry dormant hybrid genes

### Revelation 4: Dagon's Purpose
Not conqueror—Dagon is seal keeping Far Realm separate, his awakening is breach

### Revelation 5: The Real Enemy
Something from Far Realm pushing Dagon awake to invade material plane

### Final Truth: No Victory
Stopping ritual delays inevitability; stars will align again; cycle continues forever

## Corruption Track (Physical Transformation)

**Stage 1: Minor (Corruption 1-25)**
- Webbing between fingers/toes
- Skin slightly gray-green
- Enhanced swimming (+10 ft speed)
- Can hold breath 3x longer

**Stage 2: Notable (Corruption 26-50)**
- Visible scales on arms/legs
- Eyes bulge slightly, improved darkvision
- Gills develop (can breathe water)
- Voice becomes raspy

**Stage 3: Severe (Corruption 51-75)**
- Face elongating, frog-like features
- Full body scales
- Cannot digest human food (needs raw fish)
- Prefer cold water environments

**Stage 4: Complete (Corruption 76-100)**
- Full Deep One transformation
- Lose character to NPC
- Immortality achieved
- Humanity lost forever

### Gaining Corruption
- Prolonged exposure to Deep One presence: +1d4/day
- Swimming in corrupted water: +1d6/hour
- Consuming Deep One food: +2d6
- Reading forbidden texts: +1d10
- Witnessing rituals: +2d10
- Willing acceptance: +20

## Campaign Endings Detailed

### Ending 1: "The Seal Holds" (Victory)
- Third seal reinforced with sacrifice
- Dagon remains dormant
- Innsmouth destroyed, cultists killed
- Party survives but changed
- Knowledge haunts them forever
- Prevents apocalypse for another century

### Ending 2: "The Stars Were Right" (Defeat)
- Ritual completes
- Dagon awakens
- Reality merges with Far Realm
- Ocean becomes nightmare
- Humanity's age ends
- Party witnesses apocalypse

### Ending 3: "We Are Become Deep Ones" (Dark)
- Party accepts transformation
- Join Deep One society
- Gain immortality, lose humanity
- Become NPCs or villains
- Horror ending where heroes fall

### Ending 4: "The Greater Evil" (Revelation)
- Discover Far Realm invasion plan
- Temporarily ally with Deep Ones
- Stop true enemy
- Dagon remains sealed
- Uneasy truce with Deep Ones
- Party knows terrible truth

This campaign embraces cosmic horror's inevitability and humanity's insignificance while giving players agency in how they face the abyss.
