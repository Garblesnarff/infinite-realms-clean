# Whalefall Conspiracy - World-Building Specification

## The Isles Geography
**Gristol**: Main island, contains Dunwall capital. Industrial powerhouse.
**Morley**: Northern territory, conquered by Gristol. Resentful population.
**Tyvia**: Frozen wastes, prison colonies, harsh survival.
**Serkonos**: Southern islands, wine country, more relaxed culture.

## Whale Oil Economy
**Extraction**: Massive leviathans hunted in deep ocean. One whale = months of city power.
**Processing**: Refineries render blubber into fuel. Extremely dangerous work.
**Applications**: Lighting, heating, arc pylons, walls of light, tallboy pistons—everything runs on whale oil.
**Crisis**: Whales becoming extinct. 20-year timeline until collapse. Denied by industrialists.

## The Outsider's Lore
**Origin**: 4,000 years ago, child named "The Nameless" sacrificed by cult. Became void god.
**Nature**: Neither good nor evil. Fascinated by human choice. Grants marks to "interesting" people.
**Marked Individuals**: Throughout history—Granny Rags, Daud, party members, others. Tests mortals.
**The Void**: Realm between reality and nothing. Black ocean, floating debris, impossible physics.
**Mark's Cost**: Power attracts void entities. Eventual madness or corruption likely.

## The Abbey of the Everyman
**Religion**: Strictures (seven religious laws) governing morality. Essentially Christianity analog.
**Organization**: High Overseer leads from Holger Square. Overseers police heresy.
**Corruption**: Campbell visits brothels, extorts citizens, tortures "heretics."
**Music Boxes**: Technology disrupting supernatural powers. Abbey's weapon against Outsider.
**Internal Conflict**: True believers vs. power-hungry officials.

## Plague Details
**Rat Plague**: Engineered by Sokolov on Burrows' orders. Spread through rats.
**Symptoms**: Fever, delirium, bleeding, transformation into "weeper" (zombie-like end stage).
**River Krusts**: Parasitic crustaceans feeding on infected corpses.
**Quarantine Walls**: Infected districts sealed. Death squads execute plague victims.
**Elixir**: Sokolov's cure, distributed only to wealthy and essential workers. Poor left to die.
**Political Tool**: Burrows uses emergency to justify authoritarian measures.

## Technology Level
**Industrial Revolution**: Steam power, early electricity (arc pylons), mechanical sophistication.
**No Gunpowder**: Pistols use spring mechanisms or compressed air. Quiet but deadly.
**Whale Oil Everything**: Fuel source for all advanced tech. Monopoly by powerful families.
**Natural Philosophy**: Science called natural philosophy. Piero and Sokolov are leading minds.

## Social Classes
**Aristocracy**: Own everything, live in luxury. Pendletons, Boyles, etc. Decadent and corrupt.
**Military/Government**: Burrows, Havelock, Campbell. Power through position.
**Merchants**: Rising class but limited by aristocratic control.
**Working Class**: Whiskey distillery workers, dockworkers, servants. Exploited brutally.
**Outcasts**: Gang members, plague survivors, marked by Outsider. Society's rejects.

## Factions Extended
**Loyalist Conspiracy**: Havelock, Martin (High Overseer after Campbell), Pendleton (Lord). Plan coup then betray party.
**Bottle Street Gang**: Slackjaw's territory. Protection racket and whiskey production.
**Hatters**: Rival gang. More violent, less organized.
**City Watch**: Increasingly brutal under Burrows. Gold masks, halberds, combat training.
**Assassins Guild**: Daud's Whalers (extended lore). Outsider-marked killers for hire.

## Key Locations Extended
**Coldridge Prison**: Torture chambers, interrogation rooms, gallows, yard where Empress cremated.
**Dunwall Whiskey Distillery**: Slackjaw's base. Industrial equipment repurposed for gang war.
**Flooded District Refinery**: Abandoned whale oil processing. Weepers everywhere. Daud's base (if expanded).
**Office of the High Overseer**: Chapel, torture chambers, Campbell's private vice chamber, confessional recordings.
**Golden Cat**: Art Gallery entrance, various themed rooms, basement prison, roof escape route.
**Boyle Estate**: Party hall, gardens, multiple identical targets requiring identification.
**Dunwall Tower**: Throne room, broadcast station (Burrows' propaganda), courtyards, moat, security office.
**Kaldwin's Bridge**: Massive drawbridge, assassination site, memorial to Empress.
**Rudshore Waterfront**: Shipping district, plague overflow, weepers shambling.

## Chaos System Details
**Low Chaos Effects**: Fewer rats, friendly NPCs, hopeful dialogue, Emily optimistic, Samuel loyal, good ending.
**Medium Chaos**: Increased security, some plague spread, neutral responses, mixed ending.
**High Chaos**: Rat swarms, weeper epidemic, dark dialogue, Emily traumatized, Samuel betrays, dark ending.
**Mechanical Impact**: More enemies spawn in high chaos. Detection difficulty increases. NPC survival decreases.

## Supernatural Powers Extended
**Blink I-II**: Short teleport → Long range with time slowdown.
**Dark Vision I-II**: See living things → See items and sound visualization.
**Possession I-II**: Possess rats and fish → Possess humans temporarily.
**Bend Time I-II**: Slow time → Stop time completely.
**Windblast I-II**: Push enemies → Lethal force explosion.
**Devouring Swarm I-II**: Summon rats → Aggressive attacking swarm.
**Shadow Kill I-II**: Bodies turn to ash when killed → Works on aware enemies too.
**Agility**: Faster movement, higher jumps, double jump.
**Vitality**: More health.
**Blood Thirsty**: Adrenaline rush on kills (promotes high chaos).

## Non-Lethal Alternatives
Every target has non-lethal solution:
**Campbell**: Brand as heretic, exile from Abbey.
**Pendleton Brothers**: Deliver to enemies who enslave them in mines.
**Lady Boyle**: Deliver to stalker Brisby (morally questionable).
**Burrows**: Expose confession, arrested and imprisoned.
**Havelock**: Mercy after combat, imprisoned or exiled.

## Bone Charm Effects
Passive abilities from carved whale bones:
- Albinos (rats don't attack)
- Carrion Killer (eating food heals more)
- Clockwork Malfunction (machines take longer to detect)
- Healthy Appetite I-II (food gives mana)
- Plague Resistant (plague doesn't damage)
- Robust (potions more effective)
- Strong Arms (choke out faster)
- Undertaker (carrying bodies costs no speed)

## The Heart Artifact
Made from Empress Jessamine's literal heart. Functions:
- Points to runes and bone charms
- Reveals NPC secrets when targeted
- Provides location lore
- Becomes disturbing as used frequently
- Represents violation—beloved Empress weaponized

## Endings Variations
**Best Ending**: Low chaos, Emily happy, plague cured, Dunwall golden age, party honored heroes, Samuel proud.
**Good Ending**: Low-medium chaos, Emily wise, recovery underway, some scars, party respected.
**Medium Ending**: Medium chaos, Emily hardened, city damaged, difficult recovery, party seen as necessary evil.
**Dark Ending**: High chaos, Emily cynical, plague rampant, brutal rule, party feared tyrants.
**Worst Ending**: High chaos, Emily dead or fled, Dunwall collapse, plague wasteland, party hunted or ruling ashes.

## Campaign Customization
**Party as Royal Protectors**: All guards/assassins working for Empress.
**Party as Conspirators**: Various faction members coming together.
**Party as Outsiders**: Foreigners investigating Dunwall who get caught in coup.
**Solo vs. Group**: Adapt for single character (Corvo equivalent) or team dynamics.
**Mark Distribution**: All party marked, some marked, or none marked with gadgets only.
