# Wings of the Crows

*A Sports Tournament / Coming-of-Age campaign — Medium Campaign (15-20 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Wings of the Crows
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Sports Tournament / Coming-of-Age / Teamwork
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 15-20 sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **1** → Finish **10-12**
**Tone (keywords):** underdog, soaring, teamwork, determination, nationals, fly high, evolution
**Core Premise (2-4 sentences):**
The party joins the fallen Karasuno Guild—once legendary as the "Flightless Crows" but now dismissed as has-beens in the competitive world of Skyblade tournaments. Skyblade is a magical aerial sport where teams of six launch enchanted spheres over mystical nets using combination attacks and coordinated flight magic. Through grueling training, innovative tactics, and learning to trust each other completely, they must climb from practice matches to regional qualifiers, and finally to the National Championships where the greatest guilds in the realm compete for glory.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 15-20 sessions)

A three-act journey from fallen reputation to soaring champions.

*   **Act I — Rebuilding the Nest (Sessions 1-7):** The party assembles as unlikely recruits to revive Karasuno Guild's Skyblade team. They face internal conflicts as clashing personalities struggle to synchronize. Through practice matches against local guilds and their first tournament, they discover that true strength comes from making each other better. Their ace spiker and brilliant setter learn to trust each other.
*   **Act II — The Flight to Nationals (Sessions 8-13):** Having proven themselves locally, Karasuno enters the Regional Championships facing powerhouse guilds with legendary reputations. They face Aoba Johsai (led by a genius setter-king), Shiratorizawa (a school of overwhelming individual talent), and learn new techniques like synchronized quick attacks. Each match pushes them to evolve their teamwork and discover new combination abilities.
*   **Act III — Where Eagles Dare (Sessions 14-20):** At Nationals, Karasuno faces the top guilds in the realm—teams that have trained together for years, teams with innovative strategies never before seen. They must overcome injuries, self-doubt, and opponents who seem insurmountable. In the final match, they face Inarizaki or Itachiyama, guilds with perfect synchronization. Victory requires them to transcend individual limits and truly fly as one.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Crow's Return"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Team Assembly
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Characters arrive at declining Karasuno Guild seeking the Skyblade team
    *   They meet the enthusiastic but inexperienced coach who was once a great player
    *   Reveal of each character's position and special skills
    *   First practice reveals they can't coordinate at all
**Escalation:** Challenge from rival guild Aoba Johsai for a practice match in three days

### Sessions 2-4 — "Learning to Fly Together"
*   **Focus:** Training / Team Building
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Setter and spiker clash over timing and trust issues
    *   Introduction of quick-attack synchronization requiring perfect magical timing
    *   Practice with the libero who can receive any attack
    *   First practice match ends in defeat but shows potential
**Escalation:** Invitation to Interguild Tournament—their first real test against multiple schools

### Sessions 5-7 — "Interguild Tournament"
*   **Focus:** First Competition / Finding Rhythm
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Multiple matches showcase different guild strategies
    *   Party develops their first synchronized combination attack
    *   Ace spiker overcomes fear of failing the team
    *   Climactic match against Date Tech's "Iron Wall" defensive guild
**Escalation:** They qualify for Regionals, but their ace is scouted by Shiratorizawa's legendary coach

### Sessions 8-10 — "The King of the Court"
*   **Focus:** Rivalry / Growth Through Opposition
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Regional Championships begin with Karasuno facing Aoba Johsai
    *   Enemy setter Oikawa is a tactical genius who knows their every move
    *   Party must innovate new attacks mid-match to counter his predictions
    *   The setter proves he's no longer a tyrant but a true teammate
**Escalation:** They win, but next is Shiratorizawa—an undefeated guild with overwhelming power

### Sessions 11-13 — "The Concept of Talent"
*   **Focus:** Peak Challenge / Belief in Teamwork
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Shiratorizawa's ace Ushijima seems unstoppable with his power attacks
    *   Karasuno's synchronized quick attacks are initially ineffective
    *   They develop new variations—using decoys, feints, and wild adaptations
    *   Five-set epic battle where they must win by two points
**Escalation:** They achieve the impossible and earn their place at Nationals

### Sessions 14-16 — "The National Stage"
*   **Focus:** Elite Competition / New Techniques
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Nationals feature guilds with perfect synchronization and unique strategies
    *   Party faces teams with twin spikers, perfect receives, and innovative serves
    *   They scout opponents and learn the meta-game of national competition
    *   Character development as pressure reveals past traumas and doubts
**Escalation:** They advance to face Inarizaki, a guild with the most aggressive twin attackers

### Sessions 17-19 — "The Greatest Challengers"
*   **Focus:** Ultimate Teamwork / Transcendence
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Battle against Inarizaki's Miya twins who can perform impossible synchronized attacks
    *   Party members must each make crucial plays trusting teammates blindly
    *   Development of "Minus Tempo" attack—attacking before the set is complete
    *   Victory through perfect trust rather than individual skill
**Escalation:** Final match against the undefeated favorites, Itachiyama or Fukurodani

### Session 20 — "Fly High"
*   **Focus:** Climax / The Ultimate Match
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Championship finals where both teams are perfectly matched
    *   Party pushes beyond their limits, playing injured and exhausted
    *   The final point requires all six members executing their roles flawlessly
    *   Win or lose, they've proven Karasuno can soar again
**Resolution:** Epilogue shows them inspiring a new generation of recruits to Karasuno, having restored the guild's legendary status

## Major NPCs

1.  **Coach Ukai** — *The Experienced Mentor*
    *   **Role:** Former Karasuno Skyblade champion who retired early, now coaching to redeem guild reputation
    *   **Personality:** Tough love, strategic mind, pushes players to their limits, believes in their potential
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Retired due to injury right before Nationals; sees party as his second chance

2.  **Tōru Oikawa** — *The Tactical Genius*
    *   **Role:** Setter for rival guild Aoba Johsai, analytical genius who can predict opponents
    *   **Personality:** Charming but petty, obsessed with defeating Karasuno's setter, studies relentlessly
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Fears he lacks natural talent compared to prodigies, overworks to compensate

3.  **Wakatoshi Ushijima** — *The Overwhelming Ace*
    *   **Role:** Captain of Shiratorizawa, considers teamwork unnecessary when you have absolute power
    *   **Personality:** Blunt, honest, supremely confident, respects strength above all
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Cannot understand why "weaker" players don't just get stronger; struggles to inspire teammates

4.  **Kiyoko Shimizu** — *The Manager*
    *   **Role:** Guild manager who handles logistics, scouting, and keeping the team organized
    *   **Personality:** Calm, observant, sees potential others miss, provides crucial emotional support
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Former player who quit due to lack of confidence; lives vicariously through team

5.  **Kenma Kozume** — *The Strategic Setter*
    *   **Role:** Setter for Nekoma Guild, treats matches like puzzle games to be solved
    *   **Personality:** Introverted, analytical, energy-conserving, becomes fierce when engaged
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Lacks physical stamina; relies entirely on perfect positioning and prediction

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores themes of teamwork over individual talent, rising from fallen glory, and the beauty of coordinated effort. It asks: Can trust and synchronization overcome raw power? What does it mean to fly when you were once grounded? How do you inspire others when you were inspired by legends? The central conflict pits the party's belief in team evolution against guilds that rely on individual prodigies or rigid hierarchical systems.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Synchronization System**
*   Characters build Sync Points with teammates through successful cooperation
*   Higher Sync unlocks combination attacks and allows prediction of ally actions
*   Synchronization can be broken by miscommunication or conflicting strategies
*   Perfect Sync moments grant bonus actions when teammates act in complete harmony

### 2. **Position Roles**
*   Each party member has a specialized position: Setter (support/buff), Spiker (striker), Libero (defense/receive), Blocker (control), Server (opener)
*   Positions grant unique abilities but require team coordination to be effective
*   Formations change based on rotation, requiring adaptability
*   Cross-position training unlocks versatility but requires time investment

### 3. **Quick Attack System**
*   Special synchronized attacks that occur "before" enemies can react
*   Requires perfect timing between setter and spiker
*   Higher levels allow variations, feints, and multiple spiker options
*   Failed quick attacks leave party vulnerable to counters

### 4. **Match Flow & Momentum**
*   Matches use set/point system where teams must win by 2 points
*   Momentum shifts based on successful plays, affecting morale and abilities
*   Timeouts allow party to regroup, change strategy, and recover resources
*   Serving rotation forces different characters into leadership positions

### 5. **Training Arcs**
*   Between tournaments, party can choose focus areas: new techniques, conditioning, team bonding, studying opponents
*   Training montages grant skill increases and unlock new abilities
*   Overtraining risks injury and fatigue for matches
*   Joint training with rival guilds builds relationships and teaches techniques

### 6. **Evolution Through Observation**
*   Party can study opponent techniques during matches and tournaments
*   Successful observation checks allow learning and adapting enemy strategies
*   Some techniques require specific physical attributes or magic types
*   Adapting techniques mid-match grants surprise advantage

### 7. **Rally Counter**
*   Longer rallies increase stakes and potential rewards
*   Each successful receive/attack in a rally builds tension
*   Breaking opponent's long rally grants morale boost
*   Final point of crucial sets requires winning a dramatic extended rally
