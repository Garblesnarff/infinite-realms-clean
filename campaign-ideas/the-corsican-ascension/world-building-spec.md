# The Corsican Ascension - World-Building Specification

## Historical Period (1789-1821)
French Revolution → Revolutionary Wars → Napoleonic Empire → Restoration

## Factions
- **French Empire**: Revolutionary state → Empire, meritocratic, reformist
- **Coalition Powers**: Britain, Austria, Prussia, Russia—old monarchies vs. revolution
- **Satellite Kingdoms**: Naples, Holland, Westphalia, etc.—French puppet states
- **Neutrals**: Switzerland, Ottoman Empire, USA (Louisiana Purchase)

## Napoleonic Reforms
- **Code Napoléon** (1804): Modern civil law, still basis of many legal systems
- **Meritocracy**: Careers open to talent
- **Education**: Lycées, centralized system
- **Bank of France**: Financial stability
- **Concordat**: Peace with Catholic Church
- **Metric System**: Standardized measurements

## Major Campaigns
- **Italy** (1796-97): First brilliance
- **Egypt** (1798-99): Orientalist adventure
- **Austerlitz** (1805): Masterpiece
- **Jena** (1806): Prussian annihilation
- **Peninsula** (1808-14): Bleeding wound
- **Russia** (1812): Catastrophe
- **Germany** (1813): Desperate defense
- **France** (1814): Brilliant but doomed
- **Waterloo** (1815): Final roll

## The Marshals (18 Created 1804)
**Combat Excellence**: Murat, Ney, Lannes
**Administrative**: Davout, Soult
**Political**: Bernadotte (became Swedish King!)
**Controversial**: Augereau, Victor
Party members join this elite

## Legacy
- **Military**: Studied in every academy globally
- **Legal**: Napoleonic Code influenced legal systems worldwide
- **Political**: Nationalism awakened in conquered peoples
- **Cultural**: Romantic era sparked by Napoleonic wars
- **Mixed**: Liberation + Conquest, Reform + Tyranny

## Causality Chains
1. **Toulon**: If fails, no rise
2. **Egypt Return**: If stays/dies, no Brumaire
3. **Russia 1812**: If wins/avoids, empire survives
4. **Waterloo**: If wins, Europe reordered again
5. **Code Napoleon**: If not created, legal legacy lost
