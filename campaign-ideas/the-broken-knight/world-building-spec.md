**The Broken Knight**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise.
*   **Content:** The Nightwatch, the city's legendary protector, has been broken by Shatterbane in a calculated trap. As the hero recovers in secret, Shatterbane claims the Nightwatch's mantle, ruling through fear. The players must help the broken hero recover, train their successor Jean-Paul, and reclaim the hero's identity, while the city falls into darkness under a twisted version of its greatest champion.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Directive:** The Lore Generation Agent must create detailed entries for each of the following prompts. These entries will form the historical and cultural bedrock of the world.
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the history of the Nightwatch: how they became the city's protector and what their symbol means.
    *   Write about Shatterbane's origin and how he orchestrated the trap that broke the Nightwatch.
    *   Describe Jean-Paul's background with the Order of Azrael and their extreme methods.
    *   Explain the city's relationship with vigilante justice and masked heroes.
    *   Detail previous times the Nightwatch has been challenged or replaced.
    *   Write about the psychological and spiritual significance of the Nightwatch symbol to the city.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Directive:** The Faction Generation Agent must create a detailed profile for each faction listed below. Each profile must contain the specified fields.
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Nightwatch's Allies** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** Help the Nightwatch recover and reclaim their identity from Shatterbane.
        *   **Hierarchy:** The players, loyal allies, the recovering Nightwatch.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Operate in secret to avoid revealing the Nightwatch's survival.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Some question whether the Nightwatch can ever truly recover.
        *   **Assets:** The Sanctum, medical resources, loyal contacts, eventually Jean-Paul.
        *   **Relationships:** Opposed to Shatterbane; wary of Jean-Paul; supported by Commissioner Reynolds secretly.
    *   **Shatterbane's Empire** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** Control the city through fear while wearing the Nightwatch's symbol.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Shatterbane at the top, with organized crime lieutenants and enforcers.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Present as the "real" Nightwatch bringing order through strength.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Prove superiority over the original Nightwatch and remake the city in his image.
        *   **Assets:** Criminal empire, stolen Nightwatch armor and symbol, fear-based control.
        *   **Relationships:** Opposed by Nightwatch allies and some law enforcement; partially accepted by desperate citizens.
    *   **The Order of Azrael** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Spread their interpretation of divine justice through their agents.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Religious order led by a Grand Master, with Jean-Paul as their sword.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To create warriors who bring God's judgment to evil.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** To establish Jean-Paul permanently as the Nightwatch and spread their influence.
        *   **Assets:** Training facilities, psychological conditioning programs, religious zealots.
        *   **Relationships:** Supportive of Jean-Paul; suspicious of the original Nightwatch's "weakness"; neutral to Shatterbane.
    *   **The City Watch** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Maintain law and order through official channels.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Led by Commissioner Reynolds, with various captains and officers.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Work with whoever protects the city, even if methods are questionable.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Reynolds knows the truth and secretly aids the original Nightwatch's recovery.
        *   **Assets:** Police resources, legal authority, intelligence networks.
        *   **Relationships:** Officially cooperate with "Nightwatch" (Shatterbane); secretly aid true Nightwatch's allies; concerned about Jean-Paul's methods.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Directive:** The Character Generation Agent must create full profiles for all NPCs listed. Tier 1 NPCs are unique individuals. Tier 2 & 3 are archetypes to be instantiated multiple times with unique details as needed by the simulation.
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **The Nightwatch (Bruce):** Legendary protector, physically and mentally shattered, struggling to recover and wondering if they can ever be the hero again.
    *   **Shatterbane:** Brilliant villain who broke the Nightwatch and now wears their symbol, ruling through fear.
    *   **Jean-Paul (Azrael):** Zealous warrior chosen as temporary successor, increasingly brutal and uncontrollable.
    *   **Commissioner Reynolds:** Pragmatic law officer, conflicted about working with various "Nightwatch" versions.
*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Archetypes for Generation):**
    *   "Loyal Nightwatch Ally" (wants the hero to return)
    *   "Desperate Citizen" (supports whoever brings order)
    *   "Shatterbane Lieutenant" (criminal enforcer wearing twisted version of symbol)
    *   "Order of Azrael Monk" (religious zealot supporting Jean-Paul)
    *   "Conflicted Police Officer" (unsure which Nightwatch to support)
    *   "Victim of Vigilante Justice" (suffered under brutal methods)

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Directive:** The Environment Generation Agent must create a detailed blueprint for each location below, populating all specified sub-sections.
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **The Sanctum:** The Nightwatch's hidden base, now a recovery center.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The Recovery Ward, the Training Hall, the Trophy Room (memories of past victories), the Command Center.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Recovering Nightwatch, loyal allies, medical staff, eventually Jean-Paul during training.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Medical treatment, equipment repair/upgrade, intelligence gathering, training.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Physical therapy session, nightmare/flashback, strategy meeting, Jean-Paul training brutally, emotional breakdown.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The Nightwatch's injuries may never fully heal." "Jean-Paul is becoming too dangerous." "Shatterbane knows where the Sanctum is."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (dimly lit, medical equipment, training gear), Sound (grunts of pain, clashing weapons), Smell (antiseptic, sweat, determination).
    *   **The City Above:** Gothic metropolis under Shatterbane's rule.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The Grand Cathedral, the Old Quarter, the Commerce District, the Commissioner's Office.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Desperate citizens, criminals, police, Shatterbane's enforcers.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Information from street contacts, black market equipment, citizen testimonies.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** "Nightwatch" (Shatterbane or Jean-Paul) brutal intervention, citizen being terrorized, criminal activity, public debate about the symbol, execution.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The 'new' Nightwatch killed someone innocent." "Some criminals are turning themselves in out of fear." "The real Nightwatch is still alive."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (gothic architecture, rain-slicked streets, the symbol projected on clouds), Sound (distant sirens, screams, thunder), Smell (rain, garbage, fear).
    *   **Shatterbane's Territory:** Criminal fortress in the old quarter.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The Throne Room, the Trophy Chamber (Nightwatch memorabilia), the Training Pit, the Armory.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Shatterbane, criminal lieutenants, enforcers, captured enemies.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** None for players—this is enemy territory.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Patrol of enforcers, torture interrogation, Shatterbane's theatrical speech, trapped hero rescue, evidence of his obsession.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Shatterbane is building something to permanently destroy the Nightwatch's legacy." "He has the original armor as a trophy." "A captive knows his true weakness."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (corrupted symbols, theatrical villain aesthetic), Sound (screams, villain monologues), Smell (blood, stone, obsession).
    *   **The Church of Azrael:** Jean-Paul's religious order headquarters.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The Great Hall, the Conditioning Chambers, the Armory of Judgment, the Grand Master's Sanctum.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Zealous monks, Grand Master, warriors in training.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Religious blessing (mechanically buffs), specialized training, information on Jean-Paul's condition.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Zealous sermon, brutal training session, conditioning program in action, debate about Jean-Paul's worthiness, revelation of their secret agenda.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The Order's conditioning makes warriors unstable." "They plan to keep Jean-Paul as Nightwatch permanently." "There's a way to reverse the conditioning."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (religious iconography, weapon displays, harsh lighting), Sound (chanting, combat training, prayers), Smell (incense, metal, fanaticism).

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Directive:** The World Simulation Agent must implement the following trigger-based state changes. For each "IF" condition, the agent must pre-generate the narrative and environmental consequences for the "THEN" outcome.
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** The Nightwatch's Recovery score reaches 50.
    *   **THEN:** They can begin light training and advising. Generate scenes of them watching Jean-Paul with growing concern.
    *   **IF:** Jean-Paul's Zeal meter reaches maximum.
    *   **THEN:** He crosses a line—kills an innocent or refuses orders. Generate a crisis confrontation.
    *   **IF:** Shatterbane discovers the Nightwatch is alive.
    *   **THEN:** He escalates attacks, targeting known allies. Generate a dramatic confrontation or trap.
    *   **IF:** Public Faith in the symbol drops below 30.
    *   **THEN:** Citizens begin rejecting all vigilantes. The symbol becomes associated with fear and tyranny.
    *   **IF:** The Nightwatch successfully confronts and defeats Jean-Paul.
    *   **THEN:** Generate an emotional scene of reclaiming the mantle. Jean-Paul may be redeemed, imprisoned, or killed depending on player choices.
    *   **IF:** The final confrontation with Shatterbane succeeds.
    *   **THEN:** Generate the aftermath—how the city reacts to the real Nightwatch's return, whether the symbol is redeemed, and what becomes of those who wore it.
