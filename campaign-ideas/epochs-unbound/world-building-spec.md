# Epochs Unbound - World-Building Spec

## Timeline of Future

### Year 1,000: The Golden Millennium
**Society:** Advanced magical-technological fusion
**Humanity:** Enhanced, longer-lived, prosperous
**Achievements:** Planar colonization, disease eliminated, art flourishing
**Challenges:** Complacency, loss of drive, subtle decline beginning
**Key Figure:** Councillor Meridian shows party achievements
**Party's Impact:** Inspire them, give warning they don't heed

### Year 10,000: The Age of Expansion
**Society:** Multi-planar empire at peak
**Humanity:** Multiple subspecies, some unrecognizable
**Achievements:** Magic perfected, immortality achieved, godlike power
**Challenges:** Hubris, internal conflict, overreach
**Key Event:** Party witnesses beginning of fall
**Warning Signs:** Civilization eating itself from within

### Year 25,000: The Great Collapse
**Society:** Empire fallen, dark age
**Humanity:** Scattered, knowledge lost, magic failing
**Achievements:** Forgotten
**Challenges:** Survival, raiders, monsters
**Key Event:** Not visited but evidence seen in next era
**Result:** Reset to primitive state

### Year 100,000: The Divergence
**Surface Dwellers (Eloi equivalent):**
- Beautiful, innocent, childlike
- Live in daylight
- No technology
- Peaceful but doomed

**Underground Dwellers (Morlocks equivalent):**
- Adapted to darkness
- Maintained some technology
- Prey on surface dwellers
- Tragic mirror of humanity

**Tragedy:** Both are human, both degraded, cannot reconcile

### Year 500,000: The Silent Age
**Society:** None, scattered individuals
**Humanity:** Barely sentient
**Earth:** Ecosystems failing
**Sun:** Noticeably dying
**Atmosphere:** This era is skipped, too depressing

### Year 1,000,000: The Crystal Refuge
**Society:** Last civilization, artificial
**Humanity:** Post-human, crystalline beings
**Technology:** Beyond comprehension but failing
**Purpose:** Await death of world
**Hope:** Planning final migration to elsewhere
**Party's Role:** Prophesied visitors, bring meaning to ending

### Year 100,000,000: The Ending
**Society:** None
**Life:** The Last One only
**Earth:** Dying, sun red giant phase
**Purpose:** Deliver message across time
**Revelation:** Time machine's true purpose
**Choice:** Party must complete cycle or break it

## Professor Antiqua's Message

### Part 1 (Found at Start)
"If you're reading this, time machine worked. Go forward. Learn. Witness."

### Part 2 (1,000 years)
"See what we can become. Hold onto hope."

### Part 3 (100,000 years)
"See what we can lose. Remember to preserve."

### Part 4 (1,000,000 years)
"See the cost of hubris. All things end."

### Part 5 (100,000,000 years)
"You are the message. Take knowledge back. Change present. Bootstrap complete."

### The Paradox
- Party learns from future
- Changes present based on that
- Creates the future they visited
- Self-fulfilling prophecy
- Accept or fight it?

## Evolutionary Paths

### Baseline Humanity (Present)
- Current form
- Magic capable
- Tool users
- Social species

### Enhanced Humanity (1,000-10,000 years)
- Longer lived
- Greater magical affinity
- Physically perfected
- Mentally expanded

### Divergent Humanity (100,000 years)
**Surface Branch:**
- Smaller, weaker
- Adapted for daylight
- Lost intelligence
- Gained innocence

**Underground Branch:**
- Larger, stronger
- Nocturnal
- Retained cunning
- Lost empathy

### Post-Humanity (1,000,000 years)
- Silicon-based life
- Crystalline structure
- Pure thought
- Barely physical

### Trans-Humanity (100,000,000 years)
- Beyond classification
- Last intelligence
- Neither alive nor dead
- Cosmic awareness

## The Time Machine

### Design
- Ancient beyond current technology
- Steampunk aesthetic meets magic
- Seat for traveler
- Control mechanism
- Viewing window

### Function
- Accelerate forward through time
- Local bubble of normal time
- Observer protected
- Can stop at any point
- Cannot reverse

### Limitations
- One-way journey
- Fuel/magic depleting
- Each jump harder
- Eventually will fail
- Stranding possible

### True Purpose (Revealed Late)
- Not for exploration
- Message delivery system
- Bootstrap knowledge transfer
- Professor Antiqua's gambit
- Save present by witnessing future

## Philosophical Questions

**If you know future, must you create it?**
**Can witnessing change outcome?**
**Is knowledge from future cheating?**
**What is humanity's responsibility to descendants?**
**Is it better to know end or live in ignorance?**
**Can species avoid evolutionary decline?**

## The Emotional Journey

### Wonder (Early)
- Amazement at progress
- Hope for future
- Excitement of discovery

### Horror (Middle)
- Witnessing decline
- Loss of civilization
- Tragedy of devolution

### Acceptance (Late)
- All things end
- Beauty in impermanence
- Cosmic perspective

### Resolution (Return)
- Appreciation for present
- Wisdom to change what can be changed
- Peace with what cannot

## Victory Details

### Return with Knowledge
- Warn present about pitfalls
- Share future innovations
- Preserve critical knowledge
- Alter course slightly

### The Bootstrap Paradox
- Accept that visiting creates visited future
- Work within it
- Make peace with determinism
- Or rebel and create new branch

### The Choice to Stay
- One party member remains in future
- Becomes Professor Antiqua
- Closes the loop
- Heroic sacrifice
- Campaign circles back to beginning

This creates contemplative campaign about time's vastness and humanity's place in eternity.
