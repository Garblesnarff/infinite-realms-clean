# The Three Hundred Shields - Creative Brief

## Art Style: Mythic Historical Realism

**Visual:** Frank Miller's 300 aesthetic meets actual history. Heightened realism: bronze muscles, crimson cloaks, bronze shields, slow-motion combat.

**Color Palettes:**
- **Sparta:** Blood red (cloaks), bronze, iron gray, austere stone
- **Persia:** Gold, exotic patterns, diverse armor, overwhelming numbers
- **Battle:** Bronze catching sun, blood spray, dust clouds, arrow storms
- **The Final Stand:** Twilight colors, heroic doom, ash and bronze

**Music:** Ancient Greek (aulos, drums) meets modern epic. Heavy percussion for phalanx. Choir singing in ancient Greek.

**Themes:**
- **Sparta:** War drums, tight harmony (unity), relentless rhythm
- **Persia:** Exotic instruments, overwhelming scale, diverse cacophony
- **The 300:** Individual instruments forming perfect whole (phalanx metaphor)

**Voices:**
- **Leonidas:** Gerard Butler energy—gravelly, commanding, Spartan
- **Xerxes:** Rodrigo Santoro—otherworldly, divine arrogance, Persian-accented
- **Dilios:** Narrator voice, bardic, making legend from history
- **Gorgo:** Lena Headey—strong, intelligent, political equal

**Cinematic Moments:**
- The kick: "This is Sparta!"
- Phalanx formation: perfect synchronized violence
- Arrow storm blotting sun: "Then we'll fight in the shade"
- Final stand: slow-motion, individual heroics
- Leonidas's final spear throw at Xerxes
- Monument reveal: "Go tell the Spartans..."
