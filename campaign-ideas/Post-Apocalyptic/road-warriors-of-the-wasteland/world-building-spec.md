**Road Warriors of the Wasteland**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** In post-apocalyptic Wasteland where water and gas are worth more than life, the party are Road Warriors surviving through vehicular combat. Recruited to protect the legendary War Rig on its journey to mythical Green Place, they face war boy suicide riders, cannibal gangs, and Immortan Joe's forces. They must decide: complete the mission to save humanity's remnants, or embrace the Wasteland's brutal chrome-sprayed glory.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the apocalypse that created the Wasteland—resource wars, ecological collapse, civilization's end.
    *   Write the history of Immortan Joe's rise controlling water supply and creating Citadel hierarchy.
    *   Describe war boy death cult formation—worshiping V8 engines and chrome Valhalla.
    *   Explain the Green Place's history as pre-apocalypse paradise and its eventual death.
    *   Detail the formation of Gas Town and Bullet Farm as resource-controlling tyrannies.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Citadel** (Major): Immortan Joe's fortress controlling water, enforced by war boy cult.
    *   **War Boys** (Major): Death cult believing glorious vehicle deaths lead to chrome Valhalla.
    *   **Gas Town** (Major): Industrial hellscape controlling gasoline supply, ruled by People Eater.
    *   **Bullet Farm** (Major): Weapons manufacturing ruled by sadistic general.
    *   **The Vuvalini** (Minor): Green Place survivors on motorcycles, keepers of seeds and knowledge.
    *   **Road Warriors** (Minor): Independent survivors taking convoy jobs, neither tyrants nor slaves.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Furiosa (War Rig driver), Immortan Joe (water tyrant), Nux (reformed war boy), Vuvalini leaders (memory keepers), People Eater (gas warlord).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "War Boy" (death cultist), "Road Warrior" (independent survivor), "Citadel Slave" (oppressed worker), "Motorcycle Gang" (raiders), "Refugee" (seeking Green Place).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The Citadel:** Vertical fortress carved from mesa, water cascade from top, slave levels below, Immortan's palace above.
*   **The Wasteland:** Endless desert with toxic patches, dust storms, scattered wreckage, sparse survivor camps.
*   **The Canyon:** Narrow passages through rock formations, controlled by motorcycle gangs, ambush territory.
*   **Gas Town:** Industrial refinery complex, constant fires, ruled by People Eater, gasoline processing.
*   **Bullet Farm:** Weapons manufacturing facility, ammunition stores, ruled by military warlord.
*   **Green Place (Dead):** Former oasis now toxic swamp, symbol of what was lost.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** War Rig successfully protected → **THEN:** Refugees survive, hope remains alive, mission continues.
*   **IF:** War boy converted from death cult → **THEN:** Valuable ally gained, other war boys might question doctrine.
*   **IF:** Resources critically low → **THEN:** Must raid or barter, creates desperate situations.
*   **IF:** Immortan Joe's forces defeated → **THEN:** Power vacuum creates opportunity for new order.
*   **IF:** Green Place found dead → **THEN:** Must create new destination or return to overthrow tyranny.

**7. Resource System & Power Dynamics**
*   **Water Control:** Immortan Joe's primary power, controls survival itself.
*   **Gasoline Scarcity:** People Eater's leverage, vehicles useless without fuel.
*   **Ammunition Value:** Bullet Farm's contribution, violence currency.
*   **Vehicle Importance:** In Wasteland, vehicles are life—transportation, combat platform, shelter.
*   **Knowledge Power:** Vuvalini hold seeds and pre-apocalypse understanding, represents future.

**8. Economy & Resources**
*   **Water:** Most precious resource, measured in drops, controlled by tyrants.
*   **Gasoline:** Second most valuable, enables movement and combat, hoarded by Gas Town.
*   **Ammunition:** Currency of violence, manufactured by Bullet Farm.
*   **Vehicle Parts:** Scavenged from wreckage, improvised repairs, creative problem-solving.
*   **Blood Bags:** War boys use healthy humans as "blood bags" for medical purposes.

**9. Conflict Escalation Framework**
*   **Early Game:** Independent survival, convoy escort jobs, learning vehicular combat.
*   **Mid Game:** War Rig protection, constant pursuit by Joe's forces, Green Place journey.
*   **Late Game:** Green Place dead, decision to return and overthrow Citadel, massive convoy battle, new order.

**10. Cultural Details & Flavor**
*   **War Boy Cult:** Death worship, chrome spray ritual, steering wheel iconography, "witness me" glory seeking.
*   **Vehicle as Self:** In Wasteland, your vehicle is extension of identity, life support, and legacy.
*   **Resource Religion:** Water and gasoline as divine substances, their control as godhood.
*   **Wasteland Language:** Evolved slang—"chrome," "witness," "guzzoline," "blood bag," "war boys."
*   **Improvisation Culture:** Everything rebuilt from scrap, creativity from necessity, practical aesthetics.
