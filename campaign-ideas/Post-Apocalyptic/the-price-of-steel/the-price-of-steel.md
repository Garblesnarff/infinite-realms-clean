# The Price of Steel

*A Grimdark Low Fantasy campaign — Long Campaign (15+ sessions) — Medium to High difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Price of Steel
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Grimdark Fantasy / War and Politics / Morally Grey Characters / No True Heroes
**Estimated Length:** Long Campaign — 15+ sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **12**
**Tone (keywords):** grimdark, cynical, morally-grey, war-torn, political-intrigue, brutal-realism, dark-humor
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
In the Union, a declining empire locked in perpetual war with the North and threatened by the Gurkish Empire from the south, there are no true heroes—only survivors making terrible choices. The players are a diverse group: a torturer for the Inquisition, a disgraced northern barbarian, a nobleman-turned-soldier, a pragmatic mercenary—each morally compromised and seeking redemption or revenge they'll never find. As they navigate a brutal war where magic is rare and ugly, leaders are incompetent or cruel, and victory is pyrrhic at best, they must choose whether to become monsters to fight monsters, or try to keep some scrap of humanity in a world that punishes decency. There are no good endings, only choices about what you can live with.

---

## Story Arc (Long Campaign — 15+ sessions)

A campaign inspired by Joe Abercrombie's First Law trilogy, focusing on morally grey characters, the futility of war, and the corruption of power.

*   **Act I — The Bloody Nine Returns (Sessions 1–5):** The players are forced into an unlikely alliance when the Union's Closed Council (ruling oligarchy) sends them on a desperate mission to the North. The Gurkish Empire is preparing invasion, and the Union needs the barbarian clans as allies—but the clans are led by Bethod, a king who the infamous Bloody-Nine once followed and then betrayed. The players must survive northern politics, brutal clan warfare, and the revelation that one of them IS the Bloody-Nine—Logan Ninefingers, a man trying to escape his monstrous past but constantly pulled back into violence. They learn that war makes everyone complicit, and survival often requires becoming what you hate.

*   **Act II — The Siege and the Betrayal (Sessions 6–11):** Returned to the Union, the players are thrown into the disastrous siege of the northern city of Dagoska by the Gurkish Empire. As supplies dwindle, commanders prove incompetent, and fellow soldiers reveal themselves as cowards or monsters, the players must make impossible choices: sacrifice innocents to survive, follow orders they know are wrong, or betray their own side for a chance at ending the slaughter. They encounter Bayaz, the First of the Magi—a wizard who claims to fight for civilization but whose methods are ruthless and whose true agenda is unclear. They discover that the Union's leaders care nothing for soldiers' lives, and Bayaz's magic comes from a terrible, cannibalistic source.

*   **Act III — The War Makes Monsters (Sessions 12–15+):** Back in Adua (Union capital), political conspiracy comes to a head. The Gurkish invade in overwhelming force, and the players must defend a city ruled by the incompetent and corrupt. Bayaz reveals his true nature: he is no hero but an ancient tyrant maintaining his power through manipulation and forbidden magic. The players face a choice: support him (preserving order through tyranny), oppose him (likely dying or being broken), or walk away (abandoning those they've come to care for). No choice is good. The campaign ends with pyrrhic victory at best—the war is won but the cost is souls, and the players must face what they've become. There are no heroes, only survivors who carry their scars.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "You Have to Be Realistic"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Moral Greyness / Unlikely Alliance
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players meet in Adua's House of Questions (torture chambers) where Inquisitor Glokta interrogates them on suspicion of treason.
    *   Each reveals their dark past: a torturer who was once tortured, a barbarian fleeing his reputation as the Bloody-Nine, a nobleman's bastard seeking redemption.
    *   The Closed Council forces them to work together on a northern mission—they're expendable assets.
    *   First combat is ugly and desperate, not heroic. Magic, if it appears, is disturbing and costs dearly.
**Escalation:** They realize one of them is Logan Ninefingers, the legendary Bloody-Nine—and northern clans want him dead or deified.

### Sessions 2-4 — "The Blade Itself"
*   **Focus:** Northern Politics / Survival / Violence
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Traveling North, they encounter brutal clan warfare, where honor is a lie and strength rules.
    *   They must negotiate with Bethod, the Northern King, who the Bloody-Nine once betrayed.
    *   Logan struggles to contain the Bloody-Nine—a berserker rage that makes him unstoppable but inhuman.
    *   They participate in northern trials: finger-games, honor duels, and casual brutality.
    *   They discover Bayaz, claiming to be humanity's protector, but his arrogance and ruthlessness disturb them.
**Escalation:** Bethod betrays them, revealing he serves powers beyond the North. They barely escape, realizing no one can be trusted.

### Sessions 5-7 — "Before They Are Hanged"
*   **Focus:** Siege Warfare / Degradation / Impossible Choices
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Deployed to Dagoska, besieged by the Gurkish Empire, they endure horror: starvation, disease, incompetent commanders, and slow degradation.
    *   The nobleman player must lead soldiers he knows will die. The torturer player interrogates prisoners for information.
    *   They discover the commanding general is corrupt, hoarding supplies while soldiers starve.
    *   As the siege tightens, they must choose: mutiny against leadership, sacrifice civilians for military advantage, or attempt hopeless rescue.
**Escalation:** The Gurkish breach the walls. In the chaos, the Bloody-Nine emerges and slaughters dozens—saving lives but losing his humanity again.

### Sessions 8-11 — "The Magic of Politics"
*   **Focus:** Conspiracy / Forbidden Magic / Disillusionment
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Bayaz reveals he needs them for a mission into the Old Empire's ruins to retrieve the Seed—a weapon of terrible power.
    *   They venture into ancient, monster-filled ruins and discover the Seed is powered by consuming human souls—this is the source of Bayaz's magic.
    *   Political conspiracy unfolds: the Closed Council is incompetent, nobles scheme for power, and common people suffer.
    *   They witness Bayaz casually commit atrocities "for the greater good," realizing he's no hero but an ancient tyrant.
    *   Personal relationships develop and break—love affairs end badly, friendships are betrayed by circumstance.
**Escalation:** The Gurkish Empire launches full invasion of the Union. Adua itself is under siege.

### Sessions 12-15+ — "Last Argument of Kings"
*   **Focus:** Siege Defense / Moral Reckoning / Pyrrhic Victory
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The players defend Adua alongside broken, cynical soldiers, incompetent nobles, and Bayaz's ruthless magic.
    *   Bayaz uses the Seed, destroying a massive section of the Gurkish army—and obliterating part of Adua and thousands of civilians.
    *   The players confront their choices: they've become monsters fighting monsters. Logan is the Bloody-Nine more than himself. The torturer has inflicted horror. The nobleman has sacrificed his principles.
    *   The conspiracy reveals that Bayaz manipulated everything, ensuring his power continues regardless of who "wins."
    *   Final choice: Submit to Bayaz's order, oppose him (likely futilely), or walk away from it all.
    *   No happy ending is possible. Victory means living with what you've done. The powerful remain powerful. The cycle continues.
**Escalation:** The campaign ends ambiguously—the war is won, but the players have lost themselves. They must decide if survival was worth the price.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Bayaz, the First of the Magi** — *Ancient Wizard and Manipulator*
    *   **Role:** Claims to protect civilization, actually an ancient tyrant maintaining power through manipulation and forbidden magic.
    *   **Personality:** Arrogant, charming, utterly ruthless. Believes the ends justify any means. Treats mortals as game pieces.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** His magic requires consuming souls. His conflicts with other Magi (Khalul) are personal vendettas, not good vs evil. He's a monster pretending to be a savior.

2.  **Sand dan Glokta** — *Inquisitor and Torturer*
    *   **Role:** Crippled torturer for the Union's Inquisition, bitter and brilliant, seeking revenge on those who abandoned him.
    *   **Personality:** Cynical, darkly humorous, self-aware of his monstrosity. Hates what he does but does it masterfully anyway.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He was once a beautiful soldier, tortured for years and returned broken. His cruelty is driven by pain and spite, not evil. He's capable of surprising compassion buried under layers of cynicism.

3.  **Logan Ninefingers / The Bloody-Nine** — *Barbarian Seeking Redemption*
    *   **Role:** Legendary northern warrior trying to escape his violent past, but the berserker rage always returns.
    *   **Personality:** Kind, philosophical, genuinely trying to be better. But under stress, the Bloody-Nine emerges—a monster who kills without thought.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** The Bloody-Nine isn't separate—it's who Logan is when he stops pretending. His quest for redemption may be impossible because violence is his nature.

4.  **Jezal dan Luthar** — *Vain Nobleman Turned Reluctant Hero*
    *   **Role:** Spoiled aristocrat thrust into war, slowly stripped of illusions about honor and glory.
    *   **Personality:** Starts vain and shallow, slowly develops genuine care for others, but is manipulated into becoming a puppet king.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He genuinely tries to be good, but the system won't let him. He becomes a powerless figurehead, proving that good intentions mean nothing against systemic corruption.

5.  **Ferro Maljinn** — *Vengeful Escaped Slave*
    *   **Role:** Former slave of the Gurkish Empire, consumed by hatred, possessing mysterious power.
    *   **Personality:** Angry, distrustful, violent. Incapable of forgiving or letting go. Sees everyone as potential enemy or tool.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Her rage is justified but consuming her. She's part-devil (Eater heritage), giving her power but isolating her from humanity.

6.  **King Bethod** — *Pragmatic Tyrant of the North*
    *   **Role:** United the northern clans through cunning and alliance with dark powers, betrayed by Logan.
    *   **Personality:** Charming, intelligent, utterly practical. Not cruel for pleasure, but will do anything to maintain power.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He's made deals with the Other Side (demons) for power, and those debts will come due.

## Key Locations

1.  **Adua:** Capital of the Union, city of contrasts—elegant Agriont (noble district) and squalid slums. Houses the Closed Council, the House of Questions (torture chambers), and massive city walls.

2.  **The North:** Harsh, mountainous land of feuding clans. Honor is claimed but rarely practiced. Site of brutal inter-clan warfare.

3.  **Dagoska:** Port city besieged by the Gurkish Empire, slowly starving and degrading under siege conditions.

4.  **The Old Empire Ruins:** Ancient cities destroyed in magi wars, now monster-infested and containing forbidden artifacts.

5.  **The House of the Maker:** Ancient tower in Adua, indestructible and mysterious, symbol of Bayaz's enduring power.

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores the corruption of power, the futility of war, and whether anyone can stay good in a bad world. There are no clear villains—everyone has understandable motivations and everyone commits atrocities. The central question is what you're willing to become to survive, and whether redemption is possible for those who've done terrible things. The campaign critiques the idea of heroes, showing that "great men" are often monsters with good PR, and that systemic problems can't be solved by violence alone.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Grim Realism Combat**
*   Combat is brutal, quick, and unglamorous. No epic duels—just desperate struggles where luck matters as much as skill.
*   Mechanically: Combat encounters are deadlier. Injury has lasting consequences (scars, limps, trauma). Healing is slow and incomplete.
*   After combat, players must describe physical and emotional toll—exhaustion, terror, what it cost them.

### 2. **Moral Compromise System**
*   Players track Compromise—representing how much of their principles they've sacrificed.
*   Each time they choose expedience over morality, they gain Compromise and mechanical benefits (bonus to ruthless actions, fear effects on enemies).
*   But high Compromise changes the character: NPCs react differently, certain diplomatic options close, the character becomes what they fought.
*   At thresholds, players must roleplay character degradation: becoming cynical, cruel, or broken.

### 3. **The Bloody-Nine Mechanic**
*   If Logan Ninefingers is a PC or major NPC, he has a berserker mechanic.
*   When taking damage or allies fall, roll to resist the Bloody-Nine. On failure, he enters unstoppable rage.
*   While berserk: Immune to fear/pain, massive combat bonuses, attacks nearest target (friend or foe), remembers nothing after.
*   The Bloody-Nine can win battles but destroys relationships and leaves Logan horrified by his actions.

### 4. **Political Complications**
*   Major actions create political ripples. Kill the wrong person, and factions shift. Save the wrong people, and you make new enemies.
*   The DM tracks faction influence: Union Nobles, Closed Council, Inquisition, Northern Clans, Bayaz's Agenda.
*   Players can't win everyone over—helping one faction alienates others. Trying to stay neutral means everyone distrusts you.

### 5. **No Happy Endings Table**
*   At campaign end, roll on outcomes for each PC based on their choices:
    *   **Redeemed** (rare): Survived with some humanity intact, but scarred forever.
    *   **Broken**: Survived but became a monster/cynic/puppet.
    *   **Dead Heroically**: Died preventing something worse—small comfort.
    *   **Dead Pointlessly**: Died for nothing, like most soldiers.
    *   **Disappeared**: Walked away from it all, but can never escape what they did.
