# Starfire Rebellion - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Dominion Empire

**Imperial Structure**
- **The Emperor**: Tyrannus, absolute ruler for 300 years (life extension technology)
- **Imperial Council**: 12 Moffs governing sectors (competitive, infighting)
- **Military**: Navy (space), Army (ground), Intelligence (secret police)
- **Bureaucracy**: Vast administrative system maintaining control

**Rise to Power**
- 300 years ago: The Fragmentation Wars devastated the galaxy
- Multiple factions warring for decades
- Tyrannus unified them through military conquest
- Initial rule was stabilizing, even welcomed
- Gradual shift to tyranny over centuries

**Methods of Control**
- Military supremacy through super-weapons
- Surveillance state (informants everywhere)
- Economic dependence (control trade routes)
- Propaganda (Emperor as savior figure)
- Fear (public executions, planet-destroying weapons)

**Military Capabilities**
- **Star Destroyers**: Massive capital ships, city-killers
- **TIE Fighters**: Fast, unshielded attack craft (pilots expendable)
- **Stormtroopers**: Elite infantry, loyalty through indoctrination
- **Apocalypse Cannons**: Planet/system destroying super-weapons
- Total fleet: Thousands of capital ships, millions of troops

**Internal Factions**
- **Loyalists**: True believers in Imperial order (Admiral Rexus starts here)
- **Pragmatists**: Support Empire for stability, not ideology
- **Schemers**: Moffs positioning for post-Tyrannus power
- **Reformers**: Want gradual change from within (few, suppressed)

### The Starfire Rebellion

**Formation**
- Began 5 years ago as separate resistance cells
- General Vance unified them after her homeworld's destruction
- Named for ancient prophecy about liberator bringing light
- Grew from hundreds to millions over course of campaign

**Structure**
- **High Command**: General Vance, civilian representatives, party advisors
- **Military**: Fleet commanders, ground forces, intelligence
- **Support**: Logistics, medical, propaganda, recruitment
- **Cells**: Independent units across galaxy, loosely coordinated

**Resources (Start of Campaign)**
- 50 combat ships (vs. Empire's thousands)
- One hidden base (Refuge)
- Limited supplies, always rationing
- Funding from sympathetic systems and the Syndicate

**Ideology**
- Democracy vs. autocracy
- Freedom vs. security
- Individual rights vs. state control
- Peaceful coexistence vs. forced unity

**Internal Tensions**
- **Militants**: Aggressive tactics, accept collateral damage
- **Pacifists**: Resist but minimize violence
- **Pragmatists**: Do whatever works
- **Idealists**: Victory must be morally pure
- Party's choices determine which faction dominates

### The Syndicate

**Organization**
- Criminal network spanning hundreds of systems
- Smuggling, black market, protection rackets, slavery
- Loosely affiliated crime families under the Council of Lords

**Leadership**
- **The Council**: Five major crime lords including Vex Kavros
- Each controls different territory/market
- Compete but don't war (bad for business)
- Meetings held at neutral space stations

**Relationship with Empire**
- Empire suppresses Syndicate publicly
- Secretly, many officials take bribes
- Syndicate provides services Empire officially can't
- Mutually beneficial corruption

**Relationship with Rebellion**
- Initially neutral (war disrupts business)
- Vex Kavros rescued by party, becomes sympathetic
- Provides weapons, supplies, safe houses
- Post-war, want legitimacy and territory

**Operations**
- Spice running (addictive substance)
- Weapons dealing (both sides)
- Slavery (major moral issue for party)
- Information brokerage

### Neutral Systems Alliance

**Membership**
- Systems neither Imperial nor Rebel
- Want to avoid conflict
- Trade with both sides
- Fear reprisals

**Leadership**
- Council of planetary governors
- Meet irregularly to coordinate
- No unified military
- Defensive pacts only

**Strategy**
- Appeasement of Empire (pay tribute)
- Secret support of Rebellion (deniable)
- Protection of trade routes
- Attempt at diplomatic solution

**Campaign Role**
- Recruitment targets for Rebellion
- Safe havens (until discovered)
- Economic support
- Post-war, form government core

## Location Blueprints and Star Maps

### The Galaxy - Political Map

**Imperial Core** (100 systems)
- Heavily fortified
- Emperor's throne world: Dominion Prime
- Industrial centers, military academies
- Impossible to attack directly until Act IV

**Imperial Territory** (1,000+ systems)
- Varying levels of control
- Some occupied, some willingly joined
- Moff-governed sectors
- Resource extraction and taxation

**Contested Space** (200+ systems)
- Active warfare zones
- Systems changing hands
- Civilian populations suffering
- Key strategic locations

**Rebel Territory** (50+ systems by campaign end)
- Liberated worlds
- Fragile, need protection
- Economic rebuilding
- Democratic governance forming

**Neutral Space** (300+ systems)
- Officially uninvolved
- Secret dealings with both sides
- Trade hubs
- Potential recruits

**The Unknown Regions**
- Unexplored galactic rim
- Possible safe haven
- Ancient civilizations (Ghost Fleet origin)
- Post-campaign expansion area

### Key Campaign Locations - Detailed

**Ironhold Station** (Sessions 1-2)
- **Size**: 10km diameter orbital platform
- **Population**: 5,000 Imperial personnel
- **Security**: Heavy. Multiple checkpoints, biometric scans, patrol droids
- **Layout**:
  - Docking ring (visitor access)
  - Administrative levels (cover for party)
  - Research levels (restricted, vault location)
  - Command center (monitoring everything)
  - Reactor core (potential sabotage target)
- **The Vault**: Triple-sealed, Apocalypse Cannon blueprints
- **Escape Route**: Maintenance tunnels to emergency hangar

**Refuge Asteroid Base** (Sessions 3-9)
- **Location**: Asteroid field in neutral system
- **Size**: Hollowed asteroid, 2km diameter
- **Population**: 2,000 Rebels, 500 civilians
- **Facilities**:
  - Hangar bay (hidden entrance)
  - Barracks (cramped)
  - Command center (General Vance's office)
  - Mess hall (social hub)
  - Workshop (repairs, modifications)
  - Medical bay (always busy)
- **Defense**: Minimal. Concealment over firepower
- **Destruction**: Session 9, Imperial assault, total loss

**Keppra-5 Mining World** (Sessions 4-5)
- **Environment**: Toxic atmosphere, underground cities
- **Population**: 10 million miners (oppressed)
- **Imperial Presence**: Garrison, overseers, corporate representatives
- **Mines**: Deep tunnels, dangerous conditions, rich minerals
- **Resistance**: Underground network led by foreman Jin
- **Liberation**: Party inspires uprising, key recruitment victory

**The Ghost Yards** (Sessions 10-11)
- **Location**: Dead system, no planets, just shipyard
- **Age**: 10,000+ years old (pre-Imperial)
- **Size**: Massive, 100km+ of automated facilities
- **AI**: Hostile initially, traumatized by creator's extinction
- **Technology**: Advanced beyond current galactic standard
- **Production**: Can build 50 ships simultaneously
- **Ships**: Sleeker, faster, better shields than Imperial designs
- **Defense**: Automated weapon platforms, fighter drones

**Holonet Hub Station** (Sessions 12-13)
- **Function**: Galaxy-wide communication relay
- **Ownership**: Nominally civilian, Imperial monitored
- **Layout**:
  - Broadcast studios
  - Corporate offices
  - Server farms (massive data storage)
  - Civilian residential
  - Imperial monitoring center
- **Mission**: Infiltrate, broadcast Apocalypse plans, escape before traced

**Sanctuary-3** (Destroyed Session 16)
- **Type**: Garden world, paradise planet
- **Population**: 2 billion (were)
- **Features**: Oceans, forests, beautiful cities
- **Strategic Value**: None. Pure civilian target
- **Destruction**: Apocalypse Cannon test firing
- **Impact**: Rebellion's 9/11 moment, changes everything
- **Survivors**: Few, traumatized, become Rebellion's voice

**Antares System** (Session 19-20)
- **Type**: Industrial system with shipyards
- **Strategic Value**: High. Control = fleet production
- **Features**:
  - Gas giant (mine for fuel)
  - Orbital platforms (ship construction)
  - Asteroid belt (resource extraction)
  - Imperial garrison (significant)
- **Battle**: First major fleet engagement
- **Outcome**: Rebellion victory, captures shipyards

**Dominion Prime** (Sessions 34-35)
- **Type**: City-planet, Imperial capital
- **Population**: 50 billion
- **Features**:
  - Emperor's Palace (monumental architecture)
  - Government complexes
  - Military academies
  - Orbital defense platforms (hundreds)
  - Apocalypse Cannon control center
- **Final Battle**: Assault on palace, confrontation with Emperor

## Causality Chains and Galactic Conflicts

### The Fragmentation Wars (350-300 Years Ago)

**Causes**
- Old Republic collapsed (corruption, overextension)
- No central authority
- Warlords seized systems
- Economic collapse, trade routes severed
- Refugee crisis, mass starvation

**Key Players**
- Dozens of warlord factions
- Corporate states (profit from chaos)
- Pirate kingdoms
- Religious movements
- Tyrannus (minor warlord initially)

**Tyrannus's Rise**
- United several factions through military genius
- Offered stability vs. chaos
- Genuine improvement at first
- Consolidated power over 50 years
- Declared himself Emperor
- Those who resisted were crushed

**Legacy**
- Galaxy remembers chaos, fears return
- Empire's propaganda uses this
- Older generation supports Empire (they remember)
- Younger generation resents it (they don't)

### The Technological Arms Race

**Pre-Empire Technology**
- Standard: lasers, shields, hyperdrives
- FTL communication limited
- Weapons destructive but not planet-killing

**Imperial Innovation**
- Apocalypse Cannons (system destroyers)
- Advanced surveillance
- Thought-crime prediction algorithms
- Life extension for Emperor

**Rebellion Innovation**
- Can't match Empire tech-for-tech
- Instead: tactics, improvisation, morale
- Ghost Fleet provides temporary advantage
- Syndicate black market tech

**Extra-Galactic Threat** (Foreshadowed)
- Tyrannus knows something
- Apocalypse Cannons originally defensive
- Threat from beyond galaxy
- Empire's tyranny partially justified (barely)

### The Prophecy of Starfire

**Origin**
- Ancient text from unknown civilization
- Discovered 500 years ago
- Predicts "Starfire" liberator

**Prophecy Text**
*"When stars are extinguished and hope fades to ember,*
*The Starfire shall rise from the ashes of the fallen,*
*Neither one nor many, but light itself made manifest,*
*To kindle rebellion in hearts long darkened,*
*And burn away the shadows of tyranny."*

**Interpretations**
- **Literalists**: Specific person will be chosen
- **Metaphorists**: Symbol, not individual
- **Skeptics**: Coincidence, seeing patterns
- **Rebellion**: Propaganda tool, true or not

**Campaign Use**
- People think party member is Starfire
- Alternatively, entire party collectively
- Or symbol they represent
- Prophecy self-fulfilling through belief

### Key Historical Events Referenced

**The Intervention Catastrophe** (200 years ago)
- Empire contacted pre-spaceflight species
- Uplifted them "for their own good"
- Cultural contamination destroyed them
- Empire uses as cautionary tale
- Rebellion uses as example of Imperial arrogance

**The Keppra Massacre** (50 years ago)
- Miners striked for better conditions
- Imperial response: orbital bombardment
- 1 million dead
- Set tone for Imperial labor policy
- Why Keppra-5 so eager to rebel

**The Moff Rebellions** (30 years ago)
- Three Moffs attempted coup against Tyrannus
- Briefly held 100 systems
- Crushed within months
- Public executions, families enslaved
- Lesson: Don't cross the Emperor

## Additional Lore and World History

### Species of the Galaxy

**Humans**
- Most numerous
- Dominate Empire and Rebellion
- Diverse cultures across systems
- No special abilities, just adaptable

**Zytherians** (Insectoid)
- Hive-mind connection
- Excellent coordination
- Many in Imperial military
- Some break conditioning, join Rebellion

**Verdani** (Plant-based)
- Photosynthetic humanoids
- Pacifist culture
- Forced into Empire
- Support Rebellion quietly

**Gorians** (Reptilian)
- Large, strong, tough
- Warrior culture
- Vex Kavros's species
- Split between Empire and Syndicate

**Luminari** (Energy beings)
- Rare, mysterious
- Can interface with technology
- Hunted by Empire for experimentation
- Refugees in Rebellion

**Dozens of Others**
- Too many to detail
- Multi-species galaxy
- Empire favors humans, oppresses others
- Rebellion celebrates diversity

### Technology and Daily Life

**Hyperspace Travel**
- FTL via hyperspace dimension
- Days to weeks between systems
- Dangerous without calculated routes
- Interdictors can pull ships out

**Communication**
- Holonet: Galaxy-wide network
- Real-time communication
- Imperial monitored and censored
- Rebellion uses encrypted channels

**Currency and Economy**
- Credits (digital currency)
- Empire controls banks
- Black market in physical goods
- Rebellion runs on donations and theft

**Daily Life Under Empire**
- Core worlds: Comfortable but surveilled
- Mid-rim: Functional but oppressed
- Outer rim: Poor and exploited
- Permits required for everything
- Checkpoints, curfews, informants

**Rebellion Life**
- Constant relocation
- Limited resources
- Strong community bonds
- Hope despite hardship
- Everyone multi-role capable

### Force/Magic Equivalent: The Resonance

**The Resonance**
- Quasi-mystical energy field
- Few can sense/manipulate it
- Not widely known (Empire suppressed knowledge)
- Prophecies based on Resonance visions

**Resonance Users**
- Born with talent (1 in 10 million)
- No formal training remains
- Instinctive abilities
- Imperial Inquisitors hunt them

**Abilities**
- Enhanced reflexes (pilot aces)
- Danger sense (party member survival)
- Influence emotions (inspiration/fear)
- Rarely, telekinesis or precognition

**Campaign Integration**

- Explains improbable victories
- Connects to Starfire prophecy
- Tyrannus also Resonance-user (dark side equivalent)

- "Order brings peace"
- "The Emperor protects"
- Rebels portrayed as terrorists
- Planetary destruction as "necessary"

**Rebellion Symbols**
- Starfire (rising flame)
- Broken chains
- United species holding hands
- Hope colors: Gold and white

**Music and Art**
- Empire: Martial, classical, rigid
- Rebellion: Folk songs, protest art
- Banned: Anything questioning authority
- Underground: Thriving despite danger

**Holidays**
- Empire Day (celebration of unification)
- Remembrance Day (Rebellion, for fallen)
- Liberation Days (each freed system)
- Old holidays suppressed or co-opted
