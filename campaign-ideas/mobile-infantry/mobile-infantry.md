# Mobile Infantry

*A Military Sci-Fi / War campaign — Medium Campaign (10–14 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Mobile Infantry
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Military Sci-Fi / War / Powered Armor Combat
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 10–14 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **2** → Finish **9–11**
**Tone (keywords):** militaristic, citizenship through service, bug hunt, power armor, brutal warfare, fascistic propaganda, camaraderie
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party consists of fresh recruits in the Mobile Infantry—humanity's elite powered armor troops—fighting the Arachnids, a hive-mind insectoid species threatening human colonies. Through brutal drop operations, the party experiences the horrors of interstellar war while navigating a militaristic society where citizenship and voting rights are earned only through military service. As they rise through the ranks and uncover the Arachnid's true intelligence, they must decide whether humanity's war is defense or genocide, and whether their fascistic government deserves their loyalty.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Encounters:** Large-scale battles featuring swarms of Arachnids, powered armor combat mechanics, orbital drops.
*   **Investigation:** Understanding Arachnid tactics, discovering their intelligence, questioning propaganda.
*   **Consequences:** Character death is common—this is war. However, new characters can join as reinforcements.

---

## Session-by-Session Outline (Abbreviated)

### Sessions 1-3 — "Boot Camp"
*   Training at Camp Currie, learning powered armor, forming squad bonds, first deployment to a colony under attack.

### Sessions 4-7 — "The Bug War"
*   Multiple planetary drops against Arachnid infestations, escalating battles, witnessing civilian casualties, capturing a Brain Bug.

### Sessions 8-10 — "The Brain Bug Revelation"
*   Interrogating the captured Brain Bug reveals Arachnid intelligence, questioning whether they're mindless monsters or defending their territory.

### Sessions 11-14 — "Operation Endgame"
*   Final assault on the Arachnid homeworld, massive casualties, party must choose—complete genocide or negotiate peace.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Lieutenant Jean Rasczak** — *The Mentor*
    *   Hardened officer who teaches the party survival, later killed in action.

2.  **Sergeant Zim** — *The Drill Instructor*
    *   Brutal but fair, prepares recruits for the realities of war.

3.  **Sky Marshal Dienes** — *The Warmonger*
    *   Military leader pushing for total Arachnid extermination.

4.  **The Brain Bug** — *The Enemy Intelligence*
    *   Captured Arachnid leader, reveals species' intelligence and defensive motivations.

## Key Locations

1.  **Camp Currie:** Boot camp training facility.
2.  **Klendathu:** Arachnid homeworld—massive disaster of first invasion.
3.  **Planet P:** Outpost colony where Brain Bug is captured.
4.  **The Valley of Death:** Canyon where Rasczak's Roughnecks make final stand.

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Powered Armor**
*   Characters wear power armor granting enhanced strength, jump jets, and integrated weapons.
*   **Malfunction:** Armor can be damaged, reducing capabilities.

### 2. **Orbital Drop**
*   Missions begin with drop pods—risky but allows precise planetary insertion.

### 3. **Casualty Replacement**
*   Dead characters are replaced by new recruits joining the squad.

### 4. **Citizenship vs. Civilian**
*   Only veterans earn citizenship and voting rights—campaign explores this fascistic society.
