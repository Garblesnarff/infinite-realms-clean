### **World-Building Specification Brief**

**The 36th Chamber of Shaolin**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** After witnessing a Qing massacre, the party seeks refuge at Shaolin Temple to learn kung fu. They must progress through all 35 Chambers of training, with each chamber teaching different martial and philosophical skills. The campaign culminates in creating a revolutionary 36th Chamber to teach common people, transforming revenge into justice.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the history of Shaolin Temple and its 35 Chambers training system.
    *   Write about the Qing Empire's oppression and why resistance movements formed.
    *   Describe the philosophy behind each major chamber (balance, weapons, meditation, wooden men).
    *   Explain why Shaolin traditionally keeps its teachings secret from laypeople.
    *   Detail the legend of previous students who completed all chambers.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions:**
    *   **Shaolin Temple** - Goal: Preserve sacred martial knowledge while avoiding worldly conflicts.
    *   **The Qing Empire** - Goal: Suppress rebellion and maintain order through force.
    *   **The Resistance** - Goal: Free their people from oppression using Shaolin knowledge.
    *   **The 36th Chamber** - Goal: Democratize martial arts training for all people.

**4. NPC Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** San Te, The Abbott, General Tien Ta, Brother Wu, Master Killer.
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Shaolin monk (various chambers)," "Qing soldier," "Resistance fighter," "Civilian student (36th Chamber)."

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The 35 Chambers:**
        *   Landmarks: Each chamber has unique training apparatus and layout.
        *   Encounters: Skill challenges specific to each chamber's focus, progression tests.
    *   **Chamber 15 (Balance):**
        *   Landmarks: Poles over water, narrow beams, unstable platforms.
        *   Encounters: Balance contests, combat while balancing, falling into water.
    *   **Chamber 35 (Wooden Men):**
        *   Landmarks: Multiple animated training constructs, narrow passage through them.
        *   Encounters: Pattern recognition fights, final graduation test.
    *   **Village Training Grounds (36th Chamber):**
        *   Landmarks: Simplified training equipment, practice areas, student quarters.
        *   Encounters: Teaching challenges, Qing raids, mass combat training.

**6. Causality Chains**
*   **IF:** Party completes all 35 chambers → Gains full mastery and Abbott's respect.
*   **IF:** Party fails multiple chambers → Takes longer but shows persistence.
*   **IF:** The 36th Chamber is successfully established → Common people gain martial knowledge, changing society.
*   **IF:** Party seeks only revenge against Tien Ta → Misses the point of training, hollow victory.
*   **IF:** Party teaches students who then help in final battle → Demonstrates the power of democratized knowledge.
