# Death Metal Apocalypse - Creative Brief

## Art Style and Visual Tone

**Overall Aesthetic:** Post-apocalyptic horror meets death metal album covers. Visceral decay, exposed bone, beautiful grotesque. Think Zdzisław Beksiński's paintings meets Fallout wastelands, filtered through death metal artwork.

**Visual Influences:**
- Death metal album art (Death, Cannibal Corpse, Morbid Angel)
- Beksiński's apocalyptic paintings
- Giger's biomechanical horror
- Post-apocalyptic media (Mad Max, Fallout)
- Medical/anatomical illustration
- Decomposition time-lapse photography

## Color Palettes

**The Wastes:** Ash gray (#696969), dried blood (#8B0000), bone white (#F5F5DC), putrid green (#556B2F)
**Sky:** Perpetual dusk oranges and purples
**Brutal Death:** Deep reds, blacks, visceral
**Technical Death:** Clinical whites, surgical steel
**Melodic Death:** Northern lights colors, cold beauty
**Death-Doom:** Browns, grays, earth tones
**Progressive Death:** Shifting colors, evolution visible

## Music and Sound Design

**Ambient:** Grinding, industrial decay sounds, distant thunder
**Death Metal Variations:**
- **Brutal:** Cannibal Corpse, Suffocation
- **Technical:** Death, Cynic, Necrophagist
- **Melodic:** At the Gates, Dark Tranquillity, In Flames
- **Death-Doom:** My Dying Bride, Paradise Lost
- **Progressive:** Opeth, Gojira, Ne Obliviscaris

**Horsemen Themes:**
- **War:** Aggressive, martial
- **Famine:** Hollow, empty
- **Pestilence:** Diseased, corrupted sounds
- **Death:** Majestic, inevitable, strangely beautiful

## Voice Direction

**Schuldiner:** Wise mentor, death growl but intelligible
**War-Chief:** Aggressive shouting, constant intensity
**Prime Cynic:** Clinical, precise, cold
**Valkyrie:** Operatic clean vocals mixed with growls
**The Watcher:** So slow and deep it's barely speech
**Opeth:** Shifts between clean and harsh (like the band)
**Horsemen:** Inhuman, resonant, cosmic horror

## Cinematic Moments

**Session 1:** First view of The Wastes, scope of devastation
**Session 11:** War's Arena, violence reaching peak then breaking
**Session 14:** Death's philosophical argument about endings
**Session 16:** Whatever ending—show transformation or conclusion beautifully
