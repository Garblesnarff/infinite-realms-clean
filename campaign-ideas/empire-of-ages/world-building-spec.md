**Empire of Ages**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The party leads a civilization from Dark Age through Feudal, Castle, and Imperial Ages, managing resources, training armies, and conquering rivals. They balance economic development with military expansion, advance through technological ages, and compete with unique civilizations for dominance through conquest, wonder construction, or trade supremacy.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the founding of each major civilization and their cultural backgrounds.
    *   Write the history of the Crusades analog—religious wars shaping alliances.
    *   Describe the Mongol Invasion that swept across the continent.
    *   Explain the development of gunpowder and its revolution of warfare.
    *   Detail the Wonder-building tradition and legendary structures' cultural significance.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **British** (Major): Longbow civilization, strong economy, defensive bonuses.
    *   **Mongols** (Major): Fast cavalry, mobile tactics, raiding focus.
    *   **Franks** (Major): Powerful knights, strong cavalry, castle bonuses.
    *   **Saracens** (Major): Balanced civilization, strong siege and cavalry archers.
    *   **Teutons** (Major): Heavily armored units, slow but powerful.
    *   **Vikings** (Minor): Naval supremacy, infantry focus, raiding culture.
    *   **Chinese** (Minor): Gunpowder focus, rapid economy, unique units.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Khan Temujin (Mongol conqueror), King Richard (British monarch), Sultan Saladin (Saracen leader), Emperor Frederick (Teutonic emperor), The Merchant Prince (Venetian trader).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Villager" (resource gatherer), "Knight" (heavy cavalry), "Longbowman" (ranged specialist), "Monk" (converter), "Trebuchet" (siege weapon), "Trade Cart" (merchant).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The Capital:** Party's primary settlement with Town Center, multiple resource gathering sites, markets, universities, wonder construction site, castle defenses.
*   **Resource Regions:** Forests with lumber camps, mountains with mines (gold/stone), fertile plains with farms, rivers with fish traps.
*   **Castle Fortresses:** Strategic strongholds controlling provinces, heavily fortified with walls and defensive towers.
*   **Wonder Sites:** Locations where great wonders can be constructed—cultural masterpieces granting victory.
*   **The Imperial City:** Final enemy capital requiring massive siege to conquer, multiple layers of walls.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Advance to new age → **THEN:** Unlock new buildings/units/technologies, gain strategic advantage over slower civilizations.
*   **IF:** Economy raided → **THEN:** Resource gathering stops, must defend villagers or rebuild, delays advancement.
*   **IF:** Castle captured → **THEN:** Gain strategic control of region, produce unique units, deny enemy production.
*   **IF:** Wonder constructed → **THEN:** Begin countdown to wonder victory, all enemies attempt to destroy it.
*   **IF:** Trade routes established → **THEN:** Passive gold generation, but vulnerable to raids.
*   **IF:** All relics controlled → **THEN:** Monks generate gold, spiritual victory possible.
*   **IF:** Coalition forms against party → **THEN:** Must fight multiple civilizations simultaneously or break alliance through diplomacy.
*   **IF:** Final capital destroyed → **THEN:** Enemy civilization eliminated, conquest victory achieved.
