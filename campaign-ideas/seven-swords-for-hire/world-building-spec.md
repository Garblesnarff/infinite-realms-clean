### **World-Building Specification Brief**

**Seven Swords for Hire**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** Seven gunslingers accept near-zero payment to defend Mexican village from forty bandits, seeking redemption more than gold. Through training farmers, building defenses, and ultimate last stand, they discover that protecting the defenseless redeems past sins and gives meaning to wasted lives.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the post-Civil War frontier—displaced soldiers turned outlaws, lawless territories.
    *   Write the history of Mexican villages raided by bandits—cycle of violence.
    *   Describe gunslinger culture—code of the west, violence as profession.
    *   Explain why farmers need help—can't fight bandits, government provides no protection.
    *   Chronicle similar "seven defenders" legends—historical precedents.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions:** The Seven (Major), Village Farmers (Major), Calvera's Bandits (Major), Frontier Towns (Minor), Mexican Federales (Minor, absent)

**4. Causality Chains**
*   **IF:** Seven train farmers → **THEN:** Farmers gain courage and skill to fight.
*   **IF:** First raid repelled → **THEN:** Villagers believe they can win.
*   **IF:** Seven bond with villagers → **THEN:** Personal stakes make sacrifice meaningful.
*   **IF:** Party members die heroically → **THEN:** Inspire others, remembered as legends.
*   **IF:** Village defended → **THEN:** Farmers keep freedom, seven find redemption.
