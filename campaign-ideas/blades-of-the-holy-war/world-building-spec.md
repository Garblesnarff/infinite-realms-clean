**Blades of the Holy War**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The players are crusaders who answered the Pope's call to reclaim the Holy Land, finding themselves in a world where religious conviction clashes with political reality, where "infidels" prove more honorable than fellow Christians, and where the noble goal of pilgrimage transforms into conquest and atrocity. The campaign explores religious warfare from both sides, the tragedy of cultures that might have coexisted instead warring, and whether any war can truly be holy.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail Pope Urban II's call for crusade and the religious fervor that swept Europe.
    *   Write about Islamic civilization in the Holy Land—sophisticated, learned, diverse.
    *   Describe the siege warfare tactics and brutal realities of medieval holy war.
    *   Explain Saladin's unification of Muslim forces and his legendary chivalry.
    *   Detail crusader kingdoms attempting to govern the Holy Land.
    *   Write about moments of cultural exchange and mutual recognition between crusaders and Muslims.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Crusaders** (Major—Player Faction): Goals—Reclaim Holy Land, serve God, seek glory. Ranging from genuine pilgrims to cynical conquerors.
    *   **The Saracens** (Major): Goals—Defend homeland, preserve Islamic rule. Led by honorable Saladin and diverse Muslim powers.
    *   **Local Christians** (Minor): Goals—Survive between warring powers. Caught between European crusaders and Muslim rulers.
    *   **The Kingdom of Jerusalem** (Emerging): Goals—Maintain crusader states. Requiring governance not just conquest.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Godfrey of Bouillon, Saladin, Bishop Adhemar, Lady Eleanor
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Crusader Knight", "Saracen Warrior", "Pilgrim", "Muslim Scholar", "Siege Survivor", "Diplomatic Envoy"

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Jerusalem:** Holy city sacred to all three faiths, siege site, moral epicenter.
*   **Antioch:** Where crusaders learn siege warfare's brutality.
*   **Saladin's Court:** Where players witness Islamic civilization's sophistication.
*   **The Road:** Pilgrimage route becoming military campaign path.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Triggers:** IF players commit atrocities THEN moral weight increases. IF players show honor to enemies THEN mutual respect develops. IF cultural understanding grows THEN alternative diplomatic solutions emerge.
