# Kingsmen of the Crystal Crown - World-Building Specification

## The Kingsmen Order

**Founded**: 400 years ago by knights who foresaw need for secret protectors
**Original Purpose**: Serve realm without glory, politics, or profit
**Modern Mission**: Protect world from threats governments can't acknowledge
**Motto**: "Manners Maketh Man"
**Structure**: Cell-based, each Kingsman independent operator with support

### Kingsmen Titles (Round Table Theme)
- Galahad (field leader)
- Lancelot (combat specialist)
- Merlin (tech/magic specialist)
- Arthur (director)
- Percival, Gawain, Tristan, Kay, Bedivere, etc.
- Title passes to successor when previous holder dies/retires

### Selection Criteria
- Character over background
- Commoners welcomed (scandal to aristocracy)
- Test: Physical, mental, moral (the drowned dog test)
- Many candidates, few selected
- Failure means memory wipe and return home

### Training Regimen
- Combat: All styles, improvised weapons
- Social: High society protocols, diplomacy
- Technical: Modern magic-tech, hacking, engineering
- Mental: Strategy, psychology, languages
- Moral: Philosophy, ethics, judgment

### Equipment (Q-Branch)
Each Kingsman receives bespoke items:
- **Umbrella**: Bulletproof shield, stunner, grappling hook, hidden blade
- **Glasses**: Magical HUD, recording, AR displays, night vision
- **Signet Ring**: Emergency beacon, tracking, poison dart
- **Watch**: Timer, garrote wire, small explosive, tools
- **Shoes**: Poisoned blade in toe, magnetic soles, rocket propulsion
- **Suit**: Armored weave, temperature controlled, always looks perfect

### Tailor Shop Front
- Legitimate business on Savile Row
- Known for finest bespoke tailoring
- Royal family customers (unaware of secret)
- Secret entrance through fitting room
- Underground facility rivals any intelligence agency

## Valentine's Conspiracy

**Richmond Valentine's Background**:
- Tech billionaire, made fortune on crystal communication
- Witnessed environmental destruction, became radicalized
- Believes humanity is virus, planet is host
- Solution: Cull population to sustainable levels
- Recruit elite to survive, let masses kill each other

**The Crystal Network**:
- Free global communication devices
- Actually psychic control crystals
- Activation signal triggers rage response
- Victims lose inhibition, become violent
- Duration: Until deactivated or victim dies
- Scale: Distributed to billions

**The Selection**:
- Elite invited to survive (politicians, scientists, artists)
- Given implant protecting from signal
- Promised seat in new world
- Many accept (including Kingsmen traitor)
- Moral test: who deserves to live?

**Motivation Analysis**:
- Valentine genuinely trying to save planet
- Utilitarian calculus: billions die now vs. extinction later
- Climate change made him desperate
- Can't be reasoned with (already did math)
- Sympathetic but must be stopped

## The Golden Circle

**Poppy Adams' Empire**:
- Controls 90% of illegal drug trade
- Hidden jungle kingdom in Cambodia
- 1950s Americana aesthetic (nostalgia for "better" time)
- Wants legitimacy and amnesty

**The Master Plan**:
- Poison all drugs in circulation
- Antidote exists, she controls it
- Demand: Legalize drugs, grant amnesty, pay ransom
- If refused: Millions of users die
- Hostages include celebrities, politicians' kids

**Golden Circle Members**:
- International criminals wanting legitimacy
- Tech company provides distribution network
- Pharmaceutical company produces poison/antidote
- Politicians on payroll
- Legitimate business fronts

**Poppy's Methodology**:
- Cheerful '50s housewife persona
- Beauty salon aesthetic in jungle
- Robotic attack dogs
- Mincer (meat grinder execution)
- Perverse morality (drug users deserve death)

## The Statesman

**American Counterparts to Kingsmen**:
- Founded by American revolutionaries
- Bourbon distillery front (Kentucky)
- Western/cowboy aesthetic vs. British gentleman
- Similar mission, different style
- Code names from alcohol (Whiskey, Tequila, Champagne)

**Equipment Differences**:
- Whips instead of umbrellas
- Lassos, revolvers, cowboy weapons modernized
- Stetson hats (armored, tracking, throwing weapon)
- Boots (similar to Kingsmen shoes)
- Briefcases (transform into weapons platforms)

**Cultural Contrast**:
- Informal vs. formal
- Bourbon vs. tea
- Cowboys vs. gentlemen
- Effectiveness equally respected
- Partnership highlights different approaches to same mission

## Kingsmen History and Lore

**Notable Operations**:
- Prevented three world wars
- Stopped four assassination attempts on royalty
- Dismantled dozens of terrorist organizations
- Public never knows (by design)

**Fallen Kingsmen**:
- Memorial wall in headquarters
- Each codename represents sacrifice
- Stories passed to recruits
- Legacy weighs on successors

**The Old Guard vs. New Blood**:
- Traditionalists believe in aristocratic leadership
- Reformers believe character over birth
- Chester represents old prejudice
- Modern Kingsmen represent merit

**Relationship to Crown**:
- Founded by knights, still loyal
- Protect realm, not just government
- Independent funding (untraceable wealth)
- Deny existence if exposed

## Training Details

**The Drowned Dog Test**:
- Each candidate given puppy at start
- Bond throughout training
- Final test: Shoot dog or fail
- Actually blank, tests moral character
- Those who refuse valued (compassion under orders)
- Those who shoot rejected (blind obedience)
- Reveals true nature under pressure

**The Parachute Jump**:
- Candidates jump without knowing one chute faulty
- Tests courage and trust
- Actual failsafe (magical arrest)
- Survivor guilt used for growth
- Bonds team through shared trauma

**Black Tie Infiltration**:
- Must infiltrate high society event
- Perfect etiquette required
- One mistake blows cover
- Tests social skills and composure
- Commoners must learn aristocratic ways

## The Gentleman's Code

**Core Principles**:
1. Protect the innocent
2. Serve without seeking glory
3. Kill when necessary, never when not
4. Maintain dignity in all situations
5. Respect opponents (even while defeating them)
6. Never abuse power
7. Look good doing it

**Interpretation**:
- Different Kingsmen emphasize different aspects
- No absolute right way
- Judgment call in field
- Must defend choices to peers
- Code guides, doesn't dictate

## Future of the Order

**Post-Campaign Questions**:
- Rebuild with new values or old?
- Public exposure vs. continued secrecy?
- International expansion (more Statesmen partnerships)?
- New threats requiring evolution?
- Next generation of leadership?

**Player Choices Shape**:
- What traditions to keep
- What to change
- How to recruit
- What missions to take
- Legacy they leave

This world-building supports campaign about class, style, violence, and what it means to be a gentleman spy in modern world.
