# The Cube Realm Chronicles

*A Dimensional Survival campaign — Long Campaign (15+ sessions) — Medium-High difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Cube Realm Chronicles
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Dimensional Survival / Crafting / Exploration / Boss Battles
**Estimated Length:** Long Campaign — 15+ sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **15**
**Tone (keywords):** creative, exploratory, resource-driven, escalating danger, dimensional horror
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party awakens in the Cube Realm, a strange dimension where everything exists in perfect geometric forms and reality itself can be reshaped by those who understand its laws. To survive and escape, they must master the art of material harvesting and crafting, delve into progressively dangerous dimensional layers (the Nether-Below and the Void-Beyond), and ultimately face the Ender Tyrant, an ancient dragon that guards the only portal home. The world operates on strange rules: night brings terrible creatures, resources must be gathered and transmuted, and the deeper you delve, the more reality itself begins to unravel.

---

## Story Arc (Long Campaign — 15+ sessions)

A journey from survival to mastery across three dimensional planes.

*   **Act I — The Overworld (Levels 3–7):** The party learns to survive in the Cube Realm. They must gather resources, build shelter, craft better tools and weapons, and establish a base. They discover ancient temples with clues about the realm's nature and encounter their first Dimensional Rifts, unstable tears in reality that spawn hostile creatures. The act culminates in discovering the portal to the Nether-Below.
*   **Act II — The Nether-Below (Levels 8–12):** A hellish dimension of fire and obsidian, the Nether-Below is home to flame-wreathed demons and rivers of magma. The party must brave this hostile realm to gather rare materials needed to craft the Eyes of Ending, artifacts required to locate and open the final portal. They must also construct a network of fortified waypoints to survive the journey and face the Blaze Sovereign, a powerful fire elemental that guards the Nether Fortress.
*   **Act III — The Void-Beyond (Levels 13–15):** Having crafted the Eyes of Ending, the party locates the Stronghold and activates the End Portal. They enter the Void-Beyond, a surreal dimension of floating islands suspended over infinite darkness. Here, they face the Ender Tyrant and its army of void-touched guardians. Defeating the dragon is the only way to claim the Egg of Returning and open a portal back to their home plane.

## Session-by-Session Outline (Example for Act I)

### Session 1 — "First Night"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Survival Basics
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The party awakens scattered across a strange, blocky landscape with no memory of how they arrived. They have nothing but the clothes on their backs.
    *   They must quickly learn the strange laws of this realm: trees yield perfect wooden cubes, stone can be shaped with crude tools, and night brings monsters.
    *   A skill challenge to gather basic resources (wood, stone, food) before nightfall. If they fail, they face a deadly first night fighting zombies, skeletons, and creepers (explosive undead) with minimal gear.
**Escalation:** One party member is separated and trapped in a cave system. The others must decide whether to rescue them in the dark or wait until dawn.

### Sessions 2-4 — "Building the Foundation"
*   **Focus:** Base Building / Crafting System Introduction
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The party establishes a base camp. They must assign tasks: gathering food, mining for minerals, constructing fortifications, and crafting better equipment.
    *   They discover the Crafting Altars, magical structures that allow them to transmute raw materials into tools, weapons, armor, and even magical items following specific patterns.
    *   They explore the surrounding biomes (forests, mountains, deserts) and encounter other survivors—some friendly, some hostile. They learn of the Villagers, strange cube-headed merchants who trade in rare goods.
**Escalation:** A Raid. A war party of Pillagers (marauding bandits native to the Cube Realm) attacks their base, attempting to steal their resources and destroy their Crafting Altars.

### Sessions 5-7 — "Secrets of the Overworld"
*   **Focus:** Exploration / Lore Discovery
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The party discovers an Ancient Temple, a massive structure filled with traps, puzzles, and treasure. Inside, they find murals depicting the history of the Cube Realm and warnings about the Ender Tyrant.
    *   They learn they are not the first to be trapped here. The temple holds the journal of a previous adventurer who learned of the Nether-Below and the Eyes of Ending.
    *   They encounter their first Dimensional Rift, a portal that spawns waves of monsters. Closing it requires finding and destroying a Rift Crystal hidden nearby.
**Escalation:** The party discovers a Nether Portal frame in the depths of a cavern. To activate it, they need obsidian, a rare material only found deep underground near lava lakes. The mining expedition is dangerous, with cave-ins, lava flows, and hostile mobs.

## Major NPCs

1.  **The Wandering Trader** — *The Mysterious Merchant*
    *   **Role:** A enigmatic figure who appears randomly near the party's base, offering rare trades.
    *   **Personality:** Cheerful and cryptic. Speaks in riddles and seems to know far more about the Cube Realm than he lets on. Always accompanied by two llamas that spit at hostile mobs.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He is cursed to wander the Cube Realm eternally, never able to leave. He trades because he is desperately lonely and seeks connection, even if fleeting.

2.  **Elder Villager Hroon** — *The Sage of the Cube*
    *   **Role:** The leader of a hidden village, an expert on the Cube Realm's laws and history.
    *   **Personality:** Wise, cautious, and protective of his people. He speaks in simple, halting Common due to the linguistic differences. He is willing to help those who prove themselves allies.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He knows the truth: the Cube Realm is a prison dimension, created by ancient mages to contain the Ender Tyrant. Every "adventurer" who arrives is another soul trapped by the dimensional barrier, and he fears there may be no escape.

3.  **The Piglin Warlord Grakk** — *Lord of the Nether-Below*
    *   **Role:** The brutal ruler of a Piglin warband in the Nether-Below. He can be an enemy, an uneasy ally, or a trading partner.
    *   **Personality:** Greedy, pragmatic, and obsessed with gold. Piglins are intelligent, fire-resistant humanoids who will attack intruders on sight unless they wear gold armor. Grakk respects strength and wealth.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He seeks to overthrow the Blaze Sovereign and claim the Nether Fortress for himself. He will make deals with anyone who can help him achieve this goal, but his loyalty lasts only as long as the gold flows.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Party's Base Camp:** A fortified structure built by the players, evolving from a simple wooden shack to a multi-level fortress with farms, mines, and workshops.
2.  **The Ancient Temple:** A jungle temple filled with traps and lore about the Cube Realm's origins.
3.  **The Nether Fortress:** A massive, obsidian stronghold in the Nether-Below, home to Blaze Sovereigns and wither skeletons.
4.  **The Stronghold:** A labyrinthine underground structure in the Overworld that houses the End Portal.
5.  **The Void-Beyond:** A surreal dimension of floating islands and obsidian pillars, home to the Ender Tyrant.

## Central Conflict

This campaign is about survival, adaptation, and the drive to escape an alien dimension. It captures the core loop of *Minecraft*: gather, craft, build, explore, and conquer. The central conflict is the party's struggle against an environment that is both hostile and malleable, where every resource gathered brings them closer to freedom, but also deeper into danger. The Ender Tyrant represents the ultimate challenge, a creature so powerful it required an entire dimension to imprison it. The party must master the laws of the Cube Realm to have any hope of returning home.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **The Crafting System**
*   The campaign uses a simplified crafting system inspired by Minecraft's recipes. Players gather raw materials (wood, stone, iron, gold, diamonds, obsidian, etc.) and use Crafting Altars to create items.
*   Each item requires a specific "pattern" of materials. The DM provides recipes as loot, from NPCs, or as rewards for exploration.
*   Crafted items include mundane tools and weapons, magical armor with unique properties (e.g., diamond armor grants resistance to slashing damage), and consumable items like potions and enchanted food.
*   *Example Recipe: Diamond Sword = 2 Diamonds + 1 Stick. Grants +2 to hit and deals 2d6 slashing damage.*

### 2. **Resource Gathering & Base Building**
*   Downtime between adventures is spent gathering resources, improving the base, and crafting. The DM can use skill challenges or simplified rolls.
*   The base provides tangible benefits: a well-built farm ensures the party never goes hungry, fortifications make it a safe haven, and workshops allow for advanced crafting.
*   The base can be attacked, creating high-stakes defensive encounters where the party must protect what they have built.

### 3. **Dimensional Layers**
*   The Cube Realm is divided into three distinct dimensions, each with unique resources, dangers, and rules:
    *   **Overworld:** The baseline. Monsters spawn at night. Resources are plentiful but basic.
    *   **Nether-Below:** Always hostile. Fire and lava are constant threats. Rare materials like Nether Quartz and Blaze Rods are found here. Water evaporates instantly, forcing creative problem-solving.
    *   **Void-Beyond:** Gravity is strange, and the environment is sparse. Ender Tyrant and its guardians teleport unpredictably. The only goal here is to defeat the dragon and escape.
*   Travel between dimensions is only possible through specific portals that must be constructed and activated.

### 4. **The Night Cycle**
*   Each day in the Cube Realm, there is a chance for a "monster night" where undead and hostile creatures spawn in greater numbers. The party must be in a well-lit, fortified location or face overwhelming odds.
*   This creates a rhythm to the campaign: explore and gather during the day, fortify and craft at night.
