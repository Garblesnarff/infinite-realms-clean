# Threads of Fate - World-Building Spec

## Ravenmoor Across Time

### Past (1647) - The Founding
**Population:** 300, newly established
**Technology:** Medieval fantasy
**Key Event:** Town founded on mass grave from plague
**Secret:** Gustav made pact with temporal entity
**Atmosphere:** Hopeful but hiding darkness

### Present (1847) - The Mystery
**Population:** 2,000, established town
**Technology:** Industrial revolution fantasy
**Key Event:** Child disappearances begin again
**Secret:** Scientific study of temporal phenomena
**Atmosphere:** Ordinary hiding extraordinary

### Future (2047) - The Aftermath
**Population:** 50 survivors in ruins
**Technology:** Post-apocalyptic scavenging
**Key Event:** Catastrophe destroyed everything
**Secret:** Cult attempting to reverse time
**Atmosphere:** Desperate hope in despair

## The Waldmann Family Tree

### Generation 1 (Past)
**Gustav Waldmann:** Founder, made original pact
**Marta Waldmann:** Wife, knew truth
**Children:** Three, one became Stranger

### Generation 5 (Present)
**Elena Waldmann:** Descendant, investigator
**Claudia Waldmann:** Elena's mother (paradox)
**Extended family:** Multiple branches

### Generation 10 (Future)
**Marcus Waldmann:** Last survivor
**Dead relatives:** Victims of catastrophe
**Claudia's presence:** Temporal duplicate

## The Temporal Cave System

### Physical Properties
- Natural cave beneath town
- Temporal energy concentrations
- Three distinct sections corresponding to eras
- Center chamber connects all three
- Dangerous to navigate without knowledge

### Temporal Mechanics
- Walk in from Present, might exit in Past or Future
- Time flows differently inside
- Missing children disappeared here
- Entity dwelling in depths
- Gateway between worlds

### The Entity
- Ancient being predating human time
- Gustav's pact was with this
- Feeds on temporal paradoxes
- Growing stronger each cycle
- Must be confronted or appeased

## The Cycle Explained

### 200-Year Pattern
- **Year 1:** Town founded on mass grave
- **Year 200:** Child disappearances begin
- **Year 400:** Catastrophic event
- **Year 401:** Reset to Year 1 for those aware
- Endless repetition

### What Changes
- Minor details, names, specific events
- Core pattern remains
- Party's incarnations different but similar
- Learning persists subconsciously

### What's Constant
- Waldmann family central
- Cave's existence
- The Stranger's presence
- Tragedy's occurrence
- Cycle continues

### Breaking Conditions
- All three eras must be addressed simultaneously
- Sacrifice in each timeline
- Pact must be renegotiated or broken
- Entity must be defeated or satisfied
- Extreme difficulty

## The Missing Children

### Pattern
- Five children vanish each cycle
- Always at age 10
- Disappear into cave
- Never found (in their era)
- Emerge in different timeline

### Truth
- Children transformed by entity
- Become temporal beings
- One becomes the Stranger
- Others merge into entity
- Tragic fate

### Rescue Possibility
- Can be saved but requires perfect coordination
- All three eras must prevent simultaneously
- Breaking this link weakens cycle
- But creates new paradoxes
- Difficult moral choice

## The Stranger's Identity

### Reveal
- Child taken in first cycle
- Transformed into temporal observer
- Stuck in loop for thousands of iterations
- Trying to help but bound by rules
- May be party member's descendant

### Motivation
- Desperate to break cycle
- Seen too many failures
- Guides party subtly
- Cannot directly interfere
- Tragic figure seeking peace

### Resolution Options
- **Freed:** Breaking cycle releases them
- **Sacrificed:** Their death needed to end it
- **Accepted:** Chooses to continue as guardian
- **Replaced:** Party member takes their place

## Interconnected Mysteries

### Mystery 1: Gustav's Pact
- **Past Investigation:** Discover what deal was made
- **Present Evidence:** Documents hidden in house
- **Future Knowledge:** Survivors know the truth
- **Solution:** All three pieces needed to understand

### Mystery 2: The Catastrophe
- **Past Seeds:** Events that lead to disaster
- **Present Acceleration:** Actions making it worse
- **Future Experience:** Witness the result
- **Prevention:** Requires changing Past without destroying Present

### Mystery 3: Claudia's Paradox
- **Past:** She's there but shouldn't be
- **Present:** Her "normal" timeline
- **Future:** Also present, same age
- **Explanation:** Trapped in temporal loop herself

### Mystery 4: Party's Past Lives
- **Past:** Meet your previous incarnations
- **Present:** Subconscious knowledge
- **Future:** Descendants or reincarnations
- **Revelation:** You've always been part of this

## Philosophical Questions

**Can fate be changed?** If cycle always happens, is free will illusion?

**Is suffering meaningful?** If it repeats forever, does it matter?

**Identity across time:** Are you the same person as past incarnation?

**Sacrifice ethics:** Is it right to erase era to save another?

**Acceptance vs. Struggle:** Better to make peace with loop or fight endlessly?

## Victory Scenario Details

### Perfect Resolution
- Break cycle completely
- Save all three eras
- Free the Stranger
- Rescue children
- End entity's feeding
- Waldmann family freed from curse
- (Nearly impossible)

### Acceptable Resolution
- Weaken cycle significantly
- Save Present, improve Future
- Accept Past as fixed
- Some sacrifices necessary
- Entity contained not destroyed
- Waldmann curse reduced

### Tragic Resolution
- Cycle continues
- Party becomes aware participants
- Slight improvements each iteration
- Eternal struggle
- Hope in repetition
- Philosophical acceptance

These interconnected mysteries create deep, rewarding campaign focused on investigation and revelation.
