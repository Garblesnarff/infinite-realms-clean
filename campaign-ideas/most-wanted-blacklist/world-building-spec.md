### **Template: World-Building Specification Brief**

**Most Wanted Blacklist**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** In Rockport City's underground scene, the Blacklist ranks fifteen elite street racers. After being betrayed by Razor who stole their car and framed them, the party must climb from #15 to #1, defeating each racer while evading escalating police. They'll uncover conspiracy between Razor and corrupt police captain, reclaim their legendary vehicle, and choose between redemption through justice or eternal infamy through escape.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail Rockport City's evolution from industrial center to street racing capital.
    *   Write the history of the Blacklist system and how it became the underground hierarchy.
    *   Describe the party's original legendary vehicle and its reputation before Razor stole it.
    *   Explain how Captain Daniels became corrupt and formed alliance with Razor.
    *   Detail Sergeant Cross's history and why he respects skill while enforcing law.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Blacklist** (Major): Fifteen ranked street racers, each controlling territory and racing specialty.
    *   **Rockport Police** (Major): Law enforcement with escalating tactics, split between honest cops (Cross) and corrupt (Daniels).
    *   **Underground Racing Scene** (Major): Broader street racing culture, mechanics, spotters, spectators.
    *   **The Corrupt Network** (Minor): Daniels' conspiracy involving betting rings and race fixing.
    *   **Legitimate Racing World** (Minor): Legal track racing that some view as the honorable path.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Razor (betrayer #1), Sergeant Cross (honorable cop), Mia (informant), Captain Daniels (corrupt boss), Jewels (honorable rival #5).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Blacklist Racer" (#15-#2 various specialties), "Police Unit" (patrol to federal), "Mechanic" (tuning support), "Street Racer" (general scene), "Corrupt Cop" (Daniels' enforcers).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Rosewood District:** Downtown urban area with complex road networks, technical racing, many pursuit breakers.
*   **Camden Beach:** Waterfront industrial zone with long straightaways, drag racing, shipping docks.
*   **Rockport Hills:** Wealthy area with winding roads through mansions, drift territory, scenic but dangerous.
*   **Heritage Heights:** Historic district with tight streets, ambush-prone for police, cultural landmarks.
*   **County Line:** Rockport boundary where ultimate escape attempt occurs, highway chase to freedom.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Heat level increases → **THEN:** More aggressive police units deployed, higher-tier tactics, harder escapes required.
*   **IF:** Blacklist racer defeated → **THEN:** Their territory unlocked, new races available, police attention increases.
*   **IF:** Evidence gathered against Daniels → **THEN:** Cross becomes more helpful, amnesty ending possible.
*   **IF:** Razor challenged before ready → **THEN:** Sabotage more severe, police interference, higher difficulty.
*   **IF:** Cross betrayed or helped → **THEN:** Determines final confrontation ally/enemy status.

**7. Technology System & Power Dynamics**
*   **Vehicle Modification:** Extensive tuning system affecting performance—engine, transmission, suspension, tires.
*   **Visual Customization:** Paint, vinyls, body kits represent identity and culture in scene.
*   **Police Technology:** Escalating from standard cruisers to SUVs, corvettes, helicopters with thermal imaging.
*   **Pursuit Breakers:** Environmental traps—gas stations, scaffolding, water towers—tactically triggered.
*   **Radio Networks:** Both racers and police use communications, can be intercepted or jammed.

**8. Economy & Resources**
*   **Reputation:** Primary currency earned through races, milestones, escapes, unlocks challenges.
*   **Cash Rewards:** Prize money from races purchases modifications and visual customization.
*   **Pink Slips:** Defeating Blacklist members grants their vehicles as trophies.
*   **Impound System:** Police impound cars during arrests, must pay or lose forever, creates risk.
*   **Evidence Value:** Corruption evidence is currency with Cross for amnesty and assistance.

**9. Conflict Escalation Framework**
*   **Early Game:** Basic racing, low-heat police, learning mechanics, challenging lower Blacklist.
*   **Mid Game:** Elite racers, high-heat pursuits, corruption discovered, Cross becomes ally.
*   **Late Game:** Top Blacklist challenges, all-out police warfare, Razor confrontation, escape or surrender choice.

**10. Cultural Details & Flavor**
*   **Tuner Culture:** Cars are identity, modification is art, underground respect earned through skill and style.
*   **Racing Hierarchy:** Blacklist creates clear pecking order, challenging up requires reputation proof.
*   **Police Reputation:** Some cops respected (Cross) for honor, others hated (Daniels) for corruption.
*   **Pink Slip Racing:** Betting your vehicle adds ultimate stakes to races, honored tradition.
*   **Escape Legend:** Those who successfully escape Rockport become permanent legends in scene.
