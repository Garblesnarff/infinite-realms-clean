# Steam War Chronicles - World-Building Specification

## Alternate Historical Timeline

**1776-1865**: American Revolution and early industrial revolution proceed normally.

**1859**: Ray Steam's grandfather Alfred discovers revolutionary compact steam compression technique while working in Manchester mills.

**1861**: American Civil War begins but doesn't follow historical course. Both sides rapidly industrialize using emerging steam technology.

**1863**: Edward Steam (Alfred's son) perfects mathematical principles behind steam compression, creates first prototype Steam Ball.

**1865**: Ray Steam born. Raised between grandfather's workshop (labor-focused) and father's laboratory (pure research).

**1866 (Campaign Start)**: Ray creates perfected Steam Ball. Multiple factions compete for control. Civil War still raging. Victorian Britain at technological crossroads.

## The Steam Ball Technology

**What It Is**: Ultra-compact steam engine storing tremendous pressure in spherical container. Single ball = power plant in your hand.

**How It Works**:
- Multi-layered compression chamber
- Self-sustaining pressure loop
- Heat recycling preventing energy loss
- Modularâ€”can chain multiple balls
- Safe until deliberately destabilized

**Applications**:
- Power source for anything (vehicles, weapons, cities)
- Propulsion (dirigibles, steam knights)
- Direct energy weapon (pressure release as blast)
- Environmental alteration (atmospheric engine)

**Dangers**:
- Explosion if damaged (city-leveling)
- Requires precise engineering (accidents common)
- Environmental pollution from mass production
- Arms race as nations seek parity
- Enables total war

**Replication**:
- Original blueprints in Ray's head
- Father and grandfather have incomplete versions
- O'Hara Foundation trying to reverse-engineer
- Foreign powers attempting to steal or buy

## Faction Details

### O'Hara Foundation (Capitalist)

**Leadership**: Scarlett O'Hara, board of directors.

**Philosophy**: Technology should serve those who invest in it. Free market determines use. Profit motive drives innovation.

**Resources**:
- Enormous capital
- Steam Castle (mobile headquarters)
- Private army
- Corporate espionage network
- Political influence

**Goals**:
- Patent and monetize Steam Ball technology
- Sell to all sides in conflicts (perpetual war = perpetual profit)
- Establish global corporate state
- Eliminate competition (unions, governments, other corporations)

**Methods**:
- Legal manipulation
- Buyouts and hostile takeovers
- Assassination when needed
- Propaganda campaigns
- Corruption

**Weaknesses**: Profit-focused. Can be outbid or convinced catastrophe bad for business.

### Steam Union (Socialist)

**Leadership**: Alfred Steam, Emma Barton, workers' council.

**Philosophy**: Workers who build technology should control it. Democratic ownership. No exploitation.

**Resources**:
- Popular support
- Industrial knowledge (workers understand machines)
- Sabotage capabilities
- Underground network
- Moral high ground

**Goals**:
- Seize means of production
- Establish worker-controlled industrial democracy
- Prevent weaponization of Steam Ball
- Improve living/working conditions
- International labor solidarity

**Methods**:
- Strikes and work stoppages
- Sabotage of corporate property
- Direct action and revolution
- Education and organizing
- Mutual aid networks

**Weaknesses**: Idealism. Internal divisions between moderates and radicals. Lack of funding.

### Nation-States (Militarist)

**Major Powers**:
- **British Empire**: Largest, most resources, Victoria's reign
- **Prussian Confederation**: Rapidly industrializing, military focused
- **Russian Empire**: Autocratic, massive but inefficient
- **Confederate States**: Steam-powered slavery, industrial south
- **Union (USA)**: Industrial north, democratic but warmongering
- **French Republic**: Recovering from various revolutions

**Goals**: Military supremacy through steam technology.

**Resources**: Standing armies, legal authority, industrial capacity.

**Methods**: Formal warfare, treaties, espionage, conscription.

**Weaknesses**: Bureaucracy, slow to innovate, competing interests.

## Technology Detailed

### Steam Knights (Powered Armor)

**Types**:

**Mark I - Heavy Knight**:
- 10 feet tall, heavily armored
- Coal-fired boiler backpack
- Hydraulic limbs for strength
- Slow but devastating
- Working-class fighter (affordable to build)

**Mark II - Light Knight**:
- 7 feet tall, minimal armor
- Pressure-powered agility
- Melee weapons integrated
- Fast, hit-and-run
- Aristocratic duelists prefer

**Mark III - Aerial Knight**:
- Steam jet propulsion
- Wings (ornamental and functional)
- Ranged weapons
- Expensive, elite units
- Short flight time

**Mark IV - Engineer Knight**:
- Tool integration
- Repair equipment
- Construction capabilities
- Non-combat support
- Party likely uses

**Custom Builds**: Party can design own variants combining features.

**Limitations**:
- Require trained pilots
- Fuel consumption high
- Maintenance intensive
- Vulnerable to anti-armor weapons
- Overheat in extended combat

### Dirigibles and Airships

**Classifications**:

**Scout Ships**: Small, fast, lightly armed. Reconnaissance and courier.

**Merchant Vessels**: Large cargo capacity, minimal weapons, profit-focused.

**Military Cruisers**: Armed, armored, crew of hundreds.

**Steam Castle**: Unique. City-sized. O'Hara Foundation headquarters. Impossibly large.

**Construction**: Hydrogen envelopes, steam engines, gondola hanging below, weapons mounted.

**Combat**: Naval tactics applied to three dimensions. Altitude advantage crucial.

### Industrial Infrastructure

**Steam Factories**:
- Mass production facilities
- Worker exploitation common
- Pollution extreme
- Child labor widespread
- Dangerous conditions

**Coal Mines**:
- Fuel source for everything
- Worst working conditions
- Environmental devastation
- Frequent disasters

**Refineries**:
- Processing raw materials
- Chemical hazards
- Explosions regular
- Located in poor districts

### Weapons Technology

**Steam Rifles**: Pressure-powered, fast fire rate, limited range.

**Pneumatic Cannons**: Artillery-scale, devastating, slow reload.

**Heat Rays**: Experimental, unreliable, terrifying when they work.

**Pressure Bombs**: Steam Ball weaponization, city-destroyers.

**Atmospheric Engine**: Ultimate weapon. Alters global climate and air composition.

## Geographic Locations Expanded

### London

**The City**: Financial district. Banks funding all sides.

**West End**: Aristocracy. Clean air (fans blow pollution east).

**East End**: Slums. Factory workers. Pollution deadly.

**Thames**: Industrial river. Dead fish, toxic water.

**Tower Bridge**: Being retrofitted with steam mechanisms.

**Hyde Park**: Great Exhibition site. Crystal Palace modified.

**Underground**: Sewer system expanded for steam pipes. Secret travel.

### Manchester

**The Workshop of the World**: Entire city is factory complex.

**Living Conditions**:
- Average life expectancy: 35
- Air visibility: 100 feet in good weather
- River Irwell: "liquid manure"
- Infant mortality: 50%

**Production**: Textiles, machines, weapons, suffering.

**Resistance**: Steam Union strongest here. Regular strikes and violence.

### American Theaters

**Union Territory (North)**:
- Industrial cities (Pittsburgh, Chicago, New York)
- Steam-powered industry
- Free labor but exploitation continues
- Military innovation focused

**Confederate Territory (South)**:
- Steam-powered plantations
- Enslaved people working steam machines (horror)
- Cotton gin meets industrial revolution
- Desperate for foreign recognition

**Battlefields**:
- Scorched earth from steam weapons
- Ironclad land-ships
- Cavalry mounted in steam armor
- Casualties unprecedented

### Arctic Installation

**Location**: Far north, Norwegian territory or unclaimed.

**Purpose**: Atmospheric Engine development and testing.

**Environment**: Extreme cold, constant night (winter), isolated.

**Defenses**: Automated steam guardians, environmental hazards, secrets.

**Mystery**: Who built it? The Engineer? Ancient civilization? Aliens?

## The Engineer's Mystery

**Identity** (DM Choice):
- Ancient human inventor from previous civilization
- Artificial intelligence from dead world
- Time traveler from future trying to prevent apocalypse
- Actual supernatural entity (god of invention)
- Collection of minds in machine

**Knowledge**: Understands steam technology's ultimate potential and danger.

**Role**: Observer testing humanity. Will intervene at climax based on party's choices.

**Location**: Unknown. Communicates through mysterious means (radio, dreams, mechanical proxies).

## Environmental Consequences

### Short-Term (Visible During Campaign)
- Air pollution killing thousands
- Rivers becoming toxic
- Deforestation for coal
- Weather patterns disrupted locally
- Wildlife fleeing or dying

### Medium-Term (5-10 Years)
- Regions becoming uninhabitable
- Food crop failures
- Disease epidemics from pollution
- Acid rain
- Social collapse in worst areas

### Long-Term (Decades)
- Global climate change
- Mass extinction
- Human population decline
- Technological civilization collapse possible
- Or atmospheric engineering provides solution

## Social Classes in Steam Age

**Industrialists**: O'Hara class. Own everything. Live in luxury. Conscience optional.

**Engineers**: Middle class. Educated. Design machines. Caught between labor and capital.

**Skilled Workers**: Union core. Mechanics, metalworkers. Awareness of exploitation.

**Unskilled Labor**: Lowest class. Factory fodder. Desperate and dying.

**Aristocracy**: Declining. Old money can't compete with steam money.

**Military**: Cross-class. Officers aristocratic, enlisted workers.

**Youth**: Ray's generation. Growing up in steam age. Future in their hands.

## The American Civil War Evolution

**Historical Divergence**: 1863, steam technology proliferates.

**Union Developments**:
- Industrial north weaponizes factories
- Steam-powered ironclads (land and sea)
- Railroad artillery
- Repeating firearms (steam-actuated)

**Confederate Innovations**:
- Cotton wealth buys steam tech abroad
- Enslaved engineers forced to innovate
- Guerrilla steam weapons
- Diplomatic efforts to trade cotton for weapons

**Stalemate**: Neither can win decisively. Technology extends war indefinitely.

**International Involvement**: Europeans selling to both sides. Profit over principle.

**Cost**: Million+ dead and rising. Entire generations destroyed.

## Atmospheric Engine Details

**Function**: Uses Steam Ball principles at continental scale to alter atmospheric composition.

**Potential Uses**:

**Beneficial**:
- Remove pollution (reverse industrial damage)
- Increase oxygen (help endangered species)
- Regulate climate (prevent disasters)
- Terraform barren regions

**Harmful**:
- Weaponize (suffocate enemy nations)
- Create dependence (control weather, control world)
- Experiment (unknown consequences)
- Destroy accidentally (most likely outcome)

**Control**: Whoever activates determines settings. Irreversible for decades.

**Location**: Arctic to prevent unauthorized activation. Remote kill switch possibly exists.

## Campaign Moral Dilemmas

**Invention vs. Responsibility**: Should scientists limit discoveries?

**Progress vs. Environment**: Development versus destruction?

**Democracy vs. Efficiency**: Workers control (slow, fair) vs. corporate control (fast, unjust)?

**Youth vs. Experience**: Ray's idealism vs. Alfred's pragmatism vs. Edward's amorality?

**Individual vs. Collective**: Personal glory versus human welfare?

**Revolution vs. Reform**: Burn system down or work within it?

## Ending Scenarios Detailed

### Regulated Future
- International treaty limits steam weapons
- Environmental protections enforced
- Labor rights established
- Technology continues but controlled
- Difficult to achieve, requires all factions compromising

### Union Victory
- Workers seize means of production globally
- Democratic control of technology
- Could become utopia or new tyranny
- Depends on party's guidance

### Corporate Control
- O'Hara Foundation becomes global monopoly
- Efficient but unjust
- Innovation continues, exploitation continues
- Stable dystopia

### Military Supremacy
- One nation claims all steam tech
- Empire rules through force
- Others reduced to vassals
- Perpetual militarized state

### Luddite Ending
- All steam technology destroyed
- Return to pre-industrial life
- Environment recovers
- Human suffering continues, just different

### Atmospheric Salvation
- Engine used to reverse pollution
- Risky but succeeds
- Technology redeems itself
- Optimistic but uncertain

### Apocalypse
- Engine malfunctions or weaponized
- Atmosphere becomes toxic
- Humanity reduced to scattered survivors
- Total failure state

### New World
- Portal to alternate Earth opens
- Humanity evacuates dying world
- Unknown future
- Escape rather than solution

## Youth Empowerment Theme

**Ray and Party**: Teenagers/young adults making world-changing decisions.

**Adult Failures**: Previous generation created mess through greed (O'Hara), ideology (Alfred), or indifference (Edward).

**Responsibility**: Youth inheriting broken world must fix it.

**Idealism**: Young characters can imagine different future. Adults cynical.

**Growth**: Campaign transforms innocent inventors into world leaders or casualties.

**Hope**: Despite darkness, youth represents possibility of change.

## Technology Ethics Framework

**Questions Campaign Raises**:
- Should some knowledge remain undiscovered?
- Who decides how inventions are used?
- Can creators control their creations?
- Is ignorance safer than knowledge?
- Does progress require suffering?
- Can technology solve problems it creates?

**No Easy Answers**: Campaign presents dilemmas without clear solutions. Party must choose and live with consequences.
