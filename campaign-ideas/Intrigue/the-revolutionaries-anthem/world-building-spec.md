### **World-Building Specification Brief: The Revolutionaries' Anthem**

**1. Core Concept:**
Revolutionary outsiders and immigrants fight to forge a new nation through warfare and political debate, navigating personal ambition, competing ideologies, and the question of legacy while literally writing themselves into history.

**2. Lore & History:**
*   Detail the colonial system and taxation that sparked rebellion
*   Explain the political philosophy divide—federalists versus anti-federalists
*   Describe the revolutionary underground network and pamphlet culture
*   Write about the diverse immigrant communities who fuel the revolution
*   Detail dueling culture and codes of honor among gentleman politicians
*   Explain how history is written—who gets remembered and why
*   Describe the tension between revolutionary ideals and practical governance

**3. Factions:**
*   **The Revolutionaries** (Major) - Diverse coalition fighting for independence
*   **The Federalists** (Major) - Favor strong central government, financial systems
*   **The Republicans** (Major) - Favor local power, agrarian values, suspicious of banks
*   **The Loyalists** (Major) - Support the Crown, seen as traitors by revolutionaries
*   **The Immigrants' Society** (Minor) - Mutual aid network for newcomers
*   **The Foreign Powers** (Minor) - Other nations deciding whether to support or exploit

**4. NPCs:**
*   **Tier 1:** Alexander the Brilliant, General Stoneheart, Senator Virtue, Lady Liberty, Lord Chancellor, The Crown's Fury
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Revolutionary Pamphleteer," "Ambitious Captain," "Foreign Diplomat," "Political Rival," "Duellist," "Tavern Keeper / Spy," "Old Guard Noble," "Loyalist Informant"

**5. Locations:**
*   **The Underground Press** - Revolutionary headquarters and printing operation
*   **The Assembly Hall** - Where new government convenes and debates rage
*   **Battlefield of Valley Crossing** - Desperate winter military campaign
*   **The Dueling Grounds** - Where honor is settled and history changes
*   **The New Capital** - Contested symbol of the nation's direction

**6. Causality:**
*   IF party sides with federalists THEN strong government forms but common people feel betrayed
*   IF party sides with republicans THEN states remain powerful but national unity weakens
*   IF party pursues personal ambition over principles THEN negative legacy, remembered as power-hungry
*   IF party member fights a duel THEN political career endangered regardless of outcome
*   IF party writes the founding documents THEN their philosophy shapes the nation for generations
*   IF party fails to maintain alliances THEN revolution fractures and foreign powers intervene
