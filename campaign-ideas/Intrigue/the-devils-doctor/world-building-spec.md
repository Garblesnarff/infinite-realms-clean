### **World-Building Specification Brief: The Devil's Doctor**

**1. Core Concept:**
Party investigates impossible crimes connected to brilliant criminal mastermind opposing colonial powers through elaborate schemes, death traps, and secret societies, forcing them to confront both villain's methods and the legitimate grievances driving him.

**2. Lore & History:**
*   Detail The Doctor's background—brilliant scientist who witnessed colonial atrocities, turned to opposing empire through criminal means
*   Explain the Si-Fan and other secret societies—reimagined as anti-colonial resistance organizations with complex motivations
*   Describe historical context—colonial exploitation, cultural appropriation, economic imperialism
*   Write about ancient knowledge The Doctor seeks—Eastern medical and alchemical traditions
*   Detail Chinatown as actual community with real people, not just "exotic mystery"
*   Explain how legitimate resistance became twisted into villainy

**3. Factions:**
*   **The Doctor's Network** (Major) - Criminal organization with anti-colonial ideology
*   **The Si-Fan** (Major) - Secret society serving The Doctor, complex motivations
*   **Colonial Authorities** (Major) - Police, officials, military—flawed but not wholly evil
*   **Chinatown Community** (Minor) - Ordinary people caught between sides
*   **Kidnapped Scientists** (Minor) - Coerced into serving The Doctor's research
*   **Reform Movement** (Minor) - Those seeking peaceful change to colonial system
*   **Criminal Elements** (Minor) - Opportunists exploiting the conflict

**4. NPCs:**
*   **Tier 1:** The Doctor, Dr. Petrie, Nayland Smith, Karamaneh, Si-Fan Council Members, Professor Jennings
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Si-Fan Agent," "Colonial Inspector," "Chinatown Elder," "Kidnapped Scientist," "Death Trap Keeper," "Reform Advocate," "Criminal Opportunist"

**5. Locations:**
*   **Chinatown District** - Authentic community with temples, businesses, homes
*   **The Doctor's Lairs** - Various locations, each with death traps and laboratories
*   **Thames Warehouses** - Smuggling operations, secret meetings
*   **The Museum** - Repository of stolen artifacts
*   **Colonial Club** - Where imperial officials gather and scheme
*   **Hidden Laboratory** - Where The Doctor conducts research
*   **Si-Fan Meeting Places** - Secret chambers beneath legitimate businesses

**6. Causality:**
*   IF party stops scheme without addressing colonial grievances THEN creates future villains
*   IF Karamaneh's loyalty won THEN gain valuable inside intelligence
*   IF death trap failed THEN The Doctor adapts and next trap is more sophisticated
*   IF Si-Fan exposed completely THEN community suffers backlash from authorities
*   IF ancient formula completed THEN The Doctor gains terrible power
*   IF party acknowledges colonial wrongs THEN may find common ground for resolution

**7. MacGuffins & Artifacts:**
*   **The Elixir Vitae Formula** - Ancient longevity potion, The Doctor's goal
*   **Stolen Scientific Research** - Cutting-edge discoveries
*   **Ancient Medical Texts** - Eastern medical and alchemical knowledge
*   **Exotic Poisons** - Death trap components and assassination tools
*   **Si-Fan Ring** - Mark of secret society membership
*   **Colonial Artifacts** - Stolen treasures representing imperial exploitation

**8. Death Trap Types:**
*   Poison gas chambers, venomous creature pits, crushing walls, needle traps with exotic toxins, alchemical explosive devices, drowning mechanisms, fire traps, elaborate locks and puzzles, trained assassins as living traps

**9. Respectful Portrayal Guidelines:**
*   The Doctor is villain because of methods, not ethnicity
*   Asian cultures portrayed with authenticity and respect
*   Colonial system's actual historical wrongs acknowledged
*   Chinatown is real community, not just setting
*   Si-Fan members have individual personalities and motivations
*   Party should question whether they serve justice or empire
