# The Oracle Fugitives

*A Precrime Thriller / Philosophical Mystery campaign — Medium Campaign (10–12 sessions) — Medium-High difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Oracle Fugitives
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Precrime Thriller / Philosophical Mystery / Fugitive
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 10–12 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **5** → Finish **11–12**
**Tone (keywords):** precognitive, fugitive, philosophical, deterministic, surveillance, noir-thriller
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
In the city of Farsight, crime has been eliminated through the Precrime Division—elite agents guided by three clairvoyant Oracles who foresee murders before they happen. When one party member is named by the Oracles as a future murderer, they must go on the run to prove their innocence while uncovering a conspiracy—the system is being manipulated to eliminate political enemies. As fugitives hunted by their former colleagues, the party must grapple with questions of free will, determinism, and whether a crime foreseen is a crime at all.

---

## What "Medium-High" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Encounters:** Evading a surveillance state, parkour chases, social stealth, and battles against former allies convinced of your guilt.
*   **Investigation:** Proving innocence while hunted, uncovering conspiracy, and understanding the Oracles' true nature.
*   **Consequences:** Every action is predicted by the Oracles. The party must learn to act unpredictably or use the predictions against their pursuers.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 10–12 sessions)

*   **Act I — The Accusation (Sessions 1–4):** A party member is named by the Oracles as a future murderer. They flee, becoming fugitives while searching for evidence of their innocence.
*   **Act II — The Conspiracy (Sessions 5–8):** The party discovers the Precrime system is corrupt—predictions are being manipulated by those in power to eliminate enemies.
*   **Act III — The Choice (Sessions 9–12):** The party must decide whether to destroy the Precrime system entirely or reform it, all while facing the future the Oracles predicted.

---

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Vision"
*   **Hook:** The party member receives a terrifying vision: they will murder someone in 72 hours.
*   **Escalation:** Precrime agents arrive to arrest them before the murder occurs.

### Session 2 — "The Fugitive"
*   **Focus:** Escape and chase through the city.
*   **Escalation:** The party discovers evidence that the predicted murder victim is a political reformer.

### Session 3 — "Into the Underground"
*   **Focus:** Meeting the resistance who oppose Precrime.
*   **Escalation:** The Oracles predict the party's next move. They must learn to act unpredictably.

### Session 4 — "The Oracle Temple"
*   **Focus:** Infiltrating where the Oracles are kept.
*   **Escalation:** Discovery that the Oracles are drugged psychics held against their will.

### Session 5 — "The Creator"
*   **Focus:** Meeting the system's creator who has doubts.
*   **Escalation:** He reveals one of the Oracles foresaw this conversation—and predicted they would help.

### Session 6 — "Minority Report"
*   **Focus:** Finding the suppressed dissenting prediction.
*   **Escalation:** Not all three Oracles agreed—one saw a different future where no murder occurs.

### Session 7 — "The Conspiracy Revealed"
*   **Focus:** Discovering who's manipulating predictions.
*   **Escalation:** It's the Director of Precrime, using the system to eliminate political enemies.

### Session 8 — "The Predicted Murder"
*   **Focus:** The 72-hour deadline arrives.
*   **Escalation:** The party member must choose—kill their "victim" (who attacks them) or prove free will exists by refusing.

### Session 9 — "The Choice"
*   **Focus:** The party member chooses not to kill.
*   **Escalation:** The Oracles' prediction was wrong—free will exists.

### Session 10 — "Exposing the Truth"
*   **Focus:** Broadcasting evidence of corruption.
*   **Escalation:** Public turns against Precrime. The Director initiates emergency protocols.

### Session 11 — "The Temple Siege"
*   **Focus:** Final confrontation at the Oracle Temple.
*   **Escalation:** Freeing the Oracles or destroying the system entirely.

### Session 12 — "After the Oracles"
*   **Focus:** Deciding the future of Farsight—live with crime's return or find a just system.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Chief Anderton** — *The True Believer*
    *   **Role:** The party's former boss, now hunting them, genuinely believes in Precrime.

2.  **Oracle Agatha** — *The Seer*
    *   **Role:** The most powerful Oracle, who may be trying to communicate the truth.

3.  **Director Crow** — *The Corrupt Mastermind*
    *   **Role:** The villain manipulating predictions for political power.

4.  **Dr. Hineman** — *The Creator*
    *   **Role:** The system's inventor, now horrified by what it's become.

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Prediction Evasion**
*   Oracles predict party actions. Players must act unpredictably (random rolls, out-of-character choices).

### 2. **Fugitive Status**
*   Surveillance everywhere. Stealth, disguises, and avoiding patterns are essential.

### 3. **Free Will Moments**
*   Key choices where players can defy predictions earn "Free Will Points" that protect against future visions.

### 4. **The Countdown**
*   Sessions track the 72 hours until the predicted murder.
