### **Template: World-Building Specification Brief**

**The Song of Redemption**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** A former bully, suicidal from guilt, seeks out the deaf girl he tormented to apologize before ending his life. Her grace and forgiveness changes everything. He decides to live and atone by helping her find happiness, gradually healing his self-loathing while falling in love—learning that redemption is possible through genuine connection and that both deserve happiness despite their pain.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the cultural context of bullying in schools and how groups turn on individuals.
    *   Write about disability awareness and the challenges deaf individuals face in a hearing world.
    *   Describe sign language as a complete language with its own beauty and poetry.
    *   Explain the psychological impact of bullying on both victims and reformed bullies.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Elementary School Bullies (Past):** The group that tormented Shoko and then turned on Shoya.
*   **The Present Friend Group:** Attempting to move past their shared trauma, some more genuine than others.
*   **The Support System:** Shoko's family, Nagatsuka, Sahara—people offering genuine connection.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:**
    *   **Shoko Nishimiya:** Deaf cleric, kind and forgiving, self-blaming, suicidal ideation.
    *   **Shoya Ishida:** Former bully, consumed by guilt, suicidal, seeking redemption.
    *   **Tomohiro Nagatsuka:** Loyal friend, simple but perceptive, unconditionally accepting.
    *   **Naoka Ueno:** Unrepentant bully, hostile to Shoko, in love with Shoya.
    *   **Miki Kawai:** Liar about her role in bullying, needs external validation.
    *   **Miyoko Sahara:** Genuine friend who was ostracized.
    *   **Yuzuru Nishimiya:** Shoko's protective sister, tests Shoya's sincerity.

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The Bridge:** Crucial location—where Shoya meets Shoko again, where they feed koi, where the suicide attempt happens.
*   **The Academy:** Present-day setting where relationships develop and past resurfaces.
*   **Shoko's Home:** Where sign language lessons happen, family bonds form.
*   **The Cultural Festival:** Where final confrontations and resolutions occur.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** Shoya performs a genuine act of atonement (helping Shoko or others without self-punishment).
    *   **THEN:** An X mark disappears from someone's face (his guilt lessens). His hearing clears slightly. He makes eye contact more.
    *   **IF:** Shoya learns more sign language and communicates better with Shoko.
    *   **THEN:** Their relationship deepens. Misunderstandings decrease. Emotional intimacy grows.
    *   **IF:** Past bullying is confronted honestly (especially Kawai's denial exposed, Ueno's hostility challenged).
    *   **THEN:** The group fragments but those willing to face truth become closer. Real healing can begin.
    *   **IF:** Shoko attempts suicide and Shoya saves her but is injured.
    *   **THEN:** While Shoya is in coma, all characters face their guilt and feelings. Genuine change occurs. Shoko is devastated, finally understanding she's valued.
    *   **IF:** Both characters confess their love via sign language after Shoya wakes.
    *   **THEN:** The final X marks disappear. Shoya's hearing fully clears. He can look people in the eyes. Generate epilogue: both are healing together, learning to love themselves through loving each other. They deserve happiness. The silent voice has found someone who learned to listen.
