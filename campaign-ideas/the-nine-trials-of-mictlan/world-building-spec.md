# World-Building Specification: The Nine Trials of Mictlan

**1. Core Concept:** Players must survive nine deadly trials through Mictlan's levels over four years, guided by a spirit dog, hunted by death lords, carrying something precious, deciding whether to complete the journey or rebel.

**2. Lore Primer:**
*   Aztec cosmology and the journey of the dead
*   The nine levels of Mictlan and their challenges
*   Mictlantecuhtli and Mictecacihuatl as death deities
*   The Xoloitzcuintli as psychopomp guide
*   Quetzalcoatl's theft of bones from Mictlan
*   Aztec concepts of worthy death and proper burial
*   The relationship between sacrifice, death, and cosmic balance

**3. Factions:**
*   **The Death Lords' Court** - Maintain Mictlan, test souls, hunger for power.
*   **The Xolo Guides** - Spirit dogs bound to help the worthy.
*   **The Trial Guardians** - Failed souls enslaved to administer tests.
*   **Quetzalcoatl's Agents** - Secret helpers offering prohibited aid.
*   **The Trapped Dead** - Souls who failed trials, warning and obstacles.

**4. NPCs:**
*   **Tier 1:** Mictlantecuhtli, Mictecacihuatl, the Xolo Guide, Nine Trial Masters, Quetzalcoatl's Messenger.
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** Various failed souls at different levels, demon guardians, sacred animals, other psychopomps.

**5. Locations:** The nine levels, each with distinct environmental hazards, aesthetic, and challenges.

**6. Mechanics:**
*   Four-year journey tracking
*   Xolo bond system
*   Trial scars accumulation
*   Obsidian resource management
*   Identity and memory mechanics
*   Sacred bundle protection

**7. Themes:** Endurance, worthy death, cosmic order vs. rebellion, the price of life, transformation through suffering, companionship in darkness.
