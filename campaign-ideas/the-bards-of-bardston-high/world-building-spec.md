**The Bards of Bardston High**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise. The tone should be lighthearted, wholesome, and heartwarming, in the style of slice-of-life anime like K-On!.
*   **Content:** The players are students at Bardston High, a magical academy. They've joined the struggling Light Music Club and must save it from disbandment by recruiting members, practicing together, and performing at the Cultural Festival. The campaign focuses on friendship, music, personal growth, and the magic of everyday moments.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Directive:** The Lore Generation Agent must create detailed entries for each of the following prompts. Prioritize warmth, nostalgia, and gentle humor.
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the founding of Bardston High as a school for young musicians and mages 200 years ago by the legendary bard Bardston the Melodious.
    *   Write the history of the Light Music Club, which was once the school's most prestigious club before falling into obscurity.
    *   Describe the tradition of the Cultural Festival, an annual event where students showcase their talents and the entire town attends.
    *   Explain the significance of "tea time" in bardic culture as a sacred practice for bonding and creative inspiration.
    *   Detail the legend of the school's founder performing a concert that ended a war and brought lasting peace to the kingdom.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Directive:** The Faction Generation Agent must create a detailed profile for each faction listed below. Each profile must contain the specified fields.
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Light Music Club (The Players)** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** To save the club from disbandment, perform at the Cultural Festival, grow as musicians and friends.
        *   **Hierarchy:** A small group of students with no formal hierarchy, advised by Maestro Songweaver.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To bring joy through music and prove their worth as a club.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Each member seeks personal growth – overcoming stage fright, finding their voice, healing from past hurts, or discovering their passion.
        *   **Assets:** A cozy club room, mismatched instruments, unlimited tea supplies, and unshakeable friendship.
        *   **Relationships:** Friendly rivals with the Orchestra Club, beloved by supportive teachers, occasionally underestimated by other students.
    *   **The Orchestra Club** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** To maintain their reputation as the school's premier musical ensemble and win awards at competitions.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Formally structured with president Veronica von Crescendo leading, section leaders, and strict practice schedules.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To uphold the classical musical traditions of Bardston High and achieve technical perfection.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Many members feel pressured and envious of the Light Music Club's genuine enjoyment of music.
        *   **Assets:** Superior instruments, dedicated practice space, school funding, prestigious reputation.
        *   **Relationships:** Initial rivals of the Light Music Club, gradually developing mutual respect and potential collaboration.
    *   **The Student Council** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** To manage school events, allocate club funding, and maintain order.
        *   **Hierarchy:** President, vice-president, treasurer, secretary, and various committee heads.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To ensure all clubs meet requirements and support successful school events.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** The council president is secretly a fan of the Light Music Club and subtly helps them meet requirements.
        *   **Assets:** Authority over club budgets, event planning resources, ability to extend deadlines.
        *   **Relationships:** Officially neutral but generally supportive of clubs that show genuine effort.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Directive:** The Character Generation Agent must create full profiles for all NPCs listed. Tier 1 NPCs are unique individuals. Tier 2 & 3 are archetypes to be instantiated multiple times with unique details as needed by the simulation.
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **Maestro Cordelia Songweaver:** Dreamy, caring music teacher and club advisor with a mysterious past as a famous bard.
    *   **Veronica von Crescendo:** Talented but arrogant Orchestra Club president who secretly envies genuine friendship.
    *   **Pip the Enthusiast:** Boundlessly energetic first-year student who can't play instruments but brings heart to the club.
    *   **Headmaster Aldric Ironquill:** Stern but fair leader who secretly attends performances incognito and misses his youth.
*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Archetypes for Generation):**
    *   "Supportive Classmate"
    *   "Stressed Student Cramming for Exams"
    *   "Enthusiastic Festival Volunteer"
    *   "Rival Club Member"
    *   "Nostalgic Alumni Visiting for Festival"
    *   "Wandering Merchant Selling Instruments"

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Directive:** The Environment Generation Agent must create a detailed blueprint for each location below, populating all specified sub-sections. Emphasize warmth, coziness, and everyday magic.
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **The Light Music Club Room:** A cozy, slightly dusty third-floor room filled with memories and possibilities.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The tea corner with mismatched cups, the bulletin board covered in setlists and photos, the window overlooking the courtyard, the worn but comfortable sofa.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Club members, occasional visits from Maestro Songweaver.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Tea and snacks, instruments (varying quality), sheet music, a safe space to practice and bond.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** A lost first-year student wanders in, the club's pet cat appears, a mysterious alumni leaves a gift, equipment malfunctions during practice, spontaneous inspiration strikes during tea time.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "There's a legendary guitar hidden somewhere in the school's storage." "The founder's sheet music is in the library and grants magical inspiration." "Maestro Songweaver was part of a famous band that mysteriously disbanded."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Afternoon sunlight streaming through dusty windows, posters on walls, cozy clutter), Sound (Tuning instruments, kettle whistling, laughter and conversation), Smell (Fresh tea, old wood, a hint of vanilla from Pip's cookies).
    *   **The Courtyard Stage:** An outdoor performance space where students gather during lunch and festivals.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The wooden stage with good acoustics, cherry blossom trees providing shade, benches where students eat lunch, notice board with event schedules.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Students during breaks, performing clubs, occasional visiting bards.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Open performance space (must be reserved), good natural acoustics, audience of fellow students.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Impromptu performance by other students, love confession happening nearby, club recruitment drives, food vendor sets up stall, sudden rain shower.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The acoustics are magically enhanced by the school's founder." "A famous bard proposed to their partner on this stage." "Students who perform here before the festival have good luck."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Cherry blossoms falling, students chatting, sunlight and clouds), Sound (Performances echoing, student chatter, birds singing), Smell (Cherry blossoms, fresh grass, food from nearby vendors).
    *   **Tea Shop "Melodia's":** A popular student hangout near campus serving tea, sweets, and comfort.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The corner booth the Light Music Club claims, the display case of beautiful desserts, the wall of customer drawings, the vintage gramophone playing soft music.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Owner Melodia (kind elderly halfling), students from various schools, the Light Music Club during planning sessions.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Various teas, elaborate desserts, sandwiches, a sympathetic ear from Melodia, cozy atmosphere for conversations.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Meeting other club members on the same mission, overhearing useful rumors, special limited-time dessert, Melodia sharing wisdom, chance encounter with Orchestra Club members.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Melodia was friends with the school founder." "The secret menu has a tea that grants inspiration." "A famous musician wrote their greatest song in the corner booth."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Warm lighting, cozy booths, dessert displays), Sound (Soft music, quiet conversations, teacups clinking), Smell (Fresh tea, baking pastries, hint of cinnamon).

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Directive:** The World Simulation Agent must implement the following trigger-based state changes. For each "IF" condition, the agent must pre-generate the narrative and environmental consequences for the "THEN" outcome, prioritizing heartwarming and character-growth results.
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** The players successfully recruit a new club member.
    *   **THEN:** The club's reputation improves slightly. New practice dynamics emerge. The new member brings personal challenges and strengths that create new story opportunities.
    *   **IF:** The players hold a tea time and roleplay meaningful conversations.
    *   **THEN:** Award Harmony Points. Deepen character relationships. Reveal personal backstory elements. Create opportunities for character growth.
    *   **IF:** The players perform successfully at a school event.
    *   **THEN:** The club gains popularity and support. More students attend their performances. The Student Council becomes more favorable. Rival clubs show grudging respect.
    *   **IF:** The players fail a performance or practice goes poorly.
    *   **THEN:** Create a moment for comfort and encouragement. Maestro Songweaver provides wisdom. The club bonds over the shared struggle. They gain determination to improve.
    *   **IF:** The players reach the Cultural Festival finale.
    *   **THEN:** Generate a climactic, emotional performance scene. Regardless of rolls, their genuine friendship shines through. The audience is moved. The club's future is secured. Relationships with rival clubs transform into lasting friendships.
    *   **IF:** The players spend Harmony Points during a performance.
    *   **THEN:** Create magical effects that enhance the music – instruments glow, illusory effects appear, the audience is deeply moved. Describe the power of their friendship manifesting.
