# The Conqueror's Heir

*A Superhero Coming-of-Age / Alien Invasion campaign — Medium Campaign (12–15 sessions) — High difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Conqueror's Heir
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Superhero / Coming of Age / Alien Invasion Horror
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 12–15 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **1** → Finish **12–15**
**Tone (keywords):** superhero deconstruction, coming of age, extreme violence, betrayal, alien invasion, father-son conflict
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party consists of young heroes just coming into their powers, mentored by the world's greatest champion—Titanius, an alien hero who's protected the world for decades. When they discover that Titanius is actually an advance agent for the Viltrumite Empire, a brutal alien civilization that conquers worlds, they must grapple with betrayal while earth faces invasion. The campaign explores what it means to be a hero when your greatest role model becomes your greatest threat, and whether the next generation can surpass their predecessors.

---

## What "High" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Extreme Violence:** Combat is brutal and consequences are permanent. The campaign doesn't shy away from showing the cost of superhero battles.
*   **Emotional Trauma:** Characters face profound betrayals, moral compromises, and psychological horror.
*   **Power Scaling:** Enemies are vastly more powerful than the party initially, requiring creative tactics and growth.
*   **Moral Complexity:** No clear right answers to many dilemmas about heroism, violence, and collateral damage.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 12–15 sessions)

From bright-eyed young heroes to hardened defenders of earth.

*   **Act I — The Student Heroes (Sessions 1–4):** The party trains under Titanius and other veteran heroes, learning what it means to be champions while dealing with typical coming-of-age challenges.
*   **Act II — The Revelation (Sessions 5–8):** Discovering Titanius's true nature and the impending Viltrumite invasion. The betrayal of their mentor shatters their worldview.
*   **Act III — The Defense (Sessions 9–12):** Facing the Viltrumite Empire while grappling with whether they can become strong enough to matter against such overwhelming power.
*   **Act IV — The Choice (Sessions 13–15):** Final confrontation with Titanius and deciding what kind of heroes they'll become.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "It's About Time"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / First Heroics
*   **Key Events:** Party receives powers and first encounters with Titanius, who offers to train them. First successful superhero mission against street-level threats.
**Escalation:** A villain nearly kills a party member—heroes realize this is dangerous work.

### Session 2 — "The Guardians of the Globe"
*   **Focus:** Team Building / Mentorship
*   **Key Events:** Meeting the world's premier hero team, learning from veterans, understanding the scope of global threats.
**Escalation:** The Guardians are called away for a mysterious emergency meeting.

### Session 3 — "Omni-Man"
*   **Focus:** Horror / Revelation
*   **Key Events:** The party discovers the Guardians of the Globe have been murdered—brutally slaughtered. All evidence is confusing.
**Escalation:** Titanius seems deeply affected, claiming to have been elsewhere when it happened.

### Session 4 — "WHERE I REALLY COME FROM"
*   **Focus:** Investigation / Betrayal
*   **Key Events:** Through investigation, party discovers evidence suggesting Titanius killed the Guardians. Confronting this is terrifying.
**Escalation:** Titanius reveals the truth—he's from the Viltrumite Empire, sent to weaken Earth's defenses for conquest.

### Session 5 — "The Viltrumite Legacy"
*   **Focus:** Revelation / Choice
*   **Key Events:** Titanius explains Viltrumite philosophy—only the strong deserve to live, conquest is natural, Earth will be better under their rule.
**Escalation:** The party must choose whether to fight their mentor or try to reason with him.

### Session 6 — "THAT ACTUALLY HURT"
*   **Focus:** Epic Combat / Survival
*   **Key Events:** If the party fights, Titanius holds back slightly out of affection but demonstrates his overwhelming power. The "fight" is surviving.
**Escalation:** Titanius leaves to report to the Empire, giving Earth time to prepare.

### Session 7 — "The Alliance"
*   **Focus:** Faction Building / Preparation
*   **Key Events:** Rallying Earth's remaining heroes, military forces, and even some villains to prepare for invasion.
**Escalation:** The party realizes they're nowhere near powerful enough to face Viltrumites.

### Session 8 — "Get Mark"
*   **Focus:** Training / Growth
*   **Key Events:** Intense training montage. The party grows in power rapidly through trial by fire. Developing new techniques specifically to fight Viltrumites.
**Escalation:** The Viltrumite vanguard arrives—three warriors sent to begin the conquest.

### Session 9 — "I THOUGHT YOU WERE STRONGER"
*   **Focus:** Desperate Combat / Attrition
*   **Key Events:** Battle against one Viltrumite requires the entire party and multiple NPCs. The fight destroys a city district and kills thousands.
**Escalation:** They win, barely, but the cost is horrifying. And there are more coming.

### Session 10 — "The Cost"
*   **Focus:** Aftermath / Moral Crisis
*   **Key Events:** Dealing with survivor guilt, civilian casualties, media backlash. Are they heroes if their battles kill innocents?
**Escalation:** Titanius returns, seemingly conflicted about what he's done.

### Session 11 — "You'll Outlast Every Fragile Being on This Planet"
*   **Focus:** Temptation / Philosophy
*   **Key Events:** Titanius offers the party a choice—join him, accept Viltrumite rule, and they'll be allowed to help "guide" humanity properly.
**Escalation:** Some of his arguments make sense—Viltrumite rule would end war, disease, poverty. At the cost of freedom.

### Session 12 — "THINK"
*   **Focus:** Confrontation / Decision
*   **Key Events:** The party's final ideological confrontation with Titanius. Is might right? Can heroes justify authoritarian rule? What will they sacrifice for victory?
**Escalation:** The main Viltrumite fleet arrives. Earth has hours.

### Session 13 — "The Battle for Earth"
*   **Focus:** Epic Combat / Sacrifice
*   **Key Events:** All-out war. Every hero, every soldier, every resource against the Viltrumite Empire. Impossible odds.
**Escalation:** The only way to win requires a sacrifice that will haunt survivors forever.

### Session 14 — "You Can Always Start Again"
*   **Focus:** Resolution / Aftermath
*   **Key Events:** The invasion is repelled (or not, depending on choices). Earth must rebuild. The party must live with their choices.
**Escalation:** Titanius's fate is decided—execution, imprisonment, exile, or something else.

### Session 15 — "Legacy"
*   **Focus:** Epilogue / Future
*   **Key Events:** Years later—how did the party's choices shape the world? Did they become the heroes they wanted to be? What did they lose along the way?

## Major NPCs

1.  **Titanius (Omni-Man stand-in)** — *The Betrayer Mentor*
    *   **Role:** The world's greatest hero, secretly an alien conqueror.
    *   **Personality:** Genuinely came to care for Earth but loyalty to Empire wins.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Conflicted about his mission, delayed conquest for decades due to attachment.

2.  **The Guardians of the Globe** — *Murdered Heroes*
    *   **Role:** Elite team killed by Titanius to prevent opposition.
    *   **Personality:** Each represents different heroic ideals.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** They were Earth's best hope, and they died before the party was ready.

3.  **General Kregg** — *Viltrumite Commander*
    *   **Role:** Pure exemplar of Viltrumite philosophy—might makes right.
    *   **Personality:** Brutal, efficient, genuinely believes conquest helps lesser species.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** No hidden depths—he is exactly what he appears to be.

## Central Conflict

Trust versus betrayal, idealism versus pragmatism, and whether the next generation can overcome the sins of their mentors. The campaign asks: what makes someone a hero? Is it power, or choice? Can authoritarianism ever be justified? How do you fight when victory requires becoming what you oppose?

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Power Scaling**
*   Characters grow rapidly in power through the campaign, starting as street-level heroes and ending as planetary defenders.

### 2. **Collateral Damage Tracker**
*   Superhero battles cause civilian casualties. Players must balance winning fights with protecting innocents.

### 3. **Reputation System**
*   Public opinion shifts based on the party's actions—are they heroes, menaces, or necessary evils?

### 4. **Viltrumite Physiology**
*   Enemies have specific weaknesses that must be discovered and exploited through investigation and experimentation.

### 5. **The Hard Choice Mechanic**
*   Regular scenarios where every option has terrible consequences—no right answers, only choices and their costs.
