# The Condemned Isles
**Tagline:** *No guards. No rules. No escape. Only survival.*

## Campaign Overview

One hundred of the realm's most dangerous criminals have been exiled to the Condemned Isles—a remote archipelago where society's worst are dumped to kill each other. There are no guards, no rules, and absolutely no way off the islands. Supplies drop randomly from the sky, weapons are scattered across the terrain, and explosive collars ensure no one can leave the designated zones. Every two days, the "safe" area shrinks as sections of the islands are designated kill-zones where collars detonate automatically.

Unlike other battle royales, this is pure survival horror. Players must find food and water, avoid diseases and hazards, navigate hostile terrain, and constantly watch for ambushes. Some prisoners form gangs, others go solo, some lose their minds entirely. The psychological toll is immense—trapped on islands with murderers, rapists, and psychopaths, with no hope of rescue or redemption.

The campaign explores moral darkness: when you're surrounded by monsters, what does it take to survive? Do you become a monster yourself, or try to maintain humanity in hell? And rumors persist that the wealthy elite watch via hidden cameras, betting on outcomes and occasionally interfering for entertainment.

## Core Themes
- **Moral Decay:** Surviving requires abandoning ethics
- **Prison Hierarchy:** Power dynamics in absence of law
- **Psychological Horror:** Constant threat, paranoia, isolation
- **No Redemption:** Society has written you off
- **Survival over Humanity:** What are you willing to do?
- **Spectacle of Suffering:** Hidden watchers, exploitation

## Session Breakdown (15-25 Sessions)

### Arc 1: The Drop (Sessions 1-3)
- Players are convicted criminals sentenced to Isles
- Explosives collars fitted, transported via prison ship
- Dumped on beach with basic supplies
- Initial scramble for weapons and shelter
- First encounters with other prisoners
- Establishing base and gathering resources

### Arc 2: Survival and Territory (Sessions 4-9)
- Island exploration and mapping
- Resource scarcity drives conflict
- Form or join gangs for protection
- First safe-zone shrink, deaths by collar
- Psychological breakdowns begin
- Discover evidence of observers
- Raid supply drops

### Arc 3: The Shrinking (Sessions 10-16)
- Multiple zone shrinks force confrontation
- Gang warfare escalates
- Some prisoners go feral
- Discover hidden camera installations
- Moral compromises to survive
- Betrayals and shifting alliances
- Only two dozen prisoners left

### Arc 4: Endgame (Sessions 17-22)
- Final island section, nowhere to hide
- Constant combat and paranoia
- Last survivor wins... what?
- Discover wealthy elite control collars
- Option to rebel against observers
- Final confrontations
- Escape or death

### Arc 5: Aftermath (Sessions 23-25, Optional)
- Winner faces consequences
- Hunted by elite or recruited
- Expose the Isles or become next season's attraction
- Psychological recovery impossible
- What does "winning" even mean?

## Major Locations

**Main Island:** Volcanic center, jungle, beaches, abandoned structures
**Supply Depot Island:** Frequent drops, always contested
**Medical Island:** Old hospital ruins, medicine scarce
**The Prison Ruins:** Former facility, now shelter
**Quarantine Zone:** Diseased area, desperate resources
**The Observation Station (Hidden):** Elite watchers' facility

## Unique Mechanics

**Explosive Collars:** Detonate if leave safe zones, waterproof, tamper-proof. Detonate on death (prevent looting easily). Observers can trigger remotely. Loud beeping when entering kill zones.

**Zone Shrinking:** Every 48 hours, zones become kill-zones. Announced via speakers. Map increasingly smaller. Forces confrontation. Final zone tiny.

**Resource Management:** Food, water, medicine scarce. Hunting and foraging required. Disease and infection threats. Starvation/dehydration mechanics. Shelter needed for rest.

**Psychological Toll:** Sanity mechanic tracks mental state. Witnessing atrocities affects sanity. Low sanity causes breakdowns, hallucinations. Recovery requires safety (rare).

**Gang Dynamics:** Strength in numbers vs. trust issues. Hierarchies form quickly. Leaders challenged constantly. Betrayal common. Solo play dangerous but free.

**Supply Drops:** Random locations, announced. Contains weapons, food, medicine. Always draws attention. Risk vs. reward. Sometimes trapped.

**Hidden Observers:** Cameras everywhere (players discover). Elite audience bets on outcomes. Can influence drops, collar triggers. Moral horror when discovered.

## Plot Twists
- One prisoner is undercover journalist
- Collars can be hacked (with right knowledge)
- Previous "winner" is prisoner here again
- Observers occasionally hunt prisoners personally
- Island has hidden escape route (submarine, boat)
- Some prisoners are innocent (framed)
- Elite plan to expand to other islands (global)
- Final survivor offered job as observer

## Victory Conditions
- **Last Standing:** Survive all others, "freedom" (monitored)
- **Rebellion:** Kill observers, destroy collar system, actual freedom
- **Escape:** Find hidden exit, flee, hunted forever
- **Martyrdom:** Die exposing the Isles to public

## Defeat Conditions
- **Combat Death:** Killed by another prisoner
- **Collar Detonation:** Enter kill-zone or tamper
- **Environmental:** Starvation, disease, wildlife, hazards
- **Madness:** Psychological break, suicide or execution
- **Observer Termination:** Elite decides to eliminate you
