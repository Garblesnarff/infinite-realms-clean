# The Condemned Isles - World-Building Specification

## The Exile System

**Legal Framework:** Death penalty alternative. Life sentence to Isles (effectively death). No appeals, no rescues. Society considers them dead. Perfect disposal of "undesirables."

**Prisoner Categories:**
- Violent offenders (murderers, rapists)
- Political dissidents (inconvenient to regime)
- War criminals
- Organized crime leaders
- Some innocent (framed, corrupt trials)
- Mix of guilty and wrongly accused

**The Collar System:**
- Explosive device locked around neck
- Tamper-proof (detonate on attempt)
- GPS tracked by control station
- Kill-zone entry triggers countdown
- Remote detonation capability
- Waterproof, shockproof, magic-resistant
- Beeps when approaching kill-zone
- Explodes on death (anti-looting measure)

## Island Geography

**Main Island (Largest):**
- Volcanic mountain (dormant but toxic vents)
- Dense jungle (dangerous fauna)
- Beach perimeter (no boats)
- Ruins of old prison facility
- Fresh water source (contested)
- Caves (shelter but dangerous)

**Supply Depot Island:**
- Frequent drops
- Open terrain (kill zone)
- No shelter
- High-value targets

**Medical Island:**
- Abandoned hospital
- Medicine scarce but present
- Infected areas (diseases)
- Morgue (psychological horror)

**Quarantine Zone:**
- Plague-ridden
- Desperate prisoners
- Best supplies (no one dares)
- Slow death vs. quick death choice

**Observer Station (Hidden):**
- Camouflaged facility
- Elite viewing room
- Collar control center
- Supply drop mechanisms
- Escape vehicles
- Discovery is revolution trigger

## Prison Gangs and Factions

**The Predators:** Strongest violent offenders. Control main water source. Led by Kaine. Kill for sport and dominance.

**The Cartel:** Organized crime remnants. Led by Maria Vasquez. Business-like approach. Control supply routes.

**The Cult:** Religious fanatics led by Preacher. Believe Isles are hell/purgatory. Sacrifice "sinners." Growing numbers.

**The Survivors:** Pragmatic alliance. Mixed backgrounds. Just trying to live. Most sympathetic.

**The Feral:** Lost their minds. Cannibals, truly animalistic. Hunt alone or small packs. Completely unpredictable.

**Solo Operators:** Refuse to join gangs. High-skill individuals. Stealthy, ruthless, or lucky.

## The Observer Class

**Who They Are:** Ultra-wealthy elite bored with normal entertainment. Nobles, merchant princes, corrupt officials. Secret society spanning multiple kingdoms.

**What They Do:** Watch prisoners kill each other. Bet enormous sums on outcomes. Sometimes hunt prisoners personally. Viewing parties with exotic food. Debate "quality" of kills.

**Their Rules:** No interference (usually). Supply drops random (not really). Fair competition (lie). Winner freed (rarely truth). They're invisible (prisoners suspect).

**Technology:** Hidden cameras throughout islands. Magical scrying spheres. Collar control systems. Supply drop mechanisms. Personal safety ensured.

## Survival Challenges

**Food:** Hunt wildlife (dangerous), fish (exposed), forage plants (poisonous?), raid supplies (conflict), cannibalism (some resort).

**Water:** Main source controlled by Predators. Rain collection (weather dependent). Stagnant pools (disease risk). Dehydration kills.

**Shelter:** Ruins (contested). Caves (dangerous creatures). Build makeshift (visible targets). Constantly moving vs. defending territory.

**Disease:** Tropical infections, open wounds, contaminated water, plague zones, no medicine. Medical Island risky but necessary.

**Weather:** Storms, flooding, extreme heat, humidity, hypothermia at night. Exposure kills slowly.

**Wildlife:** Venomous snakes, large predators, disease-carrying insects, poisonous plants. Nature is hostile.

**Other Prisoners:** Biggest threat. Trust no one. Alliances temporary. Betrayal constant. Always watching.

## Psychological Horror Elements

**Isolation:** No rescue coming. Society forgotten you. Die among monsters. Completely alone mentally.

**Moral Decay:** Kill to survive. Steal from corpses. Betray allies. Become what you hate. No redemption path.

**Constant Threat:** Never safe. Always hunted. Sleep is dangerous. Paranoia justified. Stress breaks minds.

**Dehumanization:** Numbered prisoners (observers). Collars like animals. Entertainment for others. Life meaningless.

**Hopelessness:** No winning condition (truth). "Freedom" is lie. Death inevitable. Fighting delays it only.

**Watching Eyes:** Discover hidden cameras. Realize you're entertainment. Rage and helplessness. Perform for survival?

## The Truth About Winners

**The Lie:** Last survivor goes free. Returned to society. Pardoned.

**The Reality:** Winners are "freed" to remote location. Given money, monitored forever. Can't expose Isles (killed if try). Some recruited as staff. Others suicided mysteriously. Few escape surveillance. None truly free.

**The Option:** Work for Observers as hunter or staff. Better than death. Morally horrifying. Some accept. Perpetuate cycle.

## Escape Routes (Hidden)

**The Submarine:** Old facility had emergency sub. Hidden underwater dock. Needs fuel (scarce). Seats 5-6. Finding it is quest.

**Hack the Collars:** Dr. Crane (mad scientist prisoner) theoretically could. Needs equipment from Medical Island. Might kill everyone in attempt. Desperation move.

**Kill the Observers:** Storm their station. Seize control. Broadcast evidence. Suicide mission. But ends system. Martyrdom victory.

This world creates a battle royale with survival horror atmosphere, moral darkness, and psychological weight beyond simple combat mechanics.
