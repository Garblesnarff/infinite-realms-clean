# The Wasteland Sovereign

*A Power Fantasy / Nation-Building campaign — Medium Campaign (8 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Wasteland Sovereign
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Power Fantasy / Nation-Building / Comedic Drama / Outcast Revenge
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 8 sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **5** → Finish **14-15**
**Tone (keywords):** empowering, comedic, vindictive satisfaction, nation-building, found family, power reversal
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party is summoned to be heroes by the vain Goddess of Beauty, but she's horrified by their "ugly" appearances and "inferior" abilities. Banishing them to the Wasteland—a barren, monster-filled region deemed uninhabitable—she strips them of her blessing and expects them to die. Instead, the party discovers they're absurdly overpowered in this world, the "monsters" are intelligent beings desperate for leadership, and they have a talent for building civilization in the impossible waste. This campaign is about creating a thriving nation from outcasts, becoming more powerful and successful than the goddess ever imagined, and deciding whether to forgive or repay her cruelty.

---

## Difficulty Context

**Medium Difficulty**: This campaign balances combat power fantasy (the party is strong) with strategic challenges (nation-building, diplomacy, resource management). Combat encounters are frequent but the party has advantages. The real difficulty comes from governing a growing nation, managing diverse populations, and navigating international politics. Perfect for players who enjoy feeling powerful while facing complex strategic decisions.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 8 sessions)

A tale of the rejected becoming the rulers, building paradise from wasteland.

*   **Act I — Cast Out and Rising (Sessions 1-3):** The party is summoned by the Goddess of Beauty to be heroes but is immediately rejected for being "ugly" and banished to the Wasteland. They survive the harsh environment, discover their hidden power, and encounter the first "monsters"—an intelligent orc tribe and a dragon seeking asylum. The party establishes their first settlement, learning to govern and discovering that the Wasteland's magic enhances their abilities far beyond normal adventurers.
*   **Act II — Building the Impossible (Sessions 4-6):** The party's settlement grows as more outcasts—demihumans, monsters, and exiles—arrive seeking sanctuary. They develop agriculture, infrastructure, and trade while defending against attacks from the "civilized" kingdoms who see them as a monster threat. The party navigates complex diplomacy, creates revolutionary policies, and builds a nation that represents everything the goddess's kingdoms are not: merit-based, diverse, and actually functional.
*   **Act III — The Sovereign's Choice (Sessions 7-8):** The goddess finally notices the party's success and attempts to reclaim them as heroes, offering false apologies and demanding they serve humanity against demons. The party must decide: accept her manipulation, expose her vanity to the world, or declare independence and potential war. The climax involves defending their nation against the goddess's champions and demons simultaneously, proving the Wasteland Sovereign's power, and choosing what kind of nation they want to be.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Goddess's Rejection"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Rejection / Discovery
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The party is summoned by the Goddess of Beauty, Aristia, along with other heroes to save the world from demons.
    *   Aristia judges each person on appearance and "grace." The party is declared "unacceptable" for various petty reasons.
    *   Without blessing or supplies, the party is teleported to the Wasteland—a barren region filled with dangerous monsters.
    *   Initial survival challenges: finding water, shelter, fighting hostile monsters.
    *   Discovery: the party realizes they're absurdly strong here, easily defeating creatures that should overwhelm them.
    *   They meet Tomoe, an intelligent greater dragon, who explains the Wasteland amplifies power and that many "monsters" here are refugees from human persecution.
*   **Escalation:** The party must decide: try to return to the kingdoms that rejected them, or make a life here?

### Session 2 — "The First Citizens"
*   **Focus:** Alliance / Settlement
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Tomoe offers a contract: she'll serve the party if they create a place where outcasts can live freely.
    *   The party encounters an orc tribe led by chieftain Beren, also fleeing human persecution.
    *   First diplomatic challenge: convince the orcs and dragon to work together despite historical enmity.
    *   They establish "Asora," the first settlement—choosing a location with water and defensible terrain.
    *   Building montage: the party uses their powers to rapidly construct basic infrastructure (shelter, irrigation, fortifications).
    *   More refugees arrive: demihumans, monsters, and even human exiles from the kingdoms.
*   **Escalation:** A human adventuring party attacks, seeing the settlement as a "monster nest" that needs purging.

### Session 3 — "Defended and Defined"
*   **Focus:** Combat / Governance
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The party easily defeats the adventurers, capturing them alive to question.
    *   They learn the kingdoms view the Wasteland as a monster threat and pay bounties for "cleansing" it.
    *   First governance challenge: what to do with prisoners? Execute them, enslave them, rehabilitate them, or send them back with a message?
    *   The party establishes laws and principles for Asora—creating a society based on merit, not species or appearance.
    *   They recruit Mio, an intelligent black spider, as their third powerful companion—she brings agricultural knowledge.
    *   First successful harvest using the party's magic and Mio's expertise, proving the Wasteland can sustain life.
*   **Escalation:** News of Asora spreads. More refugees arrive daily, but so do more hostile forces.

### Session 4 — "The Merchant's Gambit"
*   **Focus:** Economics / Expansion
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The party realizes they need trade with the outside world for certain resources.
    *   They meet Rembrandt, a merchant lord afflicted with a curse. The party can cure him when no one else could.
    *   In gratitude, Rembrandt offers to be Asora's secret trade partner, circumventing the kingdoms' hostility.
    *   Economic development: Asora begins producing unique goods (Wasteland herbs, monster-crafted items, magical innovations).
    *   The party establishes a trade network, earning wealth and information about the wider world.
    *   They learn that the "hero" blessed by Aristia is incompetent and cruel, abusing their power over the kingdoms.
*   **Escalation:** Rembrandt warns that Asora is on the kingdoms' formal target list as a "monster nation."

### Session 5 — "Diplomacy of the Damned"
*   **Focus:** Intrigue / Politics
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Representatives from multiple kingdoms arrive, officially to "negotiate," actually to spy and make demands.
    *   Complex multi-faction diplomacy: each kingdom wants different things (Asora as vassal, trade monopoly, military alliance against others).
    *   The party can play kingdoms against each other, maintain independence, or make strategic alliances.
    *   They encounter other reincarnators: the goddess summoned multiple groups, and some are worse than others.
    *   Revelation: the demon threat is exaggerated by Aristia to maintain control and worship.
    *   Social event: the party hosts a feast/tournament to demonstrate Asora's civilization and power.
*   **Escalation:** A jealous reincarnator hero tries to assassinate the party, failing spectacularly and forcing a political incident.

### Session 6 — "The Nation Ascendant"
*   **Focus:** Development / Conflict
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Asora is officially declared a nation. The party must choose a governance structure and title.
    *   Major infrastructure projects: the party builds a city proper, with advanced magic-technology integration.
    *   Educational system established: Asora becomes known for valuing knowledge and accepting students of all races.
    *   Military development: the party trains a diverse defense force combining monster and demihuman tactics.
    *   A kingdom invades, sending an army to "cleanse the monster threat" before Asora becomes too powerful.
    *   Epic battle: the party and their forces defend against a numerically superior army, demonstrating overwhelming power.
*   **Escalation:** The goddess Aristia personally intervenes, appearing before the party with a "generous offer."

### Session 7 — "The Goddess Returns"
*   **Focus:** Confrontation / Choice
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Aristia appears, claiming she "always believed in the party" and that banishing them was "a test."
    *   She demands they become her champions, serve humanity, and fight the demons under her guidance.
    *   The party can: accept (and betray everything they built), refuse (risking divine wrath), or expose her (complex consequences).
    *   If they refuse/expose her, Aristia sends her "blessed heroes" to force compliance.
    *   Battle against Aristia's champions—cruel, incompetent adventurers who abuse their goddess-granted power.
    *   The party decisively defeats them, proving they've surpassed the goddess's blessing through their own effort.
    *   Aristia, in a tantrum, declares Asora "enemies of humanity" and calls for holy war.
*   **Escalation:** Both the kingdoms and the demons see opportunity in the chaos, forcing the party into complex three-way conflict.

### Session 8 — "Sovereign's Triumph"
*   **Focus:** Final Battle / Resolution
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The kingdoms, demons, and goddess's forces all converge on Asora, each with different agendas.
    *   The party can: fight all three, ally with demons against Aristia, broker peace, or other creative solutions.
    *   Epic multi-faction battle: the party coordinates their nation's defense while dealing with political maneuvering.
    *   Climactic moment: the party directly confronts Aristia, who has manifested an avatar to "personally punish her failures."
    *   Boss battle against the goddess avatar—she's powerful but the party has grown beyond her ability to control.
    *   Resolution options:
        *   Defeat her avatar, forcing her to acknowledge Asora's independence.
        *   Negotiate a treaty establishing Asora as neutral ground.
        *   Expose her manipulation to the world, causing crisis of faith in the kingdoms.
        *   Make peace with the demon lord, revealing Aristia's propaganda.
    *   Epilogue: Asora thrives as a beacon of meritocracy and acceptance. The party, once rejected as "ugly" and "inferior," rules a nation that refugees of all kinds seek out. The goddess, humbled or defiant, must accept their success.
*   **Resolution:** The Wasteland Sovereign stands as proof that worth is not determined by divine favor or appearance, but by actions and character.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Goddess Aristia** — *The Vain Divine*
    *   **Role:** The goddess who summoned and rejected the party. Embodies shallow beauty worship and arbitrary divine judgment.
    *   **Personality:** Narcissistic, petty, and genuinely believes appearances determine worth. She cannot comprehend that she was wrong.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She's a relatively weak goddess who maintains power through manipulation and the "hero summoning" system. She fears being exposed as incompetent.

2.  **Tomoe** — *The Greater Dragon*
    *   **Role:** First companion and ally, a powerful dragon who was persecuted by humans for her appearance.
    *   **Personality:** Wise, loyal, and surprisingly playful. She's grateful for a leader who judges her on merit.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She's the last of her kind in this region, carrying survivor's guilt and fear of losing another home.

3.  **Mio** — *The Black Spider*
    *   **Role:** Second companion, an intelligent arachne with agricultural and culinary expertise.
    *   **Personality:** Devoted, creative, and somewhat possessive. She sees the party as her saviors and is fiercely protective.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She was tortured by human adventurers before escaping to the Wasteland. She has deep trauma and hatred for certain humans.

4.  **Chieftain Beren** — *The Orc Leader*
    *   **Role:** Leader of the first orc tribe to join Asora, representing monster citizens.
    *   **Personality:** Honorable, pragmatic, and tired of endless war. He wants a home where his people can live peacefully.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He's hiding orc war criminals who committed atrocities against humans, creating a moral dilemma for the party.

5.  **Merchant Lord Rembrandt** — *The Secret Ally*
    *   **Role:** Human merchant who becomes Asora's economic connection to the kingdoms.
    *   **Personality:** Shrewd, grateful, and practical. He values results over species or appearance.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** His alliance with Asora is secret; if exposed, his family could be destroyed by the kingdoms.

6.  **"Hero" Trashmoki** — *The Blessed Fool*
    *   **Role:** A reincarnator blessed by Aristia who abuses his power, representing what the party could have become.
    *   **Personality:** Arrogant, cruel, and incompetent. He believes his divine blessing makes him superior to all.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Without Aristia's blessing, he's completely ordinary. He's utterly dependent on divine power he didn't earn.

7.  **Princess Sofia** — *The Rebel Royal*
    *   **Role:** A kingdom princess who secretly admires Asora and opposes her father's aggression.
    *   **Personality:** Intelligent, idealistic, and trapped by duty. She sees Asora as proof that better governance is possible.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She's planning a coup against her father and wants Asora's support, potentially dragging them into a civil war.

8.  **Demon Lord Zef** — *The Reasonable Enemy*
    *   **Role:** The "villain" who turns out to be far more reasonable than the goddess or human kingdoms.
    *   **Personality:** Pragmatic, weary of endless war, and genuinely interested in peace if possible.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** The demons are actually losing the war badly and desperate for any advantage—they'd make significant concessions for Asora's neutrality or alliance.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Wasteland:**
    *   Harsh, barren region filled with powerful monsters and environmental hazards. Dangerous to normal people but empowering to the party. Contains hidden resources and ancient ruins. Transforms from hostile waste to thriving nation over the campaign.

2.  **Asora - The Sovereign's City:**
    *   The party's nation, built from nothing. Starts as a refugee camp, evolves into a diverse city that blends multiple architectural styles and cultures. Known for accepting all races and judging by merit. Becomes the most advanced and equitable nation in the world.

3.  **The Kingdoms of Beauty:**
    *   Multiple human kingdoms that worship Aristia and practice appearance-based discrimination. Technologically stagnant, politically corrupt, but militarily strong. Represent the "civilized" world the party was rejected from.

4.  **The Demon Lands:**
    *   Territory controlled by demons, portrayed as enemies but actually more pragmatic than humans. Suffering under Aristia's crusade and willing to negotiate.

5.  **The Ancient Ruins:**
    *   Scattered throughout the Wasteland, containing lost technology and the truth about Aristia's rise to power. The party can uncover secrets that undermine the goddess's legitimacy.

6.  **The Trade City of Tsige:**
    *   Neutral merchant city where Asora conducts business. A place of intrigue where all factions meet "peacefully."

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores themes of worth beyond appearances, building something meaningful from rejection, and the corrupting nature of arbitrary power. The central conflict is not just defeating enemies, but proving that merit-based society is superior to the goddess's appearance-based judgment. The party must grapple with: how do you govern fairly when you have overwhelming power? How do you balance justice with mercy toward those who persecuted your people? Is revenge against the goddess worth the cost, or is success the best revenge?

The campaign asks: what determines someone's value—divine favor, appearance, species, or their actions and character?

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Wasteland Empowerment**
*   The Wasteland's unique magic amplifies the party's abilities far beyond normal limits.
*   **Mechanics:** While in the Wasteland or Asora's territory:
    *   The party treats all ability scores as 2 points higher (max 20 base, can exceed with items).
    *   They have advantage on Constitution saving throws against environmental hazards.
    *   Spell save DCs and attack bonuses increase by +2.
    *   Hostile creatures from the kingdoms suffer disadvantage on attacks against the party (the land rejects invaders).
*   This makes the party feel powerful in their domain while creating strategic considerations about leaving it.

### 2. **Nation Building Points**
*   The party earns Nation Points by governing well and developing Asora.
*   **Mechanics:** Award 1 Nation Point when the party:
    *   Successfully completes a development project (agriculture, infrastructure, education, etc.)
    *   Resolves a governance challenge fairly and effectively
    *   Integrates a new population group into Asora successfully
    *   Defeats a threat to the nation
    *   Achieves a diplomatic victory
*   Spend Nation Points to:
    *   Summon loyal troops for a battle (1 point = 1d4 skilled soldiers for one combat)
    *   Rapidly complete a construction project (2 points = instant completion of building)
    *   Access rare resources or technology (3 points = acquire something normally unavailable)
    *   Gain advantage on a crucial diplomatic check (2 points)
    *   Unlock a national ability (5 points = permanent benefit like "Asora's Academy" giving bonus to knowledge checks)
*   Maximum: 15 points. Represents the growing strength of their nation.

### 3. **Divine Disfavor**
*   The goddess's rejection actually freed the party from divine limitations.
*   **Mechanics:** The party is immune to:
    *   Being targeted by divine spells or abilities from Aristia or her champions
    *   Charm or compulsion effects from "divinely blessed" creatures
    *   Restrictions on entering holy sites (they can desecrate with impunity if they choose)
*   Additionally, when fighting creatures with "Aristia's Blessing":
    *   The party gains +2 to attack rolls against them
    *   Those creatures have disadvantage on saving throws against the party's abilities
    *   The party can sense the blessing, identifying the goddess's champions on sight
*   This turns rejection into mechanical advantage—they're free from divine control.

### 4. **Companion Evolution**
*   Tomoe, Mio, and other close companions grow stronger alongside the party.
*   **Mechanics:** Major NPC companions have levels like the party. Every two sessions (or major milestone):
    *   Companions gain a level
    *   They can learn a new ability chosen by the DM (with player input)
    *   Their loyalty and effectiveness in battle increases
*   Companions can be:
    *   Deployed independently on missions (roll for success)
    *   Used as specialized advisors (advantage on checks in their area of expertise)
    *   Sent as diplomatic representatives
*   If the party treats companions well, they unlock ultimate forms or powerful team combinations. If treated poorly, they may leave or betray.

### 5. **Diversity Bonus**
*   Asora's strength comes from integrating multiple species and cultures.
*   **Mechanics:** Track the number of different population groups in Asora (Orcs, Dragons, Spiders, Humans, Dwarves, Elves, etc.).
*   For each unique group integrated (maximum 10):
    *   Asora gains +1 to all defense rolls when attacked
    *   The party gains access to one unique resource or ability from that culture
    *   Nation Point maximum increases by 1
*   However, each new group also requires:
    *   Successful integration check (Persuasion or Insight DC 12-18 depending on group)
    *   Addressing conflicts between groups (roleplay challenges)
    *   Providing resources for their needs
*   This encourages the party to build a truly diverse nation while managing the realistic challenges of integration.
