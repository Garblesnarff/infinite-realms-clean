# The Crimson Games - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Vermillion Empire (The Capital)

**Leadership Structure:**
- President Carmina Bloodworth (absolute authority)
- Council of Magistrates (12 members, one from each conquered district - puppets)
- Head Gamemaker (designs the annual Games)
- Minister of Propaganda (controls all media and messaging)
- General of the Peacekeepers (military enforcement arm)

**Power Base:**
- Built on ruins of pre-war democratic federation
- Rose to power after crushing the First Rebellion (53 years ago)
- Maintains control through:
  - Military superiority (advanced magical technology)
  - Food/resource monopolies (districts produce, Capital distributes)
  - Annual Games (reminder of consequences of rebellion)
  - Surveillance state (scrying orbs, informants, Avoxes)

**Cultural Identity:**
- Worship of spectacle and beauty above all else
- Extreme body modification (magical tattoos, color-changing skin, sculpted features)
- Complete disconnect from production of goods they consume
- Decadent parties while districts starve
- View district citizens as lesser beings, almost pets
- Fashion and entertainment obsessed
- Short attention spans demanding constant stimulation

**Economic System:**
- Post-scarcity for Capital citizens (magic provides most needs)
- Artificial scarcity imposed on districts to maintain control
- Sponsorship as gambling and social currency
- Victor merchandising and betting markets
- District production quotas enforced brutally

### The Twelve Districts

**District 1: Gemstone Heights**
- **Industry:** Luxury goods, jewelry, weapons crafting
- **Wealth Level:** High (serves Capital directly)
- **Culture:** Career tribute training academies, pride in service to Capital
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Low initially, elite buy into system
- **Key Figures:**
  - Mayor Cornelius Bright (collaborator)
  - Kaelith Swiftarrow (Career tribute)
  - Master Weaponsmith Forge (secretly arms rebels)

**District 2: Stoneheart**
- **Industry:** Masonry, military equipment, Peacekeeper training
- **Wealth Level:** High (military-industrial complex)
- **Culture:** Martial honor, Career academy, generational Peacekeepers
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Low to moderate, split between loyalists and dissenters
- **Key Figures:**
  - Brutus Stone (victor, mentor, alcoholic)
  - Commander Slate (Peacekeeper hardliner)
  - The Masons (secret society planning from within)

**District 3: Circuitry**
- **Industry:** Technology, electronics, magical engineering
- **Wealth Level:** Moderate (valuable but closely monitored)
- **Culture:** Innovation stifled by Capital oversight, underground tinkerers
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** High (intellectuals see through propaganda)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Mira Ashborn (tribute, hacker)
  - Dr. Voltage (resistance tech specialist)
  - The Factory Collective (workers organizing)

**District 4: Tidewater**
- **Industry:** Fishing, naval construction, aquaculture
- **Wealth Level:** Moderate-High (food district with Career training)
- **Culture:** Maritime traditions, trident fighters, swimming excellence
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Moderate (Finnick's influence growing)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Finnick Oakheart (victor, secret rebel)
  - Admiral Deepcurrent (controls fishing fleet, smuggler)
  - Coral (tribute, Finnick's protégé)

**District 5: Powerline**
- **Industry:** Electrical generation, dams, magical energy
- **Wealth Level:** Moderate (essential but dangerous work)
- **Culture:** Rigorous safety protocols, union organizing history (crushed)
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Moderate-High (history of labor organizing)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Engineer Sparks (dam saboteur)
  - The Wirecutter Collective (resistance cell)

**District 6: Waystation**
- **Industry:** Transportation, teleportation circles, mounts
- **Wealth Level:** Moderate (drug addiction epidemic)
- **Culture:** Morphling abuse widespread, broken spirits, mobility skills
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Low (too broken to resist)
- **Key Figures:**
  - The Travelers (underground railroad network)
  - Conductor (moves refugees between districts)

**District 7: Timberfell**
- **Industry:** Lumber, paper, wooden goods
- **Wealth Level:** Low-Moderate (dangerous work, frequent accidents)
- **Culture:** Forestry traditions, axe fighters, survival skills
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Moderate (self-sufficient mindset)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Oakley (tribute, lumberjack champion)
  - The Green Guard (forest guerrilla fighters)

**District 8: Threadwork**
- **Industry:** Textiles, factories, uniforms
- **Wealth Level:** Low (sweatshop conditions)
- **Culture:** First to riot in current era, factory organizing
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Very High (recent uprisings brutally crushed)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Lysandra Silk (stylist, secret rebel)
  - The Weavers Union (underground resistance)
  - Bonnie and Twill (escaped refugees to District 13)

**District 9: Harvest**
- **Industry:** Grain farming, bread production
- **Wealth Level:** Low (work the fields they can't eat from)
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Moderate (ground down by hunger)
- **Key Figures:**
  - The Grain Smugglers (redirect food to hungry)
  - Reaper (tribute, scythe wielder)

**District 10: Rangelands**
- **Industry:** Livestock, leather, meat processing
- **Wealth Level:** Low (animal processing is brutal work)
- **Culture:** Ranching traditions, animal handling expertise
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Low-Moderate (isolated, scattered)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Wrangler (tribute, whip expert)
  - The Cattle Barons (small landowners organizing)

**District 11: Orchard**
- **Industry:** Agriculture, orchards, vegetables
- **Wealth Level:** Very Low (largest population, most oppressed)
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Very High (Thresh becomes symbol)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Thresh Ironheart (tribute, protector)
  - Rue (youngest tribute, symbol of innocence lost)
  - Elder Chaff (former victor, elder statesman)
  - The Field Workers Assembly (mass organizing)

**District 12: Coalpit**
- **Industry:** Coal mining, explosives
- **Wealth Level:** Extremely Low (poorest district, frequent cave-ins)
- **Culture:** The Seam (mining families) vs. Merchant class divide
- **Rebellion Sentiment:** Growing (player tribute's defiance inspires)
- **Key Figures:**
  - Brutus Stone (mentor, District 2 but connected here)
  - The Hob (black market trading post)
  - Greasy Sae (information broker, soup seller)
  - Ripper (butcher, resistance supporter)

### District 13: The Hidden Rebellion

**History:**
- Nuclear weapons manufacturing during the first war
- Publicly destroyed 53 years ago as example
- Actually went underground, survived in bunker systems
- Treaty with Capital: stay hidden, Capital pretends they're gone
- Built entire subterranean civilization

**Current Status:**
- Population: ~10,000 (smaller than expected due to genetic bottleneck)
- Leadership: President Alma Coin (authoritarian military government)
- Technology: Advanced military, limited civilian comforts
- Culture: Regimented, everyone has assignments, limited freedom
- Goal: Overthrow Capital, willing to sacrifice individuals for cause

**Key Figures:**
- President Coin (ruthless pragmatist)
- Beetee (tech genius, designs rebel weapons)
- Plutarch Heavensbee (defected Head Gamemaker, now rebel strategist)
- Commander Boggs (military leader, actually ethical)
- Cressida (propaganda filmmaker)

**Resources:**
- Underground farms (mushrooms, algae, limited diversity)
- Nuclear reactors (old but functional)
- Weapons manufacturing
- Bunker system extends for miles
- Hovercraft fleet (hidden aircraft)

**Strategy:**
- Infiltrate Capital through sympathizers
- Extract victors as symbols
- Coordinate district uprisings
- Propaganda warfare through hijacked broadcasts
- Willing to use players as pawns

### The Gamemakers

**Organizational Structure:**
- Head Gamemaker (Seneca Nightweave currently)
- Senior Designers (arena environment specialists)
- Combat Choreographers (manipulate tribute encounters)
- Muttation Engineers (biological horrors division)
- Technology Division (force fields, cameras, delivery systems)
- Weather Control (temperature, precipitation, disasters)

**Arena Design Philosophy:**
- Must be visually spectacular
- Should reflect current political themes
- Needs variety of death types
- Requires "pocket of romance" for audience
- Must prevent boring stalemates
- Should create hero/villain narratives

**Control Systems:**
- Central control room with holographic arena display
- Individual tribute tracking via implanted chips
- Force field boundary (lethal if touched)
- Supply drop mechanisms (parachutes)
- Muttation release triggers
- Environmental hazard activation
- Camera drone control (hundreds of angles)

**Pressure Points:**
- Extreme pressure to improve on previous years
- President directly oversees, executes for failures
- Audience ratings determine success
- Must balance spectacle with maintaining sponsor engagement
- Career pressure: most Gamemakers executed within 5 years

### The Avox Network

**Creation:**
- Political prisoners, criminals, rebels
- Tongues surgically removed, enslaved to Capital
- Magical compulsion prevents violence against masters
- Serve as silent reminder of consequences of defiance

**Secret Resistance:**
- Can communicate via sign language (illegal but done in shadows)
- Pass messages between servants across households
- Witness everything, invisible to Capital elite
- Some trained in sabotage by District 13 agents
- Key intelligence network for rebellion

**Notable Avoxes:**
- Lavinia (tribute's childhood friend, captured trying to escape)
- Darius (Peacekeeper who showed mercy, punished)
- Poll and Deli (sisters, communicate complex plans through choreographed serving)

## Location Blueprints

### The Arena - Year 73: Eternal Sylvan

**Geographical Layout:**
- Total Area: ~10 square miles, circular with force field boundary
- Central Hub: Cornucopia (golden horn, 40 feet tall)
- Primary Biome: Temperate ancient forest

**Quadrant Breakdown:**

**North Quadrant: The Frozen Wastes**
- Perpetual winter beyond certain latitude
- Snow-covered pines, ice formations
- Hazards: Hypothermia, avalanches, ice-thin lakes
- Resources: Caves for shelter, clean water (must melt snow)
- Muttations: Ice wolves, tracker jackers dormant in cold

**South Quadrant: The Burning Barrens**
- Desert landscape, scorching heat
- Scattered oases (often poisoned)
- Hazards: Dehydration, heat stroke, sandstorms
- Resources: Cacti (water and food), hidden springs
- Muttations: Serpents, venomous scorpions, fire-breathing lizards

**East Quadrant: The Rotting Marshes**
- Fetid swampland, quicksand, disease
- Massive twisted trees, hanging moss
- Hazards: Disease, poisonous gases, drowning
- Resources: Fish, edible plants (if you know which)
- Muttations: Amphibious trackers, disease-carrying insects

**West Quadrant: The Whispering Woods (Starting Area)**
- Temperate forest, streams, varied terrain
- Berry bushes (some poisonous), game animals
- Hazards: Tracker jacker nests, carnivorous plants
- Resources: Water, shelter, hunting
- Muttations: Jabberjays (mimic voices), wolf mutations

**Central Zone: The Cornucopia**
- 100-yard clearing around the horn
- Supplies arranged by value (weapons at horn, basic supplies spreading out)
- Tribute platforms arranged in circle, equidistant
- No cover, completely exposed
- Designed for bloodbath spectacle

**Hidden Locations:**
- Underground cave system (connects quadrants)
- Ancient ruins (pre-arena structure, useful for shelter)
- Gamemaker bunker access point (hidden, can be hacked)
- Weak point in force field (East sector, only discoverable with tech knowledge)

**Water Sources:**
- Northern streams (clean, cold)
- Central lake (becomes poisoned mid-game)
- Southern oases (mostly poisoned, one real)
- Rain (triggered by Gamemakers, often followed by hazards)

**Gamemaker Manipulation Points:**
- Fire wall generators (herd tributes toward confrontation)
- Flood controls (can flood caves or create streams)
- Muttation release gates (strategically placed)
- Supply drop zones (marked with smoke signals)
- Weather control (temperature, precipitation, lightning)

### Tribute Center (Capital)

**Layout:**
- 12-story tower, one floor per district
- Each floor decorated in district's industry theme
- Tributes share floor with district partner and mentor
- Roof garden (training center access)
- Ground floor: lobby, interviews stage, presentation areas

**Individual Suites:**
- Bedroom with automated closet
- Bathroom with scent-shower and styling tech
- Common room with panoramic city views
- Dining room with automated feast delivery
- All under surveillance (cameras in every room except one elevator corner)

**Training Center:**
- Weapons stations (swords, bows, spears, knives, etc.)
- Survival skills (fire-making, plant identification, trapping)
- Physical training (climbing walls, swimming, endurance)
- Camouflage and shelter building
- Hand-to-hand combat rings
- Edible plant identification
- Private sessions room for Gamemaker evaluations

**Common Areas:**
- Rooftop garden (tributes can interact)
- Dining hall (meals together)
- Interview stage (in building's atrium)

### District 13 Underground

**Level Structure:**
- Level 1-5: Living quarters (assigned by family size and status)
- Level 6-8: Hydroponics, food production
- Level 9-10: Hospital, medical research
- Level 11-12: Training facilities, shooting ranges
- Level 13: Command center, President Coin's office
- Sublevels: Nuclear arsenal (sealed after treaty)

**Daily Life:**
- Schedules printed on arms each morning (temporary tattoo)
- Communal dining (portion-controlled, nutritionally complete but bland)
- Mandatory work assignments
- Propaganda broadcasts (rebellion news, Capital criticism)
- Training for all able-bodied citizens

**Security:**
- Airtight bunker doors every level
- Can seal off compromised sections
- Decontamination chambers
- Multiple escape tunnels
- Hovercraft hangar (hidden surface access)

## Causality Chains and Political Machinations

### President Bloodworth's Calculations

**Initial Position:**
- Maintains power through fear and spectacle
- Games successfully terrorize districts for 50+ years
- Growing complacent, districts seem broken

**Inciting Incident:**
- Player tributes show defiance (berry scene or other act)
- Can't kill them publicly (too popular)
- Forced to allow dual victory

**Cascading Consequences:**
1. Districts see defiance as hope
2. Small acts of rebellion increase
3. District 8 riots openly
4. Peacekeepers respond with brutality
5. More districts join resistance
6. Quarter Quell announced as punishment (victors must fight again)
7. Players become symbols whether they want to or not

**Bloodworth's Strategy:**
- Kill player tributes in Quarter Quell (legal, in the Games)
- Crush districts one by one
- Increase surveillance and Peacekeeper presence
- Prepare for total war if necessary

**Potential Downfall:**
- Underestimates depth of rebel organization
- Doesn't know about District 13
- Paranoia leads to overreach
- Executed by rebels or own people in end

### District 13's Long Game

**Phase 1: Infiltration (Years 1-50)**
- Place agents in Capital (Plutarch, stylists, Avoxes)
- Monitor from afar, gather intelligence
- Wait for right moment

**Phase 2: Catalyst (Year 73)**
- Identify potential symbols (player tributes)
- Subtle manipulation to encourage defiance
- Extract survivors if possible
- Use their story for propaganda

**Phase 3: Open Warfare (Year 74-75)**
- Coordinate district uprisings
- Jam Capital broadcasts
- Air propaganda
- Arm rebels with smuggled weapons
- Extract key victors as symbols

**Phase 4: Assault on Capital**
- Military campaign with district armies
- Capture or kill President Bloodworth
- Dismantle Games infrastructure
- Establish new government (Coin wants power herself)

**Hidden Agenda:**
- Coin is just as authoritarian as Bloodworth
- Will sacrifice anyone for victory
- Plans to become new dictator
- Players may need to rebel against rebellion

### The Gamemaker's Dilemma

**Seneca Nightweave's Position:**
- Brilliant designer under enormous pressure
- Every year must top the last
- President executes Gamemakers who fail
- Has daughter in District 3 (secret weakness)

**His Choices:**
- Follow orders and kill tributes
- Subtly help certain tributes (player discovers his secret)
- Sabotage arena for rebellion
- Defect to District 13

**Consequences:**
- Helps players: executed by President
- Stays loyal: executed by rebels
- Defects: family endangered
- Does nothing: arena might be hacked anyway

### Sponsor Economy Politics

**Wealth Concentration:**
- Only Capital elite can afford sponsorship
- Betting markets worth millions
- Sponsors gain social status by backing winners
- Some sponsored tributes are investments

**Manipulation:**
- Mentors must grovel to secure gifts
- Tributes must perform for cameras
- Sexual favors implied for some (Finnick's situation)
- Political alliances formed through sponsorship

**Rebellion Impact:**
- Sponsors who back defiant tributes suspected
- Some sponsors secretly fund rebellion
- Economic system breaks down as war starts
- Sponsor class targeted by rebels for complicity

## Additional Lore and World History

### The Dark Days (The First Rebellion)

**Timeline:**
- Year 0: Democratic federation of districts governs peacefully
- Year 12: District 13 develops nuclear weapons
- Year 15: Civil war breaks out over resource distribution
- Year 18: District 13 threatens nuclear option
- Year 20: Capital "destroys" District 13, crushes rebellion
- Year 21: First Crimson Games held with 24 children
- Year 73: Current campaign begins

**The Treaty:**
- District 13 goes underground, pretends to be destroyed
- Capital maintains control through Games
- Neither side uses nuclear option
- Uneasy secret peace for 50 years

**Generational Trauma:**
- Every family in districts has lost someone to Games
- Mentors are all traumatized victors
- Capital citizens raised on propaganda
- Children trained to kill or be killed

### The Victor's Curse

**Life After Winning:**
- Wealth and comfort (victor's village mansion)
- Psychological trauma (killed to survive)
- Forced to mentor future tributes (watch children die)
- Capital "uses" attractive victors (Finnick situation)
- Alcoholism, drug addiction, suicide common

**Famous Victors:**
- The oldest living victor is 67, won at 16
- Some victors became Gamemakers
- District 12 has only had 3 victors in 73 years
- District 1 and 2 have victor academies

**Victor Politics:**
- Some become Capital loyalists
- Others secretly aid rebellion
- Quarter Quell forces them back to arena
- Many die in rebellion

### Magic System Integration

**Capital Technology:**
- Sufficiently advanced magic appears as technology
- Force fields (evocation magic automated)
- Muttations (transmutation and necromancy)
- Scrying orbs (divination broadcast)
- Healing pods (restoration magic industrialized)

**District Limitations:**
- Magic restricted in districts
- Peacekeepers detect unauthorized casting
- Wild magic users hidden or fled
- Some districts specialize (District 3 has artificers)

**Arena Magic:**
- Everything controlled by Gamemaker spells
- Environmental manipulation
- Creature summoning and control
- Illusion and reality blend
- Players might counter with their own magic

### Cultural Touchstones

**Capital Entertainment:**
- Reality TV culture at extreme
- Death as entertainment normalized
- Fashion and body modification as status
- Historical amnesia about pre-war era
- Bread and circuses keep citizens docile

**District Culture:**
- Each district has unique traditions
- Resilience and community bonds
- Secret resistance songs and symbols
- Black markets for forbidden goods
- Stories of pre-war freedom kept alive

**The Mockingjay:**
- Hybrid of jabberjay (Capital weapon) and mockingbird
- Symbol of rebellion (nature adapting, surviving)
- Shouldn't exist but does anyway
- Players might wear pin as symbol
- Becomes icon of revolution

### Post-Games Society

**If Rebellion Succeeds:**
- Trials for Capital war criminals
- Reconstruction of districts
- Abolishment of Games
- Risk of new tyranny (Coin)
- Generational healing needed
- Victors can finally have peace
- Or new conflict begins

**If Capital Wins:**
- Brutal suppression
- Games become even crueler
- Districts broken for another generation
- Underground resistance continues
- Hope delayed, not destroyed

**Most Likely: Complicated Victory:**
- Bloodworth dead but Coin authoritarian
- Players must prevent new tyranny
- Games end but trauma remains
- Long road to actual freedom
- Epilogue shows gradual healing

This world is designed to provide both the spectacle of the death game and the deeper political intrigue of revolution, giving players agency to be more than just pieces in the Games.
