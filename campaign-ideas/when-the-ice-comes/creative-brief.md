# When the Ice Comes - Creative Brief

## Art Style and Visual Tone

### Overall Aesthetic
**Frozen Apocalypse Cinema**
- Bleak, desaturated color palette dominated by whites, greys, and blues
- Contrast between warm firelight and cold blue shadows
- Emphasis on breath visible in cold air, frost on everything
- Visual progression from autumn colors to complete white-out

### Visual Progression
**Act 1: The Freeze Arrives**
- Golden autumn suddenly interrupted by frost
- Vibrant harvest colors frozen mid-season
- Shocking juxtaposition of life and death
- First snowflakes become blizzard

**Act 2: The White World**
- Everything covered in snow and ice
- Frozen cities like ice sculptures
- Refugee camps with orange firelight against white
- Desperate humanity clinging to warmth

**Act 3: The Deep Freeze**
- Blue-white landscape, alien and hostile
- Ice formations defy physics
- Aurora effects, strange ice crystals
- Beauty in the apocalypse

**Act 4: The Prison of Ice**
- Crystalline alien architecture
- Bioluminescent ice
- Titan imagery—colossal and ancient
- Final confrontation in ice cathedral

## Color Palettes

- **Primary**: Fire orange (#FF4500), Warm amber (#FFBF00), Cold blue exterior (#1E90FF)
- **Accent**: Soot black (#2C2C2C), Copper glow (#B87333)

### The Frozen Wastes (Sessions 11-30)
- **Primary**: Ice white (#FFFFFF), Glacier blue (#B0E0E6), Shadow grey (#4F4F4F)
- **Accent**: Blood red (rare and shocking), Aurora green (#50C878)
- **Mood**: Desolate, hostile, alien beauty

### Vorgrimm's Prison (Sessions 31-36)
- **Primary**: Crystal blue (#4169E1), Deep black (#000000), Ethereal white (#E6F3FF)
- **Accent**: Ancient gold (#CFB53B), Titan silver (#C0C0C0)
- **Mood**: Ancient, powerful, transcendent

## Music and Sound Design

### Main Theme: "The Ice Comes"
- Starts with warm orchestral autumn melody
- Slowly corrupted by cold synth sounds
- Wind instruments transition to howling winds
- Final movement: Primal percussion and choir
- 5:00 runtime, tells story of world freezing

### Location Themes

**Millhaven (Lost City)**
- Nostalgic autumn festival music
- Suddenly interrupted by dissonant cold tones
- Transition represents world's death
- Haunting music box playing broken melody in ice

**Flamekeep (Last Light)**
- Heroic brass representing hope
- Crackling fire sounds as percussion
- Underlying tension—strings suggesting time running out
- Each reprise slightly colder, more desperate

**The Frozen North**
- Minimalist soundscape
- Wind howling constantly
- Creaking ice, groaning glaciers
- Occasional ghostly whispers
- Beauty in bleakness

**Vorgrimm's Prison**
- Deep bass notes like breathing glaciers
- Crystalline chimes creating alien melody
- Titan heartbeat as percussion
- Ancient and patient power

### Character Themes

**Lord Pyrewood: "The Dying Light"**
- Warm strings gradually fading
- Fire crackle and pop sounds
- Heroic but exhausted
- Each reprise weaker, slower

**Dr. Frostbane: "Against the Cold"**
- Precise, mathematical rhythm
- Wind instruments fighting against cold sounds
- Determined, never gives up
- Hope through science

**General Arcturus: "The Hard Choices"**
- Military drums, precise and cold
- No warmth in instrumentation
- Necessary evil tonality
- Tragic undertones

**Vorgrimm: "Glacier's Dream"**
- Ancient beyond time
- Grinding ice as music
- Patient, inevitable
- Both beautiful and terrifying

## Voice Direction for Key NPCs

**Lord Aldric Pyrewood**
- Voice: Warm baritone, gradually becoming hoarse
- Speaks like gentle father even while dying
- Occasional coughs hidden
- "Stay warm, my friends. While I breathe, you'll have fire."

**Dr. Elena Frostbane**
- Voice: Rapid alto, clinical precision
- Technical jargon when stressed
- Rare emotional breaks reveal depth
- "The temperature curve suggests—no, *proves*—we have 40 days."

**General Marcus Arcturus**
- Voice: Cold bass, military precision
- No inflection, pure pragmatism
- Believes every word
- "I'll save a million by sacrificing a thousand. That's not murder. That's mathematics."

**The Ice Prophet**
- Voice: Ethereal, dual-toned (possessed)
- Serene despite madness
- Beautiful but unsettling
- "The cold is peace. The freeze is mercy. Why do you fight paradise?"

**Vorgrimm the Frozen**
- Voice: Multiple layers—creaking ice, wind, ancient speech
- Each word takes time, like glaciers moving
- Not angry, just weary
- "I have dreamed... for ten thousand years... of justice."

## Cinematic Moments

**Session 1: The Festival Freeze**
- Golden hour autumn festival, warm and vibrant
- Temperature drops visible in real-time
- Breath becomes visible, frost spreads across surfaces
- Sudden freeze—people screaming, crops dying
- Slow-motion snowflakes beginning to fall
- Shift from warm colors to cold blue filter

**Session 7: First Sight of the Ice Wall**
- Party crests hill, sees the wall on horizon
- Miles-high glacier advancing slowly but visibly
- Tiny figures fleeing before it
- Aerial shot showing scale—wall spans horizon
- Grinding, groaning sound of moving ice
- Psychological horror—inevitable doom approaching

**Session 15: Flamekeep's Lights**
- Approach city at night during blizzard
- Warm orange glow through snow
- Camera tracks through refugee camps to inner city
- Contrast: Freezing slums vs. warm elite quarters
- Lord Pyrewood atop tower, hands glowing, visibly exhausted
- City exists because one man refuses to stop burning

**Session 31: Entering the Prison**
- Descend into glacier through ancient portal
- Ice transitions from natural to architectural
- Crystalline chambers with impossible geometry
- Bioluminescent ice casting ethereal light
- Chamber of memories—ice statues of ancient imprisoners
- Final door: Massive, covered in frost, forbidding

**Session 35: Facing Vorgrimm**
- Titan revealed in stasis chamber
- Colossal beyond comprehension—mile long body
- Crystalline prison barely containing him
- Eye opens—blue as deep ice, ancient as time
- Voice shakes the chamber
- Choice moment—fight, flee, or talk?

**Session 36: The Thaw (Good Ending)**
- Sun breaks through clouds for first time in months
- Ice begins to drip—sound of melting
- Refugees emerge from shelters, feeling warmth
- Slow montage of world coming back to life
- Spring arrives impossibly fast
- Bittersweet—show the cost, the graves, the changes
- Final shot: Green shoot emerging from melting snow
