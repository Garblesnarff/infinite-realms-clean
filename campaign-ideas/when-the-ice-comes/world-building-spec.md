# When the Ice Comes - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Flamekeep Council (Government)
**Leadership**: Lord Aldric Pyrewood (dying fire mage), Council of Seven
**Population**: 100,000 original citizens, now 2 million refugees
**Goals**: Maintain order, ration resources, save as many as possible
**Resources**: City infrastructure, military, Lord Pyrewood's magic
**Conflicts**: Original citizens vs. refugees, resource allocation, class warfare

**Key Members**:
- Lord Aldric Pyrewood (Ruler, living furnace keeping city warm)
- Councilor Helena Ashworth (Treasury, controls resource distribution)
- Councilor Marcus Stone (Security, prevents riots)
- Councilor Elara Brightflame (Fire mage, Pyrewood's apprentice, successor)

### The Frost Wardens (Dr. Frostbane's Researchers)
**Leadership**: Dr. Elena Frostbane
**Membership**: 50 scholars, wizards, and scientists
**Goals**: Study the freeze, find magical/scientific solution
**Resources**: Laboratory, rare books, scrying equipment
**Base**: The Frozen Archive (research facility in Flamekeep)

**Research Divisions**:
- Meteorological Analysis (weather prediction)
- Historical Records (ancient titan lore)
- Magical Theory (counter-cold spells)
- Expedition Support (gear for party)

### The Continental Defense Force
**Leadership**: General Marcus Arcturus
**Membership**: 20,000 soldiers (all that remains of multiple nations' armies)
**Goals**: Maintain order, control resources, ensure military survival
**Base**: Fort Winter (military district in Flamekeep)

**Military Strategy**:
- Enforce strict rationing
- Control fuel distribution
- Prevent refugee riots
- Potential coup against civilian government
- "Survival of the fittest" philosophy

### The Cult of the Frozen God
**Leadership**: The Ice Prophet (possessed oracle)
**Membership**: 5,000 and growing daily
**Goals**: Embrace the cold, prepare for "ascension," serve Vorgrimm unknowingly
**Base**: The Frost Cathedral (converted warehouse, frozen interior)

**Beliefs**:
- Cold is divine purification
- Warmth is corruption and sin
- Those who embrace cold will "ascend"
- The freeze is judgment on the unworthy

**Rituals**:
- Ice Baptism: Strip and walk into blizzard (fatal)
- Frost Communion: Meditate in extreme cold until visions
- The Giving: Sabotage heat sources as "offerings"
- Transcendence: Final ritual, freeze to death willingly

### The Refugee Coalition
**Leadership**: Elected representatives from various displaced nations
**Membership**: 1.9 million desperate survivors
**Goals**: Survive, get fair treatment, find new homes
**Resources**: Only their numbers and skills

**Major Refugee Groups**:
- The Northmen (first frozen, toughest survivors)
- The Farmers (know food production)
- The Craftsmen (valuable skills)
- The Lost (those who've given up)

### The Everflame Seekers
**Leadership**: Sister Kindra (fire cleric)
**Membership**: 200 pilgrims
**Goals**: Find the legendary Everflame to save the world
**Base**: Mobile—caravan moving toward volcanic regions

**Quest**: Believe ancient eternal fire can reverse the freeze
- May be right or wrong
- Dangerous journey through frozen wastes
- Party can join or compete with them

### The Dragon Council
**Leadership**: Ignaroth the Ancient (red dragon patriarch)
**Membership**: 50 adult dragons of various types
**Goals**: Survive the freeze, protect dragon species
**Base**: Everflame Peaks (volcanic mountain range)

**Dragon Politics**:
- Reds want to charge exorbitant price for warming land
- Golds willing to help but lack power alone
- Blues indifferent to mortal suffering
- Greens see opportunity for control

## Location Blueprints and Disaster Zones

### Flamekeep (The Last Warm City)

**Inner Ring: The Fire Heart**
- Warmest zone (60°F), elite district
- Original 10,000 richest citizens
- Luxurious despite apocalypse
- Resentment from outer rings
- Lord Pyrewood's palace at center

**Middle Ring: The Commons**
- Moderate temperature (40°F)
- Original middle class + organized refugees
- Crowded but functional
- Markets, workshops, schools
- Most stable social zone

**Outer Ring: The Slums**
- Barely above freezing (35°F)
- Refugee shanty towns
- Disease, crime, desperation
- Majority of population
- Fuel theft common

**The Walls: Military Zone**
- Fort Winter, barracks, armories
- General Arcturus's domain
- Checkpoints control access
- Refugees kept out
- Well-heated, well-fed (creates resentment)

**The Frost Cathedral: Cult Territory**
- Abandoned warehouse district
- Intentionally unheated
- Ice sculptures, frozen worshippers
- Creepy and beautiful
- Dangerous to enter

### The Frozen North (Lost Kingdoms)

**The Flash-Frozen Cities**
- Entire populations frozen mid-action
- Perfect preservation in ice
- Eerie ghost towns
- Valuable salvage (food, fuel, gold)
- Haunted by frost zombies and ice ghosts

**The Ice Wall**
- Miles-high advancing glacier
- Visible from 100 miles away
- Grows southward daily (50 feet/day)
- Unstoppable, inevitable
- Climbing it is dangerous but necessary
- Inside the ice: ancient things frozen long ago

**The Howling Wastes**
- Plains between wall and civilization
- Constant blizzards, whiteout conditions
- Flash freeze zones
- Ice elementals and winter monsters
- Refugee caravans struggling south
- Abandoned vehicles and frozen corpses mark the trail

### The Everflame Peaks (Volcanic Refuge)

**Geography**: Active volcano chain, last warm zone
**Temperature**: Varies from pleasant to scorching
**Population**: 500,000 refugees, 50 dragons

**Districts**:
- The Dragon Eyries (mountain peaks, dragons only)
- Lava Fields (hot but survivable with care)
- Ash Plains (refugee camps in volcanic ash)
- The Sulfur Mines (desperate mining for fuel)

**Dragon Council Chamber**
- Inside volcanic caldera
- Impossible heat for humans
- Negotiation requires fire resistance magic
- Impressive and terrifying

### Vorgrimm's Prison (Polar Dungeon)

**Level 1: The Entrance**
- Ancient doors in polar ice
- Rune locks requiring specific keys
- Guardian creatures (frost golems)
- First hint of titan's power

**Level 2-3: The Memory Chambers**
- Ice sculptures showing history
- Echoes of imprisoners (can interact)
- Learn truth about Vorgrimm
- Environmental puzzles

**Level 4-6: The Trials**
- Each chamber tests party
- Trial of Cold (endurance)
- Trial of Loss (sacrifice something precious)
- Trial of Wisdom (answer riddles)
- Trial of Strength (combat)
- Trial of Mercy (spare enemy)

**Level 7: The Frost Giant Sanctum**
- Princess Crystalia's domain
- Negotiation or battle
- She guards father willingly
- Knows all secrets

**Level 8: The Dreaming Chamber**
- Vorgrimm's actual prison
- Colossal titan in stasis
- Crystalline restraints failing
- Final confrontation
- Multiple resolution options

## Causality Chains and Catastrophe Progression

### Timeline of the Freeze

**10,000 Years Ago**: Ancient civilization imprisons Vorgrimm
- Unjust punishment for refusing genocide
- Titan sealed in polar ice
- Prison designed to last forever
- Builders die, prison forgotten

**50 Years Ago**: Earthquake damages prison
- Natural disaster weakens seals
- No one notices—too remote
- Vorgrimm begins to stir
- Dreams start to affect reality

**6 Months Ago**: First temperature drops
- Unseasonable cold in far north
- Scientists puzzled but not alarmed
- "Just weird weather"

**3 Months Ago**: The Flash Freeze
- Northern kingdoms frozen in days
- Millions dead instantly
- Refugees flee south
- Global panic begins

**Campaign Start**: Temperature -40°F in temperate zones
- 5°F drop per week
- Organized society still functioning
- Flamekeep becomes refuge
- Party enters story

**Month 1-2**: The Great Migration
- Entire populations moving south
- Resources strained
- Social order breaking down
- Cult forms
- Military considers coup

**Month 3**: Temperature reaches -100°F
- Exposed skin freezes in seconds
- Magical heat required constantly
- Fuel critically scarce
- Desperation at maximum

**Final Month**: The Race to Prison
- Temperature -150°F and dropping
- Party at polar prison
- Vorgrimm nearly awake
- Multiple faction assaults on prison
- Convergence of all plotlines
- Resolution determines world's fate

### Environmental Progression

**Stage 1: Autumn Dies**
- Crops freeze in fields
- Livestock die
- Famine begins
- Beautiful and tragic

**Stage 2: Winter Without End**
- Snow never stops
- Sun rarely seen
- Darkness and cold
- Psychological toll

**Stage 3: The Deep Freeze**
- Ocean begins freezing
- Air itself hurts to breathe
- Technology fails (metal shatters)
- Survival requires magic

**Stage 4: The Final Cold**
- Approaching absolute zero
- Reality itself crystallizes
- Magic strained
- Life impossible without extraordinary measures

### Social Collapse Progression

**Week 1-4**: Denial and Confusion
- "This will pass"
- Governments try normal responses
- Panic buying
- Some flee, most stay

**Month 2-3**: Breakdown
- Governments overwhelmed
- Refugee crisis
- Resource wars
- Rise of strongmen

**Month 4-6**: New Order
- Survival communities form
- Barter economy
- Might makes right
- Tribalism

**Month 6+**: Post-Apocalyptic
- Scattered enclaves
- Desperate scavenging
- Cannibalism (dark rumors)
- Humanity's worst and best

## Additional Lore and World History

### The Truth About Vorgrimm

**Origin**: Primordial titan of ice and winter, born with the world
**Nature**: Not evil, embodies natural cold and winter seasons
**Crime**: Refused orders to exterminate an entire sentient species
**Punishment**: Imprisoned for "insubordination" and "mercy toward enemies"
**Reality**: Ancient civilization was genocidal, Vorgrimm was moral
**Current State**: Dreaming of freedom, unaware dreams are killing world
**Personality**: Patient, ancient, wise, weary, lonely, angry at injustice

### Princess Crystalia's Burden

Vorgrimm's daughter, born after his imprisonment. Chose to guard his prison willingly, believing it necessary to protect the world. Torn between love for father and duty to mortals. Has guarded for 8,000 years. Lonely beyond measure. Knows father is innocent but fears releasing him. Can be reasoned with. Wants someone else to make the choice so she doesn't have to.

### The Everflame Legend

Ancient eternal fire supposedly created by the gods. May or may not exist. Various groups seek it. Truth: It's a massive fire elemental bound to volcanic core. Can be negotiated with or enslaved. Powerful enough to warm continent but has own agenda. Chaotic neutral entity that enjoys burning things.

### Alternative Solutions (Player Discovery)

1. **The Sacrifice Play**: Party member becomes new prison warden, merges with containment field
2. **The Negotiation**: Convince Vorgrimm to sleep again willingly in exchange for acknowledgment of innocence
3. **The Relocation**: Move Vorgrimm to para-elemental plane of ice where he can exist freely
4. **The Balance**: Create zone where Vorgrimm can exist free but contained (permanent arctic zone)
5. **The Destruction**: Kill Vorgrimm (hard, has consequences, releases different problems)

### The Cultist's Perspective

They're not wrong that the old world was corrupt and flawed. The freeze has revealed humanity's worst: Hoarding, cruelty, selfishness. But also humanity's best: Sacrifice, cooperation, heroism. The cult sees only the worst and believes cold will "purify" it. They're being manipulated by Vorgrimm's unconscious dreams but their grievances are legitimate. Some can be saved by showing them the good in humanity.

### Long-term Consequences

**If Vorgrimm Re-Imprisoned**:
- Climate returns to normal
- But problem kicks down road
- Future generation must deal with it
- Nothing fundamentally changes

**If Vorgrimm Freed**:
- Permanent winter zone at pole
- Colder global climate
- Society adapts
- New normal, different but livable

**If Vorgrimm Destroyed**:
- Climate instability
- Extreme weather
- Loss of winter cycle
- Different problems emerge

**If Compromise Reached**:
- Vorgrimm gets limited freedom
- Seasons remain but colder
- Ongoing relationship with titan
- Best long-term solution
