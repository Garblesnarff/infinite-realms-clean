
# The 108 Stars of Rising

- **Campaign Type / Genre**: War Epic / Base Building / Tragedy / Political Intrigue
- **Estimated Length**: 25-30 sessions
- **Player Level Range**: 1-15
- **Tone**: Epic, Tragic, Hopeful, Bittersweet

### Core Premise
Two childhood friends - the player and their best friend Jowy - are drawn into a brutal war between the City-States of Jowston and the Highland Kingdom. After witnessing a massacre orchestrated by their own side to justify war, the friends are branded as traitors and flee. The campaign follows two parallel paths: the player builds an army of 108 Stars of Destiny (legendary heroes fated to gather in times of crisis) and establishes a revolutionary base, while Jowy infiltrates Highland and rises to power from within. As the war escalates and the player's Liberation Army grows, they must eventually face their best friend across the battlefield. The campaign explores the tragedy of war, the corrupting nature of power, and whether friendship can survive when two people choose opposing paths toward the same goal: peace.

### Story Arc

**Act 1: The Betrayal (Sessions 1-8)**
- The players begin as young soldiers in the Highland Army's Youth Brigade during a border skirmish with Jowston. They witness their commander massacre his own troops and blame Jowston to justify invasion. Branded as traitors for surviving, the players flee with Jowy and a small group. They're rescued by Viktor, a mercenary working for the nascent Liberation Army. The players help establish a base of operations in an abandoned castle and begin recruiting the Stars of Destiny - legendary heroes whose fates are intertwined. Each recruited Star brings new capabilities to the base and army.

**Act 2: The War Escalates (Sessions 9-20)**
- The Liberation Army grows as the players recruit more Stars, each with their own quests and character arcs. The base expands with facilities: blacksmith, inn, library, war room, trade post. Players lead battles using tactical warfare (army vs. army) and personal missions (infiltration, assassination, rescue). Meanwhile, Jowy has infiltrated Highland and rises through its ranks, genuinely believing he can end the war from within by seizing power and negotiating peace. The players face moral choices: when to show mercy, whether to accept morally gray allies, how to handle war crimes. Jowy becomes Highland's king through political maneuvering and marriage, setting up the inevitable confrontation.

**Act 3: The Final Battle (Sessions 21-30)**
- With all 108 Stars gathered, the Liberation Army is powerful enough to challenge Highland directly. But Jowy, now King of Highland, genuinely wants peace - he's just chosen a different path. The players must choose whether to destroy Highland militarily or seek diplomatic solution. Betrayals from within both sides (those who profit from war) force conflict. The campaign builds to a tragic duel between the player and Jowy, with the fate of both nations hanging in the balance. The ending depends on player choices throughout: whether they showed mercy, how many Stars survived, whether they found a way to reconcile with Jowy. The best ending is bittersweet - peace is achieved, but at tremendous cost, and the friendship may or may not be salvageable.

### Session Breakdown

- **Session 1-2: The Massacre**: Players fight in a border skirmish. Their commander slaughters the Youth Brigade and frames Jowston. Players barely escape.
- **Session 3-4: Branded Traitors**: Hunted by Highland, the players flee with Jowy and allies. Viktor's mercenaries rescue them and introduce them to the Liberation Army.
- **Session 5-8: The Base**: Players claim an abandoned castle as their headquarters. First Stars of Destiny join. Begin recruiting members with diverse skills.
- **Session 9-11: Army Building**: Recruit more Stars through quests - rescue a prisoner, challenge a master swordsman, convince a merchant, etc. Each adds capabilities.
- **Session 12-14: First Campaigns**: Lead the Liberation Army in tactical battles against Highland. Choices in warfare affect reputation and future recruitment.
- **Session 15-17: Jowy's Path**: Learn that Jowy has infiltrated Highland's royal court. He sends messages explaining his plan to achieve peace from within. Players must decide if he's betraying them or playing a long game.
- **Session 18-20: The Growing Divide**: Jowy becomes king through political maneuvering. The players liberate cities but face moral quandaries - handle surrendering enemies, deal with war crimes, balance justice and mercy.
- **Session 21-23: The 108th Star**: The final Star of Destiny appears, completing the prophesied gathering. The Liberation Army is now powerful enough to end the war.
- **Session 24-26: The Beast Rune**: Discover that Jowy wields the Beast Rune (parallel to the player's Bright Shield Rune), binding their fates. They are destined to clash.
- **Session 27-29: The Final Campaign**: Massive battle between Liberation Army and Highland. Tactical warfare on grand scale. Betrayals from war-profiteers on both sides force the conflict.
- **Session 30: The Duel**: One-on-one confrontation with Jowy. Depending on choices throughout the campaign, this can end in death, reconciliation, or bittersweet peace. The war ends but the cost is measured.

### Major NPCs

- **Jowy Atreides**: Your childhood best friend, co-protagonist turned tragic antagonist. **Role:** Deuteragonist / Tragic Friend / Final Boss. **Personality:** Idealistic, strategic, willing to bear terrible burdens for peace. **Flaw/Secret:** His path to power requires compromises that change him; wields the Beast Rune that binds his fate to yours.
- **Nanami**: Your adoptive older sister, fiercely protective and cheerful. **Role:** Heart of the party / Moral anchor. **Personality:** Optimistic, determined to keep you safe, hates war. **Flaw/Secret:** Will sacrifice herself to save you if not stopped; represents the innocent caught in war.
- **Viktor**: Gruff mercenary who becomes a loyal ally. **Role:** Mentor / Military Commander. **Personality:** Tough exterior, soft heart, experienced warrior haunted by past. **Flaw/Secret:** Failed to protect someone precious years ago; sees redemption in this war.
- **Flik**: Viktor's partner, a tactical genius with a lightning-fast sword. **Role:** Strategist / Lightning Mage. **Personality:** Cool, analytical, dedicated to justice. **Flaw/Secret:** Lost his mentor Odessa, former Liberation Army leader; carries her ideals.
- **Luca Blight**: The Highland Prince, a monster who revels in slaughter. **Role:** Primary Antagonist (Mid-Campaign). **Personality:** Sadistic, brilliant tactician, irredeemable. **Flaw/Secret:** Was tortured as a child watching his mother assaulted, breaking his mind; becomes the campaign's most terrifying boss fight.
- **Shu**: A brilliant but lazy strategist who joins the Liberation Army. **Role:** Master Tactician. **Personality:** Genius who prefers napping, will do anything to win. **Flaw/Secret:** His ruthless tactics in a previous war led to tragedy; questions whether winning justifies any means.
- **Apple**: Shu's loyal student and scribe. **Role:** Assistant / Chronicler. **Personality:** Dutiful, intelligent, devoted to recording history. **Flaw/Secret:** Struggles with Shu's ruthless methods but follows anyway.
- **Pilika**: A young girl traumatized into silence by Luca Blight's massacre of her village. **Role:** Symbol of War's Cost. **Personality:** Silent, haunted, slowly heals with kindness. **Flaw/Secret:** Her trauma represents what the war does to innocents; healing her is optional but meaningful.
- **The 108 Stars of Destiny**: A diverse cast including warriors, mages, merchants, chefs, artists, children, and more. **Role:** Army and Base Population. **Personality:** Varies wildly. **Flaw/Secret:** Each Star has their own quest and character arc; recruiting all 108 is the campaign's collection quest.

### Key Locations

- **The Liberation Army Headquarters**: An abandoned castle that becomes the player's base, expanding as more Stars join. Contains war room, barracks, shops, inn, library, bath house, and individual rooms for each Star.
- **Highland Kingdom**: Militaristic nation ruled by a mad king and his psychotic son Luca. Capital city features the royal palace, military academy, and ornate architecture hiding brutal oppression.
- **City-States of Jowston**: Democratic alliance of independent cities. Includes Muse (political capital), South Window (military city), and others. Each has distinct culture.
- **The Forest Village**: Kobold settlement in ancient woods, representative of non-human races caught between human wars.
- **Tinto**: Mining city focused on profit, reluctant to join either side. Represents neutrality's impossibility in total war.
- **Gregminster**: Capital of the Scarlet Moon Empire (from previous generation), now at peace but relevant to Viktor and Flik's backstories.
- **Tenzan Pass**: Treacherous mountain crossing where the initial massacre occurred. Site of painful memories and important battles.
- **The Final Battlefield**: Where the Liberation Army and Highland's forces meet for the decisive confrontation. Depends on player choices throughout campaign.

### Central Conflict
Two friends who want the same thing - peace - choose opposite paths and become enemies. The player builds an army from outside the system while Jowy seizes power from within. The war forces both to make terrible compromises, and the question becomes whether either path is truly righteous or if war corrupts all it touches. Themes include the cyclical nature of violence, the cost of idealism, the tragedy of war, and whether friendship and brotherhood can survive fundamental disagreement about methods. The central conflict is external (war between nations) and internal (reconciling with your best friend across the battlefield).

### Unique Mechanics

- **The 108 Stars of Destiny**: The campaign's core collection quest. Each Star is a unique recruitable NPC with specific requirements - some join automatically through story, others require side quests, some need specific conditions (have certain items, reach certain story points, make specific choices). Track them on a roster. Recruiting all 108 unlocks the best ending. Each Star contributes to the base - merchants open shops, chefs improve the inn, smiths forge better weapons, strategists provide army options.
- **Base Building System**: The Liberation Army HQ grows dynamically. As Stars join, new facilities appear: the blacksmith forges better equipment, the merchant offers rare items, the library provides lore, the bath house gives buffs, etc. Players can walk through the base between sessions, interacting with Stars and seeing how it evolves. Certain Stars enable specific base functions (a teleporter requires the magician Star, the vault requires the thief Star).
- **Tactical Army Battles**: Large-scale warfare uses a simplified tactical system. Players command units (cavalry, infantry, archers, magic) against enemy formations. Success depends on troop positioning, terrain, and strategic choices. Winning battles requires understanding rock-paper-scissors dynamics and using hero units (the Stars) at critical moments. Losing doesn't mean campaign failure but has consequences - lost territory, dead soldiers, reduced morale.
- **Duels**: One-on-one battles use a psychological prediction system. Both combatants choose from Attack (aggressive), Defend (careful), or Desperate Attack (wild). Choices are revealed simultaneously. Attack beats Defend, Defend beats Desperate Attack, Desperate Attack beats Attack. Players must read their opponent's dialogue and attitude to predict their choice. The final confrontation with Jowy is a climactic duel.
- **War Council Choices**: Before major battles, the war council (Shu, Flik, Apple, and recruited strategist Stars) debates strategy. Players choose between aggressive, defensive, or underhanded tactics. Each choice has consequences - aggressive wins faster but costs more lives, defensive preserves troops but loses time, underhanded achieves objectives through morally gray means. Track these choices as they affect the ending.
- **Bright Shield and Beast Runes**: The player bears the Bright Shield Rune (protection, defense, gathering allies) while Jowy wields the Beast Rune (power, conquest, individual might). These ancient runes bind their fates together. Mechanically, they provide powerful abilities but using them comes at a cost - the Bright Shield draws more people to your cause but puts them in danger, the Beast Rune grants Jowy strength but isolates him.
- **The Bittersweet Meter**: Track major moral choices, shows of mercy vs. ruthlessness, and relationship points with Jowy (maintained through his letters and meetings). This hidden meter determines which ending is available. The best ending requires recruiting all Stars, making compassionate choices, and maintaining connection with Jowy despite everything.
