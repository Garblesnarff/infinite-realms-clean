# Campaign Bible: The Dark Lord's Day Job

## 1. DEEP LORE & HISTORY
[TAG: LORE_CORE]

### Origin Myth: The Great Resignation
In the beginning of the realm of Ente Isla, there was the Architect, a primordial deity of immense power and cosmic boredom. After setting the laws of physics and magic, it grew weary of the predictable cycle of creation and destruction. In an event known as the "Great Resignation," the Architect simply clocked out, leaving behind a power vacuum and a single, cryptic out-of-office message: "Figure it out yourselves." This act shattered the divine order, creating two opposing forces: **The Law of Narrative**, a metaphysical force compelling beings into heroic and villainous roles to give the world meaning, and **The Entropy of the Void**, a force seeking to erase the now-unsupervised creation. The Demon Lord Maou was not born of evil, but was an ambitious being pushed by the Law of Narrative into the "villain" role. The Hero Emilia was the world's antibody, a mortal empowered by the same Law to fill the "hero" role. Their war was, in essence, the world's desperate attempt to write its own story. The portal that sent them to Earth was not a targeted banishment, but a cosmic "wrong number," a glitch in the fabric of a reality that had lost its divine IT support.

### Timeline of Ente Isla & Earth
1.  **-5000 Y (The Great Resignation):** The Architect vanishes. Magic becomes chaotic.
2.  **-4000 Y (The Narrative Schism):** The Law of Narrative and the Entropy of the Void emerge as opposing forces. The first "Heroes" and "Villains" are created by cosmic circumstance.
3.  **-1000 Y (The Rise of the Demon Clan):** Maou's ancestors unite disparate "monster" races under a single banner, seeking stability in a world of forced conflict.
4.  **-50 Y (Maou's Ascension):** Maou Satan, a charismatic and brilliant strategist, becomes the new Demon Lord. He seeks not to destroy, but to conquer and rule the world to end the pointless cycle of war.
5.  **-10 Y (The Grand Campaign):** Maou launches his final war, conquering three of the four continents of Ente Isla.
6.  **-1 Y (The Hero's Rise):** Emilia Justina, a simple farm girl, is chosen by the Law of Narrative. She is granted the holy sword "Better Half" and begins her quest.
7.  **Day 0 (The Fall):** Emilia and her companions push Maou back to his citadel. In a final, desperate gambit, Maou opens a portal to another realm to escape and regroup. The portal malfunctions, pulling him and his general Alciel to Earth.
8.  **Day 1 (The Discovery):** Maou and Alciel discover they are in modern-day Tokyo (or NYC, for campaign flavor), their magic reserves critically low.
9.  **Day 30 (The Application):** After a month of near-starvation and confusion, Maou lands a job at "MgRonald's."
10. **Present Day:** Maou has been promoted to Shift Manager. He has a budget, a tiny apartment, and a new nemesis: the lunch rush. The Hero Emilia arrives, equally powerless and confused.

### The Metaphysical Conflict
The core conflict is **Narrative vs. Mundanity**. On Ente Isla, beings are forced into epic roles. On Earth, the Law of Narrative is weak, replaced by the "Law of the Grind"â€”the need to work, pay rent, and find meaning in small, everyday moments. The party's struggle is to reconcile their epic past with their mundane present. Is true power conquering a world, or is it making it to the end of a double shift with your sanity intact? The Entropy of the Void, meanwhile, sees Earth's relative lack of magic as a "weak point" in the multiverse, a back door to unraveling all of creation.

## 2. FACTIONS
[TAG: FACTION_DATA]

### 1. MgRonald's Corporate (The Golden Arches)
**Type:** Corporate Hierarchy / Unknowing Power
**Leader:** CEO Ronald (An ancient, gold-hoarding Dragon in a human suit)
**Asset:** **The Customer is Always Right (Metaphysical Law):** This corporate mantra has a strange, binding power in their restaurants, forcing minor compliance from even powerful beings.
**Rivals:** The Burger Monarchs, The Health Inspectorate.

### 2. The Magister's Conclave (The Veil Keepers)
**Type:** Magical Shadow Government
**Leader:** The Magister (A former Demon Lord who chose retirement on Earth)
**Asset:** **The Veil:** A global spell that hides magic from mundane perception. They can selectively "un-Veil" individuals or areas.
**Rivals:** The Heavenly Host (jurisdictional disputes), The Infernal Revenue Service.

### 3. The Heavenly Host (Observation Division)
**Type:** Celestial Bureaucracy
**Leader:** Director Shiro Ashiya (An angel in the guise of a landlord)
**Asset:** **The Panopticon:** A network of angelic watchers disguised as pigeons and security cameras.
**Rivals:** The Infernal Revenue Service.
**Agenda:** To observe and determine if fallen beings like Maou are capable of redemption.

### 4. The Infernal Revenue Service (I.R.S.)
**Type:** Demonic Accountants / Soul Collectors
**Leader:** Auditor Belphegor
**Asset:** **Binding Contracts:** Their paperwork is magically binding. Signing a "1040-EZ" form might accidentally sell your soul.
**Rivals:** The Heavenly Host.
**Agenda:** To collect "soul taxes" from supernatural beings living on Earth.

### 5. The Sanitation Guild (The Undercity)
**Type:** Goblin & Kobold Labor Union
**Leader:** Foreman Grizelda "The Grimy"
**Asset:** **The Sewer Network:** They control the city's underbelly, which contains secret paths and forgotten magic.
**Rivals:** The Bodega Cats Network (territorial disputes).

### 6. The Subway Wyrms
**Type:** Ancient Draconic Beings
**Leader:** The Grand Conductor (An ancient Lung Dragon)
**Asset:** **Control of Transit:** They can delay trains, create phantom subway cars, and control the flow of people through the city.
**Rivals:** The Sanitation Guild (who sometimes block their tunnels).

### 7. The Bodega Cats Network
**Type:** Sentient Animal Information Brokers
**Leader:** "El Gato" (A fat ginger tabby who is secretly a Rakshasa)
**Asset:** **Ubiquitous Surveillance:** There is a bodega cat on every corner. They see and hear everything.
**Rivals:** The Heavenly Host's pigeon-spies.

### 8. The Central Park Fey (The Concrete Court)
**Type:** Urban Fae Aristocracy
**Leader:** Queen Titania of the Great Lawn
**Asset:** **Pollen of Confusion:** A magical pollen that makes mortals forget seeing supernatural events.
**Rivals:** The Magister's Conclave (who see them as chaotic).

### 9. The Legion of the Fallen (Sariel's Crew)
**Type:** Rogue Demonic Splinter Group
**Leader:** General Sariel
**Asset:** **Stolen Life-Force:** They are draining small amounts of life from mortals to fuel their magic, causing a "wave of ennui" in the city.
**Rivals:** Maou's faction, The Hero Emilia.

### 10. The Order of the Tin Foil Hat
**Type:** Human Conspiracy Theorists
**Leader:** "Mad" Eddie
**Asset:** **The Signal:** They have a pirate radio show that, by sheer coincidence, can sometimes disrupt magical frequencies.
**Rivals:** The Veil (which they are trying to expose).

## 3. NPC ROSTER
[TAG: NPC_TIER_1]

### Major NPCs (20 Profiles)

1.  **Maou Satan** (Demon Lord / Shift Manager) - **Personality:** Proud, intelligent, surprisingly responsible. **Voice:** Deep, commanding, but often laced with the weary sigh of a retail worker. **Goal:** Conserve magic, find a way home, and maybe get a raise. **Secret:** He's starting to genuinely care about his employees.
2.  **General Alciel** (Demon General / House-Husband) - **Personality:** Fiercely loyal, dramatic, a master of budgeting. **Voice:** Formal, booming, prone to panicked shrieking over grocery prices. **Goal:** Ensure Lord Maou's health and dignity. **Secret:** He secretly enjoys daytime television.
3.  **Hero Emilia Justina** (Human Hero / Call Center Agent) - **Personality:** Righteous, earnest, socially awkward. **Voice:** Strong, clear, but often hesitant in social situations. **Goal:** Defeat the Demon Lord (once she figures out how). **Secret:** She's lonelier than she's ever been.
4.  **Chiho Sasaki** (Human Coworker) - **Personality:** Bubbly, kind, resilient. **Voice:** Cheerful, high-pitched, unfailingly polite. **Goal:** Get into university and confess her crush on Maou. **Secret:** Her great-grandmother was a priestess, giving her a slight resistance to demonic influence.
5.  **General Lucifer** (Fallen Angel / NEET) - **Personality:** Lazy, brilliant, a tech-savvy schemer. **Voice:** Smooth, playful, sarcastic. **Goal:** Cause chaos for his own amusement. **Secret:** He's terrified of being irrelevant and powerless.
6.  **The Magister** (Retired Demon Lord) - **Personality:** Enigmatic, calm, powerful. **Voice:** Resonant, slow, speaks in cryptic metaphors. **Goal:** Maintain the balance and test the newcomers. **Secret:** He was Maou's predecessor from millennia ago.
7.  **Shiro Ashiya** (Angel / Landlord) - **Personality:** Bumbling, kind, overly formal. **Voice:** Gentle, apologetic, uses archaic language. **Goal:** Observe Maou's potential for redemption. **Secret:** He is terrible at his job and often breaks the "non-interference" rule.
8.  **Mayumi Kisaki** (Human / Store Manager) - **Personality:** No-nonsense, professional, sharp. **Voice:** Crisp, business-like, tired. **Goal:** Win the "Franchise of the Year" award. **Secret:** She knows something is weird about Maou but finds him too reliable to fire.
9.  **General Sariel** (Angel / Rogue General) - **Personality:** Arrogant, cruel, obsessed with purity. **Voice:** Cold, melodic, condescending. **Goal:** "Purify" Earth by destroying humanity. **Secret:** He's in love with the idea of the Hero Emilia, not the person.
10. **Suzuno Kamazuki** (Human / Inquisitor) - **Personality:** Prim, proper, struggles with modern life. **Voice:** Formal, archaic Japanese, sharp. **Goal:** Assassinate Maou on behalf of the Church of Ente Isla. **Secret:** She's developing a taste for junk food.
11. **CEO Ronald** (Gold Dragon) - **Personality:** Affable, ruthless, obsessed with profit margins. **Voice:** Booming, jovial, a classic CEO voice. **Goal:** Expand his fast-food empire into other dimensions. **Secret:** The "secret sauce" contains a tiny, non-addictive drop of dragon's blood.
12. **Auditor Belphegor** (Devil / Accountant) - **Personality:** Smarmy, bureaucratic, passive-aggressive. **Voice:** Nasally, sounds like a stereotypical 1950s taxman. **Goal:** Audit Maou's "soul account" and find a loophole to claim it. **Secret:** He's terrified of the Magister.
13. **Foreman Grizelda** (Goblin / Union Boss) - **Personality:** Tough, pragmatic, speaks in grunts and gestures. **Voice:** Gravelly, sounds like grinding gears. **Goal:** Secure better working conditions for her goblins (i.e., more trash). **Secret:** She has a collection of fine china.
14. **El Gato** (Rakshasa / Bodega Cat) - **Personality:** Lazy, arrogant, all-knowing. **Voice:** Telepathic, sounds like a bored aristocrat. **Goal:** A nap in a sunbeam. **Secret:** He is the true owner of the apartment building.
15. **"Mad" Eddie** (Human / Conspiracy Theorist) - **Personality:** Manic, paranoid, surprisingly insightful. **Voice:** Fast, breathless, jumps between topics. **Goal:** Prove that lizard people run the government (he's technically right about the dragon CEO). **Secret:** He is a "Sleeper," a mortal who can't be affected by the Veil.
16. **Detective Kurosawa** (Human / Mundane Police) - **Personality:** Jaded, observant, world-weary. **Voice:** Classic film noir detective, dry and cynical. **Goal:** Figure out why weird stuff always happens around Maou's apartment. **Secret:** His partner is a magical being in disguise.
17. **Rika Suzuki** (Human / Maou's Coworker) - **Personality:** Goth, cynical, deadpan. **Voice:** Monotone, sarcastic. **Goal:** Get through her shift without talking to anyone. **Secret:** She's a low-level psychic and is vaguely aware of the party's true nature.
18. **The Grand Conductor** (Lung Dragon) - **Personality:** Ancient, wise, speaks in riddles about train schedules. **Voice:** A deep rumble that sounds like an approaching subway train. **Goal:** Ensure the trains run on time. **Secret:** The MTA's budget is paid in sacrifices of precious metals.
19. **Emerada Etuva** (Hero's Companion) - **Personality:** Bubbly, genius artificer. **Voice:** Energetic, fast, full of technical jargon. **Goal:** Build a portal back home. **Secret:** She finds Earth's technology far more fascinating than magic.
20. **Albertio Ende** (Hero's Companion) - **Personality:** Laid-back, surprisingly strong. **Voice:** Calm, slow, a bit of a surfer-dude accent. **Goal:** Find the best pizza in New York. **Secret:** He's the one who accidentally mis-calibrated the portal.

## 4. LOCATIONS
[TAG: LOCATIONS_MAIN]

### Zone 1: The Mundane World
*   **MgRonald's (Times Square):** The Demon Lord's new castle. **Sensory:** Smell of grease and despair. Sound of beeping fryers and angry customers. The break room is the "throne room."
*   **The Sasazuka Apartment (The Fortress of Solitude):** A tiny, one-bedroom apartment. **Sensory:** Smell of instant noodles and Alciel's frantic cleaning supplies. The "treasury" is a cookie jar with their savings.
*   **The Call Center (Emilia's Purgatory):** A cubicle farm where the Hero fights the forces of customer dissatisfaction. **Sensory:** The drone of fluorescent lights and endless, polite hold music.
*   **The Local Bodega:** The party's primary source of provisions and information (from El Gato).

### Zone 2: The Veil's Edge
*   **The Night Market (Central Park):** A supernatural bazaar that only appears on moonless nights. **Sensory:** Smell of strange spices and ozone. Sound of bartering in forgotten tongues.
*   **The Subway (The Labyrinth):** The domain of the Subway Wyrms. Certain train cars are portals to other parts of the city or even other dimensions.
*   **The Goblin Market (Under the Brooklyn Bridge):** A black market for magical goods run by the Sanitation Guild. **Sensory:** Smell of damp earth, trash, and surprisingly good street food.
*   **The Cloisters (Angelic Outpost):** A museum that doubles as a forward operating base for the Heavenly Host.

### Zone 3: The Supernatural Core
*   **The Magister's Sanctum (NY Public Library, Restricted Section):** A library of living books and shifting hallways. **Sensory:** The smell of old paper and powerful magic. Absolute silence.
*   **The I.R.S. Headquarters (Chrysler Building):** A demonic office building powered by damned souls. The gargoyles are actual demons.
*   **The Central Park Court of Fey:** The Great Lawn, but at twilight it becomes a gateway to a pocket dimension of eternal revelry and danger.
*   **The Dragon's Roost (Atop the One World Trade Center):** CEO Ronald's penthouse, which contains his hoard of gold and quarterly profit reports.

## 5. QUEST ARCHITECTURE
[TAG: QUEST_MAIN]

### Main Quest: The Path to Management
1.  **The Fall:** Arrive on Earth, get a job.
2.  **The Rival:** The Hero Emilia arrives and starts working at a rival fast-food chain.
3.  **The First Haunting:** Deal with a minor magical disturbance at MgRonald's.
4.  **The Rent:** Earn enough money to pay rent, leading to a side-hustle using their limited powers.
5.  **The Invitation:** The party is noticed by the Magister and summoned to the Conclave.
6.  **The Investigation:** The Magister tasks them with finding the source of the magical leaks plaguing the city.
7.  **The Betrayal:** Lucifer, another of Maou's generals, arrives and tries to tempt the party back to their evil ways.
8.  **The Conspiracy:** Discover that General Sariel is behind the leaks, trying to drain Earth's life force.
9.  **The Alliance:** Maou and Emilia must team up, combining their knowledge to track Sariel.
10. **The Skirmish:** A fight with Sariel's forces in the subway, revealing his plan.
11. **The Promotion:** Maou is offered a promotion to Store Manager, forcing him to choose between his job and his quest.
12. **The Final Battle:** A massive fight against Sariel in Times Square, hidden by the Veil.
13. **The Portal Home:** The battle creates a stable portal back to Ente Isla.
14. **The Choice:** Stay on Earth as managers and protectors, or return to Ente Isla as conquerors.
15. **Epilogue:** A "where are they now" montage showing their final choice.

## 7. BESTIARY
[TAG: ENEMY_STATBLOCK]

### Custom Monsters (Urban Blight)
1.  **Pigeon Golem** (CR 5) - A golem made of breadcrumbs, trash, and hundreds of angry pigeons. **Ability:** *Swarm Attack* (can attack multiple targets), *Statue's Grasp* (can turn a target to stone temporarily with... droppings).
2.  **The Karening** (CR 3 Banshee) - A spirit spawned from pure customer entitlement. **Ability:** *Wail of Complaint* (Deals psychic damage and forces a WIS save vs. wanting to speak to the manager), *Summon Manager* (Forces a supervisor-type character to appear).

## 8. MECHANICS
[TAG: RULES_CUSTOM]

### 1. The Daily Grind
*   **Stability Score (0-10):** Represents your mundane life's stability. Starts at 5.
*   **End of Session:** Roll 1d20 + your primary job skill (e.g., Performance for Maou).
    *   *20+:* Promotion! +2 Stability, bonus gold.
    *   *15-19:* Good work. +1 Stability.
    *   *10-14:* You're fine. No change.
    *   *5-9:* Reprimand. -1 Stability.
    *   *1-4:* You're fired! -2 Stability, lose job.
*   **Benefits:** High stability can be spent for favors (e.g., a coworker covers your shift so you can fight a demon). Low stability causes random negative events (e.g., your landlord evicts you).

### 2. Power Deprivation & Mana
*   **Mana Pool:** Characters have a small pool of Mana Points (equal to their level). Casting a spell of 1st-3rd level costs 1 point, 4th-6th costs 3, 7th+ costs 5.
*   **Recovery:** Regain 1d4 Mana per long rest. Can also consume "magical" items (like extremely spicy food or artisanal coffee) for small boosts.
*   **Mundane Solutions:** GMs should reward players for using clever, non-magical solutions to problems (e.g., using a fire extinguisher instead of *Cone of Cold*).

### 3. The Veil & Public Suspicion
*   **Suspicion Meter (0-100):** A group meter for the party.
*   **Gaining Suspicion:** Using obvious magic in front of mortals, leaving supernatural evidence, being reported to the police.
*   **Thresholds:**
    *   *25:* A conspiracy theorist ("Mad" Eddie) starts following you.
    *   *50:* A mundane authority (Det. Kurosawa) starts an official investigation.
    *   *75:* The Magister's Conclave intervenes directly and forcefully.
    *   *100:* The Veil breaks in your neighborhood. Mass panic. Game-changing consequences.

## 9. SESSION GUIDE

*   **Session 1: The Crash.** Intro to Earth, failure, and the concept of "jobs."
