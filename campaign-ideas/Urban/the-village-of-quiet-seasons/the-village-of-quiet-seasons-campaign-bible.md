# Campaign Bible: The Village of Quiet Seasons

## 1. DEEP LORE & HISTORY
[TAG: LORE_CORE]

### Origin Myth: The Breath of the Mountain
Long ago, before the highways carved scars into the land, the Great Mountain, *Yama-no-Kami*, grew tired of the world's noise. It exhaled a single, long breath that settled into the valley below. This breath became the mist that clings to the rice paddies in the morning. The first settlers did not conquer this land; they fell asleep in the tall grass and woke up understanding that there was nowhere else they needed to be. The village was not built; it grew, like moss on a stone, arranged around the rhythm of the cicadas and the turning of the leaves. It is said that Asagao Village exists slightly to the left of standard timeâ€”a pocket universe where a summer afternoon can last for a hundred years if you are sitting still enough to notice it.

### Timeline of Gentle Events
1.  **The Planting of the First Rice:** The founders realized the soil tasted like sweet tea.
2.  **The Construction of the School:** Built from a single giant cedar that fell during a polite storm.
3.  **The Year of the Blue Snow:** It snowed petals instead of ice; the harvest that year was impossibly sweet.
4.  **The Arrival of the Bus Stop:** A sign was placed, but no bus came for ten years. The villagers waited patiently.
5.  **The Great Nap:** A summer heatwave so intense the entire village slept from noon to sunset collectively.
6.  **The Invention of "Sunset Watching":** Designated as an official village sport by the elders.
7.  **The Cat Mayor Incident:** A stray cat sat in the mayor's chair; the town council decided he made better decisions than the human one.
8.  **The Day the River Flowed Backwards:** For one hour, the water returned to the mountain, as if forgetting something.
9.  **The Arrival of the Vending Machine:** It only sells warm red bean soup, even in August.
10. **The Current Spring:** The cherry blossoms have bloomed two days early, a sign of a "Very Good Year."

### The Metaphysical "Why"
Asagao Village acts as a metaphysical "lung" for the region. The conflict is not between good and evil, but between **Presence** and **Distraction**. The "enemy" is the encroaching grayness of modern efficiency and the forgetting of simple joys. If the village were to be paved over, the world would lose its ability to dream.

## 4. LOCATIONS
[TAG: LOCATIONS_MAIN]

### Zone 1: The One-Room Schoolhouse
A wooden building that smells of cedar and floor wax.
1.  **The Classroom:** Five desks, a blackboard that is never fully erased, and a stove for winter.
2.  **The Hallway:** Long wooden planks that creak in F-sharp.
3.  **The Rabbit Hutch:** Home to "Snowball," a rabbit the size of a dog.
4.  **The Cherry Blossom Tree:** 100 years old. Roots create a natural bench.
5.  **The Pool:** Filled with river water. Frogs often join swimming lessons.
**Sensory:** *Smell:* Chalk dust and rain. *Sound:* A single crow cawing in the distance.

### Zone 2: The Village Center
The "bustling" metropolis (population: 40).
1.  **Candy Store "Dagashi-ya":** Jars of colorful sweets, dusty toys, smell of soy sauce.
2.  **The Bus Stop:** A rusted sign, a wooden bench, a timetable that is merely a suggestion.
3.  **The Red Postbox:** The brightest object in the village.
4.  **The Vending Machine:** The light hum is the heartbeat of the night.
5.  **The Bulletin Board:** Posters for festivals from 2015 are still up.
**Sensory:** *Smell:* Fried croquettes. *Sound:* The hum of the vending machine.

### Zone 3: The Rice Paddies
Endless green/gold mirrors.
1.  **The Irrigation Canal:** Clear water, fast-moving, cold enough to chill watermelon.
2.  **The Scarecrow Army:** Dressed in the clothes of deceased elders.
3.  **The Lone Pine:** A tree in the middle of the fields, used for navigation.
4.  **The Muddy Path:** Where boots get stuck.
5.  **The Sunset Spot:** A hillock that aligns perfectly with the sun.
**Sensory:** *Smell:* Wet earth and green growth. *Sound:* A chorus of a thousand frogs.

### Zone 4: The Secret River
Hidden behind the bamboo grove.
1.  **The Jumping Rock:** A flat stone over deep water.
2.  **The Firefly Bank:** Where the lights are thickest in June.
3.  **The Old Bridge:** Moss-covered stone, no rails.
4.  **The Waterfall (Tiny):** Only 2 feet high, but loud.
5.  **The Secret Base:** A hollow in the bushes lined with cardboard.
**Sensory:** *Smell:* River algae and ozone. *Sound:* Rushing water and laughter.

### Zone 5: The Mountain Shrine
Up the 1000 steps (actually only 200).
1.  **The Torii Gate:** Faded vermilion, peeling paint.
2.  **The Offering Box:** Contains more acorns than coins.
3.  **The Guardian Statues:** Covered in moss bibs.
4.  **The Sacred Cedar:** Wrapped in white paper rope.
5.  **The Viewpoint:** You can see the whole village, looking like a toy set.
**Sensory:** *Smell:* Incense and damp leaves. *Sound:* Wind chimes.

## 5. QUEST ARCHITECTURE
[TAG: QUEST_MAIN]

### The Cycle of Seasons (Main Arc)
1.  **Arrival:** Get off the bus. Realize you missed the return trip. Stay the night.
2.  **Introduction:** Meet the school kids. Be invited to "school" (even if adult).
3.  **Spring Planting:** Help stick rice seedlings in the mud. Get muddy.
4.  **The Bamboo Shoot Hunt:** Go into the mountain. Dig up dinner.
5.  **The Rainy Season Boredom:** Spend a session staring at rain. Make Teru-teru bozu dolls.
6.  **Summer Vacation Start:** Radio calisthenics at 6 AM.
7.  **The Pool Cleaning:** Remove the frogs from the school pool.
8.  **The Festival:** Wear a yukata. Catch a goldfish. Watch fireworks (sparklers).
9.  **The Typhoon:** Board up the windows. Tell ghost stories by candlelight.
10. **Autumn Harvest:** Cut the rice. Hang it to dry. Eat roasted sweet potatoes.
11. **The Cultural Festival:** Put on a play with the kids. Make costumes from trash.
12. **Winter Prep:** Wrap trees in straw. Get out the kotatsu (heated table).
13. **New Year's Eve:** Ring the temple bell 108 times. Eat soba.
14. **The First Sunrise:** Walk to the mountain top in the cold.
15. **Spring Returns:** The cherry blossoms bloom. The bus arrives. Decide to stay.

## 8. MECHANICS
[TAG: RULES_CUSTOM]

### Inaka-Jikan (Countryside Time)
Time is fluid. A "scene" lasts as long as the vibe requires.
*   **Mechanic:** Players do not track hours. They track "Sun Position" (Morning, Noon, Golden Hour, Night).
*   **The Rush Penalty:** If a player tries to rush or be efficient, they roll with Disadvantage.

### Presence Points (PP)
Experience points are replaced by Presence Points.
*   **Gain PP:** By describing a sensory detail, sitting still, or engaging in a "useless" activity (skipping stones).
*   **Spend PP:** To trigger a "Flashbulb Memory" (a perfect scene where nothing goes wrong) or to "Heal" (remove stress/mental fatigue).

### Nostalgia Check
Instead of History checks.
*   **Roll:** d20 + Wisdom.
*   **Success:** You remember a childhood memory that gives you a clue about the current situation (e.g., "I remember my grandpa used to fix this with gum").
