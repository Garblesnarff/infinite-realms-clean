### **World-Building Specification Brief**

**Freedom or Death**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** Scottish rebels fight for independence from English occupation through guerrilla warfare and inspiring leadership. Following William Wallace's rise and fall, they discover that freedom matters more than the lives of those who fight for it, and that inspiring defeats can change nations.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail Edward Longshanks' invasion of Scotland—why and how England occupied.
    *   Write the history of Scottish clans—internal rivalries that weaken unity.
    *   Describe William Wallace's background—commoner turned national hero.
    *   Explain Medieval warfare—knights, longbows, cavalry charges, siege tactics.
    *   Chronicle the Wars of Independence—decades-long struggle for freedom.
    *   Detail Robert the Bruce's conflicted loyalties and ultimate choice.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions:** Scottish Rebels (Major), English Crown (Major), Scottish Nobles (Major, divided), Clans (Minor, various), English Occupation Forces (Major)

**4. Causality Chains**
*   **IF:** Wallace wins at Stirling → **THEN:** Scottish morale soars, nobles must acknowledge rebellion.
*   **IF:** Nobles betray at Falkirk → **THEN:** Military defeat, Wallace loses Guardian position.
*   **IF:** Wallace executed → **THEN:** Martyrdom inspires greater rebellion or despair.
*   **IF:** Bruce commits to freedom → **THEN:** Rebellion gains legitimate royal leadership.
