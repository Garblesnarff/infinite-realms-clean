# Architects of Eternity

*A Civilization Building / Grand Strategy / Multiple Victory Conditions campaign — Epic Campaign (35-45 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Architects of Eternity
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Civilization Building / Grand Strategy / Political Sandbox / Historical Fantasy
**Estimated Length:** Epic Campaign — 35-45 sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **1** → Finish **20**
**Tone (keywords):** epic scope, historical progression, competing civilizations, multiple victory paths, wonder building, diplomatic intrigue, technological advancement
**Core Premise (2-4 sentences):**
The party leads a fledgling civilization from primitive settlements through ages of development—Ancient, Classical, Medieval, Renaissance, Industrial, and Atomic eras. They compete against rival civilizations led by historical and legendary leaders, each pursuing their own path to supremacy. Through city building, technological research, cultural development, diplomatic negotiations, and military conquest, the party must guide their people to achieve one of several victory conditions: Domination (conquer all rivals), Science (reach the stars), Culture (achieve global influence), Diplomacy (unite the world council), or Religion (convert the world to their faith).

---

## Story Arc (Epic Campaign — 35-45 sessions)

A six-act arc progressing through historical ages with escalating stakes.

*   **Act I — Ancient Era (Sessions 1-8):** The party founds their first city, establishes basic infrastructure, and meets neighboring civilizations. They research writing, pottery, and bronze working. Early conflicts involve barbarians and territorial disputes. They build their first Wonder (like the Hanging Gardens or Oracle). Relationships with rivals begin—some friendly, others hostile.
*   **Act II — Classical Era (Sessions 9-16):** Civilizations grow in power and complexity. The party develops government systems (monarchy, republic, oligarchy), builds libraries and universities, and trains legions or phalanxes. Major diplomatic summit establishes early alliances and trade routes. Religious prophets found world religions. The first great war erupts over strategic resources and wonders.
*   **Act III — Medieval Era (Sessions 17-24):** Feudalism and faith dominate. The party constructs castles, cathedrals, and universities. Knights and trebuchets replace ancient troops. The Black Death (or magical equivalent) devastates populations, testing resilience. Religious conflicts create crusade-like wars. Secret societies and spies emerge. City-states request protection and offer bonuses.
*   **Act IV — Renaissance Era (Sessions 25-32):** Enlightenment and gunpowder change everything. The party explores new continents, encountering exotic civilizations and resources. Banking, printing presses, and art flourish. Musketeers replace knights. The party must choose victory path focus: military conquest, cultural dominance, scientific progress, diplomatic unity, or religious conversion.
*   **Act V — Industrial Era (Sessions 33-40):** Factories, railroads, and coal power rapid expansion. Ideologies clash: democracy vs. autocracy vs. communism. Ironclads and artillery make wars devastating. The party faces pollution, worker unrest, and espionage. World's Fairs showcase achievements. Victory conditions become clear; rivalry intensifies.
*   **Act VI — Modern/Atomic Era (Sessions 41-45):** Technology reaches its peak with computers, nuclear weapons, and space travel. The party makes final push for chosen victory: launch colony ship (Science), achieve cultural hegemony (Culture), conquer remaining capitals (Domination), pass World Congress vote (Diplomacy), or convert last heathens (Religion). Rivals make desperate moves to prevent party's victory. Climax involves stopping rival from achieving their victory or racing to complete yours first.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Sessions 1-2 — "Dawn of Civilization"
*   **Focus:** Settlement / First Steps
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Party's tribe settles first city near river or coast (strategic location)
    *   Meet council of advisors: Science, Culture, Military, Economy, Diplomacy
    *   Research first technology: Animal Husbandry, Pottery, or Mining
    *   First encounter with barbarian raiders threatening settlers
**Escalation:** Scout discovers rival civilization nearby; first diplomatic contact

### Sessions 3-5 — "Expanding Borders"
*   **Focus:** Early Growth / Neighbors
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Found second and third cities in strategic locations
    *   Meet multiple rival civilizations, each with unique personality
    *   Trade routes established; gain gold and diplomatic favor
    *   First Wonder construction: Oracle, Stonehenge, or Hanging Gardens
**Escalation:** Rival civilization denounces party for settling too close; border tensions

### Sessions 6-8 — "Bronze and Iron"
*   **Focus:** Military Development / First War
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Research Bronze Working, then Iron Working; upgrade warriors to swordsmen
    *   City-state asks for protection from barbarians; completing quest gains alliance
    *   Rival civilization declares surprise war, claiming party's land
    *   First major battle: defend cities or launch counter-offensive
**Escalation:** War reveals rivals are competing to build same Wonder; race against time

### Sessions 9-12 — "Classical Powers"
*   **Focus:** Government / Great People
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Choose government: Autocracy (military bonuses), Oligarchy (production), Classical Republic (culture)
    *   Great People emerge: Great General (military), Great Scientist (technology boost), Great Artist (culture)
    *   Construct Classical Wonders: Colosseum, Great Library, Petra
    *   Religious prophets found religions; choose beliefs and bonuses
**Escalation:** Rival achieves Classical Era first and gains free settler; must catch up

### Sessions 13-16 — "The Great War"
*   **Focus:** Large-Scale Conflict
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Major alliance war: multiple civilizations vs. aggressive empire
    *   Siege warfare: catapults, battering rams, legions storm city walls
    *   Capture rival capital or negotiate peace treaty with territorial gains
    *   War weariness affects population; must balance conquest with happiness
**Escalation:** Rival builds Forbidden Palace; gains diplomatic edge

### Sessions 17-20 — "Faith and Feudalism"
*   **Focus:** Religion / Medieval Society
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Enhance religion with Reformation beliefs; spread faith via missionaries
    *   Construct Medieval Wonders: Alhambra, Notre Dame, Hagia Sophia
    *   Feudal contracts with city-states provide unique bonuses
    *   Religious conflict: Holy War declared against heretic civilization
**Escalation:** Black Death plague hits; lose population, production drops, must survive crisis

### Sessions 21-24 — "Age of Chivalry"
*   **Focus:** Knights / Castle Defense
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Knights and crossbowmen dominate battlefields
    *   Build castles for defensive bonuses; one city becomes impregnable fortress
    *   Espionage emerges: plant spies in rival cities to steal technology
    *   Medieval Congress: vote on world religion and trade policies
**Escalation:** Rival civilization enters Renaissance first; gains powerful bonuses

### Sessions 25-28 — "Age of Discovery"
*   **Focus:** Exploration / New Worlds
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Caravel ships explore across oceans; discover new continent with city-states
    *   Banking and economics: establish stock exchanges, international trade routes
    *   Renaissance Wonders: Sistine Chapel, Globe Theatre, Taj Mahal
    *   Meet exotic civilizations with unique resources and units
**Escalation:** Two rivals form defensive pact against party's growing power

### Sessions 29-32 — "Gunpowder and Glory"
*   **Focus:** Military Revolution / Ideologies
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Musketeers and cannons replace medieval armies; city walls become obsolete
    *   Great Artists create masterpieces; tourism begins influencing other civilizations
    *   Choose primary victory path: begin focused strategy
    *   World's Fair competition: civilizations compete for cultural and scientific prestige
**Escalation:** Rival is close to achieving Cultural Victory; must counter their influence

### Sessions 33-36 — "Industrial Revolution"
*   **Focus:** Industry / Ideological Conflict
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Factories and railroads boost production; cities grow massive
    *   Choose ideology: Freedom (culture/tourism), Order (production/happiness), Autocracy (military)
    *   Build Industrial Wonders: Big Ben, Eiffel Tower, Broadway
    *   Ironclads and artillery wage devastating wars; cities can be bombed
**Escalation:** Civilization pressure: rival ideology tries to flip party's cities through influence

### Sessions 37-40 — "Modern Conflicts"
*   **Focus:** Advanced Warfare / Victory Rush
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Tanks, bombers, and submarines dominate modern warfare
    *   Nuclear weapons researched; mutual assured destruction becomes factor
    *   Space race begins: launch satellites, moon landing
    *   Diplomatic victory attempt: emergency session of World Congress
**Escalation:** Rival begins building spaceship parts (Science Victory imminent) or converts last civilizations (Religious Victory)

### Sessions 41-45 — "Path to Victory"
*   **Focus:** Endgame / Multiple Victory Conditions
*   **Key Events:**
    *   **Science Victory:** Launch colony spaceship to Alpha Centauri; defend construction from sabotage
    *   **Culture Victory:** Achieve dominant tourism; foreign citizens demand party's blue jeans and rock music
    *   **Domination Victory:** Capture last rival capitals; massive final war with nuclear weapons possible
    *   **Diplomacy Victory:** Win World Congress vote; secure city-state alliances and favors
    *   **Religious Victory:** Convert last civilizations; religious warfare and missionaries
    *   Rival civilizations make desperate attempts to stop party or achieve their own victory
    *   Epilogue: Show golden age of party's civilization and their legacy
**Resolution:** Party achieves chosen victory; world is united, conquered, converted, or explores stars

## Major NPCs

1.  **Advisor Council** — *The Specialists*
    *   **Role:** Five advisors representing Science, Culture, Military, Economy, and Diplomacy
    *   **Personality:** Each has distinct personality reflecting their domain (scientist curious, general aggressive, etc.)
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Each pushes their agenda; party must balance conflicting advice

2.  **Emperor Maximus of Rome** — *The Conqueror*
    *   **Role:** Rival civilization leader focused on Domination victory through military might
    *   **Personality:** Aggressive, demands tribute, respects strength, will backstab if weak
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Overstretched empire; conquering him liberates oppressed cities

3.  **Queen Cleopatra of Egypt** — *The Diplomat*
    *   **Role:** Rival leader focused on Diplomatic victory through alliances and trade
    *   **Personality:** Charming, manipulative, builds alliances against threats
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Weak military; vulnerable if isolated diplomatically

4.  **Mahatma Gandhi** — *The Peaceful (Until Nuclear)*
    *   **Role:** Rival leader appearing peaceful but secretly pursuing Science/Nuclear victory
    *   **Personality:** Passive-aggressive, promotes peace while building superweapons
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Famous bug: becomes extremely aggressive with nuclear weapons

5.  **Catherine de Medici** — *The Spy Mistress*
    *   **Role:** Rival leader excelling at espionage and cultural influence
    *   **Personality:** Secretive, plants spies everywhere, steals technology and sabotages
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Over-reliance on espionage; vulnerable to counter-intelligence

## Key Locations

1.  **The Capital City:** Party's first and greatest city, center of empire with all buildings and wonders
2.  **Border Cities:** Frontier settlements constantly under threat from rivals and barbarians
3.  **Holy City:** City where party's religion was founded; pilgrimage site generating faith
4.  **Industrial Center:** Late-game city with factories, railroads, and coal plants producing most empire's goods
5.  **World Congress Hall:** Neutral city where all civilizations meet to vote on global policies

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores themes of progress vs. tradition, competing paths to greatness, balancing growth with sustainability, and leading people through history. It asks: What defines a great civilization? Is military conquest justified for security? Should culture assimilate or preserve diversity? How far would you go to ensure your people's survival? The central conflict pits multiple visions of victory against each other while navigating the challenges each era brings.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Era Progression System**
*   Campaign progresses through six distinct eras: Ancient → Classical → Medieval → Renaissance → Industrial → Modern/Atomic
*   Entering new era first grants "Era Score" bonuses and Golden Age benefits
*   Units, buildings, and strategies change completely each era
*   Technologies must be researched in order; cannot skip eras
*   Each era introduces new threats and opportunities

### 2. **Multiple Victory Conditions**
*   **Domination:** Capture all rival civilization capitals through military conquest
*   **Science:** Build and launch spaceship to Alpha Centauri (requires multiple components)
*   **Culture:** Achieve cultural dominance; foreign tourists exceed domestic tourists in all rivals
*   **Diplomacy:** Win World Congress vote by securing majority diplomatic favor
*   **Religion:** Convert all civilizations to party's religion through missionaries and religious combat
*   Party chooses focus mid-campaign but can pivot if needed

### 3. **City Building & Management**
*   Found cities in strategic locations: rivers, coast, resources, defensive terrain
*   Each city has limited tile workable; citizens assigned to tiles for food, production, gold, science, culture
*   Build districts: Campus (science), Theater Square (culture), Holy Site (faith), Industrial Zone (production), Commercial Hub (gold)
*   Construct buildings in districts for bonuses
*   Build Wonders (unique world buildings) for massive bonuses; only one civilization can build each

### 4. **Technology & Civic Trees**
*   Research technologies for new units, buildings, and abilities
*   Civic tree unlocks governments, policies, and wonders
*   Tech boosts: complete objectives for 50% research reduction
*   Eureka moments and inspirations speed progress
*   Rivals race for key technologies; falling behind is dangerous

### 5. **Diplomacy & AI Personalities**
*   Each rival leader has unique agenda and personality (aggressive, friendly, backstabbing, loyal)
*   Relationship system: Friendly, Neutral, Denounced, War
*   Trade deals: open borders, research agreements, gold exchanges, resource trades
*   Alliances: defensive pacts, joint wars, research/cultural/military alliances
*   Casus belli: justifications for war reduce warmonger penalty
*   World Congress: vote on resolutions affecting all civilizations

### 6. **Great People System**
*   Earn Great People through district points: Great Scientists, Engineers, Artists, Writers, Musicians, Admirals, Generals, Prophets, Merchants
*   Each Great Person is unique historical figure with specific ability
*   Competition: Rival civilizations also pursue Great People
*   Use Great People for crucial bonuses at key moments

### 7. **Religion & Faith**
*   Found religion early by generating faith
*   Choose beliefs: Pantheon (minor bonus), Founder (religion-wide), Follower (follower bonuses), Enhancer (spread/combat bonuses), Worship Building
*   Spread religion via missionaries and apostles
*   Religious combat: theological debates between apostles
*   Religious victory requires converting all civilizations
*   Inquisitors defend against foreign religions
