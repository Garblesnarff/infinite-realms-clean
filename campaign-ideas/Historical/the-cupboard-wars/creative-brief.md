# The Cupboard Wars - Creative Brief

## Visual Aesthetic
- **Style**: Historical realism in toy bodies
- **Colors**: Period-appropriate for each era represented
- **Lighting**: Cupboard glows when active, otherworldly
- **Scale**: Toy size, bedroom as massive battlefield

## Character Design
- Plastic toys with era-appropriate details
- Eyes show genuine historical consciousness
- Wounds and wear reflect actual battles
- Authentic period equipment (at toy scale)
- Cultural accuracy in armor, weapons, clothing

## Environment
- **Modern Bedroom**: Alien landscape to historical minds
- **The Cupboard Interior**: Timeless void, soul repository
- **Battlefields**: Bed becomes trenches, desk becomes fortress
- **Attic**: Graveyard of previous toy incarnations

## Music Direction
- **Era Themes**: Period-appropriate music for each faction
- **Cupboard**: Ethereal, timeless, magical
- **Battles**: Clash of historical themes, anachronistic
- **Peace Moments**: Universal human melodies
- **The Curator**: Ancient, weary, beyond time

## Voice Acting
- **Historical Accuracy**: Accents, speech patterns from eras
- **Jesse**: Old West drawl, laconic
- **Wilson**: 1940s military formality
- **Takeshi**: Formal Japanese, code of bushido
- **Shaka**: Zulu linguistic patterns, strategic mind
- **Erik**: Old Norse influenced, Viking culture
- **The Curator**: Timeless, exhausted, seen it all

## Marketing Tagline
"History's greatest warriors. Toy size. Real grudges."
