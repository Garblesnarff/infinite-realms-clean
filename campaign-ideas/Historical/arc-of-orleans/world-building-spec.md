### **Template: World-Building Specification Brief**

**Arc of Orleans**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The players participate in the Hundred Years' War witnessing France's darkest hour when English longbowmen crush French chivalry, when Joan of Arc claims divine visions to save France, and when the entire medieval world changes through gunpowder, national identity, and the end of chivalric warfare.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the Hundred Years' War's origins: English kings claiming French throne.
    *   Write about Agincourt and how longbows destroyed French chivalry.
    *   Describe Joan of Arc's visions, military campaigns, and martyrdom.
    *   Explain the transformation from dynastic to national war.
    *   Detail medieval warfare's evolution: chivalry to gunpowder.
    *   Write about French national identity being born through crisis.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The French Loyalists** (Major—Player Faction): Goals—Save France, crown the Dauphin. Desperate and inspired by Joan.
    *   **The English** (Major): Goals—Conquer France, secure dynastic claim. Dominant until Joan.
    *   **The Burgundians** (Minor—Traitors): Goals—Political advantage through English alliance. Capture Joan.
    *   **Joan's Believers** (Emerging): Goals—Follow divine mission. Transformed by inspiration.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Joan of Arc, Dauphin Charles, Duke of Burgundy, Sir John Talbot
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "French Knight", "English Longbowman", "Common Soldier", "Believer in Joan", "Skeptic", "Courtier"

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Agincourt Field:** Where chivalry died.
*   **Orleans Under Siege:** The city whose survival determined France.
*   **Reims Cathedral:** Coronation site legitimizing French monarchy.
*   **Rouen:** Joan's trial and martyrdom site.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Triggers:** IF Agincourt happens THEN French chivalry is shattered. IF Joan appears THEN French morale transforms. IF Orleans is relieved THEN hope returns. IF Joan is captured THEN martyrdom becomes more powerful than victories. IF war ends THEN France is united nation.
