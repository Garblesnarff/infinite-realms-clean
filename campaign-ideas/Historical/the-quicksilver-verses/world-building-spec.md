### **Template: World-Building Specification Brief**

**The Quicksilver Verses**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The protagonist discovers competitive karuta—a traditional card game based on classical poetry where players memorize 100 poems and grab matching cards faster than opponents. What seems like a simple children's game is actually a sport of lightning reflexes, perfect memory, psychological warfare, and deep poetry connection. Starting as a complete novice inspired by a karuta prodigy, they form a school club and journey from regional tournaments to the national championship, competing for the Queen title—the highest honor in karuta.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the Hyakunin Isshu—100 classical Japanese poems compiled in Heian period, foundation of karuta.
    *   Write the history of competitive karuta and its evolution into modern sport.
    *   Describe Queen and Master titles—lifetime achievements representing pinnacle of karuta.
    *   Explain Omi Jingu Shrine's significance as sacred site of national championships.
    *   Detail the ranking system from Class D to Class A to challenger to Queen/Master.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **School Karuta Clubs** (Major): High school clubs competing in team tournaments.
    *   **Shiranami Society** (Major): Traditional karuta training society, teaches fundamentals and poetry appreciation.
    *   **Karuta Association** (Major): Governing body managing tournaments, rankings, and title matches.
    *   **Regional Societies** (Minor): Local training groups preserving different karuta traditions.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Arata Wataya (prodigy), Taichi Mashima (analyst), Shinobu Wakamiya (Queen), Hideo Harada (sensei), Sudo (aggressive champion).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Class A Player" (elite competitors), "Tournament Reader" (poem reciters), "Team Captain" (club leaders), "Former Queen/Master" (legendary retired players).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **School Clubroom:** Traditional tatami room where protagonist's team practices, card sets, poetry books, casual training atmosphere.
*   **Shiranami Society Hall:** Formal karuta training facility with multiple tatami rooms, traditional teaching methods.
*   **Tournament Venues:** Various halls with tatami rooms for competitive matches, spectator areas.
*   **Omi Jingu Shrine:** Sacred site of national championship and title matches, historic traditional architecture.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Protagonist develops aggressive attacking style → **THEN:** Win quickly or lose to faults, high-risk high-reward gameplay.
*   **IF:** Memorize all poems and their meanings → **THEN:** Deeper connection improves focus, unlock poetry appreciation bonuses.
*   **IF:** Achieve Class A ranking → **THEN:** Qualify to challenge for Queen title, face absolute elite.
*   **IF:** Team reaches nationals → **THEN:** Face schools with years of experience, must overcome individual skill gaps through teamwork.
*   **IF:** Win Queen title → **THEN:** Become target for all challengers, responsibility to represent karuta's beauty.
