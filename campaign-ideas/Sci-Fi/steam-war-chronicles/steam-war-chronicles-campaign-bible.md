# Steam War Chronicles [CAMPAIGN: Sci-Fi (Steampunk/Dieselpunk)]

## Core Concept [CONCEPT: High]
Centuries after the cataclysmic "Great Steam War" reshaped the continents and plunged the world into a perpetual industrial twilight, humanity clings to survival within colossal, steam-powered city-states. These bastions of clockwork and brass are locked in a ceaseless struggle for dwindling resources â€“ coal, water, and rare aether-crystals. Rival factions, from aristocratic engineers to working-class revolutionaries, battle for control of ancient, powerful steam-tech relics and the secrets of the pre-War era. The players are mercenaries, inventors, or freedom fighters navigating this smog-choked, gear-driven world, seeking to uncover the truth behind the Great Steam War and forge a new future amidst the clanking, hissing ruins of the past.

## Setting [SETTING: Industrial Post-Apocalypse]
The world is a vast, scarred landscape dominated by the perpetual smog and clanking machinery of a bygone era. Continents are fractured, oceans are choked with industrial waste, and the sky is often obscured by the exhaust of countless steam engines. Gigantic, mobile city-states, powered by colossal boilers and intricate clockwork, traverse the desolate plains, while airships ply the smoky skies. The aesthetic is a blend of Victorian-era industrialism, dieselpunk grit, and post-apocalyptic decay, where every piece of technology is a precious, hard-won relic.

### Locations [LOCATION: Specific]
*   **Ironclad City (The Cog):** [LOCATION: City-State] A massive, mobile city-state built around a colossal, ancient steam engine. It is a hub of industry and military power, ruled by a council of powerful engineers and industrialists. Its lower levels are a sprawling, dangerous slum.
*   **Aetherium (The Crystal Spire):** [LOCATION: Research Hub] A floating city-state, powered by rare aether-crystals, dedicated to scientific research and the development of advanced steam-tech. It is a beacon of knowledge but also a target for those seeking its secrets.
*   **The Rusting Wastes:** [LOCATION: Wasteland] The vast, desolate plains between the city-states, littered with the skeletal remains of pre-War machinery and choked with perpetual rust-storms. It's a dangerous place, home to scavengers and mutated creatures.
*   **The Sunken City of Brass:** [LOCATION: Ruined City] The partially submerged ruins of a pre-War metropolis, now a dangerous underwater labyrinth. It is rumored to hold vast amounts of intact steam-tech and the secrets of the Great Steam War.
*   **The Sky-Pirate Haven (Cloud's Edge):** [LOCATION: Criminal Hub] A hidden, mobile base of operations for airship pirates, often located within dense cloud formations or atop remote, inaccessible plateaus. It's a lawless zone for trade and illicit dealings.

## Factions [FACTION: Political]
*   **The Ironclad Hegemony:** [FACTION: Militaristic Government] The dominant military and industrial power, ruling Ironclad City. They believe in expansion through force and control of resources, viewing other city-states as rivals or subordinates.
*   **The Aetherium Collective:** [FACTION: Scientific Oligarchy] The ruling body of Aetherium, composed of brilliant scientists and philosophers. They prioritize knowledge and technological advancement, often clashing with the Hegemony over resource allocation.
*   **The Gear-Grinders Union:** [FACTION: Labor Movement] A powerful, underground labor union representing the working class of the city-states. They fight for better conditions, fair wages, and often resort to sabotage or strikes to achieve their goals.
*   **The Sky-Corsairs:** [FACTION: Criminal Syndicate] Independent airship pirates who prey on trade routes, raid city-states, and operate outside the law. They are opportunistic and fiercely independent, often selling their services to the highest bidder.
*   **The Clockwork Cult:** [FACTION: Fanatical] A secretive, radical cult that worships ancient clockwork mechanisms and believes the Great Steam War was a divine cleansing. They seek to awaken dormant pre-War automatons and usher in a new age of mechanical purity.

## NPCs [NPC: Key]
*   **Baron Von Zeppelin:** [NPC: Antagonist] The ruthless, ambitious leader of the Ironclad Hegemony. A brilliant engineer and military strategist, he seeks to unify the world under his iron rule. (Male, 60s, cunning, authoritarian)
*   **Professor Aris Thorne:** [NPC: Scientist] The eccentric, brilliant lead scientist of the Aetherium Collective. He is obsessed with uncovering the true history of the Great Steam War and its forgotten technologies. (Male, 50s, intellectual, naive)
*   **"Sparky" Jax:** [NPC: Rebel Leader] The charismatic, fiery leader of the Gear-Grinders Union. A former factory worker, he fights tirelessly for the rights of the working class and dreams of a world free from oppression. (Female, 30s, passionate, determined)
*   **Captain "Crimson" Lyra:** [NPC: Pirate Captain] The infamous, daring captain of the Sky-Corsair airship, the *Cloud Serpent*. She is a master pilot and tactician, known for her daring raids and her strict code of honor. (Female, 40s, bold, independent)
*   **Unit 734 (The Archivist):** [NPC: Automaton] A pre-War clockwork automaton, discovered partially functional in the Rusting Wastes. It contains vast amounts of historical data but is damaged and communicates in fragmented, cryptic messages. (Automaton, ancient, damaged)
*   **The Cog-Priest Silas:** [NPC: Cult Leader] The enigmatic, fanatical leader of the Clockwork Cult. He believes he can commune with the "Machine Spirits" of ancient automatons and seeks to awaken them. (Male, 50s, zealous, unsettling)

## Quests [QUEST: Primary]
*   **The Missing Coal Convoy:** [QUEST: Resource Retrieval] The Ironclad Hegemony's vital coal convoy has gone missing in the Rusting Wastes. Players must locate the convoy, recover its cargo, and deal with whatever (or whoever) attacked it.
*   **Aetherium's Power Core:** [QUEST: Sabotage] The Gear-Grinders Union tasks the players with infiltrating Aetherium to sabotage its primary aether-crystal power core, hoping to force the Collective to negotiate with the Union.
*   **The Sunken City's Secrets:** [QUEST: Exploration] Professor Thorne hires the players to explore a newly discovered section of the Sunken City of Brass, seeking a legendary pre-War steam-tech device that could revolutionize energy production.
*   **Sky-Corsair Ransom:** [QUEST: Rescue] Captain Lyra's first mate has been captured by a rival Sky-Corsair crew. Players must infiltrate the rival haven, rescue the mate, and potentially retrieve stolen goods.
*   **The Clockwork Cult's Ritual:** [QUEST: Interception] The Clockwork Cult is attempting a dangerous ritual in the Rusting Wastes, seeking to awaken a colossal pre-War automaton. Players must stop the ritual before it unleashes a destructive force.
*   **Baron Von Zeppelin's Gambit:** [QUEST: Confrontation] Baron Von Zeppelin launches a surprise attack on a neutral city-state, aiming to seize its resources. Players must defend the city and repel the Hegemony forces.

## Threats [THREAT: Environmental]
*   **Rust-Storms:** [THREAT: Environmental] Frequent, corrosive storms of metallic dust that sweep across the Rusting Wastes, damaging machinery, obscuring vision, and causing respiratory illness.
*   **Mutated Wildlife:** [THREAT: Creature] The polluted environment has spawned grotesque, aggressive creatures, from giant, armored insects to feral, steam-powered beasts.
*   **Automaton Malfunctions:** [THREAT: Technological] Ancient, pre-War automatons, often damaged or corrupted, roam the wastes and ruins, attacking anything they perceive as a threat.
*   **Resource Scarcity:** [THREAT: Survival] The constant struggle for coal, water, and aether-crystals fuels conflict and leads to desperate measures by all factions.
*   **Industrial Accidents:** [THREAT: Environmental] The volatile nature of steam-tech means boiler explosions, gear failures, and toxic gas leaks are common hazards in city-states and factories.
*   **Class Warfare:** [THREAT: Social] The deep-seated resentment and open conflict between the wealthy industrialists and the oppressed working class, often erupting into riots or violent clashes.

## World-Changing Events [EVENT: Cataclysmic]
*   **The Great Boiler Burst:** [EVENT: Technological] The colossal central boiler of Ironclad City suffers a catastrophic failure, threatening to destroy the entire city-state and plunge the region into chaos.
*   **Aetherium's Fall:** [EVENT: Environmental] Aetherium's delicate aether-crystal power core is critically damaged, causing the floating city to slowly descend, threatening to crash into the Rusting Wastes.
*   **The Sunken City Rises:** [EVENT: Mysterious] The Sunken City of Brass, through unknown means, begins to rise from the depths, revealing new technologies, factions, or a long-dormant threat.
*   **The Automaton Awakening:** [EVENT: Ancient Awakening] The Clockwork Cult successfully awakens a colossal, pre-War automaton, which begins to rampage across the land, its purpose and allegiance unknown.
*   **The Gear-Grinders' Revolution:** [EVENT: Rebel Uprising] The Gear-Grinders Union, having reached its breaking point, launches a full-scale revolution across multiple city-states, challenging the established order.
*   **The Truth of the Great Steam War:** [EVENT: Revelatory] The true cause and nature of the Great Steam War are finally uncovered, revealing a shocking conspiracy or a cosmic truth that fundamentally alters humanity's understanding of its past.

## Possible Endings [ENDING: Ambiguous]
*   **The New Industrial Age:** [ENDING: Technological] The players, perhaps allied with the Aetherium Collective, usher in a new era of technological advancement, using rediscovered steam-tech to rebuild the world.
*   **The Workers' Paradise:** [ENDING: Hopeful] The Gear-Grinders Union, with player support, successfully overthrows the oppressive industrialists, establishing a more equitable society, but facing the challenges of governance.
*   **The Ironclad Empire:** [ENDING: Dystopian] The Ironclad Hegemony, with or without player aid, conquers all rival city-states, establishing a unified, but authoritarian, empire across the land.
*   **The Endless Grind:** [ENDING: Cyclical] Despite player efforts, the conflicts over resources and power continue indefinitely, with no clear victor, and the world remains a smog-choked battleground.
*   **The Great Exodus:** [ENDING: Escape] The players discover a way to escape the polluted world, perhaps via a rediscovered space-faring vessel, leading a remnant of humanity to a new, cleaner planet.
*   **The Machine's Embrace:** [ENDING: Transformative] Humanity, through extensive cybernetic and steam-tech augmentation, merges with its machines, becoming a new, post-human species, for better or worse.
