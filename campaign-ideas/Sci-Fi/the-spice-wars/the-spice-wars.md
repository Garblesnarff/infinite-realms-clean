# The Spice Wars
**He Who Controls the Spice Controls the Universe**

## Campaign Overview

The Spice Wars centers on the desert planet Arrakis—only source of "melange," the most valuable substance in the universe. Spice extends life, expands consciousness, enables faster-than-light navigation, and is fatally addictive. The party serves House Atreides, noble family granted stewardship of Arrakis by the Galactic Emperor, displacing their bitter enemies House Harkonnen. What appears as political promotion is actually a trap—the Emperor and other powers conspire to destroy the Atreides through treachery and desert warfare.

The campaign blends political intrigue, desert survival, religious manipulation, and guerrilla warfare. After the inevitable betrayal decimates House Atreides, survivors must adapt to the harsh desert and its native Fremen warriors. The party's choices determine whether they seek simple revenge, become colonizers using the Fremen, or genuinely integrate and lead a revolution that will reshape galactic civilization. Prophecy, prescience, and predetermined fate clash with free will.

This is a campaign about power and its corruptions, about cultural appropriation vs. genuine transformation, about whether greatness requires ruthlessness and whether prophecy can be subverted or only fulfilled.

## Core Themes

- **Power and Corruption**: Spice offers power, but at what cost?
- **Cultural Colonization**: Using vs. serving the Fremen
- **Ecology and Adaptation**: Desert shapes everything
- **Prescience and Free Will**: Seeing future vs. being trapped by it
- **Feudal Politics**: Great Houses maneuvering, Emperor balancing power
- **Religion as Tool**: Prophecy planted for later exploitation

## Session Breakdown (35 Sessions)

### Act I: Arrival and Betrayal (Sessions 1-9)

**Session 1-2: Caladan to Arrakis**
Party in service to Duke Leto Atreides on water world Caladan. Briefing: Granted Arrakis stewardship, replacing Harkonnens. Warned it's dangerous—possibly trap. Travel to Arrakis. First sight of endless desert. Arrival at Arrakeen city. Heat, dust, scarcity.

**Session 3-4: Desert Power**
Learning spice operation. Massive harvester machines. Sandworms (Shai-Hulud)—enormous creatures attracted by vibration, destroy harvesters. Fremen—native desert people, mysterious, dangerous. Harkonnen sabotage discovered in equipment.

**Session 5-6: Political Maneuvering**
Meeting smuggler alliances, water merchants, Fremen representatives. Duke Leto attempts fair governance vs. exploitation. Imperial ecologist Liet-Kynes shows party desert's secrets. Fremen test the party—ritual combat, water sharing.

**Session 7: The Traitor**
Dr. Yueh, trusted physician, has been compromised by Harkonnens (they hold his wife). He will betray the Atreides. Subtle clues for party to possibly detect. Depends on paranoia and investigation.

**Session 8-9: Night of Knives**
Harkonnen attack with Imperial Sardaukar support (Emperor's elite troops). Overwhelming force. Duke Leto killed (tries to take Baron Harkonnen with poison gas tooth, fails or partially succeeds). Atreides forces scattered. Party escapes into desert or captured then escapes. Everything lost.

### Act II: Desert Transformation (Sessions 10-18)

**Session 10-11: Survival**
Flee into deep desert. No water, killer heat, sandworms, Harkonnen hunters. Desperation. Rescued by Fremen or prove worth to join sietch (desert community). Conditional acceptance—must prove useful and respectful.

**Session 12-13: Learning the Ways**
Training in Fremen ways: stillsuits (water reclamation), desert navigation, sandworm riding, water discipline. Cultural immersion. Some party members adapt easily, others struggle. Fremen wary of outsiders claiming prophesied status.

**Session 14-15: The Prophesied One**
Fremen legends speak of "Lisan al-Gaib" (Voice from the Outer World) who will lead them to paradise. Bene Gesserit (mystical order) planted these prophecies for manipulation. If party member has prescient visions, Fremen see signs. Choice: Exploit prophecy or reject it.

**Session 16-17: Guerrilla Warfare**
Raiding Harkonnen spice operations. Hit-and-run attacks. Sandworm-riding strikes. Party becomes bridge between Fremen warriors and Atreides loyalists. Building resistance network. Harkonnen retaliation targets civilians.

**Session 18: Water of Life**
Fremen ritual: drinking Water of Life (sandworm poison). Survive = gains prescience/enhanced abilities. Fail = death. Party member takes it (voluntarily or required). Visions of possible futures—terrible and wonderful. Burden of foresight.

### Act III: Holy War Rising (Sessions 19-27)

**Session 19-20: Uniting the Tribes**
Northern and southern Fremen sietches divided. Party must unite them. Challenges of leadership: ritual combat, demonstrating worthiness, respecting culture while introducing new tactics. Political maneuvering among Fremen leaders.

**Session 21-22: The Weapon**
Discovery or development of thumpers (attract sandworms), sonic weapons, or method to disrupt shields (shields attract worms). Technological edge in desert warfare. Also: atomic weapons in Atreides family cache—forbidden by Great Convention, but tempting.

**Session 23-24: Harkonnen Counterinsurgency**
Baron Vladimir Harkonnen brings in more Sardaukar. Orbital bombardment of suspected Fremen sites. Captured Fremen tortured for information. Party witnesses atrocities. Moral questions: How far in retaliation?

**Session 25-26: Taking the War Public**
Revealing Emperor's complicity in Atreides destruction. Smuggler networks spread word. Other Great Houses take notice. Landsraad (noble assembly) divided. Some support, others fear upsetting balance. Galactic politics matters again.

**Session 27: Prescient Trap**
Party member with prescience sees multiple futures—all paths lead to jihad (holy war) spreading across galaxy, billions dead in their name. Can only delay, not prevent. Crushing weight of predestination. What choices remain?

### Act IV: Revolution and Throne (Sessions 28-35)

**Session 28-29: The Southern Push**
Fremen armies sweep north. Overwhelming Harkonnen garrisons through desert warfare superiority. Riding sandworms into battle—terrifying sight. Guild navigators (who need spice) pressured to embargo Harkonnen. Economic warfare.

**Session 30-31: Siege of Arrakeen**
Final assault on capital city. Fremen forces vs. Harkonnen and Sardaukar. Urban combat in ancient city. Party leads critical strikes. Baron Vladimir's fate—killed or captured, player choice affects reputation.

**Session 32: The Emperor Arrives**
Emperor Shaddam IV arrives with full Sardaukar force to restore order (protect his power). Massive fleet in orbit. Party must negotiate or fight. Threat: Destroy spice fields with atomics (mutual destruction). Standoff.

**Session 33-34: Trial by Combat**
Challenge for Imperial throne. Party champion vs. Emperor's champion. Duel before assembled Great Houses. Winner doesn't just win fight—wins political legitimacy. Intense one-on-one combat with galactic stakes.

**Session 35: New Order**
Victory reshapes galaxy. Party's choices throughout determine outcome:
- **Tyrant Path**: Seize throne, unleash jihad, rule through fear
- **Revolutionary Path**: Reform Empire, share spice, ecological restoration
- **Fremen Path**: Arrakis independence, reject imperial politics
- **Martyrdom Path**: Sacrifice self to prevent worse future, someone else rules

Epilogue shows consequences 10 years later. Jihad or peace? Desert greening or preserved? Spice monopoly or shared? Legacy defined by choices.

## Major Locations

**Arrakis/Dune** (Desert Planet)
Only source of spice. Endless desert, sandworms, Fremen, extreme heat. Harsh beauty, deadly to unprepared.

**Arrakeen** (Capital City)
Shield wall protects from storms. Harkonnen palace, spice processing, water merchants. Ancient architecture.

**Sietch Tabr** (Fremen Community)
Hidden desert community. Cave system, water storage, communal life. Respectful outsiders allowed, others killed.

**Caladan** (Atreides Homeworld)
Water world, oceans, green, gentle. Everything Arrakis isn't. Lost to party early.

**Giedi Prime** (Harkonnen Homeworld)
Industrial nightmare, polluted, slaves, gladiatorial arenas. If visited, shows enemy culture.

**Kaitain** (Imperial Throne World)
Palatial, bureaucratic, political intrigue central. Visited for final confrontation.

**The Deep Desert** (Unexplored Arrakis)
Beyond mapped regions. Massive worms, secret Fremen sietches, untapped spice fields. Freedom and danger.

**Guild Heighliners** (Massive Spaceships)
Space Navigators fold space for FTL travel. Dependent on spice. Mobile locations and leverage.

## Unique Mechanics

### Spice and Prescience
- **Spice Consumption**: Grants bonuses but addictive
- **Prescience**: See possible futures, limited agency
- **Water of Life**: Dramatic power boost or death
- **Terrible Purpose**: See paths, all lead to jihad

### Desert Survival
- **Water Discipline**: Track water, death if careless
- **Stillsuits**: Required for desert, reclaim body moisture
- **Sandworm Awareness**: Vibrations attract death
- **Heat Endurance**: Constitution checks, exhaustion

### Fremen Culture
- **Water Sharing**: Sacred trust ritual
- **Ritual Combat**: Challenges for leadership
- **Crysknife**: Sacred weapon from sandworm tooth
- **Blue Eyes**: Spice saturation visible marker

### Political Influence
- **Great Houses**: Balance of power
- **Landsraad**: Noble assembly votes
- **Guild Neutrality**: Navigators avoid sides but need spice
- **Bene Gesserit**: Subtle manipulation

### Combat Systems
- **Shields**: Energy barriers stop fast objects, swords needed
- **Weirding Way**: Bene Gesserit martial arts
- **Sandworm Riding**: Cavalry charges, terrifying
- **Lasgun-Shield Reaction**: Atomic explosion (mutually assured destruction)

### Ecology
- **Sandworm Life Cycle**: Spice is their byproduct
- **Water Transformation**: Fremen dream of green planet
- **Desert Power**: Understanding ecosystem is power
- **Terraforming Consequences**: Green Arrakis = no worms = no spice

## Plot Twists and Complications

**Twist 1: Bene Gesserit Breeding Program**
Jessica was supposed to birth daughter, had son instead (Paul). Throws off centuries of genetic planning.

**Twist 2: Guild Dependency**
Space Guild navigators are spice-mutated humans, completely dependent, will do anything to preserve it.

**Twist 3: Sardaukar are Harkonnen Kin**
Emperor's elite troops and Harkonnens share breeding lines. Conspiracy runs deep.

**Twist 4: Jihad is Inevitable**
Every prescient path leads to holy war. Can change details, not outcome. Predestination horror.

**Twist 5: Greening Kills Spice**
Ecological transformation would destroy sandworms, ending spice forever. Paradise or power?

**Twist 6: Ghola Return**
Duncan Idaho resurrected by Tleilaxu as ghola (clone with recovered memories). Tool or person?

## Victory/Defeat Conditions

**Military Victory**: Defeat Harkonnen and Emperor, control Arrakis
**Political Victory**: Reform Empire through legitimacy and alliances
**Ecological Victory**: Begin desert transformation, free Fremen from harshness
**Moral Victory**: Prevent jihad through martyrdom/different path
**Pyrrhic Victory**: Win but unleash holy war, billions die in your name
**Defeat**: Killed by Harkonnen, Fremen scattered, Atreides extinct
**Transcendent Victory**: Break prescient trap, forge new path, free will triumphant
