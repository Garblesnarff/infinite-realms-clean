
# The Slime Sovereign

- **Campaign Type / Genre**: Nation-Building / Political Fantasy / Monster Alliance
- **Estimated Length**: 6-8 sessions
- **Player Level Range**: 3-10
- **Tone**: Hopeful, Strategic, Diplomatic, Inspirational

### Core Premise
The players find themselves in the Jura Tempest, a wild frontier filled with monster tribes and dangerous dungeons. After an unexpected encounter with a powerful but benevolent slime entity, they are tasked with helping unite the scattered monster races into a peaceful nation. Armed with unique abilities gained from absorbing fallen foes, the players must navigate tribal politics, defend against hostile nations, and transform a chaotic wilderness into a thriving civilization where monsters and humans can coexist.

### Difficulty Context: **Medium**
This campaign balances combat, diplomacy, and resource management. Players must think strategically about which monster tribes to ally with first and how to defend their growing nation from external threats.

### Story Arc

**Act 1: The First Alliance (Sessions 1-2)**
- The players awaken in a cave after a mysterious transition, encountering Rimuru, a powerful slime who has gained consciousness and incredible abilities. They discover they can absorb skills from defeated creatures. Their first task: broker peace between warring goblin and dire wolf tribes, forming the foundation of their new nation.

**Act 2: Building the Nation (Sessions 3-5)**
- With the goblins and wolves united, the players must expand their influence. They encounter ogres fleeing destruction, dwarven craftsmen seeking new opportunities, and a ancient dragon who becomes an unlikely ally. Each new alliance brings unique benefits but also complications. They establish the town of Rimuru, create trade agreements, and develop unique monster-human cooperation systems.

**Act 3: The Demon Lord's Test (Sessions 6-8)**
- A neighboring human kingdom, threatened by the growing monster nation, launches an attack. Simultaneously, the players learn that their actions have caught the attention of powerful Demon Lords who seek to test or destroy this unprecedented alliance. The final sessions involve defending against a multi-front war, making difficult choices about violence and peace, and ultimately proving that their monster nation deserves to exist.

### Session-by-Session Outline

**Session 1: Awakening in the Cave**
- **Focus**: Introduction and first transformation
- **Key Events**: Players awaken with strange new powers; encounter Rimuru the slime; witness a battle between goblins and dire wolves; use newfound absorption abilities in combat
- **Escalation**: Goblin village faces annihilation by wolf pack

**Session 2: The First Summit**
- **Focus**: Diplomacy and alliance-building
- **Key Events**: Negotiate peace between goblins and wolves; name-giving ceremony grants monsters new power; establish rudimentary village; first hint of external threats
- **Escalation**: Scouts report approaching ogre refugees and pursuing orc armies

**Session 3: The Ogre Crisis**
- **Focus**: Combat and resource management
- **Key Events**: Ogre tribe arrives seeking sanctuary; orc warlord demands surrender; players must choose to fight or negotiate; victory brings powerful new allies
- **Escalation**: Word spreads of the monster alliance; dwarven envoy arrives

**Session 4: Forging the Future**
- **Focus**: Economic development and construction
- **Key Events**: Dwarven master craftsman Kaijin offers to help build infrastructure; players design and construct town improvements; first trade route established; social integration challenges arise
- **Escalation**: Ancient dragon Veldora offers a pact

**Session 5: The Dragon's Bargain**
- **Focus**: High-stakes negotiation and magical research
- **Key Events**: Players must decide whether to release/ally with imprisoned dragon; magical researchers arrive; nation gains powerful magical capabilities; formal declaration of independence
- **Escalation**: Human kingdom of Falmuth views this as a threat

**Session 6: Diplomatic Overtures**
- **Focus**: Political maneuvering and espionage
- **Key Events**: Ambassadors from multiple nations arrive; players host a diplomatic summit; uncover assassination plot; must navigate complex political landscape
- **Escalation**: Falmuth secretly prepares for war

**Session 7: The Gathering Storm**
- **Focus**: War preparation and tough choices
- **Key Events**: Intelligence reveals imminent invasion; players must fortify defenses, call in allies, and make difficult decisions about preemptive strikes; internal dissent arises
- **Escalation**: Multi-pronged attack begins - Falmuth's army and demon lord's forces

**Session 8: Birth of a Nation**
- **Focus**: Epic battle and political resolution
- **Key Events**: Large-scale warfare using allied monster forces; players confront enemy commanders and demon lord emissaries; must choose between revenge and mercy; establish lasting peace treaty
- **Resolution**: The monster nation of Tempest is officially recognized; players have created a new model for civilization

### Major NPCs

- **Rimuru Tempest**: The benevolent slime sovereign and quest giver. **Role**: Mentor / Ally. **Personality**: Curious, compassionate, surprisingly cunning when needed. Has memories of another world that inform unusual solutions to problems. **Flaw/Secret**: Sometimes too trusting; carries guilt about the circumstances of their transformation.

- **Rigurd**: Chief of the goblin tribe, now leader of the goblin corps. **Role**: Loyal Follower / Administrator. **Personality**: Earnest, devoted, emotional. Ages backwards after receiving a name. **Flaw/Secret**: Fear of failing those who depend on him; sometimes overzealous in proving worth.

- **Ranga**: Alpha of the dire wolf pack, now tempest wolf commander. **Role**: Military Leader / Mount. **Personality**: Noble, fierce, protective. Communicates telepathically with bonded allies. **Flaw/Secret**: Struggles with wolf pack's predatory instincts versus new peaceful ideals.

- **Shion**: Ogre princess turned oni general. **Role**: Personal Guard / Warrior. **Personality**: Confident, protective, terrible cook. Absolutely loyal to the players. **Flaw/Secret**: Guilt over her clan's destruction; reckless when protecting loved ones.

- **Benimaru**: Ogre prince turned oni strategist. **Role**: Military Strategist / Rival. **Personality**: Proud, intelligent, hot-headed when honor is questioned. **Flaw/Secret**: Desires revenge against those who destroyed his village; conflicts with peaceful solutions.

- **Kaijin**: Exiled dwarf master craftsman. **Role**: Infrastructure Developer / Artisan. **Personality**: Gruff, perfectionist, secretly sentimental. **Flaw/Secret**: Was exiled for political reasons; fears his past catching up.

- **Veldora Tempest**: Ancient storm dragon, sealed for centuries. **Role**: Powerful Ally / Wild Card. **Personality**: Boisterous, loves manga and entertainment, incredibly powerful. **Flaw/Secret**: Childish despite age; his release could trigger war with other true dragons.

- **King Gazel Dwargo**: Dwarven king of the Armed Nation of Dwargon. **Role**: Political Ally / Potential Threat. **Personality**: Shrewd, honorable, pragmatic. **Flaw/Secret**: Torn between ancient anti-monster prejudices and seeing the value in alliance.

### Key Locations

- **The Great Cave of Veldora**: Massive underground cavern where the campaign begins. Contains sealed dragon, unique magical herbs, and hidden treasures. Becomes the spiritual heart of the new nation.

- **Rimuru City**: The rapidly developing capital of the monster nation. Features mixed architecture combining goblin, ogre, and dwarf styles. Has residential districts, craftsman quarters, central marketplace, and diplomatic hall.

- **The Goblin Village**: Original settlement that becomes the agricultural center. Shows dramatic transformation from primitive huts to organized farming community.

- **The Dwargon Trade Route**: Mountain pass connecting to the dwarven kingdom. Strategic location for trade and defense. Features watchtowers and waystation camps.

- **The Western Frontier**: Wild borderlands where various monster tribes still roam. Dangerous but resource-rich. Site of future expansion.

- **The Falmuth Border**: Tense frontier with the human kingdom. Features both sides' military encampments and neutral meeting grounds.

### Central Conflict
The fundamental tension between building a peaceful, prosperous monster nation and the violent prejudices of the outside world. Players must prove that monsters can be more than mindless threats while defending against those who refuse to see them as anything but enemies. The campaign explores themes of identity, community-building, and whether true understanding between different peoples is possible.

### Unique Mechanics

- **Absorption & Evolution**: When a player defeats a significant enemy, they can absorb one ability or trait from that creature. The DM provides 2-3 options (combat ability, utility skill, or physical trait). Players can have up to 5 absorbed abilities at once. Absorbing a new one when at capacity requires replacing an existing ability. This encourages strategic thinking about which powers to keep.

- **Nation Building Points (NBP)**: The party collectively earns NBP through successful missions, alliances, and resource acquisition. NBP can be spent between sessions to: develop town infrastructure (+1 to all settlement defense rolls), establish trade routes (+10% discount at certain merchants), research magical technologies (unlock new crafting options), or recruit specialist NPCs (gain access to unique services). The players decide together how to spend NBP, encouraging collaborative planning.

- **Named Status**: When players give a significant NPC a true name (costs an action and an inspiration point), that NPC gains a permanent power boost and deeper loyalty. Named monsters evolve (goblins become hobgoblins, wolves become tempest wolves, etc.). This creates meaningful decisions about which allies to empower and reinforces the importance of relationships.

- **Reputation Tracks**: The campaign tracks the party's reputation with four major factions: Monster Tribes (affects recruitment), Human Kingdoms (affects diplomacy), Demon Lords (affects major threats), and Merchant Guilds (affects economy). Actions in each session shift these reputations. High reputation grants bonuses; low reputation creates complications. This makes players consider the broader impact of their choices.
