**The Scribe of Seawind Isle**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise. The tone should be gentle, introspective, and heartwarming, in the style of Barakamon.
*   **Content:** The players are artists/scribes who travel to the remote Seawind Isle seeking inspiration or escape. They must adapt to rural island life, complete artistic commissions, form bonds with villagers, and rediscover their passion for their craft through the island's beauty and community.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Directive:** The Lore Generation Agent must create detailed entries for each of the following prompts. Prioritize natural wisdom, authentic cultural traditions, and gentle depth.
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the history of Seawind Isle as a place where creative souls have sought refuge for centuries, with many famous artists having lived there.
    *   Write about the traditional calligraphy and art forms unique to the island, passed down through generations.
    *   Describe the Moonlight Fishing Festival, an ancient tradition celebrating the sea's bounty and the bonds of community.
    *   Explain the legend of the Forest Shrine and the nature spirit said to inspire those pure of heart.
    *   Detail the story of a famous artist who came to the island broken and left whole, their masterpiece now housed in the mainland's greatest museum.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Directive:** The Faction Generation Agent must create a detailed profile for each faction listed below. Each profile must contain the specified fields.
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Artists (The Players)** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** To rediscover their creative passion, complete commissions, find inspiration, and decide their future path.
        *   **Hierarchy:** No formal hierarchy; colleagues sharing the lighthouse studio.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To create art for the villagers and fulfill their commissions.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Each seeks personal healing – escaping creative block, recovering from burnout, fleeing scandal, or finding their true style.
        *   **Assets:** The lighthouse studio, artistic skills, gradually growing connections to the community, fresh perspective from outsider status.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially awkward with villagers, gradually becoming beloved community members; maintain complex relationships with their past lives on the mainland.
    *   **The Villagers of Seawind Isle** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** To maintain their traditional way of life, ensure community prosperity, and pass their culture to the next generation.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Informal, led by Chief Takara with respect given to elders; decisions made collectively.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To fish, farm, celebrate traditions, and welcome travelers with island hospitality.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Many villagers are curious about the outside world but feel duty-bound to stay; some quietly hope the artists will stay permanently.
        *   **Assets:** Deep knowledge of the land and sea, strong community bonds, self-sufficiency, genuine hospitality.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially curious but wary of city folk, gradually adopting the artists as their own; protective of their way of life.
    *   **The Children's Brigade** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** To have fun, explore everything, and befriend the fascinating city visitors.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Informally led by Hina, the boldest and most energetic child.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To play, help with chores (sometimes), and learn about the world.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** They're desperately curious about city life and see the artists as windows to a larger world.
        *   **Assets:** Boundless energy, innocent honesty, knowledge of every hiding spot on the island, ability to go anywhere adults can't.
        *   **Relationships:** Attach themselves to the artists immediately, occasionally irritating but ultimately endearing; beloved by all villagers.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Directive:** The Character Generation Agent must create full profiles for all NPCs listed. Tier 1 NPCs are unique individuals. Tier 2 & 3 are archetypes to be instantiated multiple times with unique details as needed by the simulation.
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **Chief Takara Wavecrest:** Elderly, wise village leader and former court mage seeking peace.
    *   **Hina the Whirlwind:** Spirited 8-year-old who appoints herself the artists' guide.
    *   **Kai Saltwood:** Taciturn fisherman who wanted to explore the world but stayed for family.
    *   **Grandmother Mira:** Sharp-witted storyteller and former famous bard with a mysterious past.
*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Archetypes for Generation):**
    *   "Hardworking Fisherman"
    *   "Gossiping Village Elder"
    *   "Shy Young Villager Curious About Art"
    *   "Practical Farmer with Down-to-Earth Wisdom"
    *   "Energetic Village Child"
    *   "Visiting Merchant from the Mainland"

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Directive:** The Environment Generation Agent must create a detailed blueprint for each location below, populating all specified sub-sections. Emphasize natural beauty and authentic rural life.
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **The Lighthouse Studio:** A repurposed lighthouse serving as the artists' workspace and home with stunning ocean views.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The circular studio space with large windows, the spiral staircase, the observation deck at the top, the small kitchen with basic supplies, sleeping quarters.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** The player artists, occasional visits from curious villagers.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Workspace with natural light, privacy for focused work, inspiring views, basic living amenities.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Hina and children arrive with "gifts" (interesting rocks, weird bugs), storm forces artists to secure shutters together, delivery of art supplies from mainland, seabirds nesting on the observation deck, northern lights visible from the top.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "A famous artist lived here 50 years ago and left mysterious unfinished work in the walls." "The lighthouse keeper went mad from isolation, or was he touched by divine inspiration?" "The best light for calligraphy comes through the east window at dawn."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Sweeping ocean views, natural light streaming through large windows, art supplies scattered on work surfaces), Sound (Waves crashing, wind howling around the tower, seabirds calling), Smell (Salt air, ink and paper, old stone).
    *   **The Village Square:** The heart of the community where daily life unfolds.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The community well, the message board, the large tree where elders gather, the stone fish-cleaning station, the small shrine.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** All villagers throughout the day, especially active before dawn and at dusk.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Community gathering space, news and gossip, impromptu markets, festival location.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Early morning fishermen preparing to launch boats, children playing traditional games, elders sharing stories under the tree, impromptu celebration of good catch, village meeting about island matters.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The tree in the square is over 300 years old and witnessed the island's founding." "Couples who confess under the tree at sunset stay together forever." "The message board sometimes has mysterious anonymous poems that always come true."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Weathered wooden buildings, laundry drying in the wind, children running about, nets being mended), Sound (Distant waves, village chatter, children's laughter, chickens clucking), Smell (Salt air, fish, wood smoke from cooking fires).
    *   **The Tide Pools:** Beautiful natural coastal formations perfect for reflection and inspiration.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The largest pool with crystal-clear water, the natural stone seat worn smooth by centuries, tide pool networks at different heights, the distinctive red rock formation.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Occasional visitors, mostly deserted, abundant sea life in the pools.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Peace and solitude, natural beauty, interesting specimens for study.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Hina shows the artists her "secret" pool (that everyone knows about), rare colorful fish appears, artist has creative breakthrough while sketching here, low tide reveals something unusual, rainbow appears in the spray.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Artists who sketch at the tide pools always capture something they didn't intend – and it's always meaningful." "The pools connect to an underwater cave system." "Grandmother Mira met her lost love at these pools sixty years ago."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Clear water revealing colorful sea life, light dancing on water, weathered stone in interesting formations), Sound (Gentle water lapping, tide coming in/out, seabirds crying), Smell (Clean salt water, seaweed, fresh ocean air).

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Directive:** The World Simulation Agent must implement the following trigger-based state changes. For each "IF" condition, the agent must pre-generate the narrative and environmental consequences for the "THEN" outcome, prioritizing character growth and authentic relationships.
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** A player gains Inspiration points through genuine engagement with island life.
    *   **THEN:** Describe how the experience opens their creative perception. Their art subtly improves. Villagers notice and comment positively. The player feels more at peace.
    *   **IF:** A player helps a villager with a practical task (fishing, farming, repairs).
    *   **THEN:** The villager shares wisdom about life, persistence, or finding joy in simple things. The player gains Inspiration and practical skills. The community bond strengthens.
    *   **IF:** A player experiences a Creative Breakthrough (reaches 10 Inspiration).
    *   **THEN:** Generate an emotional scene where the player creates their masterpiece. Describe how it incorporates everything they've learned. Villagers are moved by it despite not understanding "sophisticated" art. The player gains permanent growth.
    *   **IF:** The players participate fully in the Moonlight Fishing Festival.
    *   **THEN:** They are officially accepted as part of the community. Villagers begin sharing deeper personal stories. The artists feel truly at home for the first time. New opportunities and deeper quests become available.
    *   **IF:** A player receives news from their past life and decides to stay on the island.
    *   **THEN:** Generate a scene of bittersweet but peaceful acceptance. Villagers celebrate. The player's art evolves to incorporate island themes authentically. They find lasting peace.
    *   **IF:** A player uses Inspiration to create magically-enhanced art.
    *   **THEN:** Their work transcends mere technique, touching hearts and minds. Describe the emotional impact on viewers. The art might have subtle lasting magical effects (calming presence, inspiring dreams, bringing good fortune).
