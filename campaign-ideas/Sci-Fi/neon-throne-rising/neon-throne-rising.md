# Neon Throne Rising

A Dungeons & Dragons 5e campaign about a reincarnated demon lord reclaiming power in a cyberpunk future where magic and technology have merged.

*   **Genre**: Cyberpunk Fantasy, Power Reclamation, Techno-Magic Thriller
*   **Tone**: Dark and Stylish
*   **Difficulty**: Medium
*   **Length**: Medium Campaign

***

## Campaign Overview

*   **Title**: Neon Throne Rising
*   **Campaign Type/Genre**: Cyberpunk fantasy combining magic and technology, power reclamation narrative with moral complexity
*   **Estimated Length**: A medium campaign of 7-8 sessions.
*   **Player Level Range**: Starting at Level 3, ending around Level 10.
*   **Tone**: Dark but stylish. Neon-soaked cyberpunk aesthetics meet ancient demonic power. Morally complex—are demon lords evil or just powerful beings misunderstood by history? Corporate dystopia criticism. Cool factor high. Stakes personal and world-shaking.
*   **Core Premise**: 500 years ago, the party's leader was a feared demon lord slain by heroes. Now reincarnated into Neo-Tokyo 2099, a sprawling metropolis where magic and cutting-edge technology coexist, they must reclaim their power in a world that barely remembers them. The city is controlled by mega-corporations that have monopolized both tech and magic. To rebuild their former glory, the party must navigate corporate warfare, underground resistance movements, techno-magical gangs, and government conspiracies while uncovering why they were resurrected and what ancient threat looms over this neon-drenched future.

## Difficulty Context

**Medium Difficulty**: This campaign balances investigation, social maneuvering, and tactical combat. The party starts weakened (reincarnated without full power) and must gradually reclaim abilities while navigating a dangerous cyberpunk world. Resources are available through underworld connections but require risk. Combat encounters mix magic with technology—hackers, combat cyborgs, and techno-mages. Rest opportunities exist but the city never sleeps, creating time pressure. Moral choices affect faction relationships and power growth. Standard rest rules with urban complications (gangs, corp security, digital surveillance).

## Story Arc

The narrative follows a power reclamation structure over 7-8 sessions, building from forgotten shadow to feared returned power.

*   **Act I: Awakening in Neon (Sessions 1-2)**: The party's leader awakens 500 years in the future with fragmented memories of their past as a demon lord. The world has changed—Neo-Tokyo is a megalopolis where corporations rule, magic has fused with technology, and ancient demons are myths. The party forms around the reincarnated demon lord, drawn by fate or opportunity. They must survive in this unfamiliar world, begin reclaiming lost power, and discover that mysterious forces orchestrated their resurrection.

*   **Act II: The Corporate Underworld (Sessions 3-5)**: To reclaim power, the party delves into Neo-Tokyo's shadows—the underground resistance fighting corporate tyranny, techno-magical criminal syndicates, and forbidden archives containing pre-collapse history. They discover that mega-corporations harvested demonic essence to power their techno-magic, essentially enslaving fragments of the demon lord's old power. The party must steal back their essence while navigating faction politics. A government agency hunts demons, unaware they're creating the very threat they fear.

*   **Act III: The True Throne (Sessions 6-8)**: The party uncovers the truth: they were resurrected because an ancient sealed threat is awakening—something that made even demon lords look benign. The corporations accidentally weakened the seal through essence extraction. The party must decide: reclaim ultimate power for personal gain, help the city despite its corruption, or find balance between their demonic nature and this new world's needs. The climax involves corporate warfare, government intervention, and confronting the sealed horror while determining what kind of ruler they'll become in this neon age.

## Session Breakdown

*   **Session 1: The Demon Lord Awakens**
    *   **Focus**: Introduction, World-building, Identity Discovery
    *   **Key Events**: The party's leader awakens in an underground med-clinic with fragmented memories. They died 500 years ago as a feared demon lord, slain by legendary heroes. Now they're alive in Neo-Tokyo 2099, a vast cyberpunk city where magic is commercialized and technology has advanced impossibly. The clinic doctor, Dr. Kaira Vex, explains she found them in a corporate lab marked "Project: Resurrection." The party forms—some hired by Kaira, others drawn mysteriously to the demon lord. Corporate hit squads attack, seeking to recapture their "asset." The party must escape through neon-lit streets, discovering their former powers are weak but recoverable.
    *   **Escalation**: During escape, the demon lord experiences a vision of their death and glimpses their killer's descendant living in this future.

*   **Session 2: Ghost in the Machine**
    *   **Focus**: Exploration, System Learning, First Power Recovery
    *   **Key Events**: The party explores Neo-Tokyo—towering arcologies, holographic advertisements, flying vehicles, augmented reality layers, and underground slums. Dr. Kaira provides intel: the mega-corporation Nexarcana holds demon essence in their power cores, literally fueling the city with harvested demonic energy. One facility contains the demon lord's original magical signature. The party infiltrates it, combining stealth and hacking (magic or tech). They reclaim essence, partially restoring power. In the facility's data core, they discover they weren't the only demon lord resurrected—three others awakened and disappeared.
    *   **Escalation**: Security AI achieves sentience mid-heist and demands asylum in exchange for helping the party escape. Welcome to the cyberpunk age.

*   **Session 3: The Crimson Syndicate**
    *   **Focus**: Underworld Introduction, Faction Relations, Moral Complexity
    *   **Key Events**: To reclaim more power, the party needs resources and information only the criminal underworld possesses. They're introduced to the Crimson Syndicate—a techno-magical yakuza run by Akane "The Red Kitsune" Shiranui, a powerful nine-tailed fox spirit who survived from the old age. She recognizes the demon lord's aura. She offers alliance: help her take down a rival gang controlled by Nexarcana, and she'll provide access to essence caches. The mission involves gang warfare mixing swordplay, gunfights, and magic duels in neon-soaked streets. The party discovers the rival gang are desperate people forced into debt-slavery by corporations—gray morality hits hard.
    *   **Escalation**: Akane reveals she knew the demon lord 500 years ago. Their relationship was... complicated. Old flames and new agendas collide.

*   **Session 4: The Demon Hunters**
    *   **Focus**: Investigation, Paranoia, Chase Sequence
    *   **Key Events**: A government agency, SANCTUM (Supernatural Anomaly Control and Termination Unit of Ministry), hunts resurrected demons. Their elite hunter, Agent Ryu Takeda, tracks the party. He's enhanced with anti-magic cybernetics and wields weapons designed to kill demons. The session is a cat-and-mouse game through Neo-Tokyo—the party must avoid SANCTUM surveillance (both digital and magical) while continuing essence recovery. They discover SANCTUM is funded by Nexarcana—the corporation creates demon threats then profits from government contracts to eliminate them. Classic dystopia.
    *   **Escalation**: Agent Takeda corners the party. Before battle, he reveals he's descended from the hero who killed them 500 years ago and carries the legendary demon-slaying blade Kagebane, now augmented with plasma tech.

*   **Session 5: The Resistance Calls**
    *   **Focus**: Faction Choice, Ideology, Preparation
    *   **Key Events**: The underground resistance movement "Aurora's Children" contacts the party. Led by ex-corporate hacker Yuki Nakamura, they fight to free the city from corporate tyranny. Yuki reveals corporations extracted demon essence by destroying ancient sealed sites, weakening barriers that kept far worse things contained. The party must choose: ally with resistance (idealistic but weak), continue with Crimson Syndicate (powerful but morally gray), or go solo (maximum power, maximum enemies). Decision affects resources for endgame. The party learns two other reincarnated demon lords have joined Nexarcana willingly, trading freedom for power.
    *   **Escalation**: The fourth demon lord, Vorathis the Destroyer, appears. Unlike others, he wants to burn this corrupt world down and rebuild demon supremacy. He challenges the party to join him or face him later.

*   **Session 6: Corporate Warfare**
    *   **Focus**: Large-Scale Conflict, Power Demonstration, Revelation
    *   **Key Events**: Nexarcana makes their move, deploying demon-powered weapons against rival corporations and resistance simultaneously. The city erupts in warfare—corporate security forces, resistance guerrillas, government agents, demon lords, and ancient sealed horrors escaping weakened barriers. The party must navigate this chaos while pursuing the corporate CEO, Akihiko Sato, who orchestrated the demon lord resurrections. They infiltrate Nexarcana's headquarters—a massive arcology combining gothic architecture with cyberpunk aesthetics. Inside, they discover why they were resurrected: Sato planned to use them as batteries for a weapon to enforce corporate world domination.
    *   **Escalation**: The weapon activates prematurely, tearing reality. Something ancient begins pushing through—the Void Sovereign, a being that made demon lords look like heroes by comparison.

*   **Session 7: Neon Apocalypse**
    *   **Focus**: Ultimate Choice, Epic Combat, Defining Identity
    *   **Key Events**: The Void Sovereign begins manifesting, corrupting technology and magic alike. Reality glitches—digital and mystical errors cascade. Nexarcana's plan has doomed the city. The party faces multiple threats: two demon lords who joined Nexarcana (redeemable through combat or dialogue), Vorathis who wants to kill the Void Sovereign then rule the ashes, Agent Takeda who must choose between duty and survival, CEO Sato who's been corrupted by Void influence, and the Sovereign itself. The party must make alliances from former enemies and decide what they fight for—personal power, protecting this world despite its corruption, or balance.
    *   **Boss Mechanics**: The Void Sovereign exists partially between dimensions. Technology malfunctions near it, magic warps unpredictably. Requires combined tech-magic solutions to damage.
    *   **Escalation**: To defeat it requires channeling massive power. The party can absorb all essence becoming supreme demon lord (corrupting but powerful) or share power with allies (weaker individually, stronger together).

*   **Session 8: The Throne of Neon and Shadow**
    *   **Focus**: Climax, Resolution, Establishing New Order
    *   **Key Events**: Final battle against the Void Sovereign. The party uses everything learned—techno-magic combinations, factional alliances, reclaimed demon powers, and new age resources. The fight is spectacular, mixing magic, technology, corporate resources, resistance tactics, and ancient demon might. Victory requires sacrifice—either personal power, important NPC allies, or pieces of the city. The Sovereign is defeated/sealed. In aftermath, the party must establish new order: Do they become demon lord of Neo-Tokyo (ruling through power)? Work with reformed corporations (pragmatic compromise)? Support resistance democracy (idealistic but chaotic)? Or leave the city to find its own path?
    *   **Epilogue**: Shows Neo-Tokyo's future based on party choices. Their legend begins spreading—500 years ago they were defeated heroes' villains, now they're remembered as saviors... or new tyrants. The neon throne awaits its ruler.

## Major NPCs

*   **Dr. Kaira Vex**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Brilliant cyberneticist and mage, curious about blending tech and magic, morally gray.
    *   **Background**: Found the demon lord in Nexarcana lab, chose to free them rather than report it.
    *   **Role**: Questgiver and ally, provides information and augmentations.
    *   **Secret**: She's researching immortality and sees demon resurrection as key to her work.

*   **Akane "The Red Kitsune" Shiranui**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Ancient kitsune spirit, centuries old, cunning, playful, dangerous.
    *   **Background**: Survived 500 years by adapting to each age. Knew the demon lord in the past.
    *   **Role**: Powerful ally and potential romance/rivalry. Leads Crimson Syndicate.
    *   **Development**: Their past relationship was complex—allies? Lovers? Rivals? Player demon lord decides.

*   **Agent Ryu Takeda**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Dedicated demon hunter, enhanced with anti-magic tech, honorable despite cold demeanor.
    *   **Background**: Descended from hero who slew the demon lord. Wields ancestral blade Kagebane.
    *   **Role**: Primary recurring antagonist who can become ally when faced with greater threat.
    *   **Arc**: Must choose between ancestor's legacy (slay demons) and pragmatism (demons might save world).

*   **Yuki Nakamura**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Idealistic resistance hacker, believes in freedom and justice, naive about power's cost.
    *   **Background**: Former Nexarcana employee who discovered corporate atrocities.
    *   **Role**: Represents hope for a better world, challenges party's demon lord nature.
    *   **Development**: Either inspires party to heroism or becomes disillusioned by harsh reality.

*   **CEO Akihiko Sato**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Corporate mastermind, brilliant and ruthless, sees people as resources.
    *   **Background**: Rose from nothing to control Nexarcana through genius and cruelty.
    *   **Role**: Primary antagonist representing corporate dystopia.
    *   **Twist**: Void Sovereign corruption makes him partly sympathetic—victim of forces he unleashed.

*   **Vorathis the Destroyer (Rival Demon Lord)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Aggressive, supremacist, believes demons should rule and mortals should serve.
    *   **Background**: Another resurrected demon lord embracing old ways.
    *   **Role**: Represents the path of pure demon supremacy—what the party could become.
    *   **Conflict**: Either rival to defeat or ally sharing power philosophy.

*   **The Twins - Lyra and Nox (Demon Lords)**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Joined Nexarcana willingly, trading servitude for comfort.
    *   **Background**: Were lesser demon lords who accepted defeat 500 years ago.
    *   **Role**: Represent compromise and adaptation—working within the system.
    *   **Redemption**: Can be convinced to rebel against corporate masters.

*   **The Void Sovereign**
    *   **Personality & Motivations**: Alien, incomprehensible, hunger incarnate. Wants to consume reality.
    *   **Nature**: Existed before demons, sealed by ancient cooperation of all magical beings.
    *   **Role**: The true threat making everyone else's conflicts petty by comparison.
    *   **Design**: Reality-warping horror mixing Lovecraftian cosmic terror with digital corruption.

## Key Locations

*   **Neo-Tokyo 2099**: Sprawling megacity covering what was once Tokyo and surrounding regions. Vertical architecture, neon everywhere, holographic ads, flying cars, cybernetic citizens, and magitech infrastructure.

*   **The Undercity**: Below gleaming corporate towers, slums and criminal havens exist. The resistance hides here. Also contains ancient sealed sites being slowly corrupted.

*   **Nexarcana Tower**: Massive corporate headquarters, 300 stories of gothic cyberpunk architecture. Houses demon essence reactors powering the city.

*   **The Red Lantern District**: Crimson Syndicate territory. Traditional Japanese aesthetics meet cyberpunk—neon torii gates, holographic geisha, techno-magical gambling dens.

*   **SANCTUM Headquarters**: Government facility, sterile and militaristic. Holds captured demons and researches anti-magical technology.

*   **The Rift Site**: Where the Void Sovereign begins manifesting. Reality breaks down—digital glitches merge with magical corruption, impossible geometry, terrifying.

## Central Conflict

The central conflict operates on multiple levels:

**External**: Reclaim lost power in unfamiliar world while navigating corporate warfare, government persecution, and criminal politics.

**Mystery**: Uncover why the party was resurrected and what threat looms over Neo-Tokyo.

**Philosophical**: What does it mean to be a demon lord in an age where corporations commit worse atrocities than ancient demons ever did? Are old titles relevant?

**Moral**: How will the party use reclaimed power? Supremacy? Protection? Balance? The choices define their legacy.

**Existential**: Face the Void Sovereign, a threat making all other conflicts seem insignificant. Find unity amid division.

## Side Quests

*   **Essence Hunting**: Track down fragments of the demon lord's power scattered across the city in corporate vaults, black markets, and ancient sites.

*   **Augmentation Integration**: Combine demonic power with cybernetic enhancement and techno-magic, creating unique hybrid abilities.

*   **The Other Demon Lords**: Find and either recruit, defeat, or negotiate with the other three resurrected demon lords.

*   **Agent Takeda's Hunt**: Recurring cat-and-mouse encounters building relationship from pure antagonism to grudging respect to possible alliance.

*   **Crimson Syndicate Operations**: Help Akane's criminal empire while questioning if replacing corporate tyranny with organized crime is improvement.

*   **Resistance Missions**: Support Aurora's Children in their idealistic struggle, experiencing both hope and harsh reality of revolution.

## Unique Mechanics

*   **Difficulty - Medium**: Balanced investigation, social interaction, and tactical combat. Resources available through risk. Time pressure from city's constant activity. Moral choices affect faction relationships.

*   **Power Reclamation System**:
    *   Party's demon lord starts weak, missing most original power.
    *   Essence Fragments scattered across city in corporate facilities, ancient sites, and artifacts.
    *   Recovering essence grants power boosts: new abilities, enhanced stats, signature demon lord techniques.
    *   Each recovery session involves heist, negotiation, combat, or puzzle-solving.
    *   At campaign end, full power depends on essences recovered—affects final battle capability.

*   **Techno-Magic Integration**:
    *   Spells can be augmented with technology: fireballs become plasma grenades, scrying uses hacked cameras, teleportation integrates with transit network.
    *   Technology can be enhanced with magic: weapons gain elemental damage, cybernetics powered by mystical energy, hacking assisted by divination.
    *   Enemies use both: corporate mages with AR targeting, cyborg warriors with elemental implants.
    *   Encourages creative problem-solving mixing magic and tech.

*   **Faction Reputation**:
    *   Track standing with major factions: Crimson Syndicate, Aurora's Children, Nexarcana, SANCTUM.
    *   Actions affect reputation—helping resistance angers corporations, working with yakuza concerns government.
    *   High reputation grants resources, allies, and information.
    *   Can balance multiple factions or commit to one.
    *   Endgame available allies depend on faction standing.

*   **The Demon Lord's Choice**:
    *   Throughout campaign, party makes choices reflecting demon lord philosophy.
    *   **Supremacy Path**: Accumulate power selfishly, dominate others, classic demon lord.
    *   **Protection Path**: Use power to defend the weak, reject supremacist nature.
    *   **Balance Path**: Accept demon nature while finding coexistence with mortals.
    *   Choices affect available abilities, NPC reactions, and ending options.
    *   No path is "wrong"—each has benefits and costs.

*   **Urban Complications**:
    *   The city never sleeps—time pressure from constant activity.
    *   Digital surveillance complicates stealth—must hack or use magic to avoid tracking.
    *   Resources available through underworld connections but require favors.
    *   Long rests possible but enemies don't wait—downtime may advance threats.

***

*Campaign design emphasizes moral complexity in cyberpunk dystopia, blending magic and technology creatively, and exploring what ancient power means in modern age. Players reclaim demon lord legacy while deciding what that legacy should become.*
