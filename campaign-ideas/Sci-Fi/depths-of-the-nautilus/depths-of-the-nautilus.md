# Depths of the Nautilus

*An Underwater Exploration / Science Fantasy campaign — Medium Campaign (10–14 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Depths of the Nautilus
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Underwater Exploration / Science Fantasy / Mystery
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 10–14 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **4** → Finish **10–12**
**Tone (keywords):** mysterious, isolated, wondrous, claustrophobic, scientific, morally complex, deep ocean terror
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party's ship is attacked and sunk by a mysterious sea monster that proves to be a massive advanced submersible—the Nautilus. They are taken prisoner by Captain Nemo, a brilliant but bitter inventor who has renounced the surface world and rules the ocean depths. As unwilling guests, they voyage to incredible underwater wonders and horrors while uncovering Nemo's tragic past and his vendetta against the imperial powers that destroyed everything he loved.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Exploration:** Discovering underwater ruins, exotic ecosystems, and lost civilizations requires investigation and wonder.
*   **Survival:** Limited resources on the submarine, dependence on Nemo's goodwill, and the hostile environment outside.
*   **Moral Complexity:** Nemo is both captor and protector. His revenge is justified but extreme.
*   **Environmental Hazards:** Pressure, drowning, sea monsters, and mechanical failures create constant tension.

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 10–14 sessions)

*   **Act I — Captured (Sessions 1–3):** Ship sunk, rescued/imprisoned by Nemo, exploring the Nautilus, first underwater expeditions.
*   **Act II — Wonders of the Deep (Sessions 4–8):** Touring incredible locations—Atlantis ruins, underwater volcanoes, the South Pole under ice. Learning Nemo's capabilities and philosophy.
*   **Act III — The Captain's Vendetta (Sessions 9–12):** Discovering Nemo's target—an imperial fleet. Wrestling with whether to help, hinder, or escape.
*   **Act IV — The Maelstrom (Sessions 13–14):** Final confrontation, escape attempt, and resolution of Nemo's fate.

---

## Major NPCs

1.  **Captain Nemo** — *The Exiled Genius*
    *   Brilliant inventor, tragic backstory, philosophically opposed to surface nations, walks the line between hero and villain.

2.  **Professor Aronnax** — *The Marine Naturalist*
    *   A fellow "guest" who is fascinated by everything they encounter, torn between scientific wonder and moral concerns.

3.  **Ned Land** — *The Harpooner*
    *   Another prisoner, focused on escape, practical and suspicious of Nemo.

4.  **Conseil** — *The Loyal Servant*
    *   Aronnax's assistant, adaptable, provides calm analysis.

5.  **The Nautilus Crew** — *The Silent Sailors*
    *   Loyal to Nemo, speak an unknown language, mysterious past.

---

## Key Locations

1.  **The Nautilus:** Advanced submersible with viewing windows, luxurious quarters, scientific instruments, secrets.
2.  **The Sunken Atlantis:** Ancient ruins teeming with strange life.
3.  **The Underwater Forest:** Kelp forest ecosystem with alien beauty.
4.  **The South Pole Under Ice:** Journey beneath polar ice caps.
5.  **The Coral Cemetery:** A graveyard of ships Nemo has destroyed.

---

## Central Conflict

Freedom versus justice, revenge versus peace, progress versus isolation. Nemo represents justified rage against imperialism and oppression, but his vendetta makes him a danger. The party must decide: Is Nemo right to wage his underwater war? Should they help him, escape to warn his targets, or find a middle path?

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Submarine Survival**
*   Track air quality, power reserves, and hull integrity. Damage creates time-pressure for repairs.

### 2. **Underwater Expeditions**
*   Using diving suits with limited air. Pressure mechanics for deep dives.

### 3. **Marine Discoveries**
*   Award discovery points for documenting new species and locations. High discovery score provides better endings.

### 4. **Nemo's Favor**
*   Track relationship with Nemo. High favor grants privileges and reveals secrets. Low favor risks being expelled or imprisoned.

### 5. **The Nautilus's Capabilities**
*   Players can learn to operate submarine systems—weapons, navigation, scientific instruments.

### 6. **The Moral Ledger**
*   Choices about Nemo's revenge create branching endings.
