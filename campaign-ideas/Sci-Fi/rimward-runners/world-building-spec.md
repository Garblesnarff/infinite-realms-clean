# Rimward Runners - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Core Systems Authority
**Government**: Federal bureaucracy controlling inner planets
**Territory**: Earth-That-Was (mythical), dozens of core worlds
**Methods**: Laws, regulations, checkpoints, surveillance
**Military**: Alliance Cruisers, Federal Marshals, elite troops
**Ideology**: Order, civilization, progress (imposed from above)
**Corruption**: Deep and systemic, rights for wealthy only

### The Red Sun Consortium
**Type**: Corporate syndicate controlling outer territories
**Operations**: Terraforming, resource extraction, company towns
**Power**: Economic rather than military
**Methods**: Debt bondage, monopolies, private security
**Leaders**: Board of directors, profit-driven
**Research**: The Academy, behavioral modification programs

### The Outer Rim Alliance (ORA)
**Formation**: Loose confederation of outer planets
**Goal**: Independence from Core authority
**Status**: Lost the Unification War decades ago
**Legacy**: Brown coats, resistance fighters, bitterness
**Current**: Fragmented, some bandits, some legitimate governance
**Symbols**: Brown coat, independence rhetoric

### The Independents/"Browncoats"
**Origin**: Unification War veterans who fought for independence
**Current Status**: Scattered, some legitimate, some criminals
**Ideals**: Freedom, self-determination, frontier justice
**Reality**: Many became what they fought against (oppressors, warlords)
**Key Figures**: Captain Mal Cutter, other veterans
**Stigma**: Core sees them as terrorists, traitors

### Organized Crime Syndicates
**Major Players**: Niska's organization, various smuggler networks
**Operations**: Drugs, smuggling, protection, slavery
**Territory**: Operate in power vacuums on frontier
**Methods**: Brutality, loyalty through fear, honor codes (sometimes)
**Relationship to Crew**: Sometimes employers, sometimes enemies

## Location Details and Star Maps

### The Verse Layout
**The Core** (Central Systems)
- Ariel: Medical research, wealthy
- Sihnon: Cultural center, Companion training
- Londinium: Political capital
- Osiris: Alliance military headquarters
- High population, high technology, high control

**The Border Worlds** (Middle Region)
- Persephone: Half civilized, half frontier
- Santo: Manufacturing and shipping
- Greenleaf: Agriculture and terraforming
- Mixed governance, some Alliance, some independent
- Where most trade happens

**The Rim** (Outer Systems)
- Whitefall: Lawless moon, warlord territory
- Haven: Shepherd Book's eventual retirement
- Jiangyin: Isolated farming community
- Miranda: Dead world, terrible secret
- Low Alliance presence, frontier justice, desperate

### The Serenity's Edge
**Specifications**:
- Class: Firefly-class transport (mid-bulk)
- Crew: 9 maximum comfortable, cramped
- Cargo capacity: 400 tons
- Engine: Pulse drive with compression coil
- Age: 20+ years, constant repairs needed

**Deck Layout**:
1. **Bridge/Cockpit**: Pilot and co-pilot stations
2. **Upper Crew Deck**: Captain's quarters, infirmary, passenger dorms
3. **Main Deck**: Galley/dining, common area, crew quarters
4. **Cargo Bay**: Massive open space, smuggling compartments
5. **Engine Room**: Kaylee's domain, constantly maintained
6. **Shuttles**: Two external, Inara's is rented space

### Key Locations (Detailed)

**Persephone**
- Eavesdown Docks: Spaceport, cargo, traders, criminals
- Estates: Wealthy landowners, fancy balls
- Mudder Territory: Farmland, poor workers
- Population: 50 million (mixed class)

**Ariel** (Core Medical World)
- St. Lucy's Hospital: Advanced medical facility
- Extensive surveillance, high Alliance presence
- Clean, wealthy, oppressive through perfection
- Episode location: Simon's attempt to treat River

**Miranda**
- Official Story: Terraforming failure, abandoned
- Truth: Pax experiment, genocide, Reaver origin

- Discovery changes everything

**The Black** (Space Travel)
- No FTL, travel takes days/weeks between systems
- Smuggling routes avoid Alliance checkpoints
- Debris fields, derelicts, salvage opportunities
- Empty spaces between worlds, isolation

## Causality Chains and Historical Events

### The Terraforming Exodus
- Earth-That-Was became overpopulated/polluted
- Humanity left for new star system
- Dozens of planets and moons terraformed
- Process took generations, created distinct cultures

### The Unification War (10-15 years before campaign)
**Causes**: Core systems imposing authority on independent Rim
**Sides**: Alliance (Core) vs. Independents (Outer Rim)
**Major Battles**: Serenity Valley (decisive Alliance victory)
**Outcome**: Alliance won, Independents scattered
**Aftermath**: Resentment, veterans on both sides, unresolved tensions
**Legacy**: Brown coats, independence ideology, resistance cells

### The Pax Program (Secret History)
**Purpose**: Behavioral modification experiment
**Location**: Miranda, 30 million population
**Method**: Atmospheric dispersal of chemical agent
**Intended Result**: Pacify population, end aggression
**Actual Result**: 99.9% stopped moving, laid down and died
**Horrific Side Effect**: 0.1% became hyper-aggressive (Reavers)
**Cover-Up**: Terraforming failure story, planet quarantined
**Discovery**: Party finds evidence, must expose or suppress

### The Academy Program
**Purpose**: Creating psychic/enhanced operatives
**Methods**: Brain surgery, conditioning, experiments on children
**Success Rate**: Low, many dead or driven mad
**River Tam**: Most successful subject, brother rescued her
**Pursuit**: Red Sun Consortium wants her back or dead
**Secret**: River knows about Pax, buried in her mind

### The Companion Guild
**Status**: Registered, legal, respected profession
**Training**: Years of education, arts, psychology
**Guild Protection**: Independent organization, even Alliance respects
**Inara's Secret**: Why she left the core, personal reasons
**Role**: Provides social access, cover for crew's activities

## Additional Lore and World Details

### Technology Levels
**Ships**: Sub-light fusion drives, no FTL
**Communication**: Laser-based, limited by light speed
**Weapons**: Ballistic firearms, some energy weapons (rare/expensive)
**Medicine**: Advanced by our standards, common in Core, rare on Rim
**Computing**: Present but not AI level
**Gravity**: Artificial on ships through rotation or tech

### Language and Culture
**Chinese-English Hybrid**: Most common language blends both
**Cursing**: Chinese swear words bypass censorship
**Dialects**: Vary by region and class
**Cultural Mix**: Western frontier meets Eastern philosophy
**Religion**: Diverse, respected (Shepherd Book), sometimes extremist

### Economic Realities
**Currency**: Platinum (physical currency still exists)
**Barter**: Common on frontier
**Debt**: Controls many lives, especially company towns
**Smuggling**: Profitable but dangerous
**Jobs**: Cargo runs (legal), smuggling (illegal), passenger transport, salvage
**Profit Margins**: Thin, always one catastrophe from broke

### Social Hierarchies
**Core Elite**: Wealthy, educated, political power
**Core Citizens**: Middle class, comfortable, conformist
**Rim Settlers**: Independent, poor, resourceful
**Criminals**: Operate in gray areas, necessary evil
**Companions**: Respected regardless of location
**Former Independents**: Stigmatized, struggle to fit in

### The Reaver Threat
**Official Story**: Humans driven mad by edge of space
**Truth**: Pax experiment victims
**Behavior**: Rape, murder, cannibalize, mutilate
**Ships**: Captured vessels, disturbing modifications
**Territory**: Outer Rim, avoided by all
**Horror**: Not monsters, but humans broken beyond recognition
**Myth vs. Reality**: Stories exaggerated, but core truth is worse

### Miranda and the Broadcast
**Discovery**: Party finds Miranda, learns truth
**Evidence**: Video logs from dying scientist
**Decision**: Broadcast widely or use as leverage
**Consequences**: Public revelation destabilizes Consortium
**Aftermath**: Political upheaval, party become heroes/targets
**Long-term**: Begins process of reform or cover-up doubles down

### Ship Life
**Routine**: Jump between systems, cargo runs, repairs
**Meals**: Social bonding time, family dinners
**Quarters**: Personal space is precious
**Repairs**: Constant, Kaylee works miracles
**Fuel**: Expensive, limits operational range
**Supplies**: Rationing common when funds low

### Legal and Illegal
**Legal Work**: Registered cargo, passenger transport, salvage (licensed)
**Gray Area**: Unregistered cargo, avoiding taxes, unlicensed salvage
**Illegal**: Smuggling contraband, harboring fugitives, theft
**Enforcement**: Alliance patrols, Federal Marshals, local law
**Bribery**: Common, expected, part of doing business

### Cultural Touchstones
**Brown Coat Culture**: Independence, freedom, resistance to authority
**Companion Respect**: Even outlaws treat them professionally
**Frontier Justice**: Quick, harsh, sometimes necessary
**Crew as Family**: Chosen family stronger than blood
**Keep Flying**: Core philosophy, persistence despite everything
