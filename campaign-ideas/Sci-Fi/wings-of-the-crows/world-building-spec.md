**Wings of the Crows**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The party joins the fallen Karasuno Guild to revive its Skyblade team—a magical aerial sport where teams launch enchanted spheres over mystical nets. Once legendary as champions, Karasuno is now dismissed as washed-up has-beens. Through training, innovative tactics, and perfect synchronization, they climb from practice matches to the National Championships, proving that grounded crows can learn to fly again.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the origins of Skyblade as an ancient magical sport created to promote teamwork and flight magic mastery.
    *   Write the history of Karasuno Guild's golden age, when they were known as the "Sky Guardians" and won consecutive championships.
    *   Describe the legendary "Little Giant"—the short spiker who proved height didn't determine greatness.
    *   Explain Karasuno's fall from grace after their ace and setter graduated, leading to years without nationals qualification.
    *   Detail the tournament structure: local practice matches, interguild tournaments, regional championships, and nationals.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **Karasuno Guild** (Major): The party's home, fallen from glory but rebuilding with innovative tactics and team spirit.
    *   **Shiratorizawa Academy** (Major): Elite guild relying on overwhelming individual talent and strict hierarchical training.
    *   **Aoba Johsai Guild** (Major): Strategic powerhouse led by analytical genius, focused on perfect game-planning.
    *   **Nekoma Guild** (Minor): Traditional defensive specialists known as "Cats" with strategic setters.
    *   **Date Tech** (Minor): "Iron Wall" guild specializing in blocking and defensive formations.
    *   **Inarizaki** (Minor): Aggressive offensive guild with twin-spiker synchronized attacks.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Coach Ukai (mentor), Tōru Oikawa (rival genius setter), Wakatoshi Ushijima (overwhelming ace), Kenma Kozume (strategic mastermind).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Guild Captain" (leadership roles), "Ace Spiker" (primary attackers), "Defensive Specialist" (liberos), "Rookie Prodigy" (talented newcomers), "Veteran Coach" (experienced mentors).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Karasuno Guild Hall:** Aging facility with main gymnasium, practice courts, strategy room with opponent analysis boards, dormitories, outdoor training area.
*   **Metropolitan Gymnasium:** Regional tournament venue with multiple regulation courts, spectator seating, warm-up areas, vendor stalls.
*   **National Arena:** Capital's massive stadium with central championship court, surrounding practice courts, broadcast towers, player facilities, VIP sections.
*   **Training Camp Mountain:** Remote facility in mountains where guilds gather for intensive joint training, outdoor courts, shared dormitories, natural obstacles for conditioning.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Party wins against Aoba Johsai → **THEN:** Oikawa becomes obsessed with defeating them, studies their every move, becomes recurring rival.
*   **IF:** Ace spiker overcomes fear of failure → **THEN:** Unlocks advanced quick attack variations, inspires teammates to take risks.
*   **IF:** Party defeats Shiratorizawa → **THEN:** Qualifies for nationals, gains national recognition, attracts scouts and sponsors.
*   **IF:** Perfect synchronization achieved → **THEN:** Unlocks "Minus Tempo" attacks that strike before opponents can react.
*   **IF:** Party loses crucial match → **THEN:** Training arc intensifies, must learn from failure, develops new strategies to counter specific weaknesses.
*   **IF:** Karasuno reaches nationals finals → **THEN:** Guild reputation fully restored, recruitment increases, former glory acknowledged.
