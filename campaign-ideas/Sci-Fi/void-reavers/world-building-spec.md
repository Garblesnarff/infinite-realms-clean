# Void Reavers - World-Building Specification

## The Galaxy and Its Powers

### The Nova Corps (Space Police)
- Peacekeeping force, Xandar-based
- Thousands of officers across systems
- Starblasters: Individual fighters
- Nova Force: Energy source granting powers
- Richard Rider: Corps member (later)
- Defeated repeatedly but rebuilds

### Xandar (Capital World)
- Advanced peaceful civilization
- Kree wars nearly destroyed them
- Rebuilt, thriving metropolis
- Nova Corps headquarters
- Billions of civilians
- Target of Ronan's genocide

### The Kree Empire
- Militaristic, blue-skinned (some pink)
- Supreme Intelligence: AI leader
- Accuser Corps: Judge-warriors (Ronan)
- Ancient war with Xandarians
- Created Inhumans on Earth (experiments)
- Fanatical elements despite peace treaty

### The Ravagers
- Space pirate clans
- Honor code among thieves
- Yondu's clan: Centaurian whistler
- Multiple factions compete
- Funeral tradition: Ravager arrow salute
- "We steal from everybody"

### The Sovereign
- Gold-skinned supremacists
- Genetically perfect (believe themselves)
- Birth pods, no natural reproduction
- Remote-control drone fleets
- Easily offended, pursue vendettas
- Adam Warlock creation (future threat)

### The Collector (Taneleer Tivan)
- Elder of Universe (ancient being)
- Obsessive collector of specimens
- Knowhere resident
- Servant (enslaved): Carina
- Power Stone destroys part of collection
- Connected to other Elders (Grandmaster)

## Cosmic Artifacts and Powers

### Infinity Stones (Power & Beyond)
**Power Stone** (purple):
- Destroy anything touched
- Impossible for mortals to wield
- Shared burden allows temporary use
- Originally in Orb on Morag

**Other Stones** (for expansion):
- Space (blue): Tesseract
- Mind (yellow): Loki's scepter
- Reality (red): Aether
- Time (green): Eye of Agamotto
- Soul (orange): Vormir sacrifice

### Celestials (Ancient Gods)
- Created universe, conducted experiments
- Knowhere: Severed head of dead Celestial
- Ego: Living planet, Celestial aberration
- Immense power, created Infinity Stones
- Mostly gone or hidden

## Key Locations in Detail

### Knowhere
- Mining colony in dead Celestial's skull
- Lawless port
- The Collector's museum
- Bars, gambling, crime
- Boot's bar: Social center
- Bone and brain matter mined

### Morag (Abandoned World)
- Once inhabited, now ocean-drowned ruins
- Power Stone hidden in temple
- Periodic tidal retreat exposes land
- Orloni: Small predators
- Multiple factions seeking Orb

### The Kyln (Prison)
- Brutal incarceration
- Gang violence common
- Corrupt guards
- Where party first meets
- Breakout establishes team dynamics

### Contraxia (Pleasure Planet)
- Ice world with warm establishments
- Brothel planet
- Ravager recreation
- Neon-lit casinos and clubs
- Robot prostitutes, dancers

### Ego's Planet
- Living Celestial world
- Paradise surface (facade)
- Brain/core at center
- Seed extensions to thousands of worlds
- Beautiful but sinister
- Dies when Ego dies

### Vormir (Soul Stone Location)
- Desolate, mountainous
- Red Skull: Guardian (cursed)
- Soul Stone requires sacrifice
- "Soul for soul"
- Tragic location

## The Guardians' Backgrounds

### Peter Quill "Star-Lord"
- Abducted from Earth (1988) age 8
- Mother died of cancer (Ego's fault)
- Raised by Ravagers (Yondu)
- Awesome Mix Tape: Last gift from mother
- Half-Celestial (Ego's son)
- Only one who could touch Celestial power

### Gamora
- Last of Zen-Whoberis
- Adopted/tortured by Thanos
- Trained as assassin
- Seeking redemption
- Knows soul stone location (reluctantly)
- Thanos's "favorite daughter" (abusive love)

### Drax
- Family killed by Ronan (Thanos ordered)
- Revenge-driven warrior
- Literal-minded (comedy and tragedy)
- Eventually finds family in Guardians
- Tattoos mark his pain

### Rocket
- Genetically modified raccoon
- Experiment subject 89P13
- Halfworld creation
- Anger from body dysmorphia
- Genius weapons and tactics
- Defensive mechanism: Push away

### Groot
- Flora Colossus from Planet X
- Only says "I am Groot"
- Meaning changes by inflection
- Regenerates from sprouts
- Sacrifices for team
- Baby Groot: Rebuilt, same soul?

### Mantis
- Empathic abilities
- Raised by Ego as servant/sleep aid
- Innocent, sheltered
- Joins team, finds freedom
- Antennas enhance empathy
- Brutally honest (no social training)

### Nebula
- Gamora's "sister" (both adopted by Thanos)
- Lost every fight to Gamora
- Thanos replaced body parts as punishment
- Bitter, cybernetic
- Seeks to kill Thanos
- Eventual redemption and sisterhood

## The Infinity Saga Context

### Thanos (Background Threat)
- Mad Titan seeking balance
- Wants Infinity Stones
- "Universal mercy" through half-killing
- Ronan serves him (until rebellion)
- Gamora and Nebula: Adopted daughters
- Future campaigns: Direct confrontation

### Avengers Connection
- Guardians exist alongside Earth heroes
- Separate spheres initially
- Convergence in Infinity War
- Thor connection (later)
- Time Stone on Earth (Dr. Strange)

## Technology and Species

### Xandarian Tech
- Nova Force: Helmet-channeled energy
- Advanced healing, reconstruction
- Starblasters: Atmospheric fighters
- Planetary defense grid

### Kree Technology
- Dark Aster: Massive warship
- Universal Weapon: Ronan's hammer
- Exo-suits, enhanced armor
- Genetic experimentation legacy

### Ravager Tech
- M-ships: Various classes
- Yondu's arrow: Yaka-controlled weapon
- Whistling control (rare Centaurian)
- Scavenged, mismatched equipment

### Milano's Revenge/Benatar
- Customized M-ship
- Quad laser cannons
- Aero-rigs (spacesuits)
- Tape deck (essential)
- Upgrades throughout campaign

## Culture and Customs

### Ravager Code
- Don't steal from each other
- No trafficking children
- Honor funeral traditions
- Flame arrows for respected fallen
- Exile for code-breaking

### Xandarian Society
- Peaceful, democratic
- Value life and culture
- Art, music, civilization
- Military only for defense
- Integrate refugees

### Kree Military Culture
- Strength and purity obsession
- Accusers: Elite enforcers
- Fanatical elements
- Honor through conquest
- Supreme Intelligence worship

## The Family Theme

### Found Family
- None have birth families (lost or abandoned)
- Guardians become family
- Yondu as father figure
- "He may have been your father, but he wasn't your daddy"
- Chosen family stronger than blood

### Parent-Child Dynamics
- Ego vs. Yondu (biological vs. real father)
- Thanos and daughters (abusive parent)
- Rocket and Groot (protective friendship)
- Peter and Yondu (complex love)

### Volume 2: Family Issues

- Sisterhood (Gamora and Nebula)
- Belonging and acceptance
- Death and resurrection (Yondu, Groot)
