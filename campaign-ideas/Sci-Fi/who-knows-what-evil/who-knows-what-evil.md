# Who Knows What Evil

*An Urban Vigilante / Pulp Mystery campaign — Medium Campaign (12–15 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Who Knows What Evil
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Urban Vigilante / Pulp Mystery / Dual Identity
**Estimated Length:** Medium Campaign — 12–15 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **10–12**
**Tone (keywords):** noir mystery, dual identities, psychological warfare, clouding men's minds, "the weed of crime bears bitter fruit," urban shadows, maniacal laughter
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
By day, the party are respected members of society—wealthy dilettantes, radio personalities, or consultants to law enforcement. By night, they don the guise of The Shadow and their agents, striking fear into criminal hearts with eerie laughter and the power to cloud men's minds. They battle crime lords, corrupt officials, and occult threats in a city where the line between justice and vengeance grows thin. Who knows what evil lurks in the hearts of men? The Shadow knows.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Dual Identity Management:** Maintaining separate personas, avoiding exposure.
*   **Investigation:** Solving crimes through detective work, interrogation, and deduction.
*   **Psychological Combat:** Using fear, intimidation, and mind-affecting abilities.
*   **Moral Ambiguity:** The Shadow operates outside the law—how far is too far?

---

## Story Arc (Medium Campaign — 12–15 sessions)

*   **Act I — The Voodoo Master (Sessions 1–3):** Party establishes their dual identities. First major case involves a crime lord using occult rituals to control victims. Introduction to The Shadow's methods.
*   **Act II — The League of Crime (Sessions 4–8):** Five crime families unite under mysterious leadership. Party must infiltrate, turn criminals against each other, and uncover the mastermind.
*   **Act III — The Blackmail Conspiracy (Sessions 9–12):** Someone has dirt on the city's elite, including party members' secret identities. Race to stop release while maintaining cover. Corrupt police commissioner revealed.
*   **Act IV — The Red Menace (Sessions 13–15):** International spy ring threatens the city with stolen weapon technology. Final confrontation where party must choose between maintaining cover and saving thousands.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Lamont Cranston** — *The Shadow (or party member)*
    *   Wealthy playboy by day, dark avenger by night. Learned mind-clouding techniques in the Orient. Voice of justice with maniacal laugh.

2.  **Margo Lane** — *The Agent*
    *   Society lady, secretly The Shadow's most trusted operative. Sharp mind, fearless heart, romantic interest.

3.  **Commissioner Weston** — *The Honest Cop*
    *   By-the-book police commissioner. Unknowingly aided by The Shadow. Represents lawful authority.

4.  **Shiwan Khan** — *The Mastermind*
    *   Descended from Genghis Khan, possesses psychic powers equal to The Shadow. Ultimate nemesis. Plans to conquer through crime and chaos.

5.  **Clarice** — *The Informant*
    *   Taxi driver who overhears everything. One of Shadow's street-level agents. Comic relief with heart of gold.

6.  **The Voodoo Master** — *Occult Crime Lord*
    *   Uses genuine dark magic to control victims. First major antagonist. Shows supernatural exists in this world.

7.  **Dr. Roderick Tam** — *The Mad Scientist*
    *   Brilliant inventor whose creations are used by criminals. Morally gray—can be ally or threat depending on party actions.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Sanctum:** The Shadow's hidden base. Dark, filled with files on criminals, communications equipment.
2.  **The City:** 1930s metropolis—art deco skyscrapers, smoky nightclubs, dangerous waterfront, corrupt city hall.
3.  **Cranston Estate:** Opulent mansion where wealthy playboy identity is maintained.
4.  **The Blue Jade:** Nightclub secretly owned by The Shadow. Information hub.
5.  **Chinatown:** Mysterious district where The Shadow learned his arts. Allies and enemies alike.
6.  **The Docks:** Smuggling operations, warehouse fights, noir atmosphere.

---

## Central Conflict

Justice versus law. The Shadow operates outside legal channels because the system is too corrupt or slow to stop true evil. The party must wrestle with how much power one should wield over others' minds and lives. Is controlling someone's perception different from killing them? Can fear be righteous? The city needs heroes, but are vigilantes heroes or just criminals with better PR?

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Cloud Men's Minds**
*   Illusion magic allowing party to become invisible to others, cast shadows without light source, or appear as different people. Requires concentration and can be resisted by strong will.

### 2. **The Shadow's Laugh**
*   Signature maniacal laughter. Intimidation check that can cause fear, confusion, or panic in criminals.

### 3. **Dual Identity**
*   Track both public and secret personas. Different skill sets, different reputations. Exposure has severe consequences.

### 4. **Agent Network**
*   The Shadow maintains network of informants throughout city. Players can call upon cab drivers, doormen, bartenders for information.

### 5. **Psychological Warfare**
*   Use Investigation and Insight to find criminals' fears and weaknesses. Exploit them for advantage in combat and interrogation.

### 6. **Radio Drama Moments**
*   Key scenes should feel like radio plays—dialogue-heavy, atmospheric descriptions, dramatic reveals.

### 7. **The Shadow Knows**
*   Once per session, party can declare "The Shadow knows" and gain perfect insight into NPC's immediate intentions or recent actions.

## Key Magic Items / Artifacts

*   **The Fire Opal Ring:** Girasol ring that glows when danger is near. The Shadow's signature.
*   **The Cloak of Darkness:** Enhances mind-clouding abilities, flows dramatically.
*   **Twin .45 Automatics:** The Shadow's signature weapons, never miss their mark.
*   **The Sanctum's Files:** Dossiers on every criminal in the city—information is power.
*   **Mystic Talismans:** Artifacts from the Orient granting psychic abilities.

## Tone and Atmosphere

Dark and atmospheric pulp noir. Heavy shadows, dramatic lighting, moral complexity. The Shadow is both terrifying and heroic—criminals fear him, innocents are saved but never see their savior. Period-appropriate 1930s aesthetic. Supernatural elements exist but are rare and mysterious. Psychological horror elements—what's more terrifying than your own mind betraying you? Radio drama pacing—lots of narration and internal monologue. The laugh echoes through alleys. Justice is served, but methods are questionable.

---

## Why This Campaign Is Memorable

*   **Dual Identity Management:** Living two completely different lives with secrets to protect.
*   **Psychological Powers:** Mind games instead of just combat.
*   **Noir Atmosphere:** Dark cities, darker secrets, moral ambiguity.
*   **Iconic Moments:** The laugh, the mind-clouding reveal, criminals going mad with fear.
*   **Question of Justice:** How far should heroes go to stop evil?

## Adventure Hooks

*   A string of impossible burglaries—victims claim invisible thief.
*   Wealthy philanthropist (party member) asked to investigate charity embezzlement.
*   The Shadow's network goes silent in certain district—investigation reveals why.
*   Someone claiming to be The Shadow commits murders—must clear name.
*   Old enemy from the Orient arrives seeking revenge.

---

## Ending Options

**Epic Victory:** Crime syndicates dismantled, corrupt officials exposed. The Shadow's identity remains secret. City is safer, though never truly clean. The laughter still echoes.

**Pyrrhic Victory:** Identity exposed but enemy defeated. Must fake death and assume new identity. City saved but personal cost is extreme.

**Bittersweet:** One identity must be sacrificed to save the other. Either give up being The Shadow or give up civilian life completely. Justice served, but wholeness lost.
