
# Shadows of Inaba

- **Campaign Type / Genre**: Modern Mystery / Psychological Horror / Social Links
- **Estimated Length**: 20-25 sessions
- **Player Level Range**: 1-12
- **Tone**: Mystery, Psychological, Hopeful, Slice-of-Life meets Horror

### Core Premise
The players are students and young adults in the small rural town of Inaba when a series of bizarre murders begins. Victims appear on foggy nights hanging from television antennas and telephone poles. The players discover they can enter a parallel dimension called the Midnight Realm through television screens, where people's repressed selves manifest as monsters called Shadows. They must balance their everyday lives - attending school, working jobs, building relationships - with diving into the Midnight Realm to rescue victims before they're killed by their own shadows. The murders are connected to a mysterious entity granting people access to the Midnight Realm, and the players must uncover the killer's identity before the next foggy night.

### Story Arc

**Act 1: The Murders Begin (Sessions 1-8)**
- The players arrive in Inaba just as the first body is discovered. Shortly after, a second victim appears - a student from their school. When touching a television screen during the "Midnight Hour," the players discover they can enter the Midnight Realm. Inside, they find the kidnapped student and must save them from their Shadow. They form the Investigation Team, dedicated to solving the murders and saving future victims. They learn the pattern: someone is thrown into the Midnight Realm, and if they aren't rescued before the next foggy night, they die.

**Act 2: Social Links and Shadows (Sessions 9-18)**
- The players juggle their investigation with daily life. During the day, they attend school, work part-time jobs, join clubs, and build Social Links - meaningful relationships that strengthen their powers. At night or on weekends, they delve into the Midnight Realm to save kidnapped victims. Each rescue involves confronting that victim with their Shadow - the repressed parts of themselves they can't accept. If the victim rejects their Shadow, it goes berserk. If they accept it, the Shadow becomes a Persona - a manifestation of their inner strength. The players gather clues about the killer's identity while dealing with their own personal struggles and Shadow confrontations.

**Act 3: The Truth (Sessions 19-25)**
- The players identify the killer and confront them in the Midnight Realm. However, they discover the killer was manipulated by a greater entity - an ancient god of deception who feeds on humanity's desire to remain ignorant. The final battle occurs in multiple stages: defeating the killer's twisted Persona, confronting the god in the Midnight Realm, and finally choosing whether to accept a comfortable lie or fight for the truth. The strength of the players' Social Links determines their power in the final battle. Victory saves Inaba and seals the god, but saying goodbye to the bonds they've formed proves bittersweet.

### Session Breakdown

- **Session 1: Welcome to Inaba**: Players arrive in the small town and meet locals. First murder is discovered - a woman hanging from a telephone pole. Rumor spreads about the Midnight Channel.
- **Session 2: The Midnight Hour**: Players discover the Midnight Channel phenomenon and learn they can enter TVs. A student from their school is kidnapped.
- **Session 3-4: First Rescue**: The team enters the Midnight Realm, a twisted castle representing the victim's repressed desire for attention. They confront the victim's Shadow and save them.
- **Session 5-6: Investigation Begins**: Police are baffled. The team realizes a pattern - kidnappings occur after appearances on the Midnight Channel. They must identify victims before it's too late.
- **Session 7-8: Social Links**: Focus on daily life. Players build relationships with classmates, join clubs, work jobs. Each Social Link provides character development and mechanical benefits.
- **Session 9-11: Second Murder**: Another victim, another Midnight Realm - this time a bathhouse representing gender identity struggles. More clues about the kidnapper emerge.
- **Session 12-14: Building Bonds**: Players strengthen Social Links. A teammate faces their own Shadow, requiring party support. Investigation continues during school hours.
- **Session 15-17: The Stakes Rise**: Multiple kidnappings occur. The killer is escalating. Players must manage time carefully - who to save first, which leads to follow, which relationships to prioritize.
- **Session 18: The Suspect**: Evidence points to a local figure. But is it too convenient? Players must decide whether to trust their instincts or the evidence.
- **Session 19-20: The True Killer**: Players confront the actual culprit in the Midnight Realm. Battle through their twisted Persona manifestations.
- **Session 21-22: The God of Deception**: The killer reveals they were granted power by an ancient entity. Players must enter the deepest part of the Midnight Realm.
- **Session 23-24: The Final Battle**: Confronting the god requires all Social Links. Players face their own Shadows one last time, achieving true Persona evolution. Multi-stage boss battle.
- **Session 25: Farewell**: With the case solved and the god sealed, players face leaving Inaba and the friends they've made. Final Social Link scenes and epilogue.

### Major NPCs

- **Dojima Ryotaro**: Your uncle and guardian, the detective investigating the murders. **Role:** Mentor / Father Figure / Social Link. **Personality:** Gruff but caring, dedicated to his work, struggles as a single father. **Flaw/Secret:** His obsession with work damaged his relationship with his daughter.
- **Nanako Dojima**: Your young cousin, around 7 years old. **Role:** Innocent / Victim / Social Link. **Personality:** Sweet, lonely, mature for her age. **Flaw/Secret:** Desperately wants her father to spend time with her; may become a kidnapping victim.
- **Teddie**: A strange bear mascot-like being native to the Midnight Realm. **Role:** Guide / Comic Relief / Party Member. **Personality:** Cheerful, pun-loving, desperate to understand humanity and find his purpose. **Flaw/Secret:** Is actually a Shadow who gained self-awareness; fears learning his true nature.
- **Yukiko Amagi**: Daughter of the famous Amagi Inn family. **Role:** Party Member / Social Link. **Personality:** Graceful, traditional, hiding frustration with expectations. **Flaw/Secret:** Her Shadow represents her desire to escape Inaba and the inn she's expected to inherit, revealing she feels caged.
- **Kanji Tatsumi**: A tough delinquent with a secret sensitive side. **Role:** Party Member / Social Link. **Personality:** Aggressive exterior, artistic soul, struggles with masculinity. **Flaw/Secret:** His Shadow represents his confusion about gender identity and sexuality, manifesting in a bathhouse.
- **Naoto Shirogane**: A famous teenage detective investigating the case. **Role:** Party Member / Social Link / Red Herring. **Personality:** Logical, mature, presents as male. **Flaw/Secret:** Her Shadow represents her struggle with being taken seriously as a young woman in a male-dominated profession.
- **The Killer (Mitsuo/Adachi archetype)**: A seemingly ordinary person revealed as the kidnapper. **Role:** Primary Antagonist. **Personality:** Resentful of society, craves attention or control. **Flaw/Secret:** Was granted Midnight Realm access by the god; is merely a pawn.
- **Izanami (The Goddess of Deception)**: Ancient deity who created the Midnight Realm. **Role:** True Antagonist. **Personality:** Calm, philosophical, believes humanity wants comfortable lies over painful truth. **Flaw/Secret:** Granted power to select humans to test whether humanity desires truth or deception.
- **Igor**: A mysterious elderly man with a long nose who exists in the Velvet Room between reality and the Midnight Realm. **Role:** Mentor / Persona Fusion Master. **Personality:** Cryptic, knowing, speaks in riddles about destiny and bonds. **Flaw/Secret:** Serves as a guide for those with the "Wild Card" - the ability to wield multiple Personas.

### Key Locations

- **Inaba Shopping District**: The small-town main street with various shops. Social Link opportunities abound.
- **Yasogami High School**: Where players attend classes and build relationships with fellow students.
- **The Velvet Room**: A limousine existing between dream and reality where Igor helps players fuse Personas.
- **Dojima Residence**: The player's home base, where they live with Uncle Dojima and Nanako.
- **The Midnight Realm**: A parallel dimension accessed through TVs, where locations are warped reflections of repressed desires.
- **Castle of Vanity**: First dungeon, representing the kidnapped student's desire for attention and specialness.
- **Steamy Bathhouse**: Kanji's dungeon, representing confusion about masculinity and sexuality.
- **Secret Laboratory**: Naoto's dungeon, representing desire to be taken seriously and gender struggles.
- **Heaven**: Nanako's dungeon (if she's kidnapped), representing her innocent desire for family.
- **Magatsu Inaba**: The killer's twisted version of the real town within the Midnight Realm.
- **Yomotsu Hirasaka**: The deepest realm where Izanami dwells, a land of the dead from Japanese mythology.

### Central Conflict
The players must solve a serial murder case while maintaining their everyday lives and relationships. The conflict is external (finding the killer, rescuing victims) and internal (confronting their own Shadows, building authentic connections). The central theme explores the tension between comfortable lies and painful truths - both in solving the mystery and in accepting one's true self. Each kidnapping victim must face their Shadow (their repressed self), and by extension, the players must face their own.

### Unique Mechanics

- **Social Link System**: Track relationships with NPCs on a 1-10 scale. Players can spend time with NPCs during free time, making choices that increase their Social Link level. Higher levels grant mechanical benefits: bonus damage when using Personas of that Social Link's arcana, special abilities, and story revelations. Maxing certain Social Links unlocks powerful Persona fusion options. Encourage players to maintain a relationship journal.
- **Calendar and Time Management**: Each session represents multiple days. Players have limited time slots (morning, afternoon, evening) to choose activities: investigate leads, enter the Midnight Realm, study, work a job, hang out with friends. Weather forecasts are crucial - foggy days mean deadlines for rescues. Players must balance combat progression with social development.
- **Persona System**: Each player has a primary Persona representing their true self, but can acquire additional Personas by defeating Shadows or through fusion. The Velvet Room allows combining Personas to create stronger ones, inheriting abilities. Personas are powered by Social Links - using a Persona of the same arcana as a maxed Social Link deals extra damage.
- **Shadow Confrontations**: When rescuing a victim, that victim must face their Shadow - a boss battle where the Shadow verbalizes all the victim's repressed thoughts and desires. If the party defeats the Shadow before the victim accepts it, they fail the rescue. Players must use skill checks and roleplay to convince victims to accept their Shadow, transforming it into a Persona and saving their life.
- **Investigation Phases**: Between dungeon delves, players gather clues through dialogue, skill checks, and investigation. Red herrings abound. Players must connect evidence on an "investigation board" to identify the next victim or narrow down suspects. The DM tracks a hidden "truth meter" - if players identify the real killer early enough, they can prevent later kidnappings.
- **Weather and Deadlines**: Track weather forecasts. Victims appear on the Midnight Channel during rainy nights. The next extended fog period (usually 5-7 in-game days later) is when they die. Players must complete that person's Midnight Realm dungeon before the fog arrives. Multiple victims can be kidnapped simultaneously in later sessions, forcing hard choices.
- **All-Out Attacks**: When all enemies are knocked down, players can perform an All-Out Attack - a cinematic finishing move where everyone attacks simultaneously. Describe this dramatically with player input on how their character contributes to the stylish combination.
