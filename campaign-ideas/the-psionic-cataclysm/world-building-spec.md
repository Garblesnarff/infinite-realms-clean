**The Psionic Cataclysm**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** In Neo-Kingsport, the party—members of a biker gang—discovers their friend Ryder is developing psychic powers like those that destroyed the old city thirty years ago. When government agents kidnap Ryder, the party must navigate gang warfare and government conspiracies to rescue them. Deep beneath the city, Akuma—a godlike psychic—is awakening, and only by mastering their own powers can the party prevent apocalypse.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the original catastrophe when Akuma destroyed the old city thirty years ago with psychic power.
    *   Describe Neo-Kingsport's rebuilding and the secret government program capturing psychics.
    *   Explain the nature of psychic power—tied to emotion, amplified by rage and fear, capable of warping reality.
    *   Detail the biker gang culture in Neo-Kingsport.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Crimson Phantoms (Party's Gang)** — Rebellious biker gang, family-like bonds, emerging psychic powers.
    *   **The Government Psychic Program** — Secret military project weaponizing psychics, led by Colonel Yamada.
    *   **The Resistance** — Underground movement protecting psychics from government capture.
    *   **Akuma** — The transcendent psychic entity, neither good nor evil, offering evolution or destruction.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Ryder, Kei, Colonel Yamada, Akuma.
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** Gang members, government soldiers, psychic children, resistance fighters.

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Neo-Kingsport:** Cyberpunk megacity with neon lights, hoverbike highways, gang territories.
*   **The Facility:** Underground government complex for psychic experiments.
*   **The Old City Ruins:** Destroyed city beneath Neo-Kingsport where Akuma sleeps.
*   **The Psychic Realm:** Surreal dimension where thought becomes reality.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Triggers:** Psychic power use causes corruption; Akuma's awakening warps reality; violence vs. transcendence determines ending.
