# Empire of Endless Courses - World-Building Specification

## The Guild of Golden Plates

### Structure and Power
- Governing body for all fine dining establishments
- Three-tier rating system: Half-Plate, One-Plate, Two-Plate, Three-Plate
- Council of Twelve (all Three-Plate holders) makes all decisions
- Controls licensing, can shut down any restaurant
- Most powerful economic force in world

### Corruption
- Ratings can be bought by wealthy empires
- Inspectors can be bribed or threatened
- True talent often overlooked for political connections
- Reform movement growing among younger inspectors
- Party's merit-based rise threatens system

### Inspection Criteria
- Food quality and consistency
- Service excellence
- Ambiance and presentation
- Innovation while respecting technique
- Business practices and ethics
- Cultural impact

## The Restaurant Emperors

### Margaux DuBois Empire
- "La Couronne d'Or" - Crown jewel flagship
- 47 locations across 12 kingdoms
- French haute cuisine specialty
- Known for ruthless business practices
- Staff treated as replaceable

### Antonio Ragusa Empire
- "Casa Ragusa" - Family style Italian
- 31 locations, passed through three generations
- Emphasis on tradition and family
- Respects competition more than Margaux
- Possible ally against greater threats

### Empress Zhao Ming Empire
- "Dragon Phoenix" - Eastern fusion
- 28 locations in exotic markets
- Mystery surrounding success (possible magic use?)
- Plays long game, patient strategist
- Unpredictable ally or enemy

### The Underdog Alliance
- Party's restaurant empire
- Starts with 1, grows to 20+
- Known for innovation and quality over politics
- Staff treated as family
- Becomes legendary

## Restaurant Types and Specializations

### Fine Dining (Three-Plate Territory)
- Small seating, high prices
- Molecular gastronomy, artistic presentation
- Celebrity chef culture
- Requires massive investment

### Casual Upscale (One to Two-Plate)
- Larger capacity, accessible prices
- High quality without pretension
- Party's sweet spot
- Best profit margins if managed well

### Street Food (Usually unrated)
- Saffiya's domain
- Highest margins but lowest prestige
- Guild looks down on this
- Reform might include these

### Specialty Concepts
- Seafood houses
- Steakhouses
- Ethnic cuisine
- Dessert-only establishments
- Breakfast specialists

## Business Mechanics Deep Dive

### Financial Management
- Startup costs: 10,000 gold per location
- Monthly expenses: Rent, ingredients, staff, maintenance
- Revenue: Covers multiplied by average check
- Profit margins: 15-25% if managed well
- Expansion costs exponentially more

### Staff Hierarchy
- Head Chef (most important, hardest to replace)
- Sous Chefs (run day-to-day)
- Line Cooks (quality depends on their skill)
- Servers (interface with customers)
- Manager (operations, party can fill or delegate)
- Dishwashers (often overlooked, crucial)

### Supply Chain Strategy
- Exclusive supplier deals provide advantage
- Bulk purchasing reduces costs
- Local sourcing builds community support
- Rare ingredients enable premium pricing
- Disruption is primary sabotage vector

### Menu Engineering
- Signature dishes drive reputation
- Daily specials keep things fresh
- Pricing psychology matters
- Seasonal menus reduce costs
- Too many options reduces quality

## Corporate Warfare Tactics

### Sabotage
- Contaminating ingredients
- Bribing staff to fail
- Fake bad reviews
- Health inspector harassment
- Fire and "accidents"

### Espionage
- Stealing recipes
- Poaching staff
- Learning financial details
- Discovering expansion plans
- Counter-intelligence necessary

### Hostile Takeovers
- Buying out investors
- Leveraging debt
- Forcing partnership
- Legal warfare
- Must protect ownership structure

### Market Manipulation
- Undercutting prices temporarily
- Monopolizing supplier
- Opening directly next door
- Controlling Guild inspectors
- Creating artificial scarcity

## Regional Expansion Challenges

### Coastal City (Seafood Focus)
- Fresh ingredients daily
- Different customer expectations
- Fishing rights and supplier politics
- Pirate problems affecting supply
- Success here opens maritime markets

### Mountain Kingdom (Hearty Food)
- Different palate preferences
- Limited supply routes
- Seasonal extreme weather
- Mining community culture
- Defensive location against rivals

### Desert Sultanate (Spice Trade Hub)
- Exotic ingredients available
- Different dining customs
- Intense heat affects operations
- Cultural sensitivity required
- Gateway to eastern markets

### Elven Forest (Vegetarian Fine Dining)
- No meat dishes allowed
- 500-year-old competition
- Nature magic in cooking
- Earning respect here is prestigious
- Hardest market to crack

## The Corruption Conspiracy

### How Deep It Goes
- Guild Council all bought positions
- Inspectors extorted for favorable ratings
- Real talent suppressed systematically
- Money laundering through restaurant fronts
- Criminal elements control some Empires

### Evidence Party Finds
- Financial records show payments
- Whistleblower inspectors
- Pattern of suspicious ratings
- Helena's investigation reveals truth
- Must decide: Use system or reform it

### Reform Movement
- Young inspectors want merit-based ratings
- Independent critics publishing truth
- Customer advocacy groups
- Saffiya's street food revolution
- Party can lead or join

### Final Choice
- Accept third Plate through corruption
- Expose corruption, lose everything but integrity
- Reform from within after earning Plate
- Create alternative rating system
- Destroy Guild entirely

## Legacy Endings

### Empire Builder
- Party retires, empire continues
- Trained successors run locations
- Becomes multi-generational dynasty
- Signature dishes become cultural icons
- Statues in Culinary Haven

### Reformer
- Guild reformed into fair system
- All restaurants judged on merit
- Party sacrificed empire for principle
- Remembered as heroes
- Smaller but more meaningful success

### Revolutionary
- Guild destroyed entirely
- New decentralized system
- Street food elevated to equal standing
- True culinary democracy
- Most radical change

### Tragic Fall
- Empire grew too fast
- Quality suffered, collapsed
- Cautionary tale
- But: Some locations survived and reformed
- Lessons learned the hard way
