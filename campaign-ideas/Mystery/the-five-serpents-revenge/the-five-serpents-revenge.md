# The Five Serpents Revenge

*A Revenge / Martial Arts campaign — Short Campaign (6–8 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Five Serpents Revenge
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Revenge Quest / Martial Arts / Character-Driven
**Estimated Length:** Short Campaign — 6–8 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **6** → Finish **10–11**
**Tone (keywords):** revenge-driven, martial-mastery, stylized-violence, honor, training-montage, cathartic
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party leader was once part of the Five Serpents—an elite assassin squad serving the Dragon Emperor. Betrayed and left for dead on their wedding day, they awaken from a coma years later with one burning purpose: hunt down each former teammate and the master who ordered their death. The party must track each Serpent across the realm, learning their current lives and facing them in spectacular duels, while uncovering why they were betrayed and whether revenge will bring peace or more pain.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Escalating Duels:** Each Serpent is a major boss fight with unique martial arts style requiring different tactics.
*   **Personal Stakes:** The campaign is emotionally intense, dealing with themes of betrayal, forgiveness, and the cost of revenge.
*   **Investigation Required:** Finding each Serpent requires detective work and social encounters.
*   **Moral Complexity:** Some Serpents have sympathetic motivations for the betrayal, creating ethical dilemmas.

---

## Story Arc (Short Campaign — 6–8 sessions)

A five-act arc structured as a sequential hunt through a revenge list.

*   **Act I — The Awakening (Session 1):** The party leader wakes from a years-long coma, discovers what happened, and makes their list. Act I introduces the world, the betrayal, and the quest for vengeance.
*   **Act II — The First Blood (Sessions 2–4):** The party tracks and confronts the first three Serpents: Copperhead (the assassin), Cottonmouth (the bodyguard), and Sidewinder (the crime lord). Each duel reveals more about the betrayal.
*   **Act III — The Truth (Sessions 5–6):** The party confronts Rattlesnake (the enforcer) and learns the shocking truth about why the betrayal happened. The quest becomes more complex than simple revenge.
*   **Act IV — The Master (Sessions 7–8):** The final confrontation with Diamondback, the betrayer, and ultimately the Dragon Emperor who ordered it. The party must decide: complete their revenge or choose a different path.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Bride Wakes"

*   **Focus:** Introduction / Motivation
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Hook: The party leader wakes from a coma, barely alive, and learns years have passed.
    *   The List: They create their revenge list—the Five Serpents who attacked them and the Dragon Emperor who ordered it.
    *   The First Lead: Investigation reveals Copperhead is now living as a seemingly normal tavern owner in a distant town.

**Escalation:** Attempts to prepare for the journey are interrupted by assassins—someone knows the leader has awakened and wants them dead.

### Session 2 — "The Viper"

*   **Focus:** Investigation / Boss Fight
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The Hunt: Track Copperhead to her tavern, learn about her new life and family.
    *   The Confrontation: Copperhead fights with poison and twin daggers, desperate to protect her new identity.
    *   First Blood: Defeat Copperhead. The party must decide whether to finish her or show mercy.

**Escalation:** Before dying/yielding, Copperhead reveals: "We didn't have a choice. He would have killed all of us."

### Session 3 — "House of Blue Steel"

*   **Focus:** Iconic Battle / Army Fight
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The Trail: Copperhead's information leads to Cottonmouth, now bodyguard to a corrupt noble.
    *   The Army: To reach Cottonmouth, the party must fight through the noble's elite guards in an iconic dojo battle.
    *   The Duel: Cottonmouth wields a massive warhammer with brutal efficiency. Honorable warrior who accepts defeat.

**Escalation:** Cottonmouth reveals the wedding attack wasn't about the party leader—it was about someone they were marrying. But why?

### Session 4 — "The Crime Lord's Throne"

*   **Focus:** Social / Criminal Underworld
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Following Money: Sidewinder has become a successful crime lord running an opium trade.
    *   The Deal: Sidewinder tries to buy their way out, offering information and resources.
    *   The Snake Pit: If refused, Sidewinder's arena becomes the battleground—fighting while surrounded by poisonous serpents.

**Escalation:** Sidewinder reveals the betrayal happened because the party leader's fiancé was secretly an heir to a rival empire. The Dragon Emperor couldn't allow the marriage.

### Session 5 — "Gogo and The Rattler"

*   **Focus:** Elite Combat / Bodyguard Battle
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The Castle: Rattlesnake serves as elite guard to the Dragon Emperor's inner sanctum.
    *   The Bodyguard: Before reaching Rattlesnake, face their protégé—a deadly young warrior.
    *   The Truth Revealed: Rattlesnake was in love with the party leader and participated in the attack to save them from worse fate.

**Escalation:** Rattlesnake offers to help the party reach the Dragon Emperor, revealing Diamondback—the leader's former lover—was the one who pulled the trigger.

### Session 6 — "My Name is..."

*   **Focus:** Revelation / Training
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The Speech: The party confronts their need for revenge versus the truth they've learned.
    *   Seeking the Master: They learn Diamondback has left the Emperor's service and is training in isolation at a mountain monastery.
    *   Training Montage: The party undergoes intense training to prepare for the ultimate duels.

**Escalation:** A messenger arrives—the Dragon Emperor knows they're coming and welcomes it. It's a trap or a test.

### Session 7 — "Showdown at Temple Mountain"

*   **Focus:** Epic Duel / Character Resolution
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The Monastery: The party climbs the mountain and finds Diamondback has become a monk seeking redemption.
    *   The Duel: An intricate, highly-choreographed martial arts duel between the party leader and Diamondback.
    *   The Choice: Diamondback is defeated but not killed. They explain everything and beg forgiveness.

**Escalation:** The Dragon Emperor arrives with an army. The monastery becomes a siege, and Diamondback fights alongside the party in redemption.

### Session 8 — "The Final Chapter"

*   **Focus:** Climax / Resolution
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The Emperor's Test: The Dragon Emperor reveals this was all a test of the party's skill and determination.
    *   The Real Reason: The fiancé was an assassin sent to kill the party leader. The Emperor "saved" them with the betrayal.
    *   The Final Choice: Accept this truth and rejoin the Emperor's service, or reject it and fight. Either way, the revenge quest ends.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Diamondback** — *The Betrayer / Former Love*
    *   **Role:** The party leader's former lover who pulled the trigger on their wedding day.
    *   **Personality:** Conflicted, skilled, seeking redemption in isolation.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Was forced to choose between following orders or watching the party leader be tortured to death. Chose the quicker "mercy."

2.  **The Dragon Emperor** — *The Master*
    *   **Role:** Mysterious ruler who ordered the attack.
    *   **Personality:** Calculating, sees people as chess pieces, believes ends justify means.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Genuinely believed he was protecting the party leader, but his methods destroyed their life anyway.

3.  **The Five Serpents:**
    *   **Copperhead:** Poison specialist, now a tavern owner trying to forget.
    *   **Cottonmouth:** Brutal warrior with honor, now a bodyguard.
    *   **Sidewinder:** Clever strategist, now a crime lord.
    *   **Rattlesnake:** Disciplined enforcer who secretly loved the party leader.

---

## Key Locations

1.  **The Monastery of the Crane:** Where the party leader trained before joining the Five Serpents.
2.  **House of Blue Steel:** A prestigious dojo where Cottonmouth works, site of an army battle.
3.  **Sidewinder's Palace:** An opium den and crime headquarters disguised as luxury.
4.  **Temple Mountain:** Remote monastery where Diamondback seeks redemption.
5.  **The Dragon's Throne:** The Emperor's palace, where truth and lies intertwine.

---

## Central Conflict

The central conflict explores whether revenge brings peace or just more pain. It asks: can you forgive those who destroyed your life? Is vengeance justice or just more violence? And what do you do when you learn your enemies had sympathetic reasons? The campaign is personal, cathartic, and ultimately about choosing what kind of person you want to be.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **The List**

*   The party tracks their revenge list. Crossing off names should feel significant.
*   **Mechanics:** After each Serpent is defeated, provide a dramatic moment of closure and information that complicates the narrative.

### 2. **Stylized Combat**

*   Duels with the Serpents should be cinematic, one-on-one affairs with environmental elements.
*   **Mechanics:** Encourage descriptive combat. Grant bonuses for creative use of environment and martial arts flavor.

### 3. **Martial Arts Styles**

*   Each Serpent has a unique fighting style the party can learn from by studying or defeating them.
*   **Mechanics:** After defeating a Serpent, the party can acquire a technique or ability representing that style.

### 4. **Flashback Training**

*   Use flashbacks to when the party trained together to provide context and emotional weight.
*   **Mechanics:** Before major encounters, allow flashback scenes that grant temporary bonuses or reveal tactical information.

### 5. **The Mercy Decision**

*   After defeating each Serpent, the party chooses: kill them or show mercy.
*   **Consequences:** These choices affect the final confrontation and determine which ending is available.
