**The Dude Abides**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** In a city where bowling leagues are fronts for criminal enterprises, the party is mistaken for a wealthy merchant's adventuring party and swept into an absurd kidnapping plot. Through encounters with nihilists, avant-garde artists, and eccentric crime lords, they navigate a world where nothing makes sense but everyone takes it very seriously—except them.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the emergence of the city's bowling league as a criminal negotiation system.
    *   Write the history of the Lebowski merchant family and their rivalry with other wealthy houses.
    *   Describe the Nihilist Cult's formation and their philosophy of aggressive nothingness.
    *   Explain the Underground Art Collective and how it launders money through "installations."
    *   Detail the Great Rug Wars—a conflict between merchant houses over textile trade routes.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Bowling League** (Major): Criminal organization disguised as sports league, each team represents a faction.
    *   **The Nihilists** (Major): German-accented cultists who believe in nothing but enjoy petty crime.
    *   **The Art Collective** (Minor): Avant-garde artists who launder money and traffic in exotic goods.
    *   **Merchant Houses** (Minor): Wealthy families competing for status and trade dominance.
    *   **The League Purists** (Minor): Bowlers who actually care about the sport and hate the criminal element.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** The Stranger (narrator/guide), Big Lebowski (wealthy fraud), Maude (artist manipulator), Walter (volatile friend), The Nihilists' Leader.
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Bowling Champion" (skilled athlete/criminal), "Art Patron" (wealthy eccentric), "League Enforcer" (maintains order), "Rug Merchant" (trades in status symbols).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The Dude's Tavern:** Run-down establishment with attached bowling alley, party's home base.
*   **Bowl-O-Rama:** Main bowling league arena, multi-level with private lanes for important meetings.
*   **Big Lebowski's Manor:** Ostentatious mansion filled with empty achievements and bitter memories.
*   **The Nihilists' Warehouse:** Industrial space with terrible electronic music equipment and living quarters.
*   **Maude's Loft:** Artist studio with bizarre installations and a suspended harness for painting.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Party accepts compensation from Big Lebowski → **THEN:** Drawn into kidnapping plot, nihilists target them.
*   **IF:** Party joins bowling league → **THEN:** Access to criminal underworld, must compete in tournaments.
*   **IF:** Party loses their cool → **THEN:** Violent confrontations, loss of Abiding benefits, temporary combat edge.
*   **IF:** The Stranger appears → **THEN:** Cryptic guidance, perspective shift, philosophical interlude.
*   **IF:** Someone goes "over the line" → **THEN:** Immediate faction response, rules debate, potential violence.
