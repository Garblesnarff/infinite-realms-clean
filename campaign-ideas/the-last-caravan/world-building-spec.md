**The Last Caravan**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** When the sun freezes and the Darkness begins consuming the world, the party leads a caravan of hundreds fleeing south. They make agonizing choices allocating supplies, deciding who fights/rests, and navigating political tensions. Every decision has permanent consequences—NPCs die in battle or from starvation, morale affects loyalty, caravan size changes. The journey culminates at Arberrang where they decide whether to fight the Darkness, flee, or sacrifice everything.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the gods' death—how divine beings fell and the world began dying.
    *   Write the history of the Varl—giant horned race created by lost gods.
    *   Describe the ancient war between men and Dredge that ended centuries ago.
    *   Explain the Darkness—living void consuming reality, possibly escaped from gods' prison.
    *   Detail the Godstones and ancient magic that might stop the world's end.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Caravan** (Major): Player-led refugee exodus seeking survival, hundreds of civilians and warriors.
    *   **Arberrang** (Major): Last great human city, under siege, destination of exodus.
    *   **The Dredge** (Major Enemy): Ancient stone warriors awakening as world dies, led by Bellower.
    *   **Rival Caravans** (Minor): Other refugee groups competing for resources and shelter.
    *   **The Ravens** (Minor): Mercenary band offering services for price or competing for supplies.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Hakon (old warrior), Alette/Rook (young heir), Oddleif (huntress), Bellower (immortal Dredge), Juno (mysterious mender).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Varl Warrior" (giant tanks), "Human Fighter" (versatile warrior), "Archer" (ranged support), "Dredge Grunt" (stone soldier), "Caravan Civilian" (vulnerable refugee).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **The Caravan:** Mobile base, size changes based on choices, contains: supply wagons, refugee tents, warrior camps, command tent where party makes decisions.
*   **Frozen Wastes:** Endless snowy plains with dead forests, abandoned villages, dangerous crossing points, Dredge ambush sites.
*   **Godstone:** Massive ancient monument with Norse runes, location where ritual might stop Darkness.
*   **Arberrang:** Last great walled city, crowded with refugees, under siege by Dredge, wooden walls and Nordic architecture.
*   **The Darkness:** Living void spreading from north, turns land to ash, consumes all life, unstoppable conventionally.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Full rations maintained → **THEN:** Morale high but supplies deplete rapidly, may run out before reaching safety.
*   **IF:** Half or quarter rations → **THEN:** Supplies last longer but morale drops, starvation begins, weakness in combat.
*   **IF:** Important NPC dies → **THEN:** Permanently lost, other NPCs mourn, affects future story options and morale.
*   **IF:** Rescue strangers → **THEN:** Caravan grows, more mouths to feed but more fighters, renown increases.
*   **IF:** Leave strangers → **THEN:** Supplies preserved, morale may drop from guilt, renown decreases.
*   **IF:** Morale drops too low → **THEN:** Desertion, rebellion, possible loss of leadership, caravan fractures.
*   **IF:** Reach Arberrang → **THEN:** Final choice: fight (heroic stand), flee (survival), ritual (sacrifice to stop Darkness).
*   **IF:** Complete ritual → **THEN:** Darkness stopped but requires sacrifice of beloved character or party member.
