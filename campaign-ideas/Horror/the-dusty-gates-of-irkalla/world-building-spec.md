# World-Building Specification: The Dusty Gates of Irkalla

**1. Core Concept:** Players descend through seven gates to Irkalla, the Mesopotamian land of no return, where all dead become dust-eating feathered beings. They must survive increasing stripping away of self, face Ereshkigal's lonely court, discover why escape attempts fail, and either break the ancient pattern or accept death's inevitability.

**2. Lore Primer:**
*   Mesopotamian cosmology and the underworld
*   Irkalla as the land of no return
*   Ereshkigal's mythology and her sister Ishtar
*   The descent of Ishtar through seven gates
*   Nergal's descent and decision to stay
*   The Epic of Gilgamesh and his failed immortality quest
*   Namtar and the role of disease/death
*   Sumerian/Akkadian concepts of death and afterlife
*   The seven gate ritual of stripping

**3. Factions:**
*   **Ereshkigal's Court** - The dusty throne, lonely rulers, ancient enforcers
*   **The Seven Gatekeepers** - Implacable guardians of descent
*   **The Forgotten Dead** - Dust beings who've lost themselves
*   **The Seekers** - Rare souls still trying to escape
*   **The Ancient Heroes** - Failed legendary attempts (Gilgamesh, others)

**4. NPCs:**
*   **Tier 1:** Ereshkigal, Nergal, Seven Gatekeepers, Namtar, Gilgamesh's Shade
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** Various dust beings at different stages, ancient spirits, court attendants

**5. Locations:** Seven Gates, House of Dust, Ereshkigal's Throne, Ganzer River, Forgotten Archives, the mythical Eighth Gate

**6. Mechanics:**
*   Seven Strippings system
*   Dust sustenance horror
*   Fading Into Dust transformation
*   Weight of Ages psychological aging
*   Ancient lore requirements
*   Pattern of failed attempts investigation

**7. Themes:** Mortality's inevitability, horror of meaningless existence, acceptance vs. fighting, death as necessary, the oldest human fears, what remains when everything is stripped away.
