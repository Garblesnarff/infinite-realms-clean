# The Ancient Ones' Entertainment

*A Meta-Horror / Survival Comedy campaign — Short Campaign (6–8 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Ancient Ones' Entertainment
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Meta-Horror / Survival / Dark Comedy / Cosmic Horror
**Estimated Length:** Short Campaign — 6–8 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **5** → Finish **9**
**Tone (keywords):** meta-horror, trope-aware, sacrificial, cosmic horror, dark comedy, systematic
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party is lured to a remote woodland retreat under false pretenses, only to discover they're part of an ancient sacrificial ritual. A secret organization is manipulating events from underground facilities, forcing the party into horror movie tropes to appease sleeping cosmic entities called the Ancient Ones. The party must survive an arsenal of classic monsters while uncovering the conspiracy—and deciding whether to play by the rules, break them, or embrace the chaos and let the world end in spectacular fashion.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Trope Awareness:** The campaign is deliberately filled with horror movie clichés. Players can exploit this knowledge or be punished for overconfidence.
*   **Environmental Manipulation:** The organization controls the cabin, the woods, and even monster behavior to force specific narrative outcomes.
*   **Monster Variety:** Dozens of classic horror creatures are available, creating unpredictable threats.
*   **The Choice:** The party can try to survive traditionally, rebel against the system, or embrace apocalypse. Each path has unique challenges.

---

## Story Arc (Short Campaign — 6–8 sessions)

A meta-horror story escalating from standard horror tropes to full cosmic apocalypse.

*   **Act I — The Classic Setup (Sessions 1–2):** The party arrives at a remote cabin for a vacation/adventure. Weird things happen that match horror movie logic. They discover they're being watched and manipulated by an underground facility forcing them into roles: the Virgin, the Athlete, the Scholar, the Fool, and the Whore (archetypal sacrifices).
*   **Act II — The System Revealed (Sessions 3–5):** The party discovers the truth: organizations worldwide perform these rituals to keep the Ancient Ones sleeping. They must survive increasingly dangerous monsters while deciding whether to play along or rebel. The facility's technicians and security forces become antagonists if the party deviates from the "script."
*   **Act III — The Reckoning (Sessions 6–8):** The party makes their choice: complete the ritual (sacrificing members to save the world), break the system (fighting both monsters and the organization), or push the button that releases all monsters and wakes the Ancient Ones, ending the world but on their terms.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "Let's Get This Party Started"
*   **Focus:** Classic Horror Setup / Introduction
*   **Key Events:** The party arrives at a remote cabin. They discover a mysterious basement with strange artifacts. That night, they play "Truth or Dare" with an enchanted object that influences them to split up and make poor decisions.
*   **Escalation:** Monsters attack—zombies from the lake. The party fights them off but discovers there's something very wrong about how "movie-like" this feels.

### Session 2 — "Puppets on Strings"
*   **Focus:** Discovery / Paranoia
*   **Key Events:** The party finds cameras hidden everywhere and a one-way mirror. Behind it: an underground facility watching and betting on their survival. The technicians discuss them in terms of archetypes and "scenario completion."
*   **Escalation:** The facility deploys pheromone mists and environmental controls to force party members into specific behaviors (the "Athlete" becomes aggressive, the "Virgin" becomes pure-hearted, etc.).

### Session 3 — "The Monster Catalogue"
*   **Focus:** Horror Variety / Action
*   **Key Events:** The party discovers the facility houses hundreds of monsters in containment. They see the list: werewolves, demons, slashers, aliens, Japanese ghosts, etc. The technicians release them in specific orders based on a ritual script.
*   **Escalation:** A new threat is unleashed: the party must choose which monster to face from limited options, each representing a different horror subgenre.

### Session 4 — "Breaking the Script"
*   **Focus:** Rebellion / Action
*   **Key Events:** The party attempts to deviate from the expected roles. They attack facility personnel, sabotage equipment, or refuse to follow tropes. The facility responds with force—security teams and emergency protocols.
*   **Escalation:** The Director (facility leader) warns them: if the ritual fails, the Ancient Ones wake and destroy all humanity. The party must decide if this threat is real.

### Session 5 — "System Purge"
*   **Focus:** Chaos / Combat
*   **Key Events:** Whether intentional or accidental, the monster containment system is breached. All monsters are released simultaneously into the facility. The underground base becomes a warzone as creatures slaughter technicians and security.
*   **Escalation:** The party can flee, help survivors, or use the chaos to reach the Director's office and learn the full truth.

### Session 6 — "The Ancient Ones Stir"
*   **Focus:** Cosmic Horror / Revelation
*   **Key Events:** The party finds the ritual chamber where the Ancient Ones sleep—vast, incomprehensible beings that dwarf mountains. If the ritual fails, they will wake. The party learns this has happened for millennia across the world.
*   **Escalation:** The final choice: complete the ritual by dying in the right order, find a way to stop the Ancient Ones without sacrifice, or embrace apocalypse.

### Session 7 — "The Button"
*   **Focus:** Climax / Moral Choice
*   **Key Events:** The party finds the emergency system purge—a button that releases every monster worldwide and wakes the Ancient Ones. They can push it (ending the world), protect it (allowing the ritual system to continue), or destroy it (uncertain consequences).
*   **Escalation:** Combat with remaining facility personnel, surviving monsters, and possibly the embodiments of the Ancient Ones' will.

### Session 8 — "The End of the World (or Not)"
*   **Focus:** Epilogue / Apocalypse
*   **Multiple Endings:**
    *   **Ritual Completed:** Party members die in order. The Ancient Ones sleep. The world is saved but the system continues.
    *   **System Destroyed:** The party breaks the ritual but must face the Ancient Ones directly in a hopeless final battle.
    *   **Apocalypse:** The party pushes the button. The Ancient Ones rise and destroy the world, but humanity finally knows the truth.
    *   **Impossible Victory:** Through creativity and luck, the party finds a fourth option, possibly using the monsters against the Ancient Ones.

## Major NPCs

1.  **The Director** — *The Mastermind*
    *   **Role:** Runs the facility, orchestrates the ritual.
    *   **Personality:** Tired, pragmatic, not evil—just doing what's necessary to prevent apocalypse.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Has run dozens of these rituals. Has seen countless people die. Hates his job but believes it's necessary.

2.  **Dana (The Virgin archetype)** — *Possible PC or NPC*
    *   **Role:** Represents innocence. Must survive or die last for ritual success.
    *   **Personality:** Intelligent, moral, reluctant hero.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** The "Virgin" doesn't need to be literally virginal—just represent purity in the ritual logic.

3.  **Marty (The Fool archetype)** — *The Genre-Savvy One*
    *   **Role:** Sees through the manipulation, warns others.
    *   **Personality:** Laid-back, perceptive, often dismissed until proven right.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** May be the only one aware enough to break the system or doom everyone trying.

4.  **The Ancient Ones** — *Sleeping Gods*
    *   **Role:** Cosmic entities that predate humanity.
    *   **Personality:** Beyond comprehension, utterly indifferent to human life.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** They demand entertainment and suffering. If not appeased, they will wake and destroy everything.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Cabin:** Classic horror movie setting. Seems normal but filled with manipulation tech.
2.  **The Woods:** Surrounding forest where initial monsters appear.
3.  **The Underground Facility:** High-tech control center monitoring everything.
4.  **The Monster Containment:** Massive vault holding every type of horror creature.
5.  **The Ritual Chamber:** Where the Ancient Ones sleep beneath the earth.

## Central Conflict

The central conflict is between predetermined narrative and free will. The organization has perfected forcing people into horror movie roles to satisfy cosmic entities. The party must decide: accept the roles and die as sacrifices (saving humanity), rebel against the system (risking apocalypse), or embrace chaos (definitely causing apocalypse but on their terms). It's a meta-commentary on horror tropes, player agency, and the price of survival.

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Archetype System**
*   Each party member is assigned an archetype: Virgin, Athlete, Scholar, Fool, Whore.
*   Pheromones and manipulation push them toward stereotypical behaviors.
*   Players can resist (skill checks) but at the cost of alerting the facility.

### 2. **The Monster Lottery**
*   When new threats appear, the DM randomly selects from horror archetypes.
*   Players sometimes get limited choice: "Pick your death—zombies, werewolves, or demonic entities?"

### 3. **Trope Awareness**
*   Players can identify horror movie logic and exploit it (or be punished for overconfidence).
*   Example: "If we split up, people die. If we stay together, we're trapped. What's the third option?"

### 4. **The Ritual Countdown**
*   Sacrifices must occur in specific order and ways. If the pattern breaks, the ritual fails.
*   The facility tracks this and intervenes if the party doesn't follow the script.

### 5. **The Button**
*   A final, terrible choice: release all monsters and wake the Ancient Ones, ending the world but breaking the cycle forever.
