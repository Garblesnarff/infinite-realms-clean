# Threads of the Pattern

*A High Fantasy Epic campaign — Long Campaign (15+ sessions) — Medium to High difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Threads of the Pattern
**Campaign Type / Genre:** High Fantasy / Epic Fantasy / Prophecy and Fate / Gender-Based Magic
**Estimated Length:** Long Campaign — 15+ sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **18**
**Tone (keywords):** epic, prophetic, wheel-of-destiny, light-versus-dark, gender-magic-divide, heroic-burden
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
In a world where the Dark One strains against his prison and the Wheel of Time turns toward the Last Battle, the players discover they are Ta'veren—individuals around whom the Pattern itself bends, forcing probability and destiny to converge. As channelers of the One Power (divided into male Saidin, tainted with madness, and female Saidar, pure but distrusted), they must navigate a world where prophecy claims one of them may be the Dragon Reborn—the champion destined to face the Dark One, or the destroyer who will break the world again. They must unite fractured nations, battle the Dark One's forces (Shadowspawn, Darkfriends, and the Forsaken—immortal servants from the Age of Legends), and decide whether to fulfill prophecy or defy it.

---

## Story Arc (Long Campaign — 15+ sessions)

A campaign inspired by Robert Jordan's Wheel of Time, focusing on destiny versus choice, the corruption of power, and gender dynamics in magic.

*   **Act I — The Pattern Stirs (Sessions 1–5):** The players, from a remote village in the Two Rivers, are attacked by Trollocs (beast-men serving the Dark One) seeking someone—anyone—who matches fragments of prophecy. An Aes Sedai (female mage) arrives claiming one of them is Ta'veren and possibly the Dragon Reborn. Fleeing their burning home, they must evade Myrddraal (eyeless Shadowspawn commanders), Darkfriends (human servants of evil), and even the Children of the Light (fanatic witch-hunters who kill channelers). They discover their nascent abilities: men who can channel Saidin (doomed to madness), women who can join the Aes Sedai, and all of them warping probability simply by existing.

*   **Act II — The Hunt for the Horn (Sessions 6–11):** Separated and scattered across nations, the players each pursue different threads of destiny. One seeks training at the White Tower (where Aes Sedai politics are as dangerous as the Shadow). Another discovers Wolfbrother abilities, communicating with wolves and entering Tel'aran'rhiod (the World of Dreams). A third finds the Horn of Valere—an artifact that can summon dead heroes to fight in the Last Battle. They must navigate Cairhien's deadly political games (Daes Dae'mar), unite the Aiel warriors from the Waste, and resist the Forsaken—thirteen immortal channelers freed from their prison, each a master manipulator with powers from the Age of Legends.

*   **Act III — Tarmon Gai'don, the Last Battle (Sessions 12–15+):** The seals on the Dark One's prison are breaking. The players must unite the nations through the ancient peace of the Dragon, claim powerful sa'angreal (magic amplifiers), and lead armies to Shayol Ghul—the mountain where the Dark One's prison lies. They face impossible choices: Should the male channeler who is the Dragon Reborn break the world to save it? Can the female Aes Sedai among them trust him despite Saidin's taint? Will they bind the Dark One again (perpetuating the cycle) or kill him (risking the destruction of free will itself)? The final confrontation is not just physical battle, but a philosophical conflict in Tel'aran'rhiod where reality is shaped by will and belief.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "Winternight in the Two Rivers"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Horror / Flight
*   **Key Events:**
    *   During Bel Tine festival, Trollocs attack the village seeking someone who matches prophecy.
    *   Moiraine Sedai arrives with her Warder Lan, revealing one player might be the Dragon Reborn.
    *   The players flee, pursued by Trollocs and a Myrddraal whose gaze can freeze the soul.
    *   First manifestations of power: a male player channels Saidin accidentally (feeling incredible power mixed with oily corruption), a female player touches Saidar (but cannot control it).
**Escalation:** A Myrddraal marks one player with the Dark One's touch—they now appear in the creature's dreams and can be tracked.

### Sessions 2-4 — "The Road to Tar Valon"
*   **Focus:** Journey / Discovery / Pursuit
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Fleeing toward the White Tower (Aes Sedai's seat of power), they must evade Children of the Light who would execute male channelers.
    *   They encounter Padan Fain, a Darkfriend peddler who has been twisted into something worse—he can sense the players and is obsessed with them.
    *   In the cursed city of Shadar Logoth, they find a ancient evil predating the Dark One—Mashadar, a consuming fog. One player takes a dagger corrupted by this evil.
    *   They split up, scattered by Trolloc attacks, and must reunite while being hunted.
**Escalation:** One of the Forsaken takes notice—Ba'alzamon (who claims to be the Dark One himself) appears in their dreams, offering power and threatening destruction.

### Sessions 5-7 — "The White Tower's Politics"
*   **Focus:** Intrigue / Training / Conspiracy
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Female channelers begin training at the White Tower, learning Aes Sedai politics are Byzantine and deadly—each Ajah (faction) has different goals.
    *   They discover the Amyrlin Seat (leader of Aes Sedai) is secretly guiding them, but the Black Ajah (Aes Sedai serving the Dark One) infiltrates the tower.
    *   Male channelers must hide their abilities or seek the dangerous training offered by Mazrim Taim's False Dragon faction.
    *   They learn of the prophecy in detail: The Dragon Reborn will break the world to save it, go mad, and die in the process.
**Escalation:** The Black Ajah attempts to gentle (sever from power) or kill the male channelers. An Aes Sedai the players trusted is revealed as Black Ajah.

### Sessions 8-11 — "The Aiel Waste and the Prophecy of the Car'a'carn"
*   **Focus:** Exploration / Cultural Challenge / Identity
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Following prophecy fragments, players journey to the Aiel Waste, a harsh desert homeland of legendary warriors.
    *   They learn shocking truth: the Aiel were once servants of Aes Sedai in the Age of Legends, sworn to peace—their current warrior culture is built on shame.
    *   The Dragon Reborn candidate must prove themselves Car'a'carn (chief of chiefs) by entering Rhuidean and surviving visions of the past.
    *   They discover the Choedan Kal—sa'angreal so powerful they could destroy or rebuild the world.
    *   They must unite fractured Aiel clans, some of whom serve the Shadow.
**Escalation:** One of the Forsaken, Lanfear, reveals she loved the original Dragon (Lews Therin) and wants to turn the current Dragon to the Shadow, offering godlike power.

### Sessions 12-15+ — "The Last Battle"
*   **Focus:** Epic Warfare / Moral Choice / Reality-Bending Conflict
*   **Key Events:**
    *   The seals on the Dark One's prison shatter. Trolloc armies pour forth in numbers that blacken the earth.
    *   The players must unite all nations—Andor, Cairhien, Tear, Illian, Arad Doman, and more—under the Dragon's Peace.
    *   Battles rage at Tar Valon, Caemlyn, and the Fields of Merrilor as Shadowspawn attack on all fronts.
    *   The Dragon Reborn and companions enter Shayol Ghul to confront the Dark One in a reality shaped by will in Tel'aran'rhiod.
    *   The philosophical confrontation: The Dark One offers to remove all suffering by removing choice. The players must argue for free will's value.
    *   Final choice: Re-seal the Dark One (preserving the cycle), kill him (potentially destroying free will), or create a new prison that requires constant vigilance.
**Escalation:** The Dark One begins unmaking reality itself. Players must hold their sense of self and purpose while existence dissolves around them.

## Major NPCs

1.  **Moiraine Damodred** — *Aes Sedai of the Blue Ajah*
    *   **Role:** The players' mentor and guide, searching for the Dragon Reborn for twenty years.
    *   **Personality:** Mysterious, commanding, utterly devoted to her mission. Hides warmth beneath cool formality.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She has seen her own death in prophecy and knows she must sacrifice herself to give the Dragon a chance to succeed.

2.  **Lan Mandragoran** — *Warder and Uncrowned King*
    *   **Role:** Moiraine's Warder (magically bonded bodyguard), last king of fallen Malkier, weapon-master who trains the players.
    *   **Personality:** Stoic, deadly, honor-bound. Emotion tightly controlled but deeply felt.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He has sworn to die fighting the Shadow in Malkier's ruins, and this death-wish conflicts with his duty to Moiraine.

3.  **Ishamael/Ba'alzamon** — *The Forsaken Philosopher*
    *   **Role:** One of the Forsaken, appears in dreams claiming to be the Dark One himself. The Dragon's eternal nemesis across turnings of the Wheel.
    *   **Personality:** Nihilistic, brilliant, believes he serves mercy by ending the cycle. Sees himself as tragic hero, not villain.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He is insane from three thousand years of partial imprisonment, and despite his power, he is tired—he wants the cycle to end as much as anyone.

4.  **Lanfear** — *The Forsaken of Dreams*
    *   **Role:** Most powerful female Forsaken, can manipulate Tel'aran'rhiod, was lover of the original Dragon (Lews Therin).
    *   **Personality:** Obsessive, possessive, believes she and the Dragon are destined to rule together. Capable of terrible acts "for love."
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** She loved Lews Therin, not the Dark One's cause, and might be redeemed if the current Dragon can reach her—but her love is toxic and controlling.

5.  **Padan Fain** — *The Hound of the Shadow*
    *   **Role:** Darkfriend peddler twisted by the Dark One into a tracker, then corrupted by Shadar Logoth's evil—now he serves neither side but hunts the players obsessively.
    *   **Personality:** Increasingly insane, driven by contradictory compulsions. Groveling and grandiose by turns.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He carries two kinds of evil that hate each other, tearing him apart. He is pitiable and terrifying simultaneously.

6.  **Rand al'Thor (if not a player)** — *The Dragon Reborn*
    *   **Role:** If players aren't the Dragon, this NPC fulfills that role—a shepherd's son bearing impossible destiny.
    *   **Personality:** Starts noble and determined, slowly hardened and darkened by burden and Saidin's taint, must learn to laugh and love again to succeed.
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** He contains memories of Lews Therin Telamon, the original Dragon, and the two personalities fracture his mind.

## Key Locations

1.  **The Two Rivers:** Peaceful rural homeland, now burned and scarred by Trolloc raids.
2.  **Tar Valon:** Island city of the Aes Sedai, dominated by the White Tower, center of female channeling and politics.
3.  **The Aiel Waste:** Vast desert, homeland of the warrior Aiel, containing the sacred city of Rhuidean.
4.  **Shayol Ghul:** Mountain in the Blight where the Dark One's prison is located, a place where reality itself is corrupted.
5.  **Tel'aran'rhiod:** The World of Dreams, where dreamers can manipulate reality with will, accessible to Dreamwalkers and Wolfbrothers.
6.  **The Ways:** Corrupted magical pathways between cities, now home to Machin Shin (the Black Wind that devours souls).

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores destiny versus free will, the corruption that comes with power, and gender dynamics in magic and society. The central question is whether prophecy can be defied, or if trying to avoid destiny only fulfills it. The dual nature of the One Power—Saidin (male, tainted with madness) versus Saidar (female, pure but often wielded arrogantly)—creates tension and requires cooperation despite mistrust. The players must decide whether removing all evil is worth removing all choice, and whether breaking cycles of violence requires new thinking or just repeats old mistakes.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Ta'veren and the Pattern**
*   Players are Ta'veren—threads around which the Pattern bends. They cause improbable events simply by existing.
*   Mechanically: Each session, players can declare "The Pattern favors me" to force re-rolls, create coincidences, or trigger narrative opportunities.
*   But: Being Ta'veren draws events and enemies to you. The DM can invoke "The Pattern demands" to force dramatic confrontations or difficult choices.
*   The more the players use Ta'veren abilities, the tighter the Pattern's weave becomes around them—eventually, they have almost no choice in their actions, fulfilling prophecy whether they want to or not.

### 2. **Saidin vs Saidar (Gender-Based Magic)**
*   Male channelers wield Saidin, female channelers wield Saidar. Both access the One Power, but through different methods.
*   **Saidar (Female):** Controlled by surrendering to it, flowing and guiding. Mechanically: spend time preparing and maintain concentration. Effects are precise and sustained.
*   **Saidin (Male):** Controlled by seizing and dominating it. Mechanically: explosive, powerful, but less precise. Each use risks exposure to the taint.
*   **The Taint:** Male channelers using Saidin gain corruption points. At thresholds, they suffer madness effects: paranoia, hallucinations, violent outbursts, eventually death. Only cleansing Saidin (high-level quest) removes this.
*   **Weaves:** Spells are weaves of the Five Powers (Air, Fire, Water, Earth, Spirit). Players must describe how they weave elements, creating tactical variety.

### 3. **Warder Bond**
*   Aes Sedai can bond a warrior as their Warder, creating magical connection.
*   Benefits: Warder gains enhanced combat abilities, can sense Aes Sedai's direction/emotional state, minor magic resistance. Aes Sedai always knows Warder's location and condition.
*   Drawback: If one dies, the other suffers devastating grief. Warders whose Aes Sedai die often go berserk seeking death in battle.
*   Roleplaying: Bonded players must navigate intense magical intimacy—they feel each other's emotions constantly.

### 4. **Tel'aran'rhiod (World of Dreams)**
*   Dreamwalkers, Dreamers, and Wolfbrothers can enter the dream world consciously.
*   In Tel'aran'rhiod: Reality is mutable. Your will and belief shape the world. Death here is death in reality.
*   Mechanically: Contested will checks determine reality. Believing you're invulnerable makes you so—unless someone believes harder.
*   Dangers: Nightmares manifest, the Forsaken hunt here, losing yourself in dreams means never waking.
*   Opportunities: Spy on anyone by entering their dreams, travel instantly to any location, practice dangerous weaves safely.

### 5. **The Horn of Valere**
*   Legendary artifact that summons dead heroes bound to the Wheel to fight for the Horn-sounder.
*   If players claim it: Grants army of legendary warriors (Artur Hawkwing, Birgitte Silverbow, etc.) for one pivotal battle.
*   But: The Horn binds the sounder to it. If an enemy takes it and sounds it, the heroes must fight for them instead.
*   Ethical dilemma: The heroes are bound against their will. Is using it different from the Dark One's slavery?
