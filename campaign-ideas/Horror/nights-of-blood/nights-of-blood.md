# Nights of Blood

*A Gothic Vampire / Political Intrigue campaign — Long Campaign (20-25 sessions) — High difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** Nights of Blood
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Gothic Punk / Vampire Politics / Personal Horror
**Estimated Length:** Long Campaign — 20-25 sessions
**Player Level Range:** Start **3** → Finish **12-15**
**Tone (keywords):** vampire society, Camarilla politics, Sabbat violence, Masquerade preservation, Blood Bond, Beast within, Gothic-punk
**Core Premise (2-4 sentences):**
The party are newly-Embraced vampires (Kindred) in modern-day Chicago, navigating the deadly political games of the Camarilla—the sect enforcing the Masquerade that hides vampire existence from mortals. They must balance their clans' ancient rivalries, resist the Beast's urges for violence, avoid Blood Bonds that enslave through vampiric blood, and survive power struggles between the Prince, Primogen Council, and invading Sabbat fanatics. When a war threatens to shatter the Masquerade and expose all Kindred to mortal hunters, the party must choose: preserve the old order, join the Sabbat's honest monstrosity, or forge something new.

---

## Story Arc (Long Campaign — 20-25 sessions)

A four-act journey from fledgling vampires to potential power brokers.

*   **Act I — The Embrace & First Nights (Sessions 1-6):** The party is Embraced (transformed into vampires) by different sires for various reasons. They learn Kindred society's rules: respect the Prince's domain, hide from mortals (the Masquerade), don't create childer without permission, feed without killing. They meet their clans—Ventrue blue-bloods, Toreador artists, Tremere blood sorcerers, Brujah rebels, etc.—and navigate Elysium (vampire court). First lessons: the Beast lurks within, sunlight kills instantly, and older vampires hold all power through centuries of accumulated influence.
*   **Act II — Blood and Politics (Sessions 7-13):** The party becomes embroiled in Primogen Council politics as their sires use them as pawns. They learn about Blood Bonds—drinking vampire blood three times creates unbreakable loyalty—and must avoid being bound. The Sabbat (a vampire sect embracing monstrosity) attacks Chicago, creating chaos. They meet Anarchs (young vampires rebelling against elder control) who offer alternative to Camarilla hierarchy. The party must navigate three factions while maintaining Masquerade and managing their Beast.
*   **Act III — The Gehenna War (Sessions 14-19):** The Sabbat siege intensifies, threatening to expose all Kindred. Ancient vampires (Methuselahs) manipulate from shadows. The party discovers Gehenna prophecies—when Antediluvians (progenitor vampires) will rise and devour their descendants. Some Sabbat believe Gehenna is coming and only embracing monstrosity will save them. Camarilla dismisses it as myth. Party investigates whether prophecy is real while surviving assassination attempts, blood hunts, and their own Beast's growing power.
*   **Act IV — Revolution in Blood (Sessions 20-25):** The truth emerges: an Antediluvian has awakened beneath Chicago, using factions as pawns. The party must unite Camarilla, Sabbat, and Anarchs—ancient enemies—to face the elder's threat. But the Antediluvian offers power beyond imagining: become its servants and rule, or fight and likely die. Final choice defines them: preserve Masquerade and old order, embrace Sabbat's honest monstrosity, forge Anarch revolution, or serve the Antediluvian's dark designs. Their choice shapes vampire society forever.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "The Embrace"
*   **Focus:** Transformation / World Introduction
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Each character's Embrace—death and rebirth as vampire
    *   Wake in their sire's haven, learn basic truths: sunlight kills, blood sustains, humanity fades
    *   First feeding—party must drink blood without killing (Masquerade violation)
    *   Introduction to the Beast—uncontrolled hunger and rage within
**Escalation:** Prince's sheriff arrives—unauthorized Embraces require explanation

### Sessions 2-4 — "Welcome to Kindred Society"
*   **Focus:** Politics / Clan Introduction
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Presented to Prince Maxwell at Elysium (formal vampire court)
    *   Learn clan stereotypes: Ventrue leaders, Toreador artists, Tremere blood mages, Brujah rebels, Nosferatu information brokers, Malkavians madness-seers
    *   First political mission for sires: gather intelligence, intimidate rival, protect interest
    *   Witness Masquerade breach—vampire feeds openly, hunters mobilize
**Escalation:** Party member frenzies (Beast takes control), nearly breaks Masquerade

### Sessions 5-6 — "The Traditions"
*   **Focus:** Rules & Consequences
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Learn the Six Traditions governing Kindred society
    *   Meet Primogen Council—elders representing each clan
    *   First exposure to Blood Bonds—party member drinks elder's blood, feels unnatural loyalty
    *   Investigate Masquerade breach to find perpetrator
    *   Witness blood hunt called—entire city hunts rule-breaker to final death
**Escalation:** Sabbat scout discovered in city—war is coming

### Sessions 7-10 — "Blood Bonds & Betrayals"
*   **Focus:** Manipulation / Personal Horror
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Party members pressured to drink elder blood (create Bond)
    *   Must navigate being pawns while maintaining autonomy
    *   Meet Anarchs offering freedom from elder control but chaos
    *   Investigation reveals Primogen member plotting against Prince
    *   Choice: report to Prince (become his agents), side with plotter (join coup), tell Anarchs (side with rebellion)
**Escalation:** Sabbat launches first attack—fire-bombing Camarilla haven, killing dozens

### Sessions 11-13 — "The Sabbat Comes"
*   **Focus:** Factional War / Survival
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Sabbat siege begins—vampire fanatics who embrace their monstrosity
    *   They use blood magic, suicide tactics, and mass Embracing mortals as shock troops
    *   Party fights alongside Camarilla or explores Sabbat's honest philosophy
    *   Meet Sabbat defector who explains: they believe Gehenna (apocalypse) is coming
    *   Only embracing vampire nature will allow survival when Antediluvians rise
**Escalation:** Party member is captured by Sabbat, faces Vaulderie (Sabbat blood ritual)

### Sessions 14-16 — "Prophecies of Gehenna"
*   **Focus:** Investigation / Cosmic Horror
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Research Gehenna prophecies: when Antediluvians wake, they'll devour all vampires
    *   Camarilla dismisses as myth; Sabbat prepares for it; Anarchs don't care
    *   Discover ancient Kindred beneath Chicago—Methuselah manipulating all sides
    *   Signs suggest something ancient is stirring
    *   Party must decide if apocalypse is real or propaganda
**Escalation:** Methuselah's agent assassinates Primogen member; succession crisis

### Sessions 17-19 — "The Ancient Stirs"
*   **Focus:** Revelation / High Stakes
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Confirmed: an Antediluvian (one of Caine's grandchilder) is beneath Chicago
    *   It's been pulling strings, orchestrating war to gather power
    *   Can diablerize (soul-drink) all of Chicago's vampires to awaken fully
    *   Sabbat was right about threat but wrong about solution
    *   Party must unite factions or all vampires die
**Escalation:** Antediluvian's influence corrupts elders; some willingly serve

### Sessions 20-22 — "Unholy Alliance"
*   **Focus:** Factional Unity / Difficult Choices
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Party brokers temporary alliance between Camarilla, Sabbat, and Anarchs
    *   Each faction must compromise deeply-held beliefs
    *   Plan assault on Antediluvian's underground haven
    *   Antediluvian offers deal: serve it, gain power beyond imagining
    *   Some elders accept; party must face former allies turned servants
**Escalation:** Antediluvian begins mass-diablerizing vampires to fuel awakening

### Sessions 23-25 — "Revolution in Blood"
*   **Focus:** Climax / Society's Future
*   **Key Events:**
    *   Assault on Antediluvian's sanctum beneath Chicago
    *   Epic battle against ancient vampire with millennia of power
    *   Must use strategy, factional cooperation, and personal sacrifice
    *   Victory requires diablerizing the Antediluvian—absorbing its soul and power—but risking corruption
    *   Choice: who drinks the power? How is vampire society rebuilt?
    *   **Four Endings:**
        *   **Camarilla:** Restore old order, Masquerade preserved, elders rule
        *   **Sabbat:** Embrace honest monstrosity, abandon Masquerade
        *   **Anarch:** Revolution succeeds, young vampires overthrow elders
        *   **Dark:** Party claims Antediluvian's power, becomes new tyrants
**Resolution:** Based on choice, Chicago's vampire society transforms; party either rules, liberates, or becomes the new enemy

## Major NPCs

1.  **Prince Maxwell** — *The Domain Lord*
    *   **Role:** Ventrue Prince of Chicago, enforces Traditions, main Camarilla authority
    *   **Personality:** Calculating, traditional, genuinely believes in Masquerade's necessity
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Is Blood Bound to an even older vampire manipulating through him

2.  **Mira Severine** — *The Toreador Primogen*
    *   **Role:** Artist vampire, represents Toreador clan on council, potential ally or rival
    *   **Personality:** Passionate, aesthetic-obsessed, surprisingly ruthless about beauty
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Is addicted to mortal art scenes, risks Masquerade regularly

3.  **Archbishop Castellano** — *The Sabbat Leader*
    *   **Role:** Sabbat siege commander, offers alternative to Camarilla's lies
    *   **Personality:** Fanatical but honest, believes embracing vampiric nature is only truth
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Truly believes he's saving vampires from Gehenna; not simply evil

4.  **Nyx** — *The Anarch Revolutionary*
    *   **Role:** Young vampire leading Anarch movement, rejects both Camarilla and Sabbat
    *   **Personality:** Idealistic, charismatic, fights for vampire freedom and autonomy
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Is willing to sacrifice anything for the cause, including allies

5.  **The Cappadocian** — *The Awakening Antediluvian*
    *   **Role:** Ancient vampire, thought extinct, manipulating all factions
    *   **Personality:** Incomprehensibly old, views modern vampires as insects, patient and terrible
    *   **Secret/Flaw:** Genuinely doesn't see itself as evil—simply reclaiming what's rightfully its

## Central Conflict

This campaign explores the monster within versus remaining human, freedom versus security through control, the corruption of immortality, and whether honesty or deception is better for society. It asks: Can you remain human while being a monster? Does the end justify means? Should dangerous beings be controlled or freed? The central conflict pits the party's struggle to maintain humanity against vampire society's ancient, corrupt power structures.

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Humanity vs. The Beast**
*   Track Humanity score (conscience and self-control)
*   Low Humanity: Beast takes control easier, monstrous acts feel normal
*   Inhuman acts lower Humanity; redemption through atonement
*   Zero Humanity: become Wight (mindless monster, lose character)
*   Must constantly balance survival needs against human morals

### 2. **Blood Bonds**
*   Drinking vampire's blood creates supernatural attraction
*   Three drinks over time creates complete Blood Bond—unbreakable loyalty
*   Bonded thralls will die for their regnant (blood source)
*   Elders use Bonds to control younger vampires
*   Resisting requires exceptional willpower; breaking requires time and distance

### 3. **Frenzy & Beast**
*   Hunger, rage, or fear can trigger frenzy—Beast takes control
*   During frenzy: attack indiscriminately, ignore pain, break Masquerade
*   Must roll to resist when provoked
*   Repeated frenzies lower Humanity
*   Killing while frenzying haunts character

### 4. **The Masquerade**
*   Breaking Masquerade (exposing vampires to mortals) is cardinal sin
*   Penalties: blood hunt (all vampires hunt you), Prince's punishment, final death
*   Must feed without being seen, hide supernatural powers, dispose of evidence
*   Modern technology makes Masquerade harder—cameras everywhere
*   Party must cover up breaches or face consequences

### 5. **Disciplines (Vampire Powers)**
*   Each clan has unique supernatural abilities
*   Ventrue: Dominate minds, Presence inspires awe, Fortitude resists damage
*   Toreador: Presence, Celerity (super speed), Auspex (heightened senses)
*   Tremere: Blood magic (Thaumaturgy)
*   Powers grow with age and experience
*   Using obvious powers in public breaks Masquerade

### 6. **Vampire Vulnerabilities**
*   Sunlight causes instant final death (no save, no recovery)
*   Fire causes aggravated damage (difficult to heal)
*   Stake through heart paralyzes (not kills, but helpless)
*   Must rest in haven during day
*   Invitation required to enter mortal homes
*   Running water is painful
