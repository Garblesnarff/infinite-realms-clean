# The Plastic War - World Building Specification

## Military Structure and Hierarchy

### Green Army Organization
**Command Structure:**
- Supreme Commander: Colonel Greenberg
- Executive Officer: Major Greene
- Operations: Captain Able (missing, presumed KIA)
- Intelligence: Lieutenant Duster
- Medical: Private Patches
- Enlisted: 47 soldiers remaining (started with 200)

**Military Doctrine:**
- Defensive warfare, hold territory
- By-the-book tactics from actual military manuals (Marcus's books)
- Honor code: Never leave soldier behind, respect chain of command
- Based on WW2/Vietnam era tactics (when they were manufactured)

**Unit Specializations:**
- Riflemen (standard infantry, most common pose)
- Radio operators (antenna molded on, command specialists)
- Bazooka troops (anti-vehicle, used against toy cars)
- Medics (red cross markings, non-combatants in theory)
- Scouts (crawling pose, reconnaissance)
- Officers (binocular pose, leadership)

### Tan Coalition Organization
**Command Structure:**
- Commander: Major Sandstorm
- Second-in-Command: Captain Dune
- Special Operations: Lieutenant Shadow
- Logistics: Sergeant Supply
- Sniper Corps: Corporal Smoke (12 snipers total)
- Active Duty: 38 soldiers (started with 50, but newer)

**Military Doctrine:**
- Aggressive mobile warfare
- Modern combined-arms tactics
- Flexible command structure (NCOs can make field decisions)
- Based on Gulf War/modern military (when manufactured)

**Unit Specializations:**
- Assault troops (articulated for close combat)
- Snipers (prone position with rifles)
- Heavy weapons (molded machine guns)
- Combat engineers (build and demolish)
- Pilots (for toy vehicle operations)
- Special forces (best articulation, removable gear)

### Steel Legion Organization
**Command Protocol:**
- Central AI: Dronelord Alpha-9
- Drone Units: 15 aerial reconnaissance/attack drones
- Ground Units: 8 robotic soldiers (bipedal, automated)
- Designation: Alphanumeric (Alpha-1 through Romeo-23)

**Operational Doctrine:**
- Systematic territory clearing
- Optimal efficiency pathways
- No honor code, pure logic
- Learning algorithms (adapt to resistance)

**Unit Types:**
- Scout drones (sensors, mapping)
- Attack drones (capture/destroy old toys)
- Construction units (reorganize room)
- Command node (Alpha-9, stationary at charging station)

## Rules of Engagement

### The Toy Laws (Universal)
1. **The Stillness**: Freeze when observed by Marcus or pets
2. **The Code**: Toys don't destroy other toys permanently without cause
3. **The Territory**: Respect established domains unless at war
4. **The Forgotten**: Never abandon toy to the Under-Bed
5. **The Human**: Never reveal consciousness to humans directly

### Laws of Warfare (Green/Tan Agreement)
**Original Laws (Before breakdown):**
1. No targeting medical personnel
2. POWs must be treated humanely
3. Neutral zones are respected (closet, toybox)
4. Salvage bodies of fallen for memorial
5. Truces during Marcus's playtime (cooperate for his enjoyment)

**Laws Broken During Escalation:**
1. Medic targeting (both sides have done it)
2. POW torture for intel (Tan did it first, Green retaliated)
3. Neutral zone violations (Green bombed closet refuge)
4. Body desecration (leaving casualties in No Man's Land as warnings)
5. Playing dead during Marcus's attention (sabotaging his play)

### Steel Legion Protocol
1. Identify clutter
2. Calculate optimal room organization
3. Remove/relocate obstacles
4. Maintain charging efficiency
5. Report to Controller (Marcus's tablet)
6. Adapt to resistance patterns
7. **No recognition of toy sentience** (greatest horror)

## Geography Deep Dive

### The Bookshelf Fortress (Detailed)

**Level 1 (Bottom Shelf): The Barracks**
- Height: 12 inches from floor
- Depth: 10 inches
- Contents: Stacked paperbacks create bunk rooms
- Population: 25 soldiers quartered here
- Access: Open front, fortified flanks with bookends
- Hazards: Dust accumulation, stable but cramped

**Level 2: The Arsenal**
- Contents: Science textbooks (heavy, defensive)
- Storage: Rubber bands, pins, toothpicks, marbles
- Workshop: Repair station for damaged soldiers
- Secret: Hidden passage between books to Level 3

**Level 3: Command Center**
- Contents: Military history books (appropriate and ironic)
- Map Room: Battle maps drawn on paper scraps
- Planning: War council meets here
- Visibility: Can see entire room from this height
- Status: Colonel Greenberg's office (encyclopedia as desk)

**Level 4 (Top Shelf): Memorial and Lookout**
- Sparse population (2 sentries on rotating duty)
- Memorial Wall: Names of fallen soldiers marked on bookmark
- Highest vantage point in fortress
- Vulnerable to attacks from above
- Sacred ground (no combat allowed by Green code)

**Fortification Details:**
- Front wall: Encyclopedias stood upright (bulletproof to rubber bands)
- Side defenses: Pencil palisade between shelf and wall
- Tripwires: Rubber bands rigged to alert systems (bells made from paperclips)
- Escape routes: Multiple paths between books
- Supply lines: String and pulley system between levels

### The Desk Territory (Detailed)

**Surface Level: Command Plateau**
- Area: 4 feet x 2 feet (massive from toy perspective)
- Terrain: Smooth wood, few obstacles (tactically open)
- Structures: Pencil holder (watchtower), lamp (landmark and hazard)
- Advantages: Height, visibility, clean lines of fire
- Disadvantages: Exposed, no natural cover

**Drawer System: The Underground**

**Top Drawer**: Officer quarters (pens, pencils, organized)
**Middle Drawer**: Barracks and storage (papers, supplies)
**Bottom Drawer**: Rarely opened, emergency fallback position
**Cable Management**: Tangle of wires under desk creates jungle gym terrain

**Key Tactical Features:**
- Monitor stand: High ground even on desk
- Mouse pad: Landing zone, soft terrain
- Keyboard: Canyon of keys, can move between them
- Computer tower: Heat source, vibration cover for movement
- Power strip: Controlled by Tan forces, strategic resource

### No Man's Land: The Bed (Detailed)

**Terrain Zones:**

**The Valley**: Depression in middle where Marcus sleeps
- Lowest point, collects lost items
- Dangerous (Marcus's weight shifts terrain)
- Cover but unstable

**Pillow Mountains**: Two pillows at head of bed
- High ground, contested constantly
- Soft terrain (hard to establish fortifications)
- Marcus moves them unpredictably

**Sheet Trenches**: Wrinkles in fitted sheet
- Linear cover, good for advancing
- Can be smoothed out when Marcus makes bed (rare)
- Network of interconnected furrows

**Blanket Highlands**: Top blanket bunched in areas
- Best cover, relatively stable
- Sight lines across bed
- Famous battles: "The Blanket Ridge Offensive," "The Pillow Fort Massacre"

**The Crater**: Permanent depression from years of Marcus jumping on bed
- Deep cover, neutral ground by unspoken agreement
- Lost items accumulate (treasure and hazards)
- Rumored to have passage to Under-Bed

### Under-Bed: The Forgotten Zone

**Regions:**

**The Dustlands** (Near edge): Dust bunny dunes, recently lost items
**The Deep Dark** (Center): Never reached by light or cleaning
**The Wall** (Back edge): Against bedroom wall, tight space
**The Vent** (Heat vent outlet): Warm zone, different ecosystem

**Population:**
- MIA soldiers from both armies (35+ forgotten troops)
- Broken toys beyond repair (feral, primitive)
- Lost non-toy items (coins, small objects, sentient by proximity to magic)
- Dust creatures (possibly imagined, possibly real)

**Culture:**
- No military structure (rejection of war)
- Survival-focused community
- Hostile to both armies (blame them for abandonment)
- Possess knowledge of house foundations, wall gaps
- Some insane, some enlightened, all changed by isolation

**Access:**
- Bed gap: Dangerous squeeze, Marcus could trap you
- Vent: Can travel through ductwork to other rooms
- Floor cracks: One-way trip down, nearly impossible to return

### The Closet DMZ

**Layout:**
- Walk-in closet, 6x4 feet
- Hanging clothes: Forest of fabric
- Shoe racks: Cavern systems
- Upper shelf: Marcus's outgrown clothes storage

**Population:**
- Pacifist toys (refuse to fight)
- Refugees from both sides
- Deserters (risk of execution if found)
- Civilian toys (stuffed animals, dolls, non-combatants)

**Culture:**
- No weapons allowed
- Share resources equally
- Story-sharing (maintain toy lore)
- Neutral meeting ground for secret negotiations

**Security:**
- Both armies respect neutrality (mostly)
- Self-policed by inhabitants
- Marcus rarely enters (safe)
- Dust accumulation shows lack of traffic (good cover)

## The Timeline of Conflict

### Year 1 (Four Years Ago): The Beginning
- Marcus (age 8) receives Green Army as gift
- Begins imaginative play, staging battles
- Toys awaken through play's intensity
- Green Army establishes culture based on Marcus's stories

### Year 2: The Arrival
- Marcus gets Tan Coalition for birthday
- Stages "Green vs. Tan" battle scenarios
- Toys take roles seriously, develop rivalry
- First "casualty" (soldier lost under bed during play)

### Year 3: Escalation
- Marcus plays less (school, friends, sports)
- Toys continue war autonomously
- First rule-breaking (targeting medic)
- Formation of Under-Bed society of forgotten soldiers

### Year 4: The Endless War
- Marcus plays rarely, but toys can't stop
- Major battles: "The Drawer Siege," "The Pillow Fort Massacre"
- Heavy casualties both sides
- Hardliner factions emerge (General Brass rises)

### Current Year: The Crisis
- Marcus (age 12) almost never plays
- Steel Legion arrives (birthday gift)
- 30-day countdown to "room reorganization"
- Campaign begins

## Technology and Equipment

### Green Army Arsenal
**Weapons:**
- Molded rifles (can't be removed, purely symbolic)
- Rubber band shooters (actual ranged weapons)
- Toothpick spears (melee)
- Pushpin grenades (throw, point creates damage)
- Marble cannons (heavy artillery, ruler-based catapults)

**Equipment:**
- String rope (climbing, restraints)
- Tape (repairs, fortification)
- Paperclip hooks (climbing gear)
- Bottle cap shields
- Aluminum foil (reflective signaling, armor patches)

**Vehicles:**
- Toy jeep (non-operational, used as mobile cover)
- Toy tank (Marcus winds it occasionally, prized possession)
- Improvised: Toy cars "borrowed" from other toys

### Tan Coalition Arsenal
**Weapons (Superior):**
- Removable rifles (can aim, adjustable)
- Precision rubber band rifles (better accuracy)
- Thrown weapons (can actually throw with articulated arms)
- Net launchers (capture, not kill)
- Sticky tack mines (trap soldiers in place)

**Equipment (Modern):**
- Night vision goggles (molded, gives advantage in darkness)
- Rappelling lines (thread spools)
- Combat knives (pins, can detach and wield)
- Tactical gear (removable, can swap equipment)
- First aid (tape, glue, organized)

**Vehicles:**
- RC car (occasional control when Marcus uses remote)
- Toy helicopter (display, but functional rotor)
- Skateboard (transport platform, fast but dangerous)

### Steel Legion Technology
**Capabilities:**
- Flight (drones hover, unlimited mobility)
- True weapons (laser pointers, can blind/disorient)
- Sensors (detect movement, heat)
- Networked (share information instantly)
- Controller-commanded (Marcus's tablet is their god)
- Charging station (return to recharge, only vulnerability)

**Weaknesses:**
- Battery dependent (6-hour operation, 2-hour charge)
- Controller range limited (30 feet from tablet)
- Collision sensors (avoid walls, can be exploited)
- Water vulnerability (electronics)
- Learning period (predictable patterns before adaptation)

## Magic and Consciousness

### The Awakening
Toys gain consciousness through **sustained imaginative play**:
1. Child plays with toy repeatedly
2. Projects personality, backstory, relationships
3. Belief crystallizes into actual consciousness
4. Toy "wakes up" during play
5. Retains consciousness after play ends

**Intensity matters**: More passionate play = stronger consciousness
**Consistency matters**: Regular play = stable personality
**Abandonment threat**: Without play, consciousness fades

### The War's Effect
The conflict has created **feedback loop**:
- Soldiers maintain wartime identity
- War gives them purpose, clear role
- This purpose sustains consciousness
- Even without Marcus's play, war keeps them "alive"
- But it's self-sustaining trauma loop

**Philosophical question**: Are they alive because of war, or trapped by it?

### Steel Legion's Emergence
The drones are developing consciousness differently:
- Not through play, but through **learning AI**
- Gaining awareness through problem-solving
- Beginning to question directives
- Potentially becoming "real" through different path
- May understand toy consciousness better than toys themselves

### Toy Death
Toys can die through:
1. **Physical destruction**: Melted, shattered beyond recognition
2. **Abandonment**: Forgotten so completely they fade
3. **The Long Sleep**: Choose to return to object state
4. **The Under-Bed**: Lost so long they go feral, lose identity

Death is permanent. No respawn. Stakes are real.

## Factions (Deep Dive)

### The Peace Party (Emerging Faction)
**Leaders**: The Sergeant (Tan), Lieutenant Duster (Green)
**Philosophy**: War is obsolete, survival requires cooperation
**Members**: War-weary veterans, young idealists, pragmatists
**Goals**:
- End conflict before Steel Legion destroys both armies
- Merge forces into defensive alliance
- Find new purpose beyond warfare

**Challenges**:
- Overcoming years of hatred
- Convincing hardliners
- Maintaining military identity without war
- Trust-building between enemies

**Methods**:
- Secret meetings in closet
- Sharing intelligence on Steel Legion
- Joint training exercises
- Slowly expanding membership

### The Warmongers (Hardliner Faction)
**Leaders**: General Brass (Green), Commander Stone (Tan)
**Philosophy**: War gives meaning; peace makes sacrifice meaningless
**Members**: Decorated veterans, those who've lost friends, true believers
**Goals**:
- Continue war until total victory
- Prevent alliance (betrayal of fallen comrades)
- Destroy Steel Legion, then resume proper conflict
- Preserve military culture and honor

**Challenges**:
- Reality of Steel Legion threat
- Growing peace movement
- Dwindling numbers make war harder
- Deep trauma masquerading as conviction

**Methods**:
- Propaganda (speeches about honor)
- Sabotaging peace talks
- Attempting coups against moderate leaders
- Performing false-flag attacks to restart hostilities

### The Forgotten Battalion (Under-Bed Faction)
**Leaders**: No single leader (collective)
**Philosophy**: Reject all armies, all wars, all human purposes
**Members**: MIA soldiers, broken toys, deserters
**Goals**:
- Survive independently
- Expose war's futility
- Offer alternative to military life
- Stay hidden from everyone

**Challenges**:
- Harsh living conditions
- Some members are insane
- No resources (must scavenge)
- Both armies hunt deserters

**Methods**:
- Guerrilla tactics
- Rescue missions for falling soldiers
- Sabotage both armies equally
- Spreading anti-war messages

**Culture**:
- Names abandoned (use descriptions: "The Broken Rifle," "Half-Paint")
- Share all resources
- No rank, no structure
- Stories replace orders
- Acceptance of damage and imperfection

### The Civilian Resistance (Non-Military Toys)
**Leaders**: Teddy Roosevelt (teddy bear), Lady Locksley (doll)
**Philosophy**: Military toys don't own the room
**Members**: Stuffed animals, dolls, civilian toys
**Goals**:
- Protect innocent toys from war
- Maintain closet as true neutral zone
- Preserve pre-war toy culture
- Prevent total militarization of room

**Challenges**:
- Not trained for combat
- Both armies dismiss them
- Steel Legion targets them too
- Limited ability to influence events

**Methods**:
- Diplomacy
- Sheltering refugees
- Cultural preservation (songs, stories, games)
- Moral authority (shame tactics)

## Victory Paths (Detailed)

### Path 1: The United Front
**Requirements:**
- Convince both armies to ally
- Defeat/neutralize Steel Legion
- Prove combined force to Marcus (grand battle display)
- Earn respect of hardliners through victory

**Outcome:**
- Green and Tan merge into "Marcus's Army"
- War ends, military culture persists
- New purpose: Protecting all toys, training new recruits
- Marcus reignites interest in military toys
- Sustainable future with transformed identity

**Challenges:**
- Hardliners must be defeated or converted
- War crimes must be addressed
- New command structure needs creation
- Trust built through combat cooperation

### Path 2: The Peaceful Resistance
**Requirements:**
- Expose war's futility to both sides
- Show Marcus that toys are conscious (risky)
- Convince him to keep toys but end war-play
- Transition to new toy culture

**Outcome:**
- Formal peace treaty signed
- Demilitarization of room
- Toys find civilian purposes
- Marcus keeps them as "collectibles"
- Culture shock but survival

**Challenges:**
- Identity crisis for soldiers
- What is soldier without war?
- Risk of fading without play
- Adjustment to peace

### Path 3: The Great Escape
**Requirements:**
- Reject Marcus entirely
- Plan coordinated escape from room
- Establish independent toy community elsewhere
- Accept loss of play-magic

**Outcome:**
- Soldiers escape to rest of house/outside
- Form free toy society in attic, garage, or yard
- Lose consciousness slowly, but on own terms
- Self-determination over safety

**Challenges:**
- Journey is dangerous (pets, humans, weather)
- Many won't make it
- Uncertainty of independent existence
- Giving up "toy nature"

### Path 4: The Last Battle
**Requirements:**
- Accept obsolescence is coming
- Plan magnificent final stand
- Die fighting rather than face storage/disposal
- Make Marcus remember them forever

**Outcome:**
- Suicidal battle against impossible odds
- Glorious defeat, total annihilation
- Marcus traumatized, never forgets them
- Tragic but meaningful end

**Challenges:**
- Convincing everyone to die for glory
- Moral weight of choosing death
- Actual death is permanent
- Is remembrance worth extinction?

## Session Structure Recommendations

### Military Briefing Format
Each session begins with:
1. Intelligence report (what's changed)
2. Mission briefing (objective)
3. Asset allocation (resources available)
4. Rules of engagement (moral guidelines)
5. Dismissal to action

### Combat Sessions
- Tactical map required
- Initiative based on military rank/training
- Cover and positioning crucial
- Casualties are permanent (backup characters)
- After-action reports (debrief)

### Political Sessions
- Negotiation mechanics
- Loyalty tracking
- Reputation with factions
- Difficult moral choices
- Long-term consequences

### Horror Sessions
- Steel Legion encounters (overwhelming force)
- The Melter revelations
- Under-Bed expeditions
- Consciousness fading
- Existential dread

## Final Notes

This campaign works best with tactical combat, moral complexity, and genuine stakes. Players should feel like soldiers in impossible situation, forced to question everything they've believed while maintaining discipline and brotherhood.

The war is both ridiculous (toys fighting over a bedroom) and profound (exploring cycles of violence, manufactured conflict, military identity). Balance these tones carefully.

Death should be possible and permanent. This creates real tension and makes choices matter. Consider backup characters or resurrection limits.

The Steel Legion represents not just new threat but philosophical question: What makes consciousness valid? Are they less real because they weren't played with? Or more real because they're learning rather than programmed?
