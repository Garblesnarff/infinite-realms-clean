# The Phantom Gauntlet - World-Building Specification

## The Phantom World

**Physical Properties:**
- Empty copy of real city/world
- Infrastructure functions (power, water)
- No wildlife, no people (except players)
- Weather occurs
- Time passes normally (subjectively)
- Cannot leave city boundaries (force fields)
- Buildings accessible, resources available
- Occasional supernatural phenomena

**The Transported:**
- Specific individuals pulled from real world
- Selection criteria unknown initially
- Mix of ages, backgrounds, skills
- All "died" or nearly died in real world? (Maybe)
- Number varies (hundreds in city)
- New players arrive periodically
- No children (ethical game master rule?)

## Game Mechanics Deep Dive

**Game Generation:**
- Arenas appear with cards
- Cards found randomly or delivered
- Must be nearby to be forced in
- Can't run from activated game
- Multiple games occur simultaneously
- Difficulty scaling over time
- Face cards are milestones

**Visa Economy:**
- Clear 2 of X: 2 days added
- Clear 10 of X: 10 days added
- Face cards: Variable rewards
- Can't trade or steal visas
- Expiration is instant death
- Creates constant urgency
- Players must keep competing

**Game Types by Suit:**

**Diamonds - Logic/Puzzles:**
- Escape rooms with death traps
- Mathematical challenges
- Pattern recognition
- Code breaking
- Memory tests
- Physics puzzles
- Time pressure common

**Clubs - Physical/Team:**
- Sports competitions (deadly)
- Coordinated challenges
- Strength/endurance tests
- Team trust exercises
- Group survival scenarios
- Communication crucial

**Hearts - Psychology/Betrayal:**
- Prisoner's dilemmas
- Social deduction
- Trolley problems
- Sacrifice choices
- Trust exercises
- Moral no-win scenarios
- Emotional torture

**Spades - Combat/Death:**
- Direct fighting
- Survival horror
- Hide and seek with death
- Strategic warfare
- Resource management
- Pure violence
- Kill or be killed

**Face Card Special Rules:**
- Multi-stage games
- Longer duration (hours/days)
- Higher casualties
- More complex rules
- Greater rewards
- Must clear all to win citizenship
- Each suit's face cards different

## The Truth Options (DM Chooses)

**Option 1 - Alien Experiment:**
- Advanced aliens testing humanity
- Humans pulled from near-death moments
- Proving worthiness for contact
- Winners represent humanity
- Failures recycled or returned
- Cold scientific observation

**Option 2 - Dimensional Bleed:**
- Reality fracture created phantom world
- Dead (or nearly) shunted here
- Must prove alive-ness through will to survive
- Winning means return to life
- Losing means true death
- Cosmic accident, no malice

**Option 3 - Collective Unconscious:**
- Shared dreamspace of humanity
- Trapped by unresolved trauma
- Games are inner trials
- Winning is self-actualization
- Psychological growth required
- Very trippy, very philosophical

**Option 4 - Cosmic Game:**
- Incomprehensible entities play with realities
- Mortals are game pieces
- No deeper meaning, just entertainment
- Nihilistic but honest
- Can negotiate with entities
- Might earn role as game master

**Option 5 - Purgatory Trial:**
- Actual afterlife processing
- Games determine destination
- Heaven/hell based on choices
- Religious/spiritual framework
- Redemption possible
- Deep moral implications

## The Beach Society

**Structure:**
- Hatter (charismatic leader)
- Executives (lieutenants)
- Militants (enforcers, led by Niragi)
- Regular members
- Hierarchy based on cards collected
- False utopia of safety

**The Lie:**
- "Collect all cards here, we all escape together"
- Actually impossible or false goal
- Leaders know but lie for control
- Eventually violent breakdown
- Players must navigate or flee

**Rules:**
- Swimsuit dress code (prevent weapon hiding)
- All cards collected shared (stolen from individuals)
- Mandatory participation in group games
- No privacy, cult-like atmosphere
- Leaving considered betrayal

## Previous Winners

**The Citizens (if that truth chosen):**
- Previous gauntlet completers
- Chose to stay (or couldn't leave)
- Now help run games
- Or live as permanent residents
- Some guilt, some acceptance
- Can be allies or obstacles

**Returned (if escape possible):**
- Winners back in real world
- Memories may fade or persist
- PTSD and survivor guilt
- Some become recruiters or helpers
- Others try to forget

## Resources and Survival

**Scavenging:**
- Grocery stores (expired but edible)
- Pharmacies (useful medical supplies)
- Homes (personal effects, weapons)
- Vehicles (don't work to leave, work in city)
- Weapons caches (for spade games)
- Communication devices (don't reach outside)

**Safe Zones:**
- Buildings between games
- Must find and secure
- Resources respawn periodically
- No permanent safety (games can appear anywhere)
- Social dynamics in safe houses

## End Game Scenarios

**All Face Cards Cleared:**
- Granted "citizenship" or "freedom"
- Depending on truth, means different things
- Return home or stay forever
- Memory manipulation possible
- Changed by experience
- What did it cost?

**Revolution Route:**
- Discover and confront game masters
- Destroy system for everyone
- Might trap all or free all
- High stakes gamble
- No guarantee of success

**Acceptance Route:**
- Some players choose to stay
- Become citizens or new game masters
- Perpetuate cycle
- Dark but stable choice

This world provides endless variety of challenges (DM can create any game), deep mystery with flexible truths, and psychological horror alongside action and puzzles. Perfect for diverse player skill sets.
