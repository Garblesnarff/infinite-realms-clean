### **Template: World-Building Specification Brief**

This document is the primary instruction set for the world-building AI pipeline. Each section provides explicit directives for specialized agents to generate the necessary assets for a complete and dynamic campaign world.

**The Unexpected Guardian**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise. The tone should be gentle, heartwarming, and realistic about parenting, in the style of Usagi Drop. Focus on domestic life, emotional growth, and the formation of found family through daily care.
*   **Content:** The players suddenly become responsible for raising a young child. Through daily parenting challenges and small victories, they form genuine family bonds. The campaign is about how love grows from responsibility and presence, and how caring for someone changes you profoundly.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Directive:** The Lore Generation Agent must create detailed entries for each of the following prompts. Prioritize emotional wisdom about family, realistic parenting knowledge, and the magic of everyday domestic life.
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the philosophy of "found family" in the realm – that family is built through choice, care, and commitment, not just blood.
    *   Write about the community support systems for guardians and parents – how neighbors, friends, and institutions help raise children.
    *   Describe the coming-of-age traditions and milestones children experience, and how guardians navigate these moments.
    *   Explain the legal and social frameworks for adoption and guardianship in this world, with all their emotional and practical complexities.
    *   Detail the story of a legendary guardian who took in many children and how their commitment transformed a whole community.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Directive:** The Faction Generation Agent must create a detailed profile for each faction listed below. Each profile must contain the specified fields. "Faction" is gentle here – these are support systems.
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The New Family (Players and Child)** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** To build genuine family bonds, provide the child with safety and love, grow as individuals and as family.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Players are guardians/parents (learning as they go), child is dependent but gradually trusting.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To create a stable, loving home and navigate daily parenting challenges.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Players are discovering that parenting is changing them – making them more patient, responsible, and capable of profound love; child is testing whether this home is real and permanent.
        *   **Assets:** Growing love, commitment to try, support from community, learning from mistakes, daily presence.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially awkward and uncertain; gradually becoming naturally affectionate; growing into authentic family through shared life.
    *   **The Support Network (Friends and Community)** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** To help the new family succeed, provide advice and practical help, ensure the child thrives.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Informal network of friends with children, neighbors, teachers, shopkeepers, and community members.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To support all families in the community, especially new or struggling ones.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Many see themselves in the guardians' struggle; helping others helps heal their own parenting regrets or validates their choices; community bonds strengthen through mutual care.
        *   **Assets:** Parenting experience, practical advice, emotional support, childcare help, judgment-free encouragement.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially offering cautious help; gradually becoming like extended family; invested in the family's success.
    *   **The School Community** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** To educate children, support their development, partner with parents/guardians.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Teachers, administrators, other parents, structured by professional roles and parent associations.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To provide quality education and safe environment for all students.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Teachers and staff care deeply about students beyond curriculum; they watch for children who need extra support; they judge guardians but root for those trying hard.
        *   **Assets:** Educational expertise, structured environment, social opportunities for child, observation of child's growth.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially assessing the new guardians; gradually partnering with them; supporting the child's integration and happiness.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Directive:** The Character Generation Agent must create full profiles for all NPCs listed. Tier 1 NPCs are unique individuals. Tier 2 & 3 are archetypes to be instantiated multiple times with unique details as needed by the simulation.
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **Rin Rabbit-child:** The young child whose trust and love must be earned through consistent care and presence.
    *   **Kouki Childhood-Friend:** Friend with parenting experience offering advice and support without judgment.
    *   **Granny Yukari Nextdoor:** Elderly neighbor becoming surrogate grandmother, offering wisdom and help.
    *   **Teacher Reina Classroom:** Rin's teacher observing the family situation and supporting the child's needs.
*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Archetypes for Generation):**
    *   "Experienced Parent Offering Encouragement"
    *   "Fellow Guardian Learning Together"
    *   "Skeptical Community Member Who Gradually Approves"
    *   "Child's Classmate and Friend"
    *   "Professional (Doctor, Social Worker) Assessing Situation"
    *   "Person from Child's Past"

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Directive:** The Environment Generation Agent must create a detailed blueprint for each location below, populating all specified sub-sections. Emphasize domestic intimacy and how spaces transform to reflect growing family bonds.
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **The Home:** Residence transforming from individual space to family home.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** Child's room (gradually personalizing), shared kitchen (becoming heart of home), living room (filling with toys and family activities), photos appearing on walls, entrance where child's shoes appear daily.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** The guardian(s) and child, creating routines and shared life.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Safety, comfort, belonging, shared meals, bedtime rituals, morning routines, family bonding space.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Child has nightmare and seeks comfort, spontaneous cooking together, morning routine runs smoothly showing progress, family movie night bonds them, quiet moment reveals how far they've come.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Homes remember the families that live in them – the walls hold love." "The kitchen table is where families really come together." "A child's drawings on the fridge are proof of belonging."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Gradually accumulating evidence of child – drawings, toys, photos, small shoes), Sound (Morning routines, cooking together, child's laughter, bedtime stories, comfortable domestic sounds), Smell (Home cooking, child's shampoo, lived-in warmth).
    *   **The Elementary School:** Where child spends days and guardians experience parent responsibilities.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** Child's classroom, school entrance where pickup/dropoff happens, playground, teacher's office, bulletin board with school events, parent meeting room.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Teachers, students, other parents, school staff.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Education, social development, structure, school events, parent-teacher conferences, school community.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Teacher requests conference about child's adjustment, child makes first friend, school event requires parent participation, guardian meets other parents, child brings home artwork proudly.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Teachers can tell which children are loved and which are just cared for." "The pickup line is where parent friendships form." "School events show how much guardians are willing to show up."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Children's artwork, busy hallways, classroom order, parent pickup line, seasonal decorations), Sound (Children's voices, school bells, teacher instructions, parent conversations), Smell (Crayons, lunch from cafeteria, cleaning supplies, children's energy).
    *   **The Neighborhood Park:** Regular destination becoming special family place.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The favorite swingset, the playground child prefers, benches where guardian watches, the path they walk together, seasonal changes visible.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Families with children, elderly people walking, occasional other guardians who become friends.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Play space, exercise, social interaction, regular routine, special place for bonding.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Child makes park friend, guardian bonds with another parent, first successful bike ride without training wheels, seasonal celebration in park, quiet moment of contentment watching child play.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Every family has their favorite park spot that becomes 'theirs.'" "The park is where children learn about the world beyond home." "Guardians' commitment shows in regular park visits regardless of weather."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Playing children, seasonal nature changes, families together, swings in motion), Sound (Children's laughter and play, parent conversations, seasonal sounds), Smell (Fresh air, seasonal scents, sometimes snacks being shared).

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Directive:** The World Simulation Agent must implement the following trigger-based state changes. For each "IF" condition, the agent must pre-generate the narrative and emotional consequences for the "THEN" outcome, prioritizing authentic family bonding and emotional truth.
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** Players engage in daily care activities (meals, bedtime, school routines) with genuine presence.
    *   **THEN:** Increase Family Bonding track. Award Parenting Points. Describe small moments of connection. Child's trust grows incrementally. Guardians feel increasing competence and love.
    *   **IF:** Players face parenting challenge (sick child, behavior issue, school problem) and handle with care.
    *   **THEN:** Increase Bonding significantly. Generate heartwarming scene of guardian providing comfort/guidance. Child learns they can depend on guardian. Players feel profound love mixed with protectiveness.
    *   **IF:** Players spend Parenting Points to trigger bonding scene.
    *   **THEN:** Generate emotionally resonant moment – breakthrough in trust, vulnerable conversation, spontaneous affection, realization of how much they mean to each other. Describe with tenderness and detail.
    *   **IF:** Players consistently show up for child (keeping promises, attending events, being present).
    *   **THEN:** Child's behavior improves. Trust solidifies. Community approves. Child begins calling guardians by family terms naturally. Generate scene showing this progression.
    *   **IF:** Challenge to family bond occurs (external threat, person from past, child's fear of abandonment).
    *   **THEN:** Generate crisis that tests commitment. Players must prove their love and permanence. Success strengthens family; even struggle handled well deepens bond.
    *   **IF:** Family Bonding reaches high levels.
    *   **THEN:** Generate scenes showing natural family intimacy – child seeks comfort automatically, guardians respond instinctively, they function as family without thinking about it. Culminate in formal/informal recognition of permanent family status.
    *   **IF:** Campaign reaches conclusion.
    *   **THEN:** Generate emotionally satisfying ending showing they are truly family now. Flash-forward showing continued life together. Emphasize that the guardians have been changed forever by love, and it's the best thing that ever happened to them. The child is home.
