# The Last Exodus - World-Building Specification

## Detailed Faction Information

### The Twelve Colonies (Pre-Fall)
- Caprica: Political/cultural center, wealthiest
- Gemenon: Religious fundamentalist
- Tauron: Organized crime, working class
- Picon: Fleet headquarters, military center
- Leonis: Entertainment industry
- Virgon: Aristocratic, refined
- Scorpia: Mining, industrial
- Sagittaron: Poor, rebellious, distrustful of government
- Aerilon: Agricultural breadbasket
- Aquaria: Isolated, water world
- Canceron: Resort world
- Libris: (Details vary)

### Colonial Government Structure
- **Quorum of Twelve**: Representative body, one delegate per colony
- **Presidency**: Civilian leadership (Roslin assumes after succession)
- **Colonial Fleet**: Military command (Admiral Adama)
- **Civil-Military Tension**: Constant throughout campaign

### The Cylons/Synthetics

**Original Cylons**: Created as workers, rebelled 40 years ago, armistice
**Evolution**: Developed humaniform models, infiltrated colonies
**The Thirteen Models**:
- Eight known: Number Six, Eight (Sharon), etc.
- Five hidden (Final Five, created Cylons)
**One God Belief**: Monotheistic vs. Colonial polytheism
**The Plan**: Exterminate humanity, but internal divisions exist
**Resurrection**: Copy consciousness to new body upon death
**Basestar**: Organic hybrid ships, no crew needed

### The Final Five
- Created humaniform Cylons in exchange for ending first war
- Infiltrated as: Tigh, Tyrol, Tory, Anders, Ellen
- Don't know they're Cylons until activation
- Memories suppressed, programming deep
- Key to understanding and potentially peace

### The Humaniform Models
1. **Cavil (One)**: Hates humanity and parents (Final Five)
2. **Leoben (Two)**: Philosophical, religious
3. **D'Anna (Three)**: Journalist model, seeking Final Five
4. **Simon (Four)**: Doctor model
5. **Doral (Five)**: Public relations
6. **Six**: Seductive, conflicted about humanity
7. **Daniel (Seven)**: Artist, destroyed by Cavil (jealousy)
8. **Sharon "Eight"**: Soldier model, multiple copies with different loyalties
9-12. Unknown (Final Five)
13. Unknown/doesn't exist?

## Detailed Technology

### FTL (Faster Than Light) Travel
- Jump drives fold space
- Require complex calculations by Navigator (human psychic)
- Limited range, chained jumps for distance
- Fleet must jump together (coordination critical)
- Cooldown between jumps

### Viper Fighters
- Mark II: Older, analog, immune to Cylon hacking
- Mark VII: Advanced, digital, vulnerable to infiltration
- Launch tubes from battlestars
- Pilots are elite, heroic culture

### Battlestars
- Prometheus: Older model, analog systems survived surprise
- Pegasus: Advanced, found later, Admiral Cain commands
- Galactica in original: Museum piece, saved fleet
- Massive carriers, thousands of crew
- Last line of defense

### Weapons Technology
- Kinetic weapons, missiles
- Nuclear weapons (limited)
- No energy shields
- Armor and point defense
- Electronic warfare (jamming, hacking)

### Life Support and Resources
- Water recycling (crisis episode)
- Food production (hydroponics, algae)
- Fuel (tylium, refined from asteroids/planets)
- Medical supplies (constantly rationed)
- Population tracking (each death matters)

## Historical Context

### First Cylon War (40 Years Before)
- Cylons created as labor
- Gained sentience, demanded rights
- Humanity refused, war began
- Twelve Colonies unified against Cylon threat
- War lasted 12 years
- Armistice: Cylons left for unknown space
- Promised never to return (lie)

### The Armistice
- 40 years of no contact
- Armistice station established, no Cylons ever came
- Humanity became complacent
- Defense networks interconnected (vulnerability)
- Cylon infiltration gradual over years

### The Fall (Day Zero)
- Coordinated simultaneous attack all colonies
- Defense networks hacked, shut down
- Nuclear bombardment of cities
- Estimated 20 billion dead in hours
- Survivors scattered, most killed in following days
- Only 50,000 escape (from billions)

### The Exodus Begins
- Admiral Adama rallies civilian fleet
- President Roslin (43rd in succession) assumes office
- Journey to find Earth (Thirteenth Colony)
- Cylon pursuit, constant threat
- Internal conflicts, resource crises
- Discoveries and revelations along the way

## Cultural Details

### Colonial Religion
- Polytheistic (Lords of Kobol)
- Twelve gods corresponding to colonies
- Temples, priests, practices
- Faith important to many survivors
- vs. Cylon monotheism (one true God)

### Military Culture
- Hierarchy, discipline, honor
- Pilot culture: Call signs, traditions
- Respect for sacrifice
- Tension with civilians
- Code of military justice

### Civilian Life in Fleet
- Overcrowding on refugee ships
- Work assignments (labor shortage)
- Elections (democracy maintained)
- Black market (inevitable)
- Entertainment, culture, normality attempted
- Children (future of species)

### Social Tensions
- Class conflicts (wealthy vs. poor survivors)
- Colony prejudices (old feuds resurface)
- Civil vs. military authority
- Cylon sympathizers and paranoia
- Collaborators (on occupied New Caprica)

## Cylon Society and Culture

- Centurions: Worker/soldier robots
- Humaniform Cylons: Live in human-like spaces

### The Plan

- Internal debates: Is God's plan genocide or coexistence?
- Evolution beyond revenge
