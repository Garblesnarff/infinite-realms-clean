
# Curse of the Skinwalker: Campaign Bible

## [TAG: LORE_CORE]
### Origin Myth
The "Skinwalker" is not a creature, but a localized "reality-glitch," a sentient "narrative parasite" that feeds on identity. It is born when a community's sense of self becomes fracturedâ€”when people pretend to be something they're not, when secrets are kept, when trust erodes. The Skinwalker manifests as a perfect mimicry of a loved one, but subtly "wrong." It doesn't steal skin; it steals the *idea* of a person, leaving behind an empty shell. The "curse" is not on the Skinwalker, but on the community itself, which is slowly losing its ability to distinguish between truth and falsehood, between self and other. The original "Skinwalker" legends were not about witches turning into animals, but about the terrifying realization that the person you thought you knew was never truly there, replaced by a perfect, empty copy. It is the horror of the uncanny valley made manifest, a living lie that grows stronger with every act of deception.

### Timeline
1.  **The First Lie (Ancient History):** A small, isolated community, facing starvation, pretends to be a more prosperous tribe to gain aid. The collective deception creates the first, nascent "Skinwalker" energy.
2.  **The Age of Masks (c. 1500s):** European settlers arrive, bringing with them new forms of deception: false promises, hidden agendas, and the forced assimilation of indigenous cultures. The Skinwalker energy grows.
3.  **The First Mimicry (c. 1700s):** A colonist, desperate to escape a debt, fakes his own death and assumes a new identity. The Skinwalker energy coalesces, manifesting as a perfect, but subtly "wrong," copy of the colonist, who then takes his place.
4.  **The Legend Spreads (c. 1800s):** The "Skinwalker" legends begin to spread among both indigenous and settler communities, tales of shapeshifters and stolen identities.
5.  **(Surreal) The Day the Faces Shifted (c. 1900s):** For 24 hours, everyone in the affected community sees everyone else's face subtly shift and change, reflecting their hidden desires, fears, or true intentions. Many go mad.
6.  **The Great Impersonation (c. 1950s):** A major political figure in the community is replaced by a Skinwalker. The imposter is so perfect that no one notices, and the community's sense of self becomes even more fractured.
7.  **The Rise of the Face-Changers (c. 1980s):** A secret society discovers how to mimic the Skinwalker's abilities, using their powers to steal identities for profit or power.
8.  **(Surreal) The Echo of a Lie (c. 1990s):** A specific, powerful lie told by a community leader echoes through the very fabric of reality. For a week, every time someone speaks, their words are subtly twisted to reflect the original lie.
9.  **The Erosion of Trust (Present Day):** The Skinwalker's influence has grown. People are becoming increasingly paranoid, unable to trust their neighbors, their family, or even themselves. The community is on the verge of collapse.
10. **(Surreal) The Day the Mirrors Lied (1 week ago):** All mirrors in the affected community begin to show not your reflection, but the reflection of the person you *pretend* to be. The horror of self-deception becomes undeniable.

### The "Why"
The metaphysical conflict is a struggle against the erosion of identity and trust. The Skinwalker is not evil; it is a symptom. It forces people to confront the lies they tell themselves and each other. The players are caught in this cycle, and the horror comes from the realization that the monster they are hunting is a reflection of their own fractured community. The goal is not to "kill" the Skinwalker, which is impossible, but to heal the community's sense of self, to restore trust, or, perhaps, to find a way to accept the inherent ambiguity of identity without manifesting it into a monster. The ultimate horror is the realization that the Skinwalker is the truth you refuse to see.

## [TAG: FACTION_DATA]
### The Truth-Seekers
**Type:** Paranoid Investigators
**Leader:** Elder John, a man who has lost his entire family to the Skinwalker.
**Asset:** A deep understanding of the Skinwalker's patterns, and a network of informants who are also struggling with paranoia. They believe the only way to defeat the Skinwalker is to expose all lies.
**Rivals:** The Face-Changers Guild, The Silent Keepers.

### The Face-Changers Guild
**Type:** Identity Thieves / Con Artists
**Leader:** The Chameleon, a master of disguise who has no true face of their own.
**Asset:** The ability to perfectly mimic the Skinwalker's powers, stealing identities for profit or power. They believe identity itself is a performance, and they are the ultimate actors.
**Rivals:** The Truth-Seekers, The Silent Keepers.

### The Silent Keepers
**Type:** Secret-Hoarding Cultists
**Leader:** The Archivist of Whispers, a being who collects all the secrets of the community.
**Asset:** A vast network of informants who feed them secrets. They believe the Skinwalker is a sacred entity that protects the community's hidden truths, and they actively try to feed it.
**Rivals:** The Truth-Seekers.

### The Empty Shells
**Type:** Former Victims of the Skinwalker
**Leader:** None. They are a loose collection of individuals whose identities have been stolen.
**Asset:** They are immune to the Skinwalker's mimicry, as they have no identity left to steal. They wander the community, hollow and confused, a living warning.
**Rivals:** The Skinwalker (unintentionally).

### The Mirror-Makers
**Type:** Artists of Self-Reflection
**Leader:** The Reflector, an artist who creates mirrors that show not your reflection, but your deepest fears.
**Asset:** Magical mirrors that can force people to confront their inner demons. They believe the Skinwalker is a necessary evil, a tool for self-improvement.
**Rivals:** The Silent Keepers (who try to hide the truth).

### The Shifting Sands Tribe
**Type:** Nomadic Indigenous Community
**Leader:** Elder Wind-Talker, a shaman who can communicate with the spirits of the land.
**Asset:** Ancient knowledge of the land's spiritual landscape, and rituals to cleanse psychic wounds. They believe the Skinwalker is a corrupted spirit of the land.
**Rivals:** The Face-Changers Guild (who they see as defiling sacred traditions).

### The Reality-Glitchers
**Type:** Rogue Scientists / Chaos Theorists
**Leader:** Professor Anya Sharma, a former physicist who believes the Skinwalker is a portal to another dimension.
**Asset:** Improvised scientific equipment for detecting and manipulating reality-glitches. They are trying to "stabilize" the Skinwalker, hoping to use it as a gateway.
**Rivals:** The Shadow-Hunters (from the Jersey Devil campaign, who are now trying to understand this new phenomenon).

### The Uncanny Valley Collective
**Type:** AI Researchers / Body-Horror Enthusiasts
**Leader:** Dr. Elias Vance, a robotics engineer who is obsessed with creating perfect human replicas.
**Asset:** Advanced robotics and AI. They believe the Skinwalker is a natural evolution of mimicry, and they are trying to replicate its abilities in their androids.
**Rivals:** The Truth-Seekers.

### The Whispering Woods Cult
**Type:** Nature Mystics
**Leader:** The Green Man, a being who has merged with the forest.
**Asset:** A deep connection to the natural world, and the ability to communicate with animals. They believe the Skinwalker is a natural predator that keeps the human population in check.
**Rivals:** The Truth-Seekers.

### The Lost Souls of the Skinwalker
**Type:** Sentient Fragments of Stolen Identity
**Leader:** None. They are a collective of fragmented memories and personalities.
**Asset:** The ability to subtly influence human behavior, causing people to question their own identity. They are the "children" of the Skinwalker, and they are everywhere.
**Rivals:** The Silent Keepers (who try to suppress their existence).

## [TAG: LOCATIONS_MAIN]
### The Town of Liminality
A small, isolated town in the American Southwest, where the Skinwalker's influence is strongest.
*   **The Broken Mirror Tavern:** The local watering hole, where all the mirrors are cracked or subtly distorted. **Smell:** Stale beer, cigarette smoke, and the faint scent of deception. **Sound:** The murmur of conversations, the clinking of glasses, the occasional shout, and the subtle, unsettling sound of a mirror lying.
*   **The Church of the True Self:** A small, isolated church where the local preacher tries to preach about honesty and self-acceptance. **Smell:** Old wood, dust, and the faint scent of desperation. **Sound:** The preacher's strained voice, the rustle of nervous parishioners.
*   **The General Store:** The town's only general store, where the shopkeeper seems to subtly change his appearance every time you look away. **Smell:** Dust, old goods, and the faint scent of confusion. **Sound:** The creak of the floorboards, the rustle of bags, the subtle, unsettling sound of a person not being who they seem.
*   **The Town Library:** A small library where the books are constantly rearranging themselves, and the characters in the stories seem to subtly change their identities. **Smell:** Old paper, dust, and the faint scent of forgotten narratives. **Sound:** The rustle of turning pages, the whisper of changing stories.
*   **The Sheriff's Office:** The local law enforcement, trying to keep the peace in a town that is slowly losing its mind. **Smell:** Old coffee, stale donuts, and the faint scent of paranoia. **Sound:** The crackle of the radio, the tired sighs of the deputies.

### The Shifting Fields
A vast, open plain outside of town, where the landscape itself seems to subtly change, reflecting the community's fractured identity.
*   **The Uncanny Valley:** A section of the fields where the trees are almost perfectly human-shaped, but subtly "wrong." **Smell:** Pine needles, and the faint scent of unease. **Sound:** The whisper of the wind, which sounds like a human sigh.
*   **The River of Lies:** A river that flows through the fields. Its water is clear, but if you drink from it, you are compelled to tell a lie. **Smell:** Clean water, and the faint scent of deception. **Sound:** The gentle lapping of the water, which sounds like a whisper.
*   **The Hunting Grounds:** A section of the fields where the local hunters go. The animals here are perfect mimics of human beings, but subtly "wrong." **Smell:** Animal musk, and the faint scent of fear. **Sound:** The cries of animals, which sound like human screams.
*   **The Empty Shells' Camp:** A small, nomadic camp where the Empty Shells gather, trying to find their lost identities. **Smell:** Dust, and the faint scent of absence. **Sound:** A profound, unsettling silence.
*   **The Skinwalker's Lair:** A hidden cave in the center of the Shifting Fields, where the Skinwalker is strongest. **Smell:** Ozone, and the overwhelming scent of stolen identity. **Sound:** A cacophony of whispers, screams, and the sound of your own deepest fears.

### The Whispering Woods
A dense, ancient forest that borders the Shifting Fields, where the trees seem to whisper secrets.
*   **The Green Man's Grove:** A hidden grove where the Green Man resides, a being who has merged with the forest. **Smell:** Damp earth, and the scent of ancient magic. **Sound:** The rustle of leaves, which sounds like a human sigh.
*   **The Tree of Lies:** A massive, ancient tree that grows in the center of the woods. Its leaves are covered in the lies of the community. **Smell:** Pine needles, and the faint scent of deception. **Sound:** The whisper of the wind, which sounds like a thousand lies being told at once.
*   **The Cave of Lost Voices:** A cave where the echoes of stolen identities are trapped. **Smell:** Dust, and the faint scent of forgotten names. **Sound:** A cacophony of whispers, screams, and the sound of your own deepest fears.

### The Uncanny Valley Research Facility
A hidden, high-tech research facility, run by the Uncanny Valley Collective.
*   **Dr. Vance's Lab:** A sterile, high-tech lab where Dr. Vance creates his androids. **Smell:** Ozone, and the sterile scent of a clean room. **Sound:** The hum of electronics, the click of keyboards.
*   **The Mimicry Chamber:** A chamber where the androids are trained to perfectly mimic human behavior. **Smell:** Ozone, and the faint scent of artificial humanity. **Sound:** The subtle, unsettling sound of a machine trying to be human.
*   **The Identity Vault:** A vault where the Uncanny Valley Collective stores the stolen identities they have collected. **Smell:** Cold metal, and the faint scent of forgotten lives. **Sound:** An eerie silence.

### The Archivist's Sanctum
A hidden, underground bunker where the Archivist of Whispers collects all the secrets of the community.
*   **The Vault of Secrets:** A massive vault filled with filing cabinets, each containing a secret. **Smell:** Old paper, and the faint scent of a thousand secrets. **Sound:** The rustle of old documents, the whisper of forgotten truths.
*   **The Chamber of Whispers:** A room where the Archivist listens to the secrets whispered by his informants. **Smell:** Dust, and the faint scent of paranoia. **Sound:** A constant, sibilant whispering.
*   **The Archivist's Study:** A small, cramped study, filled with books and documents. **Smell:** Old paper, and the faint scent of obsession. **Sound:** The frantic scratching of the Archivist's pen.

**Type:** Artifact

**Type:** Cursed Item
**Description:** A mask made of perfectly preserved human skin. It is subtly "wrong" in its features.
**Mechanics:** When a creature wears this mask, they can perfectly mimic the appearance of any humanoid they have seen. However, the mask slowly consumes the wearer's own identity. Each day the mask is worn, the wearer must make a WIS save. On a failure, they lose a piece of their own identity (e.g., a memory, a personality trait, a skill proficiency). At 5 failures, the wearer's identity is completely consumed, and they become a new Skinwalker.

**Type:** Wondrous Item (Quill)
**Description:** A quill made from a single, grey feather. It writes in a neat, implacable, and utterly final script.
**Mechanics:** The wielder can use this quill to write a single, binding, magical contract. Any party who signs the contract is magically bound to uphold their end of the bargain, on pain of being "erased" from existence by the Skinwalker. The quill can also be used to "serve a summons" on any being, forcing them to appear for a negotiation.

### 4. The Shifting Sands Compass
**Type:** Wondrous Item
**Description:** A compass made of polished wood and brass. The needle constantly spins, never pointing North.

It can also be used to navigate the Shifting Fields, allowing the user to find their way through the constantly changing landscape. **Curse:** The compass slowly erodes the user's sense of direction. ### 5. The Empty Shell's Locket
**Type:** Artifact
**Description:** A simple, silver locket. It is empty.
**Mechanics:** The locket is a powerful tool for restoring stolen identities. If a fragment of a stolen identity (e.g., a memory, a personality trait) is placed inside the locket, it will begin to glow. If all the fragments of a stolen identity are placed inside the locket, the locket will glow brightly, and the Empty Shell will be restored to their original identity.

## [TAG: RULES_CUSTOM]
### Physics/Magic: The Law of Identity
In this world, identity is a fluid, fragile, and powerful force.
*   **Identity Resonance:** Strong, consistent identities (a person who is truly themselves, a community with a strong sense of self) create a "resonance" that repels the Skinwalker. Weak, fractured identities attract it.
*   **Mimicry Magic:** Spells that involve illusion, disguise, or shapeshifting are more powerful in this setting, but also more dangerous. A failed illusion spell might cause the caster to temporarily lose their own identity.
*   **The Uncanny Valley:** The closer a mimicry gets to perfection, the more unsettling it becomes. This is a tangible force. A perfect mimicry of a loved one will cause a profound sense of unease and dread.

### Subsystems: Identity Score and Trust
*   **Identity Score:** Each player has an "Identity Score" (from 0 to 10). This score decreases when they lie, pretend to be someone they're not, or keep secrets. Low Identity makes them more susceptible to the Skinwalker's influence and its mimicry. At 0, the character becomes an Empty Shell. Restoring trust and being honest can increase their Identity Score.
*   **Trust:** The community has a "Trust" score (from 0 to 10). This score decreases with every act of deception, every stolen identity, every manifestation of the Skinwalker. Low Trust makes the community more vulnerable to the Skinwalker's influence, as people are unable to distinguish between truth and falsehood. Restoring trust can heal the community and weaken the Skinwalker.

The players discover the Broken Mirror Tavern, where all the mirrors are cracked or subtly distorted. **Combat:** A battle against an Identity Thief from the Face-Changers Guild. **Social:** A tense confrontation with the Chameleon. **Loot:** A Face-Changer's Mask.
*   **Session 4:** **Primary Beat:** The Silent Keepers. The players stumble upon the Silent Keepers, who are trying to protect the community's secrets. **Combat:** A battle against a Secret-Hoarder. **Social:** A chilling conversation with the Archivist of Whispers. **Loot:** A Secret-Hoarder's Locket.
*   **Session 5:** **Primary Beat:** The Empty Shells. The players encounter the Empty Shells, victims of the Skinwalker, who wander the community, hollow and confused. **Combat:** A battle against a Mimic-Beast. **Social:** A heartbreaking conversation with an Empty Shell. **Loot:** An Empty Shell's Locket.
