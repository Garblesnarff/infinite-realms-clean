# The Valley of Lost Cousins

*An Epic Fantasy Adventure / Coming-of-Age campaign — Long Campaign (15–20 sessions) — Medium difficulty*

---

## Campaign Overview

**Title:** The Valley of Lost Cousins
**Campaign Type / Genre:** Epic Fantasy / Coming-of-Age / Lighthearted to Dark
**Estimated Length:** Long Campaign — 15–20 sessions (each session ≈ 3–4 hours)
**Player Level Range:** Start **1** → Finish **13–16**
**Tone (keywords):** epic fantasy, coming-of-age, humor to horror, fish out of water, ancient evil, prophecy, found family
**Core Premise (2–4 sentences):**
The party includes the Bone cousins—cartoony outcasts from distant Boneville who stumble into a mysterious fantasy valley cut off from the outside world. What begins as a comedic fish-out-of-water adventure gradually reveals an epic fantasy conflict: the valley is protected by ancient powers against the Locust—an ancient evil seeking to break free and consume the world. As the cousins become entangled in prophecy and war, they must grow from hapless wanderers into heroes who can stand against darkness itself.

---

## What "Medium" Difficulty Means Here

*   **Tonal Shift:** The campaign begins lighthearted but gradually becomes darker and more serious.
*   **Strategic Challenges:** Not just combat—puzzles, diplomacy, and understanding the valley's mysteries.
*   **Character Growth:** Characters develop from comedic to heroic over the arc.
*   **Environmental Navigation:** The valley itself is a character with secrets to uncover.

---

## Story Arc (Long Campaign — 15–20 sessions)

From comedy to epic fantasy, innocence to heroism.

*   **Act I — Lost in the Valley (Sessions 1–5):** The Bone cousins arrive in the valley, meeting its inhabitants and learning its rules. Lighthearted adventures with hints of deeper darkness.
*   **Act II — The Ancient Conflict (Sessions 6–12):** Discovering the valley's history, the imprisoned Locust, and the prophecy that involves them. Tone shifts toward epic fantasy.
*   **Act III — The Lord of the Locusts (Sessions 13–17):** The Locust breaks free. War comes to the valley. The cousins must become true heroes.
*   **Act IV — Homecoming (Sessions 18–20):** Final battle and resolution. Choosing between returning to Boneville or staying in the valley.

## Session-by-Session Outline

### Session 1 — "Out from Boneville"
*   **Focus:** Introduction / Comedy
*   **Key Events:** The Bone cousins (Fone Bone, Phoney Bone, Smiley Bone) are run out of Boneville after Phoney's get-rich-quick scheme fails spectacularly. Lost in the desert, they're separated and stumble into the mysterious valley.
**Escalation:** Fone Bone encounters rat creatures—monstrous beings hunting for someone.

### Session 2 — "Thorn"
*   **Focus:** Exploration / Meeting Allies
*   **Key Events:** Fone meets Thorn, a farm girl living with her grandmother. She's kind and mysterious, living peacefully despite the valley's dangers.
**Escalation:** Dreams of dragons hint at deeper connections.

### Session 3 — "The Great Cow Race"
*   **Focus:** Lighthearted Adventure / Community
*   **Key Events:** The cousins reunite. Phoney immediately starts scheming to make money off the locals. A comedic cow race brings the valley's community together.
**Escalation:** Rat creatures attack during the race, showing they're looking for something specific.

### Session 4 — "Barrelhaven"
*   **Focus:** Mystery / Discovery
*   **Key Events:** The group visits Barrelhaven, learning more about the valley's geography and politics. Not everyone welcomes strangers.
**Escalation:** A mysterious hooded figure appears, seeming to know about the Bones' arrival.

### Session 5 — "Rock Jaw"
*   **Focus:** Danger / Tonal Shift
*   **Key Events:** Encountering Rock Jaw, an ancient lion of the valley with his own agenda. The comedy starts giving way to real danger.
**Escalation:** Rock Jaw knows about "the Awakening"—something is changing in the valley.

### Session 6 — "Old Man's Cave"
*   **Focus:** Revelation / History
*   **Key Events:** The valley's elders reveal the truth: The valley is protected by ancient magic against the Locust, an evil force that once nearly destroyed the world. Thorn is not just a farm girl—she's the last heir of the royal bloodline.
**Escalation:** The rat creatures are servants of the Locust, searching for Thorn to prevent the prophecy.

### Session 7 — "Ghost Circles"
*   **Focus:** Horror / Investigation
*   **Key Events:** Strange circles appear in the valley where nothing can grow. They're spreading—signs that the Locust's prison is weakening.
**Escalation:** People who enter ghost circles disappear or go mad.

### Session 8 — "The Dragonslayer"
*   **Focus:** Deception / Intrigue
*   **Key Events:** A man claiming to be a legendary dragonslayer arrives, offering to protect the valley. He's actually working for darker forces.
**Escalation:** The dragonslayer manipulates the community, turning them against the dragons who actually protect the valley.

### Session 9 — "Treasure Hunters"
*   **Focus:** Comedy in Darkness / Phoney's Arc
*   **Key Events:** Phoney's greed leads to discovery of ancient treasure—which is actually part of the Locust's power sealed away.
**Escalation:** Opening the treasure vault weakens the seals holding the Locust.

### Session 10 — "Crown of Horns"
*   **Focus:** Revelation / Power
*   **Key Events:** Thorn discovers she can use the Crown of Horns, an ancient artifact of power. But using it risks corruption by the Locust.
**Escalation:** The valley's defenders are divided about whether Thorn should use this dangerous power.

### Session 11 — "The Northern Valley"
*   **Focus:** Exploration / World Expansion
*   **Key Events:** Journey to the northern regions reveals the scope of the ancient war. Ruins of the old kingdom show what was lost.
**Escalation:** Visions show what the Locust did in the past—and will do again if freed.

### Session 12 — "Solstice"
*   **Focus:** Prophecy / Preparation
*   **Key Events:** The winter solstice approaches—when the barriers are weakest. Both sides prepare for final conflict.
**Escalation:** The Lord of the Locusts begins manifesting directly.

### Session 13 — "The Lord of the Locusts"
*   **Focus:** Horror / First Contact
*   **Key Events:** The party encounters the Locust itself—an ancient, incomprehensible evil that was once mortal but became something else.
**Escalation:** The Locust offers temptations—power, knowledge, return home—to divide the party.

### Session 14 — "The Awakening"
*   **Focus:** War Begins / Allies Rally
*   **Key Events:** The seals break. The Locust's armies pour into the valley. All factions must unite or die.
**Escalation:** The Bone cousins must choose—flee to safety or stand and fight.

### Session 15 — "The Battle of the Valley"
*   **Focus:** Epic Combat / Heroism
*   **Key Events:** Massive battle against overwhelming numbers. Each Bone cousin finds their moment of heroism—even cowardly Phoney.
**Escalation:** The battle is a distraction—the Locust seeks to possess Thorn and use her power.

### Session 16 — "The Great Red Dragon"
*   **Focus:** Allies Unite / Dragons Revealed
*   **Key Events:** The Great Red Dragon and other ancient protectors emerge to aid the valley.
**Escalation:** Even with dragons, they're losing. Something more is needed.

### Session 17 — "The Crown"
*   **Focus:** Sacrifice / Choice
*   **Key Events:** Thorn must decide whether to use the Crown of Horns at risk of her soul, or find another way.
**Escalation:** Fone Bone's loyalty and love for Thorn is tested when he must help her make this choice.

### Session 18 — "The Lord Falls"
*   **Focus:** Climax / Final Battle
*   **Key Events:** The combined forces—humans, dragons, and surprisingly the reformed Bone cousins—face the Locust in final combat.
**Escalation:** Victory requires a sacrifice. Who will pay the price?

### Session 19 — "The Valley Healed"
*   **Focus:** Aftermath / Healing
*   **Key Events:** The Locust is defeated or sealed again. The valley begins to heal. Ghost circles fade.
**Escalation:** But the cost was real—not everyone survived. Grief and celebration mix.

### Session 20 — "Boneville or the Valley?"
*   **Focus:** Epilogue / Choice
*   **Key Events:** The cousins can finally return to Boneville—the path is open. But the valley is their home now too.
**Escalation:** Each must choose: return to comfortable familiarity or stay in the valley they helped save?

## Major NPCs

1.  **Fone Bone** — *The Heart*
    *   **Role:** Most levelheaded cousin, grows into unlikely hero.
    *   **Personality:** Kind, literate, loyal, romantic.
    *   **Arc:** From wanting to go home to fighting for his new home.

2.  **Phoney Bone** — *The Schemer*
    *   **Role:** Greedy, selfish cousin who gradually finds heroism.
    *   **Personality:** Conman, coward, surprisingly brave when it matters.
    *   **Arc:** From pure self-interest to actually caring about others.

3.  **Smiley Bone** — *The Innocent*
    *   **Role:** Simple, happy cousin who sees good in everyone.
    *   **Personality:** Optimistic, naive, secretly wise.
    *   **Arc:** His innocence becomes strength, not weakness.

4.  **Thorn** — *The Princess*
    *   **Role:** Farm girl revealed to be last heir of the royal line.
    *   **Personality:** Strong, kind, burdened by destiny.
    *   **Arc:** From simple farm life to accepting her role as leader.

5.  **Gran'ma Ben** — *The Warrior Queen*
    *   **Role:** Thorn's grandmother, secretly former champion of the valley.
    *   **Personality:** Tough, fast, fiercely protective.
    *   **Arc:** Coming out of retirement to fight one last time.

6.  **The Great Red Dragon** — *The Ancient Protector*
    *   **Role:** Ancient being protecting the valley, connected to Thorn.
    *   **Personality:** Mysterious, powerful, speaks in riddles.
    *   **Arc:** Revealing the deep history and magic of the valley.

7.  **The Lord of the Locusts** — *Ancient Evil*
    *   **Role:** The imprisoned darkness seeking freedom.
    *   **Personality:** Tempting, manipulative, incomprehensibly ancient.
    *   **Arc:** From imprisoned whisper to manifested threat.

## Key Locations

1.  **Boneville:** The cartoony city the Bones came from (only seen in flashbacks/epilogue).
2.  **Thorn's Farm:** Peaceful homestead that becomes a base.
3.  **Barrelhaven:** River town showing valley's diversity.
4.  **The Valley:** Mysterious, magical landscape cut off from outside world.
5.  **Old Man's Cave:** Seat of the valley's elder council.
6.  **The Northern Ruins:** Remnants of the ancient kingdom.
7.  **The Crown of Horns Site:** Where ancient power and danger meet.

## Central Conflict

Innocence versus experience, comedy versus tragedy, home versus belonging. The campaign asks: Can ordinary people become heroes? What makes a place home? Is power worth the cost? Can you return to innocence after seeing darkness?

---

## Unique Mechanics

### 1. **Tonal Progression**
*   Mechanical complexity increases as tone shifts from comedy to epic fantasy. Early sessions use simpler rules, later sessions full tactical combat.

### 2. **The Ghost Circles**
*   Environmental hazards that spread based on the Locust's power, limiting safe areas and creating urgency.

### 3. **The Crown of Horns**
*   Powerful artifact that corrupts with use. Risk-reward mechanic for using great power.

### 4. **Cousin Dynamics**
*   The three Bones have special cooperative abilities when working together, encouraging teamwork.

### 5. **The Prophecy**
*   Ancient predictions guide but don't dictate the story. Players can fulfill prophecy in unexpected ways.

### 6. **Return Home Option**
*   Throughout campaign, the option exists to return to Boneville—but choosing to stay and fight is meaningful.
