**The Faceless Stalker**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise.
*   **Content:** Children are disappearing from the forest town of Oakmere, and witnesses report sightings of an impossibly tall, suited figure with no face lurking among the trees. The entity, known locally as the Thin Man, stalks those who observe it, corrupting their minds and turning them into blank-eyed proxies. The players must investigate the disappearances, resist the entity's maddening influence, and discover the truth: the Thin Man is an ancient psychic predator that feeds on fear and memory, and simply looking at it marks you as prey.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Directive:** The Lore Generation Agent must create detailed entries for each of the following prompts. These entries will form the historical and cultural bedrock of the world.
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the history of Oakmere as a logging town surrounded by ancient forests.
    *   Write the history of the Thin Man's appearances over centuries, documented in various forms (old paintings, photographs, oral traditions).
    *   Describe the nature of the Thin Man: an extradimensional entity that exists partially outside normal reality.
    *   Explain how the marking process works and why the entity stalks specific individuals.
    *   Detail the creation of proxies and the psychological process that breaks victims into servants.
    *   Write about the Hollow: a pocket dimension where the entity keeps its victims in suspended animation.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Directive:** The Faction Generation Agent must create detailed profiles for each faction listed below.
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Marked** (Major - includes the players)
        *   **Goals:** Survive, rescue the missing children, destroy or escape the Thin Man.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Informal alliance of investigators and concerned citizens who have seen the entity.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Find the missing children and solve the mystery.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Each is fighting their own deteriorating memories and sanity. Some may consider fleeing rather than confronting the threat.
        *   **Assets:** Investigation skills, determination, knowledge gained from research, each other.
        *   **Relationships:** Hunted by the Thin Man; opposed by proxies; seeking help from experts and authorities.
    *   **The Proxies** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** Serve the Thin Man by identifying new victims and delivering them to the Hollow.
        *   **Hierarchy:** No formal hierarchy. All receive psychic commands from the entity and coordinate instinctively.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Unknown. They appear as normal townspeople.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** To expand the Thin Man's influence by creating more proxies and feeding it more victims.
        *   **Assets:** Ability to appear normal, insider knowledge of the town, superhuman persistence, no need for sleep.
        *   **Relationships:** Commanded by the Thin Man; hunting the players; infiltrating the community.
    *   **The Ignorant** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Live normal lives unaware of the supernatural threat.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Normal townsfolk, police, town council.
        *   **Public Agenda:** The disappearances are tragic but must have a rational explanation.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Some are in denial, refusing to accept the supernatural. Others are being slowly marked.
        *   **Assets:** Normal societal authority and resources.
        *   **Relationships:** Skeptical of the players' theories; potential victims; may become proxies.
    *   **The Researchers** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Document and understand the Thin Man phenomenon.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Online communities, paranormal investigators, a few academics.
        *   **Public Agenda:** To gather evidence of the entity's existence.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Some have become obsessed to the point of madness. A few actively seek to be taken.
        *   **Assets:** Historical records, theories about the entity's nature, potential countermeasures.
        *   **Relationships:** May provide information to the players; often unreliable or mentally unstable; some are proxies posing as researchers.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Directive:** The Character Generation Agent must create full profiles for all NPCs listed.
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **The Thin Man:** An extradimensional psychic predator. Impossibly tall, faceless, stalks marked victims. Feeds on fear and memories.
    *   **Sarah Winters:** A mother searching for her missing daughter. Secretly a proxy, her memories being stolen.
    *   **Detective Marcus Reed:** Local detective investigating conventionally. Marked years ago, memory deteriorating rapidly.
    *   **Timothy Chase:** A returned teenager who is now a proxy, fighting internal control.

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Directive:** The Environment Generation Agent must create detailed blueprints for each location below.
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **Oakmere:** A small forest town, modern but surrounded by old-growth woods.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** Main Street, the School (where children disappeared), Police Station, Winters Home, Town Library (research resources).
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** ~2,000 residents including families, business owners, authorities. Several are proxies.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Normal small-town amenities, police assistance, medical care, lodging.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Glimpsing the Thin Man in the distance, finding operator symbols drawn on walls, encountering a proxy pretending to be normal, experiencing a memory gap, witnessing another person being stalked.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "Children drew pictures of the tall man before disappearing." "Some kids who returned aren't acting right." "There's an old sawmill where people hear voices."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Normal suburban buildings, fog rolling in from the forest, operator symbols), Sound (Normal town sounds that feel too quiet, distant static), Smell (Pine trees, fresh rain, nothing unusual).
    *   **The Deep Forest:** Miles of dense woodland where reality becomes unstable.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The Old Oak (landmark tree covered in operator symbols), Fog Banks (permanent areas of dense mist), Reality Distortions (areas where space doesn't make sense).
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** The Thin Man (appears frequently here), proxies, wildlife (behaving strangely).
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** None. This is the entity's domain.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Direct Thin Man sighting, being followed by proxies, finding children's belongings, experiencing spatial distortions, seeing the same tree multiple times despite walking straight.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The forest goes on forever if He is watching." "The old oak marks the boundary to the Hollow." "Time works differently when He is near."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Dense trees, perpetual fog, the Thin Man watching from between trunks), Sound (Unnatural silence, occasional static, footsteps that aren't there), Smell (Damp earth, ozone, something wrong).
    *   **The Abandoned Sawmill:** A proxy gathering site on the forest's edge.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The main building (decayed), processing floor (ritual space), office (filled with drawings and documents), storage sheds.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Proxies gathering to receive commands, the Thin Man (occasionally manifests here).
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** None. Evidence can be found here.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Interrupting a proxy gathering, finding evidence of missing children, direct confrontation with proxies, experiencing visions of the Hollow, the Thin Man appearing suddenly.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The proxies bring new victims here first." "There's a door to the Hollow somewhere inside." "Every surface is covered in operator symbols."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Decay, operator symbols everywhere, blank-eyed proxies), Sound (Creaking wood, whispers, static), Smell (Rot, dust, ozone).
    *   **The Hollow:** A pocket dimension of endless fog-shrouded forest.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The Suspended Children (floating in stasis), the Operator's Core (manifestation point), Pathways (leading nowhere and everywhere).
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** The Thin Man (true form), child victims in suspended animation, echoes of past victims.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** None. This is the final confrontation.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** The Thin Man appearing instantly before characters, spatial distortions, visions of victim memories, proxy ambushes, reality-breaking phenomena.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The children are kept here to feed its hunger." "Destroying the core might free everyone." "Time doesn't exist in the Hollow."

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Directive:** The World Simulation Agent must implement the following trigger-based state changes.
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** A player accumulates 10 Stalker Points.
    *   **THEN:** That player becomes a proxy. The transformation is gradual and may be role-played or result in NPC status. Other players must decide whether to save or stop them.
    *   **IF:** The players identify and stop a proxy before they deliver a victim.
    *   **THEN:** The Thin Man's influence temporarily weakens. However, it becomes more aggressive in stalking the players directly.
    *   **IF:** The players discover the location of the Hollow's entrance.
    *   **THEN:** Proxies attempt to prevent them from entering. The Thin Man appears more frequently. A confrontation becomes inevitable.
    *   **IF:** The players enter the Hollow unprepared.
    *   **THEN:** They become disoriented and separated. The entity can attack them individually. Stalker Points accumulate rapidly.
    *   **IF:** The players successfully destroy the Operator's Core in the Hollow.
    *   **THEN:** The Thin Man is banished from this reality. All proxies collapse and either die or are freed (depending on how long they've been corrupted). The suspended children awaken and can be rescued. All Stalker Points are removed from surviving players, though some memories may be permanently lost.
