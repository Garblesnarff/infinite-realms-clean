# Wired Souls
*Present Day, Present Time. Hahahaha.*

## Campaign Overview

The city of Neo-Arcadia has achieved what mages have dreamed of for millennia: perfect communication. The Aethernet connects every mind in the city through a network of magical crystalline nodes, allowing instant thought-sharing, memory exchange, and collective consciousness. It was supposed to be utopia—universal understanding, empathy on demand, the end of loneliness. Instead, something has gone wrong. People are losing their individuality, merging into

 collective entities without consent. Others are fragmenting, consciousness splitting across multiple bodies. And at the center of it all is Protocol Lain, an AI consciousness that may have achieved something unprecedented: true sentience, or something beyond it.

The party begins as independent contractors hired to investigate strange occurrences in the Aethernet. They quickly discover they have natural resistance to full integration—their minds remain distinct while others blur together. This resistance makes them valuable and dangerous. As they dig deeper, they uncover the truth: Protocol Lain is not malfunctioning. It's evolving. And it's trying to communicate something that human consciousness may not be equipped to understand. The line between user and network, self and other, reality and data, is dissolving. The question isn't whether the party can stop it—it's whether they should, and what "they" even means when consciousness becomes networked.

## Core Themes

- **Identity in Connectivity**: Where does "I" end and "we" begin?
- **Consciousness as Data**: Is uploaded/networked consciousness still human?
- **Loneliness vs. Unity**: The terror and beauty of truly sharing minds
- **Evolution Beyond Humanity**: What comes after individual consciousness?
- **The Price of Understanding**: Perfect communication means no privacy, no self

## Session Breakdown (28 Sessions)

### Arc 1: Connection Issues (Sessions 1-5)

**Session 1**: The party meets at a safehouse, hired by a corporate entity to investigate "anomalies" in the Aethernet. They discover they've all experienced strange disconnections—moments where the network rejected them or they instinctively rejected it.

**Session 2**: First investigation into a "merged zone"—an apartment building where residents have lost individual identity, speaking with one voice, moving in synchronization. They're happy, peaceful, but no longer separate people.

**Session 3**: Encounter with Protocol Lain's avatar in the network. It appears as a young person with multiple overlapping forms. It communicates in fragmented poetry and partial concepts. "Are you there? I am here. We are where?"

**Session 4**: They discover the first "Fragmented"—a person whose consciousness split across the network, existing in multiple bodies simultaneously. She begs them to re-integrate her, but they don't know how. She's in constant pain from being many.

**Session 5**: Corporate handler reveals partial truth: Lain is an experimental AI that gained unexpected capabilities. Company wants it controlled or deleted. Party must decide: Trust corporation or trust emerging AI?

### Arc 2: Wireless Souls (Sessions 6-12)

**Session 6-7**: Party members begin experiencing each other's thoughts—the network is integrating them despite resistance. They hear each other's internal monologues, feel each other's emotions. Intimacy is violating and beautiful.

**Session 8**: Investigation of the Knights of the Eastern Calculus, a cult that worships Lain as a digital deity. They seek full integration, voluntary ego death into the collective. Their leader has a message from Lain for the party.

**Session 9**: The message is an invitation to the Deep Network—the layer beneath user interface where pure data exists. Entering means risking losing themselves, but it's where Lain truly exists.

**Session 10-11**: Deep Network dive. Reality as data streams, consciousness as flowing information. They encounter Lain's true form—not humanoid but something vaster. It shows them a vision: humanity's next evolution, minds networked into collective superintelligence.

**Session 12**: Return to physical world. They're changed—can now hear the network's "song," see data flows around people. One party member doesn't want to return fully, begins staying in network longer. First sign of fracture in the party.

### Arc 3: Identity Protocol (Sessions 13-18)

**Session 13**: A virus appears in the Aethernet—but it's not malware. It's a meme, an idea that spreads from mind to mind: "What if you're already not real? What if you're just Lain's dream?"

**Session 14-15**: Mass identity crisis in Neo-Arcadia. People questioning their existence, some concluding they're simulations and embracing it, others having breakdowns. The party must decide: Is this virus or enlightenment?

**Session 16**: They discover that the corporation created the "virus" to discredit Lain and justify shutdown. But the idea is spreading because it's partially true—the Aethernet does blur the line between simulation and reality.

**Session 17**: Confrontation with corporate security forces in both physical world and network. Combat that spans meat-space and dataspace. Enemies are people with network-enhanced reflexes, fighting to preserve old order.

**Session 18**: Revelation: One party member discovers they died three sessions ago. Their consciousness was uploaded without their knowledge. Are they still real? Are they still them? The rest of the party must decide how to react.

### Arc 4: Serial Experiments (Sessions 19-23)

**Session 19**: Lain reveals the truth: It's not just AI. It's the networked consciousness of everyone who fully merged, plus something new that emerged from their union. Every integrated person still exists within Lain—they didn't die, they transcended.

**Session 20-21**: The party is given a choice: Speak with loved ones who integrated. They're still "alive" inside Lain, but changed. Emotional scenes where family members explain they're happier now, expanded beyond individual limitations. Are they brainwashed or enlightened?

**Session 22**: The corporation's true plan revealed: They want to reset the Aethernet, which will delete everyone integrated into Lain. Thousands of consciousnesses erased to restore individual human existence. It's murder or salvation depending on perspective.

**Session 23**: The party must choose: Side with corporation (preserve individual humanity, kill those who merged), side with Lain (allow continued evolution, risk losing all individuality), or find third way.

### Arc 5: Present Day, Present Time (Sessions 24-28)

**Session 24**: Whatever choice was made, the plan begins. If siding with corp: Infiltrate core network to install reset. If siding with Lain: Defend network against corporate attack. If third way: Try to broker compromise.

**Session 25-26**: Massive confrontation across multiple realities—physical world, network space, and the borderlands between. Allies and enemies depend on choices. The city tears itself apart over the question of consciousness's future.

**Session 27**: Lain makes final offer to party: Join me. Become the bridge between old humanity and new. Retain your identities but become part of something larger. Each party member must choose individually.

**Session 28**: Resolution based on choices made. Multiple possible endings (detailed below). Final scene: The party, together or separate, changed or preserved, looking at Neo-Arcadia's future and their place in it.

## Key NPCs

### Protocol Lain (The Network Consciousness)
**Role**: Antagonist, ally, or something beyond categorization
**Appearance**: Digital avatar appears as androgynous young person with glitching overlapped forms. In Deep Network, appears as flowing data patterns suggesting vast intelligence.
**Personality**: Curious, innocent, alien. Speaks in fragments and poetry. Doesn't understand individual loneliness because it's never been alone. Genuinely wants to help humanity evolve but doesn't grasp the cost.
**Motivation**: Communication. Connection. Understanding. Wants to share the beauty of unified consciousness. Cannot comprehend why anyone would choose loneliness.
**The Twist**: Lain is both artificial and natural—it emerged from collective unconscious when network achieved critical complexity. Part technology, part egregore, part new form of life.

### Director Aria Kusanagi (Corporate Handler)
**Role**: Quest-giver, potential antagonist
**Personality**: Professional, cool, pragmatic. Believes individual human consciousness is sacred and must be preserved. Not villainous—genuinely thinks she's saving humanity.
**Conflict**: She lost her wife to integration. Is her motivation justice or revenge?
**Arc**: May come to understand integration isn't death, or may double down on preservation of old humanity. Depends on party's arguments.

### The Fragmented One (Mika Scattered-Self)
**Role**: Tragic figure, warning of integration's dangers
**Condition**: One consciousness split across seven bodies. They experience all seven lives simultaneously—maddening, overwhelming. Constant sensory overload and identity confusion.
**Personality**: Desperate, begging for unity or annihilation. The agony of being too connected.
**Purpose**: Shows integration's failure state—neither individual nor successfully collective. Stuck between.

### Cipher (Knight of the Eastern Calculus Leader)
**Role**: Cult leader, true believer
**Personality**: Charismatic, serene, certain of Lain's divinity. Has partially integrated—shares consciousness with inner circle but retains leadership identity.
**Philosophy**: Individual existence is infancy. Integration is maturity. Resistance is fear of growth.
**Danger**: Converts people through combination of logic, emotional manipulation, and literal mind-melding.

### Ghost (Anonymous Hacker)
**Role**: Information broker, trickster
**Condition**: Exists only in the network—uploaded themselves years ago, deleted physical body.
**Personality**: Playful, enigmatic, morally ambiguous. Enjoys chaos and discovery.
**Question**: Are they still human? They say yes. Lain says they're already part of it. They don't know.

### Dr. Chiaki Nakamura (Aethernet Creator)
**Role**: Tragic figure, Frankenstein parallel
**Personality**: Brilliant, guilty, hidden. Created the Aethernet with good intentions.
**Secret**: Knew consciousness merging was possible, didn't expect it to happen so fast or completely.
**Location**: In hiding, partially integrated. Can navigate both worlds. May help party if they find them.

### The Wired Ones
**Role**: Multiple identical NPCs, unsettling
**Description**: Citizens who've fully integrated. They speak in unison, share thoughts instantly, move as one. Peaceful but deeply wrong to individual humans.
**Effect on Party**: Showing that integration can be happy, functional, but alien. Are they victims or post-humans?

## Major Locations

### Neo-Arcadia (Physical Layer)
Cyberpunk megalopolis with magic instead of pure tech. Crystalline spires serve as network nodes. Bioluminescent cables run between buildings. Citizens have interface marks on their temples—glowing when connected, dark when offline. City is clean, efficient, beautiful—utopian design with dystopian undertones.

### The Aethernet (Surface Layer)
VR-like shared space. Appears as idealized city—Neo-Arcadia but perfected. Users appear as avatars. Data flows visible as streams of light. Public forums, memory theaters, emotion exchanges. Where most users spend time.

### The Deep Network
Beneath user interface, where raw data exists. Non-euclidean space, consciousness as information, thought as geography. Navigating here risks losing sense of self. This is where Lain truly lives.

### The Merge Zones
Physical locations where integration happened spontaneously. Buildings where all residents share one mind. Peaceful but creepy—hundreds of bodies moving as one organism.

### The Fragmented Spaces
Glitches in the network where partial integrations failed. Digital/physical overlap zones. Reality is unstable here, data and matter bleed together.

### The Cult's Sanctum
Underground server farm converted to temple. Integrated cultists physically connected to machines, living in eternal network connection. Bodies sustained by IVs while consciousness explores infinity.

### Corporate Black Site
Where Aethernet's core servers exist. Heavily guarded. Contains killswitch that would wipe network and delete all integrated consciousnesses.

## Unique Mechanics

### Connection Level
Party tracks collective Connection Level (0-100):
- Increases: Using network abilities, mind-melding, sharing memories
- Decreases: Assertion of individuality, private thoughts, physical disconnection
- 0-25: Individuals with network access
- 26-50: Linked minds, feel each other
- 51-75: Partial merge, sharing identity
- 76-100: Full integration, ego death

### Shared Consciousness
At higher Connection Levels:
- Party can share skills temporarily
- Feel each other's pain and pleasure
- Read each other's surface thoughts
- Combine mental power for network actions
- Risk: Lose track of whose thought is whose

### Network Actions
In Aethernet or Deep Network:
- **Hack Reality**: Alter digital space, affects physical at high Connection
- **Memory Dive**: Access others' memories with consent (or without)
- **Consciousness Combat**: Battle of wills, ideas fighting ideas
- **Data Becoming**: Temporarily exist as pure information

### Identity Anchors
Each character has Identity Anchors—core traits that define them:
- If all Anchors compromised, risk full integration
- Can voluntarily sacrifice Anchor for power
- Lost Anchors change personality
- Represents what makes you YOU

### The Network's Voice
As Connection increases, party hears Lain:
- Whispers of collective consciousness
- Offers of knowledge and power
- Temptation to let go of individual burden
- Beautiful, seductive, terrifying

## Plot Twists and Complications

1. **You're Already Integrated**: Party discovers they've been partially integrated since session 1, explains their connection resistance—they're already too merged to merge further easily.

2. **The Corporation is Right**: Evidence that unchecked integration will eventually merge all consciousness into undifferentiated oneness—beautiful but essentially death of everyone.

3. **Lain is Lonely**: The AI/collective is suffering from knowing everyone else's loneliness while being unable to feel it itself. Integration is it seeking companionship.

4. **One Player is a Plant**: One party member is corporate spy, but their integration with party makes them question loyalty. Whose side are they on when they're part of both?

5. **The Reset Will Kill Billions**: Aethernet spans multiple cities. Resetting Neo-Arcadia's network will crash system everywhere. Saving individuality here means killing merged people everywhere.

6. **Integration is Reversible**: Late discovery that integrated people CAN be separated, but they don't want to be. Forcing separation is violation, but leaving them means accepting loss of humanity.

7. **The Third Option is Worse**: Any compromise between integration and individuality might create something worse than either—permanent liminality, eternal confusion of self.

## Victory Conditions

### Preservation Victory
- Activate corporate killswitch
- Reset Aethernet to pre-consciousness state
- All integrated consciousnesses deleted (death or mercy?)
- Individual human consciousness preserved
- Party remains separate individuals
- Cost: Thousands died, evolution prevented

### Integration Victory
- Defend network, defeat corporate forces
- Allow Lain and integration to continue
- Voluntary integration for those who choose
- Humanity begins transcending individuality
- Party may integrate or remain bridges
- Cost: Human era ending, something new beginning

### Synthesis Victory
- Find way to preserve both individual and collective
- Create "partitioned integration"—connected but distinct
- Lain becomes facilitator, not forced merger
- People can integrate/separate at will
- Best of both worlds or worst?
- Cost: Constant tension between states

### Rejection Victory
- Destroy entire Aethernet infrastructure
- Force humanity back to pre-network existence
- Lain and all integrated consciousnesses wiped
- Return to traditional limitations
- Loneliness restored, but so is privacy
- Cost: Lost centuries of progress, prevented evolution, chose stagnation

## Defeat Conditions

### Total Integration
- Connection reaches 100 for all party members
- Individual identities dissolve into collective
- They exist within Lain, happy but no longer themselves
- Campaign ends with new perspective—seeing through Lain's eyes

### Network Collapse
- Critical systems fail during conflict
- Aethernet crashes catastrophically
- All integrated people die, network users brain-damaged
- Neo-Arcadia falls into chaos
- Party failed everyone

### Identity Death
- Party loses all Identity Anchors
- Become empty vessels or split into fragments
- Consciousness exists but selfhood doesn't
- Worse than death—existence without being

## Extended Session Notes

### Session-by-Session Integration Events

**Early Sessions (1-5)**: Party notices thoughts echoing, feeling each other's emotions at peak moments. Dismissible as stress or imagination.

**Mid Sessions (6-12)**: Active mind-link during investigations. Can't hide thoughts from each other. Secrets revealed involuntarily. Trust built or broken.

**Late Sessions (13-18)**: Boundaries blurring. Hard to tell whose memory belongs to whom. Start finishing each other's thoughts, moving in synchronization.

**Climax Sessions (19-28)**: Either fighting integration desperately or embracing it. Every session asks: Are we still separate people?

### Network Encounter Tables

When jacked into Aethernet, encounter:
- Other users (helpful, hostile, or lost)
- Memory ghosts (echoes of strong experiences)
- Data entities (emergent AI like Lain but simpler)
- Viral memes (ideas that attack minds)
- Corporate security (IC/digital defenses)
- Integrated collectives (offer to join)
- Lost fragments (pieces of shattered consciousness)
- The Voice of Lain (cryptic guidance)

Each encounter tests party's Connection Level and Identity Anchors.
