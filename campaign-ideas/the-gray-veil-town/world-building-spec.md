**The Gray Veil Town**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Directive:** This is the foundational context. All generated content must align with this core premise.
*   **Content:** The party is mysteriously drawn to Gray Veil, a fog-shrouded town that manifests personal guilt and trauma as physical reality. The town exists in multiple layers: the Fog World (abandoned normalcy), the Otherworld (nightmarish industrial hellscape), and the Core (pure manifestation of collective suffering). Each character faces personalized horror based on their backstory and must confront their deepest guilt to escape, knowing the town will not release them until they achieve genuine repentance or succumb to madness.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Directive:** The Lore Generation Agent must create detailed entries for each of the following prompts. These entries will form the historical and cultural bedrock of the world.
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail Gray Veil's origins as a normal town built on sacred ground where a Native American cult once performed rituals of atonement.
    *   Chronicle the catastrophe 20 years ago: a doomsday cult performed a ritual sacrifice believing it would bring purification, instead creating a permanent link to the Otherworld.
    *   Explain the nature of the town as a living entity: it draws those with unresolved guilt through mysterious letters, manifestations, or compulsion.
    *   Describe the Otherworld as a dimension overlapping our reality, where psychological trauma becomes physical law.
    *   Detail the symbolic monsters: each is a manifestation of specific traumas (medical horror, violence, sexual abuse, abandonment, etc.).
    *   Explain why some visitors become monsters themselves, permanent residents, or manage to escape based on how they confront their guilt.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Directive:** The Faction Generation Agent must create a detailed profile for each faction listed below. Each profile must contain the specified fields.
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **The Visitors (The Players)** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Escape Gray Veil, find whatever drew them here, survive, and confront personal guilt.
        *   **Hierarchy:** No formal structure. United by shared predicament.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Find missing people or solve the mystery.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Each has personal guilt they're hiding, even from each other. The town knows and will expose it.
        *   **Assets:** Whatever they brought, weapons found in town, their wits, and potential for psychological growth.
        *   **Relationships:** Initially cooperative; tested by personal revelations; may fracture if guilt is too shameful.
    *   **The Town (Gray Veil Itself)** (Major)
        *   **Goals:** Force confession and atonement, feed on suffering and guilt, maintain its existence.
        *   **Hierarchy:** The town is a singular entity with the Otherworld as its true form and monsters as its antibodies.
        *   **Public Agenda:** None. It appears abandoned.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** To judge visitors and either purify them through suffering or consume them permanently.
        *   **Assets:** Control over reality within its bounds, manifestation of fears, Otherworld transitions, unkillable symbolic monsters.
        *   **Relationships:** Hostile to those who deny guilt; offers escape to those who truly repent; neutral to those who accept their nature.
    *   **The Order / The Cult (Historical)** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Were seeking to bring about purification through suffering and rebirth.
        *   **Hierarchy:** Led by Dahlia Gillespie; inner circle of true believers; outer circle of manipulated followers.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Religious salvation.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Believed pain and guilt were the path to divinity, created the catastrophe intentionally.
        *   **Assets:** None remaining; all dead. But their ritual's effects persist.
        *   **Relationships:** Created the current situation; some members may persist as ghosts or manifestations.
    *   **The Lost** (Minor)
        *   **Goals:** Previous visitors who failed to escape, now part of the town.
        *   **Hierarchy:** No structure. Each exists in their personal hell or has become a monster.
        *   **Public Agenda:** Some offer warnings. Others are hostile.
        *   **Secret Agenda:** Some want company in damnation. Others genuinely want to help newcomers escape.
        *   **Assets:** Knowledge of the town, supernatural abilities tied to their transformation.
        *   **Relationships:** Some are potential allies; others are cautionary tales; some have become the monsters hunting the party.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Directive:** The Character Generation Agent must create full profiles for all NPCs listed. Tier 1 NPCs are unique individuals. Tier 2 & 3 are archetypes to be instantiated multiple times with unique details as needed by the simulation.
*   **Tier 1 (Unique, Major NPCs):**
    *   **Dahlia Gillespie:** Cult leader, now a manifestation/ghost, manipulative and convinced suffering is holy.
    *   **Harry Mason:** Another visitor seeking his lost daughter, can be ally or rival.
    *   **Pyramid Head / The Executioner:** Unkillable monster representing ultimate punishment and guilt.
    *   **The God of Guilt:** The entity at the town's core, either an ancient deity or mass trauma given consciousness.
*   **Tier 2 & 3 (Archetypes for Generation):**
    *   "Bubble Head Nurse" (faceless medical horror)
    *   "Lying Figure" (straightjacketed body representing hidden truth)
    *   "Mannequin" (partial humanoid forms)
    *   "Lost Visitor" (previous victims, some helpful, some hostile)
    *   "Cult Ghost" (echoes of the original ritual)
    *   "Personal Manifestation" (custom monster based on individual character's guilt)

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Directive:** The Environment Generation Agent must create a detailed blueprint for each location below, populating all specified sub-sections.
*   **Location Roster:**
    *   **The Fog World (General):** Normal Gray Veil under perpetual fog and twilight.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** Main street, shops, residential areas, the lake.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Appears empty. Monsters manifest near those with high Guilt.
        *   **Available Goods & Services:** Supplies in abandoned stores, maps, journals explaining the town's history.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Distant air-raid siren, manifestation of personal monster, finding a corpse, meeting another visitor, Otherworld transition.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The town was normal until the ritual." "People are drawn here for a reason." "The fog hides truth."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Fog everywhere, muted colors, abandoned normalcy), Sound (Footsteps, static, distant sirens), Smell (Mildew, rust water, staleness).
    *   **Alchemilla Hospital:** Medical facility in both worlds.
        *   **Key Landmarks (Fog):** Reception, patient rooms, morgue, roof.
        *   **Key Landmarks (Otherworld):** Rusted gurneys, bloodstained surgery, wire-mesh walls, body parts.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Nurses in both worlds; doctors in Otherworld; personal medical trauma manifestations.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Nurse attack, finding patient records that match character fears, surgery room nightmare, roof encounter with Pyramid Head, discovering the way to the church.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The hospital treats psychological wounds by making them physical." "Someone here knows how to escape." "The morgue has bodies that look like you."
        *   **Sensory Details (Otherworld):** Sight (Rust, blood, industrial lighting), Sound (Gurney wheels, surgical tools, patient screams), Smell (Antiseptic mixed with rot, blood, metal).
    *   **Toluca Prison:** Represents punishment.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** Cell blocks, execution chamber, solitary confinement, warden's office.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Manifestations of guilt as prisoners, executioner figures.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Locked in cell with your manifestation, witnessing executions of "sins," finding keys to deeper levels, being judged by ghost warden, combat with prison guards.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The prison holds your crimes as prisoners." "Freeing them frees yourself." "The execution chamber can be turned on yourself."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Bars, chains, execution equipment), Sound (Cell doors, echoing screams, executioner's footsteps), Smell (Despair, mildew, ozone).
    *   **The Church:** Center of atonement.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** Sanctuary, confessionals, basement ritual chamber.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** Cult ghosts, the most intense manifestations.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Forced confession in confessional, altar demanding sacrifice, confronting personalized boss, ritual recreation, meeting the God of Guilt.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "True confession grants escape." "The ritual chamber is the way to the core." "The God listens to those who repent."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Stained glass showing sins, altar of rust in Otherworld), Sound (Hymns, confessions echoing), Smell (Incense mixed with blood, candle wax).
    *   **The Abyss (Town Core):** Deepest level, permanent Otherworld.
        *   **Key Landmarks:** The God's chamber, manifestation pools, the original ritual site.
        *   **Primary Inhabitants:** The God of Guilt, ultimate manifestations, past visitors who failed.
        *   **Potential Random Encounters (x5):** Final boss for each character, the God's judgment, choice between illusion and truth, sacrifice opportunity, apocalyptic revelations.
        *   **Embedded Plot Hooks & Rumors (x3):** "The God can be bargained with." "Some visitors chose to stay willingly." "Destroying the God might trap you here forever."
        *   **Sensory Details:** Sight (Beyond comprehension, personal nightmares made physical), Sound (Heartbeat, screaming, silence), Smell (Everything and nothing).

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **Directive:** The World Simulation Agent must implement the following trigger-based state changes. For each "IF" condition, the agent must pre-generate the narrative and environmental consequences for the "THEN" outcome.
*   **Triggers:**
    *   **IF:** A character's Guilt reaches 8 or higher.
    *   **THEN:** They trigger frequent Otherworld transitions and encounter Pyramid Head, who stalks them relentlessly. Generate personalized encounters where they must hide or flee, as he cannot be defeated.
    *   **IF:** A character genuinely confesses their guilt to another party member (roleplay required).
    *   **THEN:** Reduce their Guilt by 2 points. Their personalized monsters become less aggressive. Grant a moment of respite.
    *   **IF:** A character denies their guilt when confronted by a manifestation.
    *   **THEN:** Increase Guilt by 1. Their monsters become more aggressive and numerous. The town becomes more hostile.
    *   **IF:** The air-raid siren sounds.
    *   **THEN:** Immediate transition to Otherworld for all characters. Generate chaotic transformation of environment and immediate monster threat.
    *   **IF:** A character achieves Guilt 0 through genuine repentance and acceptance.
    *   **THEN:** The town releases them. They find a path out of the fog. However, they carry the emotional weight of what they've accepted.
    *   **IF:** A character rejects escape and chooses to stay in Gray Veil.
