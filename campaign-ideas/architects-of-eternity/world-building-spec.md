**Architects of Eternity**

**1. Core Concept & Narrative Hook**
*   **Content:** The party leads a civilization from primitive settlements through six historical eras (Ancient → Classical → Medieval → Renaissance → Industrial → Modern), competing against rival civilizations for dominance. They must balance city building, technological research, cultural development, diplomatic negotiations, and military conquest to achieve one of five victory conditions: Domination, Science, Culture, Diplomacy, or Religion.

**2. Lore & History Primer**
*   **Prompts:**
    *   Detail the founding myth of the party's civilization and why they were chosen to lead.
    *   Write the major historical turning points: discovery of bronze, fall of empires, religious reformations, industrial revolution.
    *   Describe the previous age when ancient precursor civilizations existed and fell.
    *   Explain the World Congress's formation and how civilizations learned to cooperate.
    *   Detail the discovery of the New World and its impact on geopolitics.

**3. Faction Deep-Dive**
*   **Factions Roster:**
    *   **Player's Civilization** (Major): Custom civilization with chosen bonuses and unique units.
    *   **Rome (Militaristic)** (Major): Aggressive expansionist seeking Domination victory.
    *   **Egypt (Diplomatic)** (Major): Trade-focused civilization pursuing Diplomatic victory.
    *   **China (Scientific)** (Major): Technology-focused civilization racing for Science victory.
    *   **Greece (Cultural)** (Major): Culture and tourism focused pursuing Cultural victory.
    *   **Arabia (Religious)** (Major): Faith-focused civilization seeking Religious victory.
    *   **City-States** (Minor): Independent cities offering bonuses to allies: Scientific, Cultural, Trade, Military, Religious types.

**4. NPC Generation Roster**
*   **Tier 1:** Emperor Maximus (Rome - conqueror), Queen Cleopatra (Egypt - diplomat), Mahatma Gandhi (peaceful until nuclear), Catherine de Medici (spy mistress), Saladin (religious leader).
*   **Tier 2 & 3:** "Advisor" (Science/Culture/Military/Economy/Diplomacy specialists), "Great Person" (Scientists/Artists/Generals with unique abilities), "Rival Leader" (various personalities), "Barbarian Chieftain" (early game threats).

**5. Location Blueprints**
*   **Capital City:** Evolves through eras from settlement → ancient city → medieval town → renaissance center → industrial hub → modern metropolis. Contains all wonders and advanced buildings.
*   **Border Cities:** Frontier settlements with military focus, defending against rivals and barbarians.
*   **Holy City:** Religious center where party's faith was founded, generates faith and pilgrims.
*   **World Congress Hall:** Neutral meeting place where all civilizations vote on global policies.
*   **New World Continent:** Discovered mid-game, containing exotic city-states and resources.

**6. Causality Chains & Dynamic World States**
*   **IF:** Party enters new era first → **THEN:** Gains Golden Age bonuses and Era Score advantages.
*   **IF:** Rival achieves victory condition → **THEN:** Campaign ends in defeat; must prevent rivals from winning.
*   **IF:** War declared without casus belli → **THEN:** Warmonger penalty damages diplomatic relations with all civilizations.
*   **IF:** Wonder construction completed → **THEN:** Grants permanent bonus and denies rivals that wonder forever.
*   **IF:** Nuclear weapons used → **THEN:** Global warming intensifies, civilizations denounce user, terrain damaged.
*   **IF:** Religion spreads to rival cities → **THEN:** Gain pressure toward Religious victory and faith bonuses.
*   **IF:** Cultural tourism dominates rivals → **THEN:** Can flip their cities through loyalty pressure.
*   **IF:** World Congress vote won → **THEN:** Can achieve Diplomatic Victory or pass beneficial resolutions.
